# Philadelphia — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Philadelphia's economic indicators paint a nuanced picture, with a mix of positive and negative signals. The labour market shows a slight deterioration, with the unemployment rate rising to 4.0% - a 0.6 percentage point increase year-over-year. However, the civilian labour force has expanded by 0.93% over the past year, indicating a modest increase in workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have grown by 1.91% year-over-year, a relatively subdued pace that may contribute to the city's moderate cost of living, with a composite score of 3.0.

Business activity in Philadelphia appears robust, with a strong office worker ratio composite score of 3.52 and a labour demand composite score of 6.77. Nonfarm payroll employment has increased by 1.16% year-over-year, albeit at a slower pace than some other major cities. The residential construction sector is also showing signs of growth, with building permits rising by 4.14% year-over-year. The housing market remains relatively strong, with a days on market composite score of 8.03 and a median of 43.0 days, although the latter has increased by 10.26% year-over-year, potentially indicating slowing sales activity.

Despite these positive trends, the city's economy also faces challenges. The weekly hours deviation from its 12-month baseline stands at 0.844%, suggesting some volatility in labour demand. Furthermore, the moderate cost of living and rising unemployment rate may pose concerns for residents. The city's economic growth is currently moderate, driven by strong labour demand and business activity, but tempered by a rising unemployment rate and subdued earnings growth.

Looking ahead, Philadelphia's economic prospects are likely to remain steady, driven by its robust labour demand and business activity. To achieve more robust expansion, however, the city will need to address its rising unemployment rate and moderate earnings growth. With a relatively strong housing market and moderate cost of living, Philadelphia is well-positioned to attract new residents and businesses, but careful management of its economic challenges will be crucial to realizing its full potential.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Philadelphia's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, with a year-over-year increase of 0.6 percentage points, suggesting a slight deterioration in job market conditions. However, the civilian labor force has grown by 0.93% over the past year, indicating a modest expansion in workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have risen by 1.91% year-over-year, a relatively subdued pace that may be contributing to the city's moderate cost of living composite score of 3.0.

In terms of business activity, Philadelphia's office worker ratio composite score is a strong 3.52, and the labor demand composite score is a robust 6.77, suggesting a healthy demand for labor. Nonfarm payroll employment has grown by 1.16% year-over-year, a slower pace than some other major cities. The city's residential building permits have increased by 4.14% year-over-year, indicating a moderate pace of new construction. The housing market appears to be relatively strong, with a days on market composite score of 8.03 and a median days on market of 43.0, although the latter has risen by 10.26% year-over-year, which may be a sign of slowing sales activity.

Despite these positive trends, there are some stress points in the Philadelphia economy. The city's weekly hours deviation from its 12-month baseline is a relatively high 0.844%, which may indicate some volatility in labor demand. Additionally, the cost of living composite score, while moderate, may still be a challenge for some residents. Overall, Philadelphia's economy appears to be growing at a moderate pace, with some strengths in labor demand and business activity, but also some areas of concern, such as the rising unemployment rate and slowing earnings growth.

Closing outlook: Philadelphia's economic prospects are likely to remain steady, driven by its strong labor demand and business activity. However, the city will need to address its rising unemployment rate and moderate earnings growth to achieve more robust expansion. With its relatively strong housing market and moderate cost of living, Philadelphia is well-positioned to attract new residents and businesses, but it will require careful management of its economic challenges to realize its full potential.
