Philadelphia's overall economic grade of "B-" with a weighted percentile of 42.1% reflects a below-average performance, highlighting the need for continued economic diversification and support.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Philadelphia's economic landscape, as of the latest data, reflects a mixed picture with both resilient and vulnerable aspects. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, slightly above the national average, placing it in the 14th percentile, which suggests a relatively weak labor market compared to other metropolitan areas. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.89% is more in line with broader trends, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth is modest, with a year-over-year increase of just 1.01%, ranking in the 6th percentile, which may reflect subdued wage pressures or a shift towards lower-paying jobs. On a more positive note, the cost of living, measured by the Consumer Price Index, is relatively low at 7.56%, placing it in the 2nd percentile, which could provide some relief to residents.

The housing market in Philadelphia shows signs of strength, with a year-over-year increase in home prices of 14.33%, ranking in the 66th percentile. This growth, coupled with a relatively low number of days on the market (43 days), suggests a robust demand for housing. However, the decline in building permits by 0.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 48th percentile, could indicate a slowdown in new construction, which might limit future housing supply and potentially drive prices higher. Business activity, as measured by the weekly hours worked, is steady at 34.6 hours, ranking in the 66th percentile, suggesting that firms are maintaining their current levels of employment and production. The overall economic grade of "B-" with a weighted percentile of 42.1% reflects a below-average performance, highlighting the need for continued economic diversification and support.

Looking ahead, Philadelphia's economy will need to address the challenges of a relatively high unemployment rate and modest earnings growth while capitalizing on its strong housing market and stable business activity. Policies aimed at fostering job creation, particularly in higher-wage sectors, and ensuring a balanced housing supply could help sustain and improve the city's economic trajectory.
