Seattle's economy, as represented by the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area, is marked by a mix of robust and moderating trends. The labour market remains strong, with an unemployment rate of 4.6%, slightly above the national average, but supported by a high labour force participation rate of 68.19%, placing it in the 82nd percentile nationally. The housing market is a complex story. While the year-over-year change in the housing price index is slightly negative at -0.09%, suggesting a cooling in prices, the number of building permits issued has increased by 14.29% year-over-year, indicating continued investment in new housing stock. This is a positive sign for addressing the region's housing affordability issues. The median days on market for homes is 48 days, a relatively active market, albeit higher than the peak of the housing boom. Commercial real estate demand is also showing modest growth, with the year-over-year change in the price of office space up by 0.08%.

Looking ahead, Seattle's economy appears resilient but faces challenges. The strong labour market and wage growth are positive indicators, but rising costs and moderating housing prices could impact consumer and business sentiment. To sustain its economic momentum and maintain its attractiveness as a hub for innovation and business, the city will need to address these issues.
