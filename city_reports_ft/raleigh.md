## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Raleigh-Cary's economy presents a mixed picture, with strong labor market fundamentals but some signs of cooling in the housing sector. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, well below the national average, and the labor force participation rate is robust at 67.45%, indicating a healthy job market. Earnings growth has been solid, up 6.01% year-over-year, outpacing inflation, which is currently at 4.77%. However, weekly hours worked have declined to 33.4 hours, suggesting a slight moderation in work intensity that could be a short-term trend influenced by seasonal factors or industry-specific changes.

The housing market, while still showing strength, is experiencing some softening. Home prices have increased by 10.75% year-over-year, but building permits have fallen by 28.25%, signaling a potential slowdown in new construction. This decline in permits, coupled with a slight decrease in the pace of sales (-0.9% year-over-year) and an increase in days on market to 60, suggests that the rapid appreciation in home values may be moderating. Business activity remains stable, with the overall economic grade for Raleigh-Cary at B, reflecting average performance across key indicators.

Looking ahead, Raleigh-Cary's diversified economy, anchored by technology, education, and healthcare sectors, positions it well to weather potential economic headwinds. However, continued monitoring of the housing market and labor dynamics will be crucial, as these sectors can significantly influence broader economic outcomes.
