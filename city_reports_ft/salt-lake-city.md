## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economy exhibits a mix of robust fundamentals and emerging challenges, positioning it as an above-average performer among US metropolitan areas. The city boasts a strong labor market, with an unemployment rate of 3.8%, placing it in the 74th percentile nationally. This is complemented by a high labor force participation rate of 72.53%, significantly outpacing many peers and reflecting a vibrant workforce. However, earnings growth has been modest, increasing by 3.22% year-over-year, which is below the national median. The business environment remains supportive, with building permits up 36.31% year-over-year, indicating ongoing investment in residential and commercial development. Despite this, the housing market shows signs of cooling, with home prices growing at a more moderate 4.42% year-over-year, down from double-digit gains in recent years. The average days on market for homes has also increased to 59 days, suggesting a slight softening in demand.

On the downside, Salt Lake City faces pressures in the rental market, with rents rising by 5.31% year-over-year, potentially straining affordability for lower-income residents. Additionally, the city's industrial real estate market has seen a decline in square footage leased, falling by 0.76% year-over-year, which could signal a slowdown in manufacturing and logistics activities. Despite these challenges, the city's strong labor market and continued business investment suggest resilience. The high labor force participation rate and strong building permit growth indicate that Salt Lake City remains an attractive location for both workers and businesses.

**Outlook:** While Salt Lake City's economy faces some near-term headwinds, particularly in the housing and rental markets, its solid labor market and active business environment provide a foundation for sustained growth. Policymakers will need to address affordability issues to ensure that the benefits of economic expansion are broadly shared. Over the longer term, maintaining this balance will be crucial for the city's continued prosperity.
