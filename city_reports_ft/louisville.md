# Louisville — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

The economic landscape of Louisville is characterized by a mix of positive and negative indicators. The labor market shows signs of improvement, with the unemployment rate at 3.1%, down 1.3 percentage points year-over-year. Although the growth in the civilian labor force has been modest, at 0.38% year-over-year, average hourly earnings have increased by 2.67% over the past year, suggesting upward pressure on wages.

Business activity is more subdued, with nonfarm payroll employment declining 0.06% year-over-year. The labor demand composite score stands at 4.22, while the weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is -0.665%, indicating some softening in labor demand. In contrast, residential building permits have risen 2.98% year-over-year, pointing to a moderate expansion in construction activity. However, the median days on market has increased 9.76% year-over-year to 45 days, which may signal a slight slowdown in the housing market.

Louisville's cost of living, with a composite score of 2.77, is around the national midpoint, indicating a relatively average cost of living. The office worker ratio composite score is 2.22, suggesting a moderate level of office-based employment. While the city's economy faces challenges, particularly in labor demand and housing market momentum, the decline in unemployment and earnings growth are positive signals.

Looking ahead, Louisville's economy is expected to maintain a moderate growth trajectory, driven by its relatively stable labor market and average cost of living. To achieve more robust growth, however, the city will need to address the softening in labor demand and housing market momentum.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of Louisville presents a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals emerging from the data. On the labor market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, with a notable decline of 1.3 percentage points year-over-year. This suggests a degree of improvement in the job market, although the growth in the civilian labor force has been modest, at 0.38% year-over-year. Average hourly earnings have also increased by 2.67% over the past year, indicating some upward pressure on wages.

In terms of business activity, the labor demand composite score is 4.22, while nonfarm payroll employment has experienced a slight decline of 0.06% year-over-year. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is -0.665%, which may indicate some softening in labor demand. On the housing front, residential building permits have increased by 2.98% year-over-year, which could suggest a moderate expansion in construction activity. However, the median days on market has risen by 9.76% year-over-year, to a current level of 45 days, which may indicate a slight slowdown in the housing market.

The cost of living composite score is 2.77, which is around the midpoint of the national range, suggesting that Louisville's cost of living is relatively average. The office worker ratio composite score is 2.22, indicating a moderate level of office-based employment. Overall, while Louisville's economy appears to be experiencing some challenges, particularly in terms of labor demand and housing market momentum, the decline in unemployment and growth in earnings are positive signals. The city's economic outlook is likely to be shaped by its ability to balance these competing trends and foster sustainable growth in key sectors.

Closing outlook: Louisville's economy is expected to continue on a moderate growth trajectory, driven by its relatively stable labor market and average cost of living. However, the city will need to address the softening in labor demand and housing market momentum to achieve more robust growth and development.
