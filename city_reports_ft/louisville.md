## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Louisville-Jefferson County's economic landscape presents a mixed picture, with some robust indicators and others that suggest caution. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, slightly above the national average, placing it in the 66th percentile among metropolitan areas. This suggests a relatively healthy labor market, though the labor force participation rate of 63.02% is lower, ranking in the 30th percentile, which may indicate underutilization of the workforce or structural issues. Earnings growth is modest at 2.51% year-over-year, also in the 30th percentile, reflecting moderate wage pressures. The cost of living is 9% below the national average, a significant advantage for residents and businesses, and the overall wage-to-cost ratio is favorable, ranking in the 90th percentile.

Housing market dynamics are particularly noteworthy. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is robust at 16.45%, placing Louisville in the 76th percentile, which could be a sign of strong demand or limited supply. However, the pace of new housing construction, as indicated by a 18.48% increase in building permits, is also strong, ranking in the 82nd percentile, suggesting efforts to meet this demand. The days on market for homes have increased to 41 days, which is in the 78th percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. Business activity, as measured by the weekly hours worked (34.2 hours), is moderate, ranking in the 52nd percentile, and the professional services sector has seen a modest 2.3% growth in employment, placing it in the 84th percentile.

Looking ahead, Louisville's economic trajectory appears stable but not without challenges. The city's competitive cost of living and strong housing market fundamentals are positive factors, but the need to boost labor force participation and wage growth will be crucial for sustained economic health. Addressing these areas could enhance the city's attractiveness for both residents and businesses, potentially driving further economic development.
