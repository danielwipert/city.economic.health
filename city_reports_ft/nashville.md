# Nashville — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Nashville's economic indicators paint a nuanced picture, with both encouraging and cautionary signals. The labor market, for instance, boasts a low unemployment rate of 3.0%, unchanged from the previous year. The civilian labor force has expanded by 3.42% year-over-year, indicating a steady supply of workers, while average hourly earnings have risen by 3.56% over the same period, suggesting moderate wage growth. However, the labor demand composite score of 6.34, coupled with a relatively modest employment growth rate of 1.03% year-over-year, may hint at underlying labor market weakness.

In the housing and business sectors, residential building permits have fallen by 8.94% year-over-year, potentially signaling a slowdown in construction activity. The median days on market for homes have increased by 12.77% year-over-year to 53 days, which may indicate a slight cooling of the housing market. On the other hand, Nashville's office worker ratio composite score of 3.42 suggests a robust presence of office workers, which could underpin business activity. The city's cost of living composite score of 3.07 is relatively low, making it an attractive location for businesses and workers.

Despite these mixed signals, Nashville's economy appears resilient. The low unemployment rate, moderate wage growth, and strong office worker presence are all positive factors. Nevertheless, the decline in residential building permits and slowing employment growth warrant close attention. Overall, the economic outlook for Nashville is cautiously optimistic, with a need for vigilance regarding potential labor market and housing sector weaknesses.

Looking ahead, Nashville's economy is expected to continue growing at a moderate pace, driven by its robust labor market and business activity. However, policymakers and businesses must remain aware of potential stress points, such as slowing employment growth and declining residential building permits, and take proactive steps to address these challenges and ensure the city's continued economic prosperity.
