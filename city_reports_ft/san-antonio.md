## Polished FT-style column

San Antonio's economic landscape presents a nuanced picture, with some indicators pointing to resilience and others highlighting areas of concern. The city's unemployment rate of 4.4% is slightly above the national average, suggesting a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.92% lags behind the national average, potentially indicating underutilization of the workforce.

On a more positive note, earnings growth has been robust, with a year-over-year increase of 5.09%, outpacing inflation and boosting purchasing power. The cost of living has risen by 4.5% year-over-year, but this increase is manageable given the strong earnings growth.

The housing market in San Antonio is experiencing a slowdown, with a 36.19% year-over-year decline in building permits reflecting a decrease in new construction. Despite this, the housing price index has increased by 5.46% year-over-year, indicating continued demand and limited supply. The extended days on market for homes, now averaging 74 days, may signal a softening in buyer interest or a shift in market dynamics.

Commercial real estate activity, as measured by the number of square feet of commercial space leased, has decreased by 3.3% year-over-year, suggesting a cautious approach by businesses in the face of economic uncertainties.

Looking ahead, San Antonio's economy faces both opportunities and challenges. The strong earnings growth and manageable cost of living provide a solid foundation for consumer spending and economic stability. However, the slowdown in housing and commercial real estate development poses a risk to broader economic momentum. Policymakers and business leaders will need to address the labor force participation rate and support sectors showing signs of stress to ensure sustained economic growth.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

San Antonio's economic landscape presents a mixed picture, with some indicators pointing to resilience while others highlight areas of concern. The city's unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, slightly above the national average, indicating a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.92% is below the national average, suggesting a potential underutilization of the workforce. On a positive note, earnings growth has been robust, with a year-over-year increase of 5.09%, outpacing inflation and enhancing purchasing power. The cost of living has risen by 4.5% year-over-year, which, while significant, is manageable given the earnings growth.

The housing market in San Antonio shows signs of cooling, with a 36.19% year-over-year decline in building permits, reflecting a slowdown in new construction. Despite this, the housing price index has increased by 5.46% year-over-year, indicating continued demand and limited supply. The days on market for homes have extended to 74 days, up from previous levels, which may signal a softening in buyer interest or a shift in market dynamics. Business activity, as measured by the number of square feet of commercial space leased, has decreased by 3.3% year-over-year, suggesting a cautious approach by businesses in the face of economic uncertainties.

Looking ahead, San Antonio's economy faces both opportunities and challenges. The strong earnings growth and manageable cost of living provide a solid foundation for consumer spending and economic stability. However, the slowdown in housing and commercial real estate development could dampen broader economic momentum. Policymakers and business leaders will need to address the labor force participation rate and support sectors that are showing signs of stress to ensure sustained economic growth.
