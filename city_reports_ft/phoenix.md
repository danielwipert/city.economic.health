# Phoenix — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

The Phoenix economy presents a nuanced picture, with key indicators yielding both positive and negative signals. The labour market, for instance, has seen its unemployment rate rise to 3.5%, a 0.4 percentage point increase year-over-year. However, this uptick is offset by a 2.36% expansion in the civilian labour force, indicating a still-growing workforce. Moreover, average hourly earnings have surged 5.8% year-over-year, underscoring a robust wage growth trend.

In terms of business activity, Phoenix boasts a strong office worker ratio composite score of 92%, suggesting a favourable environment for office-based employment. In contrast, the labour demand composite score is less impressive, at 38%, which may signal labour market stress points. The housing market is experiencing growth, with residential building permits up 16.86% year-over-year. Nevertheless, the median days on market have increased to 54 days, a 5.88% year-over-year rise, potentially indicating slowing demand or increasing supply.

Phoenix's cost of living remains relatively low, with a composite score of 2.1, a positive factor for attracting businesses and talent. Despite mixed signals, the overall economic outlook for Phoenix appears stable, with no major concerns. The city's ability to balance labour market growth with wage increases, combined with its relatively low cost of living, will be crucial in maintaining economic momentum. As Phoenix navigates its labour market and housing sector complexities, its strong office worker ratio and manageable cost of living are likely to remain key strengths.

Looking ahead, Phoenix's economy is expected to continue on a stable trajectory, driven by strong wage growth, a favourable office employment environment, and a relatively low cost of living. To ensure sustained growth, the city must closely monitor its labour market and housing sector, addressing potential stress points as they arise.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Phoenix economy presents a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals emerging from its key indicators. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, having increased by 0.4 percentage points year-over-year. However, this rise is accompanied by a 2.36% growth in the civilian labour force, suggesting that the workforce supply is still expanding. Average hourly earnings have also seen a notable increase of 5.8% year-over-year, indicating a strong wage growth trend.

In terms of business activity, Phoenix's office worker ratio composite score is a notable strength, ranking high at 92%. This suggests a favourable environment for office-based employment and economic activity. The labour demand composite score, however, is less impressive, coming in at 38%, which may indicate some stress points in the labour market. The city's housing market is also experiencing some growth, with residential building permits increasing by 16.86% year-over-year. Nevertheless, the median days on market have risen to 54 days, representing a 5.88% year-over-year increase, which could be a sign of slowing demand or increasing supply.

The cost of living in Phoenix remains relatively manageable, with a composite score of 2.1, which is a positive factor for attracting businesses and talent. Despite some mixed signals, the overall economic outlook for Phoenix appears stable, with no major red flags. The city's ability to balance labour market growth with wage increases and its relatively low cost of living will be crucial in maintaining its economic momentum. As the city continues to navigate the complexities of its labour market and housing sector, its strong office worker ratio and manageable cost of living will likely remain key strengths.

Closing outlook: Phoenix's economy is expected to continue on a stable trajectory, driven by its strong wage growth, favourable office employment environment, and relatively low cost of living. However, the city will need to monitor its labour market and housing sector closely to address potential stress points and ensure sustained economic growth.
