# Minneapolis — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Minneapolis's economic indicators paint a nuanced picture, with a mix of positive and negative trends. The labour market is characterized by a moderate unemployment rate of 4.0%, which has risen by 1.6 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labour force has expanded at a modest 0.64% pace over the same period, indicating a relatively stable workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have increased by 1.77% year-over-year, suggesting upward pressure on wages. However, the city's affordability is a concern, as reflected in a cost of living composite score of 2.74.

Business activity in Minneapolis is showing signs of stress, with nonfarm payroll employment growth slowing to 0.55% year-over-year. The labour demand composite score of 4.8 indicates a relatively weak employment signal, while a decline in weekly hours of 0.271% from the 12-month baseline suggests a slight reduction in working hours. The office worker ratio composite score of 2.12 is also relatively low, pointing to potential challenges in the city's office market. In contrast, the residential housing market exhibits some strength, with a median days on market of 36 days, although this has lengthened by 9.09% year-over-year. Residential building permits have declined by 12.25% year-over-year, which may indicate caution in the housing market.

The performance of the housing market is a notable aspect of Minneapolis's economy, with a high days on market composite score of 68. However, the decline in residential building permits and the increase in median days on market year-over-year suggest potential stress points. Overall, Minneapolis's economic indicators point to a city facing challenges, particularly in the labour market and business activity. While rising average hourly earnings are a positive signal, affordability concerns and slowing employment growth are significant weaknesses.

Looking ahead, Minneapolis's economy is likely to face headwinds in the short term, driven by slowing employment growth and affordability concerns. Nevertheless, the city's relatively stable workforce supply and rising wages may provide some support for the labour market. Policymakers and businesses will need to closely monitor the city's economic indicators to address the challenges and capitalize on the strengths, particularly in the housing market.
