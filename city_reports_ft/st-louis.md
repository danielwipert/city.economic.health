St. Louis, the 23rd-ranked major U.S. metropolitan area, presents a mixed economic picture. The city's unemployment rate of 4.5% is slightly above the national average, while its labor force participation rate of 65.69% is robust, placing it in the 68th percentile among peers. The city's overall economic health is rated as average, with a weighted percentile score of 52.8 and a grade of B. The low cost of living and strong labor force participation are significant strengths, but the city will need to address the slow earnings growth and potential housing supply issues to sustain its economic momentum.

To achieve sustained growth, St. Louis will need to focus on strategies to boost earnings and productivity while ensuring that the housing market remains accessible and responsive to demand. Addressing these issues could help the city attract more businesses and talent, further solidifying its economic position.
