# Virginia Beach — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Virginia Beach's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative trends emerging. The city's unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, a relatively low level, although it has increased by 0.8 percentage points year-over-year. This rise is accompanied by a decline in the civilian labor force growth rate, which has fallen by 2.41% year-over-year, potentially signaling a slowdown in workforce supply growth. However, workers in the city are experiencing significant wage growth, with average hourly earnings rising by 11.8% year-over-year.

In terms of business activity, the office worker ratio composite score is 2.4, indicating a moderate level of office worker presence. The labor demand composite score is 1.72, suggesting some employment and weekly hours growth, albeit at a modest pace. While nonfarm payroll employment has declined by 1.34% year-over-year, which may indicate labor market stress, the weekly hours deviation from the city's 12-month baseline is a relatively small 0.705%. The cost of living composite score is 1.44, relatively high, suggesting affordability issues.

The housing market in Virginia Beach is also facing challenges. Residential building permits have declined by 46.79% year-over-year, and the days on market composite score is 6.47, indicating potential stress. However, the median days on market has decreased by 5.26% year-over-year to 36 days, which may suggest some demand for housing. Overall, Virginia Beach's economy has strengths, such as rising earnings, but also faces labor market and housing sector challenges that may impact its growth prospects.

The economic outlook for Virginia Beach is cautious, with policymakers and businesses needing to address the decline in workforce supply growth, nonfarm payroll employment, and residential building permits, as well as the high cost of living and stressed housing market. By focusing on these areas, they can work towards ensuring sustainable economic growth and development.
