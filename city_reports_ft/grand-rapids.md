## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Grand Rapids, a metropolitan area encompassing Wyoming and Kentwood, presents a mixed economic picture, reflecting both resilience and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, slightly above the national average, placing it in the 20th percentile for employment conditions. However, the labor force participation rate of 65.6% is robust, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth of 4.35% year-over-year is moderate, aligning with broader economic trends. The cost of living in Grand Rapids is relatively low, at 69% of the national average, which enhances the purchasing power of residents. Despite this, the over-the-week ratio of 1.74 suggests a modest but positive balance between job openings and hires, indicating a somewhat tight labor market.

In the housing sector, Grand Rapids has seen a significant increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 20.55%, placing it in the 90th percentile. This rapid appreciation is a double-edged sword, reflecting strong demand but also posing affordability challenges. The number of building permits has declined by 8.64% year-over-year, which could signal a slowdown in new housing supply, potentially exacerbating affordability issues. The median days on market for homes is 42 days, a reasonable timeframe that suggests a balanced market. Business activity, as measured by the weekly hours worked (34.8 hours), is solid, and the professional services and finance sectors are showing healthy growth, with a 5.0% year-over-year increase in professional services firms.

Looking ahead, Grand Rapids' economy appears to be on a stable trajectory, supported by a strong labor market and relatively low cost of living. However, the rapid increase in home prices and the decline in building permits suggest that the housing market may face challenges in maintaining affordability. Policymakers and businesses will need to address these issues to ensure sustainable growth and maintain the city's appeal as a place to live and work.
