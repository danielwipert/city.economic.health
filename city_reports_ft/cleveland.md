## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Cleveland's economic landscape presents a mixed picture, with some indicators pointing to resilience while others highlight ongoing challenges. The city's unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, suggesting a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is significantly lower, ranking in the 2nd percentile, which may indicate structural issues such as an aging population or skill mismatches. Earnings growth is modest at 1.36% year-over-year, placing Cleveland in the 10th percentile, reflecting a slower recovery in wage growth compared to other metropolitan areas. On the positive side, the city's housing market shows strength, with a 4.67% year-over-year increase in home prices and a 12.86% rise in the price per square foot, both well above national averages. The median days on market for homes is 55 days, indicating a balanced market with neither a surplus nor a shortage of housing.

Business activity in Cleveland is also showing signs of improvement, with building permits up 14.96% year-over-year, a positive signal for future economic growth and investment. However, the city's overall economic performance, as measured by a weighted percentile score of 53.7, suggests an average standing, with room for improvement in several key areas. The labor market, while strong in terms of unemployment, needs to address low participation rates to fully capitalize on economic opportunities.

Looking ahead, Cleveland's economic trajectory will depend on its ability to enhance labor force participation and accelerate wage growth. The housing market's strength is a positive factor, but it will be crucial to ensure that this growth is sustainable and inclusive. Addressing these challenges will be key to solidifying Cleveland's economic position and fostering long-term prosperity.
