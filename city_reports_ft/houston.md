# Houston — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Houston's economic indicators paint a nuanced picture, with both encouraging and cautionary signals. The labor market, for instance, shows a mixed trend: the unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, up 0.2 percentage points year-over-year, but this increase is tempered by a 0.93% year-over-year growth in the civilian labor force. Furthermore, average hourly earnings have risen 4.45% over the past year, indicating wage growth for workers. The labor demand composite score of 5.54, combined with a 0.43% year-over-year increase in nonfarm payroll employment, suggests a relatively robust job market.

In contrast, the housing sector is experiencing a slowdown, with residential building permits declining 12.89% year-over-year and median days on market increasing 11.11% to 50 days. This could signal a cooling market, potentially leading to more favorable conditions for buyers. While the cost of living composite score of 1.08 is relatively high, the city's strong office worker ratio composite score of 1.14 indicates a vibrant business environment. A weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline of 0.639% may suggest some labor market stress, although its impact is unclear without further context.

The city's business activity is a notable strength, driven by a high office worker ratio composite score. However, the decline in residential building permits and the increase in median days on market are potential stress points that warrant monitoring. Overall, Houston's economy appears to be experiencing moderate growth, with areas of concern that require attention. The key to sustaining economic momentum will be the city's ability to balance labor market growth with housing market dynamics.

Looking ahead, Houston's economic prospects are cautiously positive, driven by a robust labor market and strong business activity. To ensure sustained growth and maintain its competitive edge, the city must address potential stress points, particularly the slowdown in the housing sector.
