Riverside, part of the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area, exhibits a mixed economic profile with notable strengths and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 6.1%, placing it in the lower 2nd percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.02% is modest, suggesting some underutilization of the workforce. On a positive note, earnings growth is robust at 8.72% year-over-year, significantly outpacing the national average and placing Riverside in the 90th percentile. This strong earnings growth is a key driver of the local economy, supporting consumer spending and overall economic activity.

The housing market in Riverside shows signs of both strength and stress. The housing price index has increased by 9.42% year-over-year, reflecting strong demand and limited supply. However, the number of building permits has declined by 18.53% year-over-year, indicating a slowdown in new construction that could exacerbate housing affordability issues. The median days on market is 63, which is relatively high, suggesting that while demand is strong, the market is not as brisk as in some other regions. Business activity, as measured by the cost of living and the owner-occupied housing rate, is moderate, with the cost of living at 5.43% above the national average and the owner-occupied rate at 1.3, both of which are within the lower half of national percentiles.

In the short term, Riverside's economy is supported by strong earnings growth and a relatively low unemployment rate. However, longer-term challenges include a declining rate of new housing construction and a modest labor force participation rate, which could limit economic expansion. The overall economic grade of C+ reflects these mixed signals, with the city needing to address housing supply and labor market participation to sustain its economic momentum.
