# Riverside — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

The Riverside economy presents a complex picture, with both positive and negative indicators. The labour market shows a mixed trend, with an unemployment rate of 5.1% and a modest year-over-year increase of 0.2 percentage points. However, this rise is somewhat offset by a 1.15% year-over-year growth in the civilian labour force, indicating a steady supply of workers. Moreover, average hourly earnings have increased by 7.55% year-over-year, suggesting meaningful wage growth for workers.

Business activity is subdued, with a labour demand composite score of 3.68 and nonfarm payroll employment growing at a sluggish 0.18% year-over-year pace. Additionally, working hours have declined slightly, with a -0.875% deviation from the 12-month baseline. Nevertheless, residential building permits have surged 18.04% year-over-year, potentially signalling renewed investment in the local housing market. Although the median days on market for homes has risen to 54 days, with a 5.88% year-over-year increase, this may indicate a slight cooling in the housing market.

Riverside's cost of living and office worker ratio composite scores, at 2.82 and 1.3 respectively, suggest challenges in terms of affordability and attracting high-skilled workers. Despite this, the city's overall economic grade of C+ and weighted percentile score of 41.5 indicate that the economy is not in distress. As the labour market and business activity continue to evolve, close monitoring of these indicators will be crucial to assessing the city's progress.

In conclusion, Riverside's economy is marked by a mix of positive and negative trends, with labour market and business activity signals warranting close attention. While the city faces challenges, the growth in residential building permits and average hourly earnings are encouraging signs. To ensure a more sustainable and balanced growth trajectory, policymakers and stakeholders must focus on addressing Riverside's affordability and labour market challenges.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Riverside economy presents a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals emerging from the data. On the labour market front, the unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, with a modest year-over-year increase of 0.2 percentage points. While this uptick may be a cause for concern, it is somewhat mitigated by the 1.15% year-over-year growth in the civilian labour force, indicating a steady supply of workers. Furthermore, average hourly earnings have risen by 7.55% year-over-year, suggesting that workers are experiencing meaningful wage growth.

In terms of business activity, the labour demand composite score of 3.68 suggests a relatively weak employment market, with nonfarm payroll employment growing at a sluggish 0.18% year-over-year pace. Additionally, the weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is -0.875%, indicating a slight decline in working hours. However, the residential building permits have seen a robust 18.04% year-over-year increase, which could be a sign of renewed investment in the local housing market. The median days on market for homes has risen to 54 days, with a 5.88% year-over-year increase, potentially indicating a slight cooling in the housing market.

The cost of living composite score of 2.82 and the office worker ratio composite score of 1.3 suggest that Riverside may face challenges in terms of affordability and attracting high-skilled workers. Nevertheless, the city's overall economic grade of C+ and weighted percentile score of 41.5 indicate that while there are areas for improvement, the economy is not in distress. As the labour market and business activity continue to evolve, it will be essential to monitor these indicators closely to gauge the city's progress.

Closing outlook: Riverside's economy is characterized by a mix of positive and negative trends, with labour market and business activity signals warranting close attention. While the city faces challenges, the growth in residential building permits and average hourly earnings are encouraging signs. As the economy navigates these crosswinds, policymakers and stakeholders will need to focus on addressing the city's affordability and labour market challenges to ensure a more sustainable and balanced growth trajectory.
