Economic Analysis

Memphis earns a B+ grade, driven by its above-average economic performance, with a low unemployment rate of 4.00% and modest nonfarm employment growth of +0.06% YoY. The city's cost advantage, with a cost of living composite score of 0.40, ranking it among the most affordable metros, combined with solid wage growth of +6.16% YoY, creates a positive environment for consumer spending and business operations.

However, beneath the surface, tensions emerge. The housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 11.3% YoY to 79 days, indicating a stabilizing force. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% is critically low, posing a significant structural constraint for businesses considering expansion or relocation. The office and professional worker share is also low, ranking in the 2nd percentile, which may hinder knowledge-economy businesses.

Memphis offers a unique combination of affordability and solid economic growth, making it an attractive location for companies seeking to minimize costs while maintaining access to a growing labor market. However, the low labor force participation rate and limited professional workforce pose significant risks that businesses must carefully consider when evaluating Memphis as a potential location.
