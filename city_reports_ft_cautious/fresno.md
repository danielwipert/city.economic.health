## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Fresno's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 7.5%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings, which may indicate a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 61.03% suggests that a significant portion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labor market, though this figure is below the median when compared to other metropolitan areas. Annual earnings growth of 4.88% is notably higher than the median, indicating some improvement in income levels. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.45%, which is slightly above the median, potentially eroding purchasing power.

Housing market dynamics in Fresno show robust activity, with a 61.97% year-over-year increase in building permits, significantly outpacing many other regions. This surge in construction permits may reflect strong demand for new housing or commercial properties. The housing price index has risen by 9.4% year-over-year, suggesting continued appreciation in property values. However, the supply of homes for sale has decreased by 1.01% over the same period, which could be contributing to the rising prices. The average days on market for properties is 58 days, which is moderate and indicates a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that while Fresno faces some economic challenges, particularly in employment and cost of living, there are positive signs in earnings growth and the housing market.
