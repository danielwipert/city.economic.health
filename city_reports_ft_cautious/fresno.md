Fresno's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 7.9%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings, which may reflect challenges in the local job market. The labor force participation rate of 60.66% is also below average, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market.

This suggests a strong demand for new housing. However, the supply of existing homes appears to be tightening, as indicated by the 1.01% decline in the number of properties for sale year-over-year.

Overall, the data indicates that while Fresno faces some economic challenges, particularly in employment and labor force participation, there are also signs of strength in earnings growth and the housing market.
