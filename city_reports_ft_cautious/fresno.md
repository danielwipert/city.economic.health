## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Fresno's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 7.9%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings, which may reflect challenges in the local job market. The labor force participation rate of 60.66% is also below average, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.59%, which is above the median, indicating some improvement in income levels.

Housing market dynamics in Fresno show contrasting trends. Building permits have increased by 61.97% year-over-year, a strong indicator of construction activity and potential economic expansion. However, the housing price index has risen by 9.4% over the same period, which, while positive, may reflect affordability concerns. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased slightly by 0.76%, and the average days on market is 59 days, which is relatively high and could indicate a slowdown in sales velocity.

Overall, the data suggests a cautiously optimistic yet uneven economic landscape in Fresno, with notable strengths in construction and earnings growth, but challenges in employment and labor force participation.
