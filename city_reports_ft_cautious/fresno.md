# Fresno — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Fresno's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 8.2%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile scores, which may reflect challenges in the local job market. The labour force participation rate of 60.33% is slightly above the median, indicating a moderate proportion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labour market.

Earnings growth year-over-year is 7.6%, ranking in the 90th percentile, indicating a significant increase in average earnings. This contrasts with the relatively low weekly hours worked, averaging 33.5 hours, which places the region in the 28th percentile. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 9.4%, and a notable rise in building permits by 61.97%, both of which rank in the upper percentiles. The days-on-market metric of 66 days indicates a moderate pace of sales.

Fresno's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be below average, with strengths in earnings growth and housing activity offset by higher unemployment and lower labour force participation.
