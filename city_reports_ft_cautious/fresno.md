## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Fresno's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 7.9%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings, which may reflect challenges in the local job market. The labor force participation rate of 60.66% is also below average, suggesting a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.59%, which is above the median, indicating some improvement in income levels. However, the cost of living increase of 5.44% suggests that wage growth may not be keeping pace with rising living costs.

Housing market indicators show a more dynamic trend. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 9.84%, which is above the median, indicating a strong housing market. However, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales is down by 0.76%, which may reflect a cooling in the sales market. The number of building permits issued has increased by 61.97% year-over-year, a significant rise that suggests ongoing construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 59 days, which is relatively moderate, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests a complex economic environment in Fresno, with some positive trends in earnings and housing prices, but challenges in employment and cost of living.
