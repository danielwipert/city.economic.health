# Fresno

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Fresno earns an overall grade of B, with its most important takeaway being rapid job creation amidst severe labor market weakness. With nonfarm employment growth at +1.86% YoY and labor force growth at +1.87% YoY, the city is experiencing an economically expanding market. The unemployment rate of 8.20% indicates that job growth has not been enough to absorb the existing labor force, posing a significant consumer spending risk.

The city's data reveals tensions, including a sharp decline in building permits, down 22.67% YoY, which signals a potential housing supply squeeze and affordability stress. This could lead to workforce attraction difficulties. The low concentration of professional and office workers, ranking in the 4th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses.

Fresno's rapid wage growth of +8.37% YoY presents a labor cost pressure for employers, while being excellent for workers. The city's severe labor market weakness and low professional worker concentration pose significant challenges. Businesses must carefully consider these factors when making location decisions, as Fresno offers an economically expanding market with rapid job creation, but also potential risks such as a housing supply squeeze and affordability stress.
