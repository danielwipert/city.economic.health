# Fresno — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Fresno's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 8.2%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings. The labour force participation rate of 60.33% is slightly above the median, suggesting that a moderate proportion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labour market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.6%, ranking in the 90th percentile. This is accompanied by a strong performance in housing, with a 61.97% increase in building permits year-over-year and an 11.92% rise in home prices, both of which are also in the 90th percentile. The weekly hours worked have decreased to 33.5 hours, below the median, and the cost of living index of 5.05 is near the middle of the distribution.

Fresno's economic landscape appears to be characterised by strong earnings and housing market dynamics, offset by higher unemployment and reduced working hours. The city's overall weighted score of 41 and grade of B- indicate a below-average economic performance relative to other metropolitan areas.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Fresno's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 8.2%, placing it at the lower end of the percentile rankings, which may reflect challenges in the local job market. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.33% is slightly above the median, suggesting that a moderate proportion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, increasing by 7.6%, which ranks in the 90th percentile, indicating a significant improvement in income levels. This is complemented by a strong performance in housing, with a 61.97% increase in building permits year-over-year and a 11.92% rise in home prices, both of which are also in the 90th percentile. These figures suggest a growing demand for housing and potential economic expansion. Conversely, the weekly hours worked have decreased to 33.5 hours, which is below the median, and the cost of living index of 5.05 is near the middle of the distribution, indicating average living costs.

Overall, Fresno's economic landscape appears to be characterized by strong earnings and housing market dynamics, offset by higher unemployment and reduced working hours. The city's overall weighted score of 41 and grade of B- indicate a below-average economic performance relative to other metropolitan areas.
