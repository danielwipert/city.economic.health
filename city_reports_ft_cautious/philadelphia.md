The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking at the 51.4th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong labor demand and labor force growth, scoring in the top tier at the 80th and 84th percentiles respectively. **Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate of +0.12% and weekly hours deviation of +1.451% from its own trend indicate a genuine demand expansion, suggesting that the city is experiencing an increase in jobs and hours worked, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to expand or relocate. This combination signals that the labor market is strong, with employers adding jobs and workers putting in more hours. The labor demand composite score of 5.92 further reinforces this notion, placing the city in the top tier.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate of 4.30% places the city near the median, indicating a relatively balanced labor market with neither too much slack nor too much tightness. This rate suggests that while it may not be overly difficult to find talent, the market is not so loose that it lacks competition for jobs. For a business trying to hire, this means a moderate level of challenge in finding the right candidates without excessive wage pressure.

This slow wage growth implies that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly, but it also means that worker purchasing power is not increasing, which could affect local consumer demand. For businesses, this could mean a flat cost environment, but it also suggests weaker bargaining power for workers.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +1.25% year-over-year, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This positive growth rate suggests that the city has a structural advantage in terms of hiring capacity, as the pool of potential workers is increasing. For businesses, this means a more favorable environment for finding and recruiting talent.

**Building Permits**
The year-over-year change in residential building permits is +3.44%, which is near the median, suggesting a modest expansion in housing supply. This growth indicates that developer confidence is present, and future housing supply is likely to increase, which could improve affordability and make the city more attractive for relocating workers. **Days on Market**
The current median days on market is 36 days, with a year-over-year increase of +2.9%, placing it near the median in terms of market speed. This indicates a relatively balanced market where homes are not selling excessively quickly, but also not lingering on the market for too long. For a worker relocating to this city, the market is neither overly competitive nor excessively slow, making it moderately accessible.

**Office Economy**
With an office and professional worker share composite score of 2.38, ranking at the 40th percentile, the city has a moderately deep talent pool in professional and office sectors. This suggests that the city is suited for businesses that require a knowledge-economy workforce, such as tech, finance, or consulting, but may not be as ideal for industries with different workforce needs.

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metro area offers businesses a strong labor demand and growing labor force, making it an attractive location for expansion or relocation. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers to factor in is the relatively high cost of living, which could necessitate wage premiums to attract and retain talent, potentially offsetting some of the benefits of the city's otherwise favorable economic conditions.
