# Philadelphia — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metropolitan area ranks 9th among major U.S. metros, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance across key economic indicators. The unemployment rate is 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, suggesting a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 63.96% is in the 42nd percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.21% is in the 34th percentile, suggesting modest wage increases that may reflect broader economic conditions.

In the housing market, the Philadelphia region has seen a 14.69% increase in home prices over the past year, placing it in the 72nd percentile, indicating a relatively robust housing market. However, building permits have declined by 0.99% year-over-year, in the 48th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 53 days, in the 54th percentile, suggesting a balanced market. The cost of living index is 5.4%, in the 32nd percentile, and the owner-occupied housing rate is 3.53%, in the 85.7th percentile, indicating moderate living costs and a high rate of homeownership.

Data from these indicators collectively shows a mixed economic environment in Philadelphia, with some areas performing better than others.
