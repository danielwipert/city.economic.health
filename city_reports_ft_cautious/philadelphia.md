# Philadelphia — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metropolitan area ranks 9th among major U.S. metros, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance across a range of economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally. This suggests that labor market conditions are neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 63.96% is in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.21% is in the 34th percentile, indicating modest wage increases.

Housing market indicators show mixed trends. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 14.69% places the region in the 72nd percentile, suggesting relatively robust appreciation. However, the number of building permits has declined by 0.99% year-over-year, ranking in the 48th percentile. This could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 64 days, in the 64th percentile, suggesting a balanced market. The cost of living index of 5.41% is in the 32nd percentile, indicating that living costs are rising at a moderate pace relative to other areas.

The data suggests that Philadelphia's economy is performing in line with national averages, with some sectors showing stronger performance than others.
