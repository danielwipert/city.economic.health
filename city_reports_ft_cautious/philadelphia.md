# Philadelphia — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metropolitan area's economic data shows a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, ranking it in the 34th percentile among comparable regions. This suggests that employment conditions are neither exceptionally strong nor particularly strained. The labor force participation rate of 63.96% is similarly positioned, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement.

Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.21% and a cost of living increase of 5.41% suggest that wage growth is lagging behind inflation. This may have implications for purchasing power. In the housing market, the year-over-year home price index increase of 14.25% places the region in the 70th percentile. This may reflect a combination of factors, including demand and supply dynamics. The number of building permits has increased by 1.56% year-over-year, ranking in the 50th percentile. The days on market for homes have remained at 64 days, which is within the 64th percentile, indicating a balanced market.

Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, ranking in the 80th percentile, suggesting a relatively high level of full-time employment. Overall, the data indicates that Philadelphia's economy is performing in line with national averages, with some areas showing strength and others indicating more moderate outcomes.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests that while employment conditions are not among the best, they are also not particularly strained. The labor force participation rate of 63.96% is similarly positioned, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.21% and a cost of living increase of 5.41% suggest that wage growth is lagging behind inflation, potentially affecting purchasing power.

In the housing market, the year-over-year home price index increase of 14.25% is relatively robust, placing the region in the 70th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. The number of building permits has increased by 1.56% year-over-year, aligning with the 50th percentile, suggesting a moderate pace of new construction activity. The days on market for homes have remained at 64 days, which is within the 64th percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, a figure that ranks in the 80th percentile, pointing to a relatively high level of full-time employment.

Overall, the data suggests that Philadelphia's economy is performing in line with national averages, with some areas showing strength and others indicating more moderate outcomes.
