# Sacramento — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. Several indicators show moderate growth, while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8% places the area in the 10th percentile, indicating a relatively tight labour market. However, this is not among the strongest in the country.

The labour force participation rate of 60.12% is slightly below average, ranking in the 8th percentile. This suggests a modest level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is reported at 2.48%, placing it in the 38th percentile. This indicates moderate wage increases that are neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions.

Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.98%, placing it in the 20th percentile. This may reflect a slightly lower cost environment relative to other areas. The housing market shows a year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.43%, placing it in the 48th percentile. There is also a decline in permits issued for new construction by 5.58%, placing it in the 58th percentile. This indicates a housing market that is expanding but with some caution in new development.

The data suggests a cautiously stable economic environment in Sacramento, with some areas of strength and others that may require further attention.
