# Sacramento — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth and others suggesting areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 60.12% is below average, ranking in the 8th percentile, suggesting that a smaller proportion of the working-age population is actively engaged in the labour market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 2.48%, placing the region in the 38th percentile, while consumer price inflation is at 6.04%, in the 20th percentile. This suggests that wage growth may not be keeping pace with rising costs. Housing market dynamics show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.43%, in the 48th percentile, and a decline in single-family home sales of 1.07%, in the 44th percentile. The number of building permits issued has increased by 5.58% year-over-year, placing the region in the 58th percentile, which may reflect ongoing demand for new housing. The median days on market for homes is 41 days, a relatively high figure in the 78th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in the pace of home sales.

The data indicates a stable economic environment in Sacramento, with some indicators pointing to modest growth and others indicating potential challenges.
