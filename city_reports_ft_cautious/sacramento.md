# Sacramento — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth and others suggesting areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 60.12% is below average, ranking in the 8th percentile, suggesting a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labour market. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 2.48%, placing the region in the 38th percentile, indicating steady but not robust wage increases.

Housing market indicators show a varied picture. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 8.43%, placing the region in the 48th percentile, suggesting a stable market. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction is 5.58%, ranking in the 58th percentile, indicating a modest expansion in building activity. The median days on market for homes is 41 days, placing the region in the 78th percentile, suggesting a slower pace of home sales. The year-over-year change in the price-to-rent ratio is negative at -1.07%, ranking in the 44th percentile, which may reflect a slight easing in housing affordability pressures.

The data suggests that the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metro area is experiencing a period of moderate economic activity with some areas of strength and others of potential concern.
