# Sacramento — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 60.12% is below the national median, ranking in the 8th percentile, suggesting a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labour market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate, at 2.48%, placing the region in the 38th percentile. The cost of living increase of 5.98% is slightly above the national average, ranking in the 20th percentile. This disparity may indicate a challenge for local residents in maintaining their purchasing power. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in housing prices of 3.1%, and a decline in single-family home sales of 1.36%, both of which are near the middle of the national distribution. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 65 days, suggesting a potential slowdown in the pace of sales.

Building permits have risen by 19.19% year-over-year, placing the region in the 82nd percentile, indicating robust construction activity. The weighted percentile score of 27.4 and a grade of "C" suggest that while some sectors are performing well, the broader economic environment in Sacramento remains challenging.
