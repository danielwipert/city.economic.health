## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth while others indicate contraction. The unemployment rate stands at 5.4%, placing the region in the 8th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 60.62% is also below the national median, as indicated by its 16th percentile ranking. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.1% is moderate, ranking in the 48th percentile, suggesting that wage increases are keeping pace with but not exceeding broader economic trends.

Housing market indicators show a more complex scenario. The year-over-year change in housing prices is 3.1%, a positive but modest increase, placing the region in the 32nd percentile. However, the number of building permits has declined by 30.14% year-over-year, a significant drop that ranks in the 14th percentile, indicating a slowdown in construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 58 days, which is in the 36th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The overall weighted percentile score of 19.9 and the grade of C- reflect a critical assessment of the region's economic health, highlighting areas of concern alongside stable indicators.

These data points suggest that while Sacramento's economy is experiencing some growth, particularly in wages and housing prices, there are notable challenges, especially in employment and construction activity.
