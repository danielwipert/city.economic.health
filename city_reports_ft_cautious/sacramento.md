## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth while others indicate contraction. The unemployment rate stands at 5.2%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, which suggests that joblessness remains relatively high compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 60.54% is also below average, ranking in the 14th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment.

Earnings growth over the past year has been positive at 4.36%, placing the region in the 44th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases. However, the cost of living has risen by 5.66%, which may erode the real value of these wage gains. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 3.1%, but a decline in single-family home sales of 1.83%. Additionally, building permits have decreased by 30.14% year-over-year, which may reflect a slowdown in construction activity. The average number of days homes remain on the market is 59 days, which is in the 40th percentile, suggesting a balanced market with neither a significant buyer nor seller advantage.

Overall, the data indicates a region experiencing moderate economic conditions with some areas of strength and others of concern.
