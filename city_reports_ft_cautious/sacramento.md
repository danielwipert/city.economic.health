The economic data for the Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative stability while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.4%, placing it in the 8th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests that joblessness remains a significant issue. Labor force participation is at 60.62%, also ranking low at the 16th percentile, indicating a relatively smaller proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. However, the number of building permits has declined by 30.14% year-over-year, a significant drop that ranks in the 14th percentile, potentially signaling a slowdown in construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 58 days, in the 36th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of home sales. Additionally, the cost of living index is at 5.65, in the 20th percentile, and the weekly hours worked average 33.3 hours, both of which rank relatively low, suggesting that living costs and work hours are below average compared to other regions.

Overall, the data suggests a cautiously stable economic environment with several areas of concern, particularly in employment and construction activity.
