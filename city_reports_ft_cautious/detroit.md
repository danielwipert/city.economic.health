## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Detroit-Warren-Dearborn metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, placing it near the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, as indicated by its percentile score of 48. Labor force participation is notably lower, at 60.93%, ranking in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect underlying challenges in the local job market. Earnings have increased by 3.04% year-over-year, aligning closely with the median performance across similar regions.

Housing market dynamics show a more positive trend, with building permits up by 57.77% year-over-year, a significant increase that places Detroit in the 88th percentile. This suggests a robust level of construction activity, potentially driven by demand for new housing. However, the housing price index has grown by only 3.7% over the same period, indicating a more moderate pace of appreciation. The number of days properties remain on the market has decreased to 44 days, suggesting a relatively active market, though this metric ranks in the 82nd percentile, indicating it is still higher than in many other markets.

Overall, the data indicates a region experiencing varied economic conditions, with strengths in housing construction and labor earnings, but facing challenges in labor force participation.
