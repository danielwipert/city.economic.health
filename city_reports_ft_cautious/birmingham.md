# Birmingham — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Birmingham's economic data presents a mixed picture. The city's unemployment rate of 2.2% places it in the 98th percentile, indicating a low joblessness rate. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not in the labor force. Earnings have decreased by 0.12% year-over-year, while the cost of living index is 7.12, ranking in the 12th percentile.

In the housing sector, Birmingham has seen a 14.39% increase in home prices year-over-year, placing it in the 70th percentile. This contrasts with a 43.07% increase in building permits, which ranks in the 88th percentile. The average days on market for homes is 78 days, in the 18th percentile. Weekly hours worked stand at 35.5 hours, in the 92nd percentile.

Birmingham's economic indicators suggest a region with a strong labor market and a growing housing sector. However, challenges remain in terms of labor force participation and earnings growth.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Birmingham's economic landscape, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture. The city's unemployment rate stands at 2.2%, placing it in the 98th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market with low joblessness. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force. Earnings have shown a slight decline year-over-year, decreasing by 0.12%, while the cost of living index is relatively moderate at 7.12, ranking in the 12th percentile. These figures may reflect a complex interplay between employment opportunities and broader economic conditions.

In the housing sector, Birmingham has seen a 14.39% increase in home prices year-over-year, placing it in the 70th percentile, suggesting a strong demand for residential properties. This contrasts with a 43.07% increase in building permits, which ranks in the 88th percentile, potentially indicating a response to this demand through increased construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 78 days, in the 18th percentile, which could suggest a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers hold a significant advantage. Weekly hours worked stand at 35.5 hours, in the 92nd percentile, indicating a relatively high level of full-time employment.

Overall, Birmingham's economic indicators suggest a region with a strong labor market and growing housing sector, though challenges remain in terms of labor force participation and earnings growth.
