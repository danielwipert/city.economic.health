# Birmingham — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Birmingham's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength and others lagging behind national trends. The city's unemployment rate stands at 2.2%, placing it in the 98th percentile, indicating a robust job market in terms of availability. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, suggesting a relatively low proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decrease of 0.12%, ranking in the 8th percentile, which may reflect challenges in wage growth or economic diversification.

In the housing sector, Birmingham's housing price index has increased by 14.39% year-over-year, placing it in the 70th percentile, suggesting strong demand for residential properties. This contrasts with a decline in the price per square foot, down 0.21% year-over-year, which ranks in the 58th percentile and may indicate shifts in the types of properties being sold or changes in market preferences. Construction activity appears robust, with building permits up 43.07% year-over-year, placing the city in the 88th percentile, which could signal confidence in future economic prospects. Properties are taking longer to sell, with an average of 70 days on the market, placing the city in the 18th percentile, which may reflect a mismatch between supply and demand or other market dynamics.

Birmingham's economic performance is characterized by a strong labor market and housing demand, but with challenges in labor force participation and wage growth. The city's weighted score of 59 out of 100, corresponding to a grade of B, indicates an average economic performance relative to other metropolitan areas.
