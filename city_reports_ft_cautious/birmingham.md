Birmingham's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and areas for concern. The city's unemployment rate stands at 2.5%, placing it in the 98th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market with low joblessness. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.1% is in the 8th percentile, indicating a relatively low share of the working-age population engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 2.45%, placing it in the 28th percentile, which is moderate compared to other regions. The cost of living index at 4.97 is in the 48th percentile, suggesting average living costs relative to the national average.

In the housing market, the year-over-year increase in home prices is 15.41%, placing it in the 70th percentile, which indicates a strong appreciation in property values. The year-over-year change in permits for new housing units is 1.56%, in the 52nd percentile, suggesting a moderate pace of new construction. The average number of days a property remains on the market is 60 days, in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a balanced market with neither a surplus nor a shortage of available homes. Weekly hours worked average 35.3 hours, in the 86th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of full-time employment.

Overall, Birmingham's economic profile is characterized by a strong labor market and housing market, but with some indicators suggesting areas for improvement in labor force participation and earnings growth.
