# Birmingham

**Grade: A (Very Good) | 63.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Birmingham earns an A grade, driven by its strong labor market with an unemployment rate of 2.20% and a 0.52% year-over-year increase in nonfarm employment. This indicates intense competition for hiring and significant leverage for workers. The city's 63.7th percentile ranking out of 50 US metros underscores its attractiveness as a business location.

The city's housing market is competitive, with homes selling at an average of 56 days on the market, although days on market have increased by 3.7% year-over-year. A 55.25% year-over-year increase in building permits indicates aggressive expansion of housing supply. This growth, combined with strong employment numbers, supports the city's economic momentum.

Birmingham offers businesses a dynamic and expanding economy, making it an attractive location for those willing to compete for talent. However, stagnant wage growth, with a -0.46% year-over-year change, poses a risk to consumer spending and the sustainability of the city's economic momentum.
