# Birmingham — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Birmingham's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 2.2%, placing it in the 98th percentile, indicating a low level of unemployment relative to other regions. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, suggesting a smaller proportion of the working-age population is actively engaged in the labor market compared to other areas. Earnings have shown a slight year-over-year decline of 0.12%, ranking in the 8th percentile.

In the housing market, Birmingham has seen a year-over-year increase in home prices of 14.39%, placing it in the 70th percentile. This contrasts with a modest decline in the price per square foot, down 0.21% year-over-year, in the 58th percentile. The number of building permits has increased by 43.07% year-over-year, ranking in the 88th percentile, indicating strong construction activity. Homes are taking longer to sell, with an average of 70 days on the market, in the 18th percentile.

The data presents a picture of a region with low unemployment and active construction, but with moderate labor force participation and wage growth, and a housing market characterized by rising prices and longer listing durations.
