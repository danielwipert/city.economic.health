# Birmingham — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Birmingham's economic indicators point to a robust labour market, with a low unemployment rate of 2.2% and a year-over-year decline of 0.8 percentage points. The civilian labour force has expanded by 1.06% over the past year, ensuring a steady supply of workers. Although average hourly earnings have risen by 1.16% year-over-year, this growth is relatively modest. A labour demand composite score of 6.65, combined with employment growth of 0.61% year-over-year, suggests a stable and slightly expanding job market.

The city's housing market is experiencing a significant surge in residential building permits, with a 55.25% year-over-year growth rate. This increased construction activity may help alleviate pressure on the market, as the median days on market has risen by 3.7% year-over-year to 56 days. With a cost of living composite score of 3.61, Birmingham remains relatively affordable, a feature that could attract businesses and individuals considering relocation. The office worker ratio composite score of 1.4 is somewhat lower than expected, indicating potential opportunities for growth in this sector.

However, some concerns persist. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline stands at 1.729%, which may indicate labour market volatility. Furthermore, the relatively low earnings growth rate could impact residents' purchasing power. Nevertheless, Birmingham's economic fundamentals appear sound, driven by a strong labour market and growing business activity. The city's affordability and relatively low cost of living are likely to remain key strengths in attracting businesses and talent.

Looking ahead, Birmingham's economy is expected to maintain a stable growth trajectory, driven by its strong labour market and increasing business activity. While modest earnings growth and potential labour market volatility are causes for concern, the city's overall economic fundamentals remain robust. As the housing market evolves, it will be important to monitor the impact of increased construction activity on affordability and demand.

The cost of living composite score of 3.61 suggests that Birmingham remains relatively affordable, which could be an attractive feature for businesses and individuals considering relocation. The office worker ratio composite score of 1.4 is somewhat lower than might be expected, potentially indicating opportunities for growth in this sector.

Despite these positive trends, there are some areas of concern. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is 1.729%, which may indicate some volatility in the labour market. Additionally, the earnings growth rate is relatively low, which could impact the overall purchasing power of residents. Nevertheless, Birmingham's economic fundamentals appear sound, with a strong labour market and growing business activity. The city's affordability and relatively low cost of living are likely to remain key strengths in attracting businesses and talent.

Closing outlook: Birmingham's economy is expected to continue on a stable growth trajectory, driven by its strong labour market and increasing business activity. While there are some areas of concern, such as modest earnings growth and potential labour market volatility, the city's overall economic fundamentals remain robust.
