# Birmingham — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Birmingham's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The city's unemployment rate of 2.2% places it in the 98th percentile, indicating a relatively low joblessness rate. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 0.12%.

In the housing market, Birmingham has seen a 10.88% increase in home prices year-over-year, placing it in the 68th percentile. However, the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 78 days, in the 18th percentile. Construction activity appears to be robust, with building permits up by 32.24% year-over-year, ranking in the 90th percentile.

Birmingham's economic performance is characterized by a range of indicators, with some areas showing strength and others areas of concern. Employment and construction activity are notable, while labor force participation and earnings growth are lower.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Birmingham's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The city's unemployment rate stands at 2.2%, placing it in the 98th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market with low joblessness. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is in the 20th percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 0.12%, which may reflect broader economic pressures or sector-specific challenges.

In the housing market, Birmingham has seen a 10.88% increase in home prices year-over-year, placing it in the 68th percentile, suggesting a relatively strong demand for residential properties. However, the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 78 days, in the 18th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in sales activity. Construction activity appears to be robust, with building permits up by 32.24% year-over-year, ranking in the 90th percentile, potentially signaling confidence in future economic conditions.

Overall, Birmingham's economic performance is average, with strengths in employment and construction activity offset by lower labor force participation and earnings growth.
