# Birmingham

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 49.4th percentile | April 2026**

---

Birmingham's economic character is defined by its strong job creation, with nonfarm employment growth at +0.61% year-over-year, placing it in the top tier, driven by an aggressive expansion in housing supply, with a +55.25% year-over-year increase in building permits, also in the top tier.

The city's economic narrative is nuanced by conflicting signals. While rapid job growth and increasing housing supply suggest a thriving market, stagnant wage growth, with hourly earnings declining by -0.12% year-over-year, raises concerns about the market's sustainability. The cost of living is above average, with a composite score of 3.72, necessitating wage premiums to attract talent. The housing market is also showing signs of normalization, with days on market increasing by 3.7% year-over-year to 56 days, indicating a moderating pace.

Birmingham offers businesses a dynamic and expanding market, with strong job growth and increasing housing supply, but the primary risk lies in stagnant wage growth and above-average cost of living, which may erode consumer spending power and necessitate higher labor costs to attract and retain talent.
