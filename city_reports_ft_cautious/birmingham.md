# Birmingham — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Birmingham's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The city ranks 47th overall with a weighted percentile score of 59.3, placing it in the average category. Unemployment stands at 2.2%, a low rate that places Birmingham in the 98th percentile. This suggests a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.56% is relatively low, ranking in the 20th percentile, which may indicate challenges in engaging a larger portion of the population in the workforce.

Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decline of 0.12%, placing the city in the 8th percentile. This contrasts with the cost of living, which has increased by 3.13%, ranking in the 82nd percentile. The housing market shows a more positive trend, with housing prices rising by 14.39% year-over-year, placing Birmingham in the 70th percentile. However, the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 70 days, indicating a potential slowdown in sales activity.

Construction activity appears strong, with building permits up by 43.07% year-over-year, placing the city in the 88th percentile. Weekly hours worked have decreased slightly to 35.5 hours, ranking in the 92nd percentile. This may reflect changes in employment patterns or work preferences. The data suggests a city with a tight labor market but facing challenges in earnings growth and cost of living, alongside a resilient housing market and robust construction activity.
