# San Antonio — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

San Antonio's economic indicators paint a nuanced picture, with both encouraging and concerning trends. The labor market, for instance, shows a 3.7% unemployment rate, up 0.3 percentage points year-over-year, indicating a slight deterioration in job market conditions. However, the civilian labor force has expanded by 1.47% over the past year, and average hourly earnings have risen by 4.08% year-over-year, outpacing the national average.

Business activity in San Antonio is characterized by a labor demand composite score of 6.44, with nonfarm payroll employment growing 1.09% year-over-year. The city's office worker ratio composite score is 1.36, suggesting a relatively low concentration of office workers, while its cost of living composite score of 1.23 indicates a moderate cost of living. In contrast, residential building permits have declined sharply, by 37.55% year-over-year, which may signal a slowdown in the housing market. This is further evidenced by a 1.67% year-over-year increase in median days on market, now standing at 61 days.

San Antonio's economic strengths, including its relatively low cost of living and moderate labor market growth, are tempered by potential stress points, such as the decline in residential building permits and the slight increase in unemployment. With a grade of B+ and a weighted percentile score of 58.2, the city's economic outlook is cautiously positive. While labor market and business activity indicators suggest moderate expansion, the housing market slowdown poses a challenge to sustained growth.

Looking ahead, San Antonio's economy is expected to continue growing at a moderate pace, driven by its relatively strong labor market and business activity. Nevertheless, close monitoring of the housing market slowdown and unemployment rate is necessary to prevent these stress points from escalating into more significant economic challenges.

San Antonio's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. Average hourly earnings have risen by 4.08% year-over-year, outpacing the national average and suggesting a relatively strong labor market.

In terms of business activity, San Antonio's labor demand composite score is 6.44, with nonfarm payroll employment growing by 1.09% year-over-year. The office worker ratio composite score is 1.36, indicating a relatively low concentration of office workers. The cost of living composite score is 1.23, suggesting a moderate cost of living. However, the residential building permits have declined by 37.55% year-over-year, which may indicate a slowdown in the housing market. The median days on market have increased by 1.67% year-over-year, with a current level of 61 days, suggesting a relatively slow pace of home sales.

The city's economic strengths lie in its relatively low cost of living and moderate labor market growth. However, the decline in residential building permits and the slight increase in unemployment rate are potential stress points that warrant attention. Overall, San Antonio's economic outlook is cautiously positive, with a grade of B+ and a weighted percentile score of 58.2. The city's labor market and business activity indicators suggest a moderate expansion, but the housing market slowdown may pose a challenge to sustained economic growth.

Closing outlook: San Antonio's economy is expected to continue growing at a moderate pace, driven by its relatively strong labor market and business activity. However, the city's housing market slowdown and slight increase in unemployment rate require close monitoring to ensure that these stress points do not escalate into more significant economic challenges.
