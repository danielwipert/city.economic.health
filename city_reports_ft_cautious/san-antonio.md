# San Antonio-New Braunfels

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 43.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

San Antonio earns a C+ overall grade, with its highly affordable environment being the single most important takeaway about this city. The city's cost of living composite score of 1.11 places it in the top tier, indicating a highly affordable environment. Labor force growth is above-average at 1.47% YoY, which is a positive workforce supply signal.

The housing market is contracting, with building permits declining sharply by 37.55% YoY, a bottom-tier performance. Days on market are increasing by 1.7% YoY, with an average of 61 days, signaling a slowing market. This scenario indicates demand erosion, particularly given the weak employment growth of 0.43% YoY. The city's professional talent pool ranks in the 26th percentile, posing constraints for specialized or senior roles.

San Antonio offers businesses a highly affordable cost environment and a growing labor force, but the primary risk lies in the city's sluggish employment growth and contracting housing supply. Decision-makers should be cautious of the potential for workforce attraction difficulties, which may offset the city's initial cost advantages. The city's affordability advantage is significant, but it is tempered by the fast-moving housing market and declining permits.
