San Antonio's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, placing it in the 38th percentile among metropolitan areas, suggesting a labor market that is neither particularly robust nor weak compared to peers. Labor force participation is at 63.01%, which is in the 30th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.99%, ranking in the 52nd percentile, a moderate increase that aligns with broader economic trends.

Housing and construction metrics show more variability. Building permits have declined by 36.19% year-over-year, a significant drop that places San Antonio in the 8th percentile, reflecting a slowdown in new residential construction. The housing price index has increased by 3.43% over the same period, a modest gain that is in the 36th percentile, suggesting stable but not rapidly appreciating home values. Rental market conditions are also noteworthy, with a 3.5% decrease in rental prices year-over-year, placing the city in the 8th percentile, and an average of 71 days on the market for properties, in the 12th percentile, indicating a relatively slow-moving rental market.

Overall, San Antonio's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, suggests a region experiencing moderate growth with some areas of concern, particularly in the housing and construction sectors.
