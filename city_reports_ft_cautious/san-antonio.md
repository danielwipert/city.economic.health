## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it at the 42nd percentile nationally, which indicates a moderate level of labor market activity. The labor force participation rate of 62.92% is relatively low, ranking in the 28th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. On a more positive note, earnings growth over the past year has been robust at 4.9%, placing the region in the 74th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening income environment for those employed.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices is 3.43%, a moderate increase that places the region in the 34th percentile. However, the number of building permits issued has declined sharply by 36.19% year-over-year, indicating a significant slowdown in new construction activity. This decline is reflected in the 8th percentile ranking for this indicator, suggesting a potential cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the days on market for properties have increased to 71 days, up from previous levels, which may indicate a softening demand in the real estate sector.

Overall, the data suggests that while San Antonio-New Braunfels is experiencing some positive trends in employment and earnings, there are signs of moderation in the housing market and labor force participation.
