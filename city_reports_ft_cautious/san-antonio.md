## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others indicate challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it slightly below the national average, which may suggest a stable labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.78% is relatively low, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 1.78%, suggesting a gradual improvement in wages but not a robust increase.

Housing market indicators present a more complex scenario. The year-over-year change in housing prices is positive at 3.43%, which suggests some appreciation in property values. However, the significant decline in building permits, down 49.04% year-over-year, indicates a sharp reduction in new construction activity. This could reflect a slowdown in demand or supply-side constraints. Additionally, the days on market metric has increased to 85.0 days, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market or a shift in buyer behavior.

Overall, the data suggests that while San Antonio's economy exhibits some areas of stability, there are also signs of slowing growth and potential challenges in the labor and housing markets.
