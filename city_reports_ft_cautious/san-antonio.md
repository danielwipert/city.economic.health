# San Antonio-New Braunfels

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 39.7th percentile | June 2026**

---

The San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 39.7th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score in the bottom tier, and its highly affordable cost of living, ranked in the top tier at the 88th percentile. The labor demand metrics, including a mere 0.04% employment growth rate and -1.862% weekly hours deviation from trend, signal a weak labor market.

**Labor Demand**
The San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area has experienced a sluggish labor market, with employment growth of only 0.04% year-over-year and weekly hours 1.862% below its own trend. This combination signals a labor market contraction, rather than genuine demand expansion or survivor squeeze. The low labor demand composite score of 2.86, ranked in the bottom tier at the 18th percentile, reinforces this assessment.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in San Antonio-New Braunfels stands at 3.80%, ranking near the median at the 46th percentile. This indicates a labor market with some slack, rather than being extremely tight. For a business trying to hire in this city, the relatively moderate unemployment rate suggests that finding talent may be somewhat easier than in tighter labor markets, but may not be as straightforward as in areas with higher unemployment rates.

**Wage Growth**
Wage growth in the San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area has been below average, with a year-over-year increase of 1.63% in hourly earnings, ranking in the 22nd percentile. This moderate wage growth rate implies that labor costs for employers are rising, but not as quickly as in other areas, and worker purchasing power is increasing, albeit slowly. The slow wage growth may help keep labor costs in check for businesses, but it also means workers have less bargaining power.

**Cost of Living**
San Antonio-New Braunfels is relatively affordable, with a cost of living score ranked in the top tier at the 88th percentile. The city's price-to-salary ratio is $173/sqft (with prices falling 5.0% year-over-year) to $31.85/hr, resulting in a ratio of 5.43. This high affordability ranking means that the city has a talent attraction advantage, as workers can maintain a good standard of living without requiring significant wage premiums.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in San Antonio-New Braunfels has grown 0.11% year-over-year, ranking above average at the 68th percentile. This positive growth rate indicates that the workforce supply is expanding, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire in the area. The growing labor force suggests that the city has the potential to support hiring needs, at least in terms of the sheer number of available workers.

The number of residential building permits in San Antonio-New Braunfels has decreased by 19.15% year-over-year, ranking below average at the 20th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that the housing supply is tightening, which could lead to future affordability issues and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. The sharp drop in building permits is a concern for businesses that rely on a steady supply of housing for their employees.

**Days on Market**
Homes in San Antonio-New Braunfels currently sit on the market for a median of 57 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 1.7%. This relatively fast pace of home sales, ranked below average at the 34th percentile, indicates a competitive market that may be challenging for relocating workers to find housing. The quick turnover in the housing market suggests that workers may face stiff competition when looking for a place to live.

**Office Economy**
The professional and office sectors in San Antonio-New Braunfels account for a share of jobs ranked near the median at the 54th percentile. This suggests a moderately deep talent pool, suitable for businesses that require specialized office workers, but not as strong as in cities with a more prominent knowledge economy. The city is likely well-suited for businesses that do not rely heavily on tech, finance, or consulting talent, but may not be the best fit for companies that require a highly specialized office workforce.

The San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area offers businesses an affordable cost of living and a growing labor force, but its low labor demand and tightening housing supply are significant concerns. The single biggest risk for a decision-maker to factor in is the potential for future affordability issues and workforce accommodation challenges, driven by the sharp decline in residential building permits, which could offset the city's current advantages and make it less attractive for businesses and workers alike.
