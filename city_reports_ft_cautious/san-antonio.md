# San Antonio — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate performance and others lagging behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing the region in the 48th percentile among similar metro areas. Labour force participation is at 62.78%, ranking in the 30th percentile, which suggests a relatively low participation rate. Year-over-year earnings growth is 1.78%, placing the area in the 32nd percentile, indicating a stable but not rapidly expanding job market.

Housing and construction metrics show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 4.55% year-over-year, placing the area in the 38th percentile. However, building permits have declined sharply by 54.45% year-over-year, ranking in the bottom percentile. The days-on-market metric for homes has risen to 85 days, also in the lower percentiles. The cost of living is 4.77% above the national average, and weekly hours worked are slightly below the national average at 35.1 hours, both of which rank in the higher percentiles.

The weighted percentile score of 35.2 and the grade of C+ indicate that the San Antonio-New Braunfels area faces challenges in several key economic areas, while maintaining some stability in others.

The economic data for the San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate performance while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing the region in the 48th percentile among similar metro areas, suggesting a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak. Labor force participation is at 62.78%, which is relatively low, ranking in the 30th percentile, indicating a potential underutilization of the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 1.78%, placing the area in the 32nd percentile, which may reflect a stable but not rapidly expanding job market.

Housing and construction metrics show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 4.55% year-over-year, placing the area in the 38th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in home values. However, building permits have declined sharply by 54.45% year-over-year, ranking in the bottom percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new residential construction. The days-on-market metric for homes has risen to 85 days, also in the lower percentiles, possibly reflecting a cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the cost of living is 4.77% above the national average, and weekly hours worked are slightly below the national average at 35.1 hours, both of which rank in the higher percentiles, suggesting a relatively high cost of living and shorter working hours.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 35.2 and the grade of C+ indicate that San Antonio-New Braunfels faces challenges in several key economic areas, though it maintains some stability in others.
