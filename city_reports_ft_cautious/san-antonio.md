## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, placing it in the 38th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak. The labor force participation rate of 63.01% is similarly positioned in the 30th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been positive at 4.9%, ranking in the 74th percentile, which may reflect a relatively healthy income environment for those employed.

Housing and construction metrics present a more complex scenario. The housing price index has increased by 3.43% year-over-year, placing it in the 34th percentile, suggesting modest appreciation in home values. However, building permits have declined sharply by 36.19% year-over-year, ranking in the 8th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new residential construction. The days on market for homes have risen to 71 days, in the 12th percentile, potentially signaling a less dynamic real estate market. Additionally, the weekly hours worked average of 35.5 hours is in the 90th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of full-time employment.

Overall, the data suggests a San Antonio economy with areas of resilience and others facing headwinds, consistent with its below-average grade of B-.
