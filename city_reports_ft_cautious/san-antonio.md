# San Antonio — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the San Antonio-New Braunfels metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, a relatively low level that may be consistent with a stable labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.78% is below the national average, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is 1.78%, while the cost of living has increased by 4.84%, indicating that real wage growth may be constrained. The average weekly hours worked are 35.1 hours, a relatively high level that could be consistent with either full employment or a tight labor market.

Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 4.55%, a moderate rate that may reflect ongoing demand for housing. However, the number of building permits has declined by 37.55% year-over-year, which could signal a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes have increased to 79 days, a level that suggests a less dynamic housing market compared to periods of higher turnover. The office vacancy rate has risen by 1.24% year-over-year, which may indicate a softening in demand for commercial space.

The data suggests a cautiously stable economic environment in San Antonio-New Braunfels, with some areas showing signs of moderation or decline.

However, the number of building permits has declined sharply by 37.55% year-over-year, which could signal a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes have increased to 79 days, a level that suggests a less dynamic housing market compared to periods of higher turnover. The office vacancy rate has also risen, with a year-over-year change of 1.24%, which may indicate a softening in demand for commercial space.

Overall, the data indicates a cautiously stable but not robust economic environment in San Antonio-New Braunfels, with some areas showing signs of moderation or decline.
