## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area reveals a mixed but generally stable picture. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, placing it in the 8th percentile nationally, which suggests that while employment conditions are not among the best, they are still within a reasonable range. The labor force participation rate of 68.65% is notably high, ranking in the 86th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is strong at 9.53%, placing it in the 92nd percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market with upward pressure on wages.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined slightly by 0.39%, placing it in the 18th percentile, suggesting a cooling trend in the real estate market. However, the number of building permits issued has increased by 2.65% year-over-year, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. The average days on market for homes is 67 days, a figure that is neither exceptionally high nor low, placing it in the 46th percentile, and may reflect a balanced market between buyers and sellers.

Overall, the data suggests that Seattle's economy is performing averagely, with strengths in labor market participation and earnings growth, but with some moderation in the housing market.
