# Seattle — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area has a weighted percentile score of 52.9 in a recent economic assessment, placing it in the average category. The labor force participation rate is 68.24%, scoring in the 84th percentile, indicating a relatively high engagement of the working-age population in the labor market.

The days on market for properties have lengthened to 77 days, suggesting a potential slowdown in housing sales activity.

The data indicates that Seattle's economy is performing in line with the average, with some strengths in labor force participation and earnings growth, but also some challenges in employment rates and housing market dynamics.
