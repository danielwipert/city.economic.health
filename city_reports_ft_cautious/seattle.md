## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area ranks 15th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other metro areas. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 14th percentile, which suggests a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 68.18% is notably high, ranking in the 84th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been strong, with a year-over-year increase of 8.65%, placing it in the 92nd percentile. This significant earnings growth contrasts with a moderate cost of living increase of 5.4%, which is in the 32nd percentile, suggesting that real wages may be improving. Housing market indicators show mixed trends; while housing prices have slightly declined by 0.39% year-over-year, rental prices have also decreased by 0.15%. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 56 days, which may reflect a more balanced market compared to previous periods of rapid price appreciation.

Construction activity appears to be robust, with building permits up 14.29% year-over-year, placing this indicator in the 76th percentile. This suggests ongoing investment in the region's housing stock and infrastructure. Overall, the data indicates a stable but not overly dynamic economic environment in Seattle, with strengths in labor market participation and earnings growth, but with some moderation in the housing sector.
