The economic data for the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 32nd percentile nationally, suggesting a labor market that is neither exceptionally tight nor loose. Labor force participation is notably high at 68.19%, ranking in the 82nd percentile, which may reflect a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been strong at 9.43%, well above the national average, as indicated by its 94th percentile score.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has slightly declined by 0.54%, placing it in the 18th percentile, which could indicate a cooling in the housing market. However, building permits have increased by 14.29% year-over-year, a figure in the 76th percentile, suggesting continued investment in residential construction. The median days on market for homes is 48 days, which is in the 58th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of sales activity. Additionally, the cost of living index is 5.35% above the national average, placing it in the 28th percentile, which suggests that while living costs are higher than the national average, they are not among the most expensive in the country.

Overall, the data suggests that Seattle's economy is performing averagely, with strengths in labor force participation and earnings growth, but with some signs of moderation in the housing market.
