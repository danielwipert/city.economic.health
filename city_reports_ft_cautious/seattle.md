# Seattle — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 6th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate is 68.24%, ranking in the 84th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.63%, placing Seattle in the 92nd percentile, a performance that reflects a competitive job market and potentially rising living costs.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in home prices is -0.13%, indicating a modest decline. The number of building permits issued has decreased by 1.35% year-over-year, suggesting a slowdown in construction activity. The days on market for homes have increased to 36 days, up from previous levels, which may indicate a softening in the housing market. The weighted percentile score of 54.2 places Seattle in the "Average" category, with a grade of B, reflecting a balanced but not exceptional economic environment.

The data collectively suggests a stable economic landscape in Seattle, with some indicators showing strength and others suggesting areas of concern.
