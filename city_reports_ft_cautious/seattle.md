## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 32nd percentile nationally, which suggests a moderate level of labor market activity. Labor force participation is notably high at 68.19%, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is strong at 9.43%, situating Seattle in the 94th percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market with upward pressure on wages.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined slightly by 0.54%, placing the region in the 18th percentile, suggesting a cooling trend in the housing market. However, building permits have increased by 14.29% year-over-year, ranking in the 76th percentile, which could indicate ongoing construction activity and potential future growth in the housing stock. The median days on market for homes is 48 days, in the 58th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that while Seattle's labor market remains strong, the housing sector shows signs of moderation.
