# Seattle — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Seattle's metropolitan area presents a mixed economic picture, with various indicators showing both positive and negative trends. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, ranking in the 6th percentile nationally, suggesting a relatively tight labour market. The labour force participation rate of 68.24% is high, ranking in the 84th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.63%, placing it in the 92nd percentile, which reflects a strong performance in this metric.

On the housing front, the year-over-year change in home prices has seen a slight decline of 0.13%, ranking in the 16th percentile, which may indicate cooling dynamics in the real estate market. The number of building permits issued has decreased by 1.35% year-over-year, ranking in the 46th percentile, potentially signalling a moderation in construction activity. The average days on market for properties has increased to 77 days, ranking in the 22nd percentile, which could suggest a more balanced buyer-seller market.

Seattle's economic performance is average relative to other metropolitan areas, with a weighted percentile score of 51.1 and a grade of B. This reflects strengths in labour market engagement and earnings growth, but also some signs of moderation in the housing sector.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area, as of the latest data, presents a mixed picture with several key indicators suggesting both strengths and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 6th percentile nationally, which may indicate a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 68.24% is notably high, ranking in the 84th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been positive at 7.63%, placing it in the 92nd percentile, which indicates a strong performance in this metric.

On the housing front, the year-over-year change in home prices has seen a slight decline of 0.13%, placing it in the 16th percentile, which may reflect cooling dynamics in the real estate market. The number of building permits issued has also decreased by 1.35% year-over-year, ranking in the 46th percentile, potentially signaling a moderation in construction activity. The average days on market for properties has increased to 77 days, in the 22nd percentile, which could suggest a more balanced buyer-seller market compared to previous periods of rapid price appreciation.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 51.1 and a grade of B indicate that Seattle's economic performance is average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in labor market engagement and earnings growth, but with some signs of moderation in the housing sector.
