## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area ranks 15th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other metropolitan areas. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, placing it in the 8th percentile, which suggests that the labor market is relatively tight but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 68.65% is notably high, ranking in the 86th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been strong, with a 9.53% increase, placing Seattle in the 92nd percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market and potentially rising living costs. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.22%, which is in the 30th percentile, suggesting moderate price pressures. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture; while housing prices have slightly declined by 0.39% year-over-year, rental prices have increased by 0.23%, and the number of days properties remain on the market is 67 days, which is in the 46th percentile, indicating a balanced market with neither a significant surplus nor shortage of available homes.

Construction activity, as indicated by building permits, has grown by 9.73% year-over-year, placing Seattle in the 64th percentile, suggesting steady investment in new housing and commercial developments. Overall, the data indicates a stable but not outstanding economic environment in Seattle, with strong labor market participation and earnings growth, balanced by moderate inflation and a mixed housing market.
