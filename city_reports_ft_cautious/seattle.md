# Seattle — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Seattle's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength and others suggesting areas of concern. The metropolitan area's unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 6th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other metro areas. The labour force participation rate of 68.24% is high, ranking in the 84th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labour market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.63%, placing Seattle in the 92nd percentile, indicating significant wage gains. The cost of living index is 5.62, in the 26th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate pace of price increases relative to other regions. Housing market indicators show a slight decline in home prices year-over-year, with a -0.13% change, and a modest decrease in permits for new housing units, down by 1.35% year-over-year. The average days on market for homes is 36 days, in the 94th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests that Seattle's economy exhibits areas of strength, including labour force participation and earnings growth, but also signs of moderation in the housing sector and relatively higher unemployment.
