# Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Dallas's strong labor market, with a 69.61% labor force participation rate, makes it an attractive location for businesses. The city's cost of living composite score of 1.43 also indicates it is one of the most affordable metros, offering a significant cost advantage for businesses.

However, the housing market presents a challenge, with a sharp decline in building permits of -20.96% year-over-year. This, combined with a 6.7% year-over-year increase in days on market, averaging 48 days, suggests a genuine demand deterioration, which could impact workforce attraction and affordability in the long run.

Dallas offers a unique combination of a deep labor pool and an affordable cost of living, making it an attractive location for talent attraction and retention. However, the city's struggling housing supply growth poses a risk, which could lead to future affordability stress and workforce attraction difficulties, making it essential for businesses to carefully consider these factors in their location decision.
