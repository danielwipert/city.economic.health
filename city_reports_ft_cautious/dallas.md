## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area ranks fourth in its overall economic performance, with a weighted percentile score of 50, indicating an average standing relative to other metropolitan areas. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, which is in line with national trends, placing it at the 50th percentile. The labor force participation rate is notably high at 69.61%, ranking in the 90th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the workforce in the local economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 3.1%, placing the region in the 44th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.88% is slightly above the median, at the 52nd percentile.

In the housing market, the Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington area has seen a decline in both home prices and permits issued, with year-over-year decreases of 1.52% and 20.74%, respectively. These figures place the region in the 8th and 22nd percentiles, indicating a cooling trend in the real estate sector. Additionally, the average days on market for properties have increased to 57 days, which may reflect a more balanced market between buyers and sellers. Weekly hours worked remain high at 35.5 hours, ranking in the 92nd percentile, which could suggest sustained demand for labor despite the moderation in earnings growth.

These data points collectively paint a picture of a metropolitan area with a strong labor market but facing some challenges in the housing sector.
