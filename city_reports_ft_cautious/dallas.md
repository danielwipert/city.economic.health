# Dallas — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area received a B- grade in a recent economic assessment, ranking fourth overall. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile, indicating a labour market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 69.61% is high, ranking in the 90th percentile, which may reflect a high level of engagement among the working-age population in the labour market. Weekly hours worked, at 35.5 hours, also rank high at the 92nd percentile, indicating strong labour market activity.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 3.1%, placing the region in the 44th percentile. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.88%, slightly above the median, which could impact real wage growth. Housing market indicators show a decline, with housing prices falling by 1.52% year-over-year and building permits down by 20.96% compared to the previous year. These declines place the region in the lower percentiles for these metrics, at the 8th and 16th percentiles, respectively, suggesting a cooling in the housing sector. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 57 days, up from previous levels, which may indicate a shift towards a more balanced market.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Dallas, with some strengths in labour market participation and hours worked, but also challenges in the housing sector.
