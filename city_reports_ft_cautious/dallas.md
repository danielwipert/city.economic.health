# Dallas — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area ranks fourth in the dataset, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance across various economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile, suggesting a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate is high at 69.61%, ranking in the 90th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 3.1%, placing the region in the 44th percentile, while the cost of living index is slightly above the national average at 4.88, in the 52nd percentile. Housing market indicators show a decline, with a 20.74% decrease in building permits year-over-year and a 1.52% drop in home prices, both of which are in the lower percentiles (22nd and 8th, respectively). The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 57 days, suggesting a slowdown in sales activity.

Weekly hours worked have remained stable at 35.5 hours, ranking in the 92nd percentile, which could indicate consistent demand for labor. Retail sales per square foot have seen a slight decline of 1.8%, placing the region in the 34th percentile, potentially signaling a softening in consumer spending.

The data suggests a mixed economic environment in Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington, with some indicators showing stability and others indicating a slowdown.
