## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area ranks fourth in the dataset, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance across various economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, placing it in the 38th percentile, which suggests a moderate level of labor market activity. The labor force participation rate of 69.85% is notably high, ranking in the 90th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been 3.82%, situating the region in the 54th percentile, while consumer price inflation is at 4.81%, in the 58th percentile, suggesting that wage growth is slightly lagging behind inflation.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices has decreased by 1.32%, placing the region in the 6th percentile, which may reflect a cooling in the real estate market. Similarly, the annual change in single-family rents has declined by 1.78%, in the 34th percentile, potentially indicating reduced demand or increased supply in the rental market. The median number of days on the market for homes is 64 days, in the 20th percentile, which could suggest a more balanced market compared to periods of rapid appreciation. Building permits have increased by 4.08% year-over-year, in the 56th percentile, indicating ongoing construction activity.

These data points collectively paint a picture of a metropolitan area with stable but not exceptional economic conditions, with some indicators suggesting moderation in previously overheated sectors.
