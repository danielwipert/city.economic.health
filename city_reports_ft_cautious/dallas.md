## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it at the 42nd percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly tight nor loose compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 69.57% is notably high, ranking in the 90th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 3.82%, situating the region in the 54th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.81% is slightly above the national average, placing it in the 58th percentile.

In the housing sector, the data reveals a cooling trend. The home price index has declined by 1.32% year-over-year, placing the region in the 6th percentile, which may reflect a shift in the housing market dynamics. Similarly, the price per square foot has decreased by 1.78%, ranking in the 34th percentile, and the median days on market for homes has increased to 64 days, significantly higher than the national average, placing it in the 20th percentile. Construction activity, as measured by building permits, has grown by 4.08% year-over-year, positioning the region in the 56th percentile, suggesting ongoing but tempered development.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not overly dynamic economic environment in Dallas, with strengths in labor market participation and some signs of moderation in the housing market.
