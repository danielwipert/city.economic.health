# Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington

**Grade: B (Average) | 52.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington's strong labor market, with a 69.61% labor force participation rate, offers a significant hiring advantage and strong consumer purchasing power, backed by an overall cost of living composite of 1.43, making it one of the most affordable metros. This combination of a deep labor pool and affordable cost of living makes it an attractive location for talent attraction and retention.

However, the city's housing supply and demand dynamics are concerning. The 20.96% year-over-year decline in building permits signals a contracting housing supply, which may lead to future affordability stress and workforce attraction difficulties. The increasing days on market, with homes taking 6.7% more days to sell than last year, averaging 48 days, suggests a slowing market and demand erosion, rather than a healthy normalization.

The city's average nonfarm employment growth of 0.33% year-over-year does not indicate strong enough job creation to drive demand, reinforcing the narrative of demand erosion. The primary risk lies in the potential for demand erosion and affordability stress due to the declining housing supply, which may impact the city's long-term economic momentum and attractiveness to businesses and workers alike.
