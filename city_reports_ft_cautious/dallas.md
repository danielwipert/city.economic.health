## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area ranks fourth in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other regions. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it near the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, which may suggest a stable but not particularly robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 70.08% is notably high, ranking in the 90th percentile, suggesting a strong engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 3.1%, aligning with the 44th percentile, while the cost of living index of 4.93 is slightly above the national average, placing it in the 52nd percentile.

In the housing sector, the Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metro area has seen a decline in both home prices and permits issued, with year-over-year decreases of 1.39% and 9.74%, respectively. These figures place the region in the lower percentiles (4th and 32nd) for these indicators, potentially reflecting a cooling in the local real estate market. The days on market metric stands at 71 days, which is relatively low and suggests that, despite the price declines, homes are still selling at a steady pace. Weekly hours worked have remained stable at 35.5 hours, a figure that ranks in the 92nd percentile, indicating consistent employment conditions.

Overall, the data suggests a mixed but generally stable economic environment in Dallas, with strengths in labor force participation and work hours, balanced against more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing metrics.
