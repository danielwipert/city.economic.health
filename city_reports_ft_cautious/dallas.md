# Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington's strong labor pool, with a 69.61% labor force participation rate, makes it an attractive location for businesses. The city's cost of living composite score of 1.41 also ranks it among the most affordable metros, offering a significant cost advantage for companies.

However, the city's employment growth of 0.33% year-over-year is steady but unremarkable. A closer examination of the housing supply sector reveals tensions, with a sharp decline in building permits of -20.96% year-over-year, indicating a contracting supply. This, combined with a 1.8% year-over-year increase in days on market, with homes taking an average of 57 days to sell, suggests a slowing market and potential demand erosion.

The city's unique combination of an engaged workforce and affordable cost of living makes it an attractive location for talent attraction and retention. However, the primary risk lies in the potential for demand erosion and affordability stress due to the declining housing supply, which may impact the city's long-term economic viability and attractiveness to businesses.
