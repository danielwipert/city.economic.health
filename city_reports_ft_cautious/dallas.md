# Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington

**Grade: A- (Good) | 62.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Dallas earns an overall grade of A- and ranks in the 62.9th percentile out of 50 US metros, driven by its strong economic character. The city boasts a healthy labor market, with an unemployment rate of 3.60% and above-average nonfarm employment growth of 0.98% year-over-year. Its cost of living composite score is 1.30, placing it in the top tier of affordability.

The city's office and professional worker share is exceptionally high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, indicating a deep talent pool that could support knowledge-economy businesses. However, the sharp decline in building permits, down 20.96% year-over-year, indicates a looming housing supply squeeze. This decline is particularly concerning given the strong employment growth, as it will lead to affordability and workforce attraction issues.

Dallas offers businesses a unique combination of a strong labor market, affordable cost of living, and deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for companies. The primary risk is the potential housing supply squeeze, which could erode the city's affordability advantage and create challenges for workforce attraction, making it essential for businesses to consider these factors in their location decision.
