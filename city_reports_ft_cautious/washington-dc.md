# Washington DC — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metropolitan area, centred around Washington DC, displays a mixed economic profile according to recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 46th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate of 67.37% is high, ranking in the 78th percentile, suggesting a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. However, earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 1.37%, placing it in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

Housing indicators present a nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 0.29% year-over-year, a slight rise that places the region in the 22nd percentile, suggesting a relatively stable but not rapidly growing housing market. The number of building permits has declined by 23.94% year-over-year, a significant drop that ranks in the 10th percentile, potentially indicating a slowdown in new construction activity. Office space vacancy rates have also risen, with a year-over-year increase of 5.12%, placing the region in the 2nd percentile, which may reflect changes in demand for commercial real estate.

Overall, the Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metro area's economic performance is characterised by a mix of stability and moderation across various indicators, with some sectors showing signs of softening.
