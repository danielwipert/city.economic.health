# Washington DC — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metropolitan area, centred around Washington DC, presents a mixed economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 46th percentile nationally, suggesting a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate of 67.37% is high, ranking in the 78th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the economy. However, earnings growth year-over-year has been modest at 1.37%, placing it in the 24th percentile, which may reflect slower wage increases compared to other regions.

Housing market data shows a mixed picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices is a slight increase of 0.29%, while the number of building permits has declined significantly by 23.94% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 10th percentile. This decline in permits may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric for home sales stands at 38 days, which is in the 88th percentile, suggesting that homes are taking longer to sell compared to other markets. The year-over-year change in office space vacancy rates has decreased by 5.52%, which could suggest a tightening in commercial real estate demand.

The data suggests a region with a strong labour market but facing challenges in housing and construction activity.
