The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metropolitan area, centered around Washington DC, exhibits a mixed economic profile based on recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 28th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but not among the tightest. These figures suggest that while wages are growing, they are doing so at a pace that may be keeping up with, but not outpacing, the cost of living.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing prices is modest at 0.47%, placing it in the 28th percentile, which may reflect a stable but not rapidly appreciating market. The year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has declined by 3.27%, in the 46th percentile, indicating a slight slowdown in new housing development. The year-over-year change in the number of single-family homes for sale has decreased by 4.38%, a significant drop placing it in the 4th percentile, suggesting a tightening supply of available homes. The average days on market for properties is 58 days, in the 68th percentile, which indicates a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

Overall, the Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metro area's economic indicators present a picture of stability with some areas of moderate growth and others showing signs of moderation or decline.
