# Washington DC — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metropolitan area, centred around Washington DC, presents a mixed economic picture based on recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 46th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labour market. Labour force participation is high at 67.37%, ranking in the 78th percentile, suggesting a strong engagement of the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.37%, placing it in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show a slight increase in home prices year-over-year by 0.29%, situating the region in the 22nd percentile. The number of building permits has declined by 23.94% year-over-year, placing it in the 10th percentile. This decline in permits could be associated with a slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric for property sales is 38 days, ranking in the 88th percentile, suggesting that properties are taking longer to sell compared to other markets. The average weekly hours worked is 34.5 hours, in the 67th percentile, indicating a moderate level of work intensity.

The data suggests a stable but not particularly dynamic economic environment in Washington DC, with some areas showing signs of moderation.
