# Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 44.3th percentile | April 2026**

---

Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metro area earns a B- grade, ranking 44th among 50 US metros, driven by a combination of labor market factors, including a top-tier labor force participation rate of 69.03% and a below-average unemployment rate of 4.00%. However, wage growth has been stagnant, with hourly earnings increasing by only 0.03% year-over-year, signaling potential softness in the market.

Despite the strong labor force participation, the data reveals tensions between different metrics. The sluggish wage growth and declining building permits, down 12.25% year-over-year, suggest a weakening demand for housing and potentially a slowing economy. The days on market have increased by 9.1% year-over-year, with homes now taking an average of 36 days to sell, indicating a moderating housing market.

Minneapolis offers a deep labor pool with high workforce engagement, making it an attractive location for businesses seeking to hire. However, the primary risk lies in the potential softening of local consumer demand due to stagnant wage growth and a slowing housing market, which may impact the overall economic momentum and affordability in the region.
