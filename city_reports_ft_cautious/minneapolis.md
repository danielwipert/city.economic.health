# Minneapolis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metropolitan area ranks 16th among major U.S. metro areas, with a weighted percentile score of 37.6, placing it in the "C+" grade category. The labour market in Minneapolis presents a mixed picture, with an unemployment rate of 4.0%, slightly above the national average, and a labour force participation rate of 69.03%, higher than the national average. Year-over-year earnings growth stands at 3.0%, indicating modest wage increases. Weekly hours worked have remained relatively stable at 33.3 hours.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing prices has increased by 2.74%, suggesting a moderate appreciation in home values. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 22.23% compared with the previous year, which may indicate a slowdown in construction activity. The inventory of homes on the market has increased, with properties taking an average of 40 days to sell, up from previous levels. The cost of living index stands at 7.29, indicating that Minneapolis is relatively more affordable compared with other major metropolitan areas.

The data indicates a stable economic environment in Minneapolis, with some indicators showing moderate growth and others suggesting potential areas of concern.
