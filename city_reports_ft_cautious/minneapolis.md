## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metropolitan area ranks 16th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 70th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 69.34% is notably high, ranking in the 88th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.74%, placing it in the 38th percentile, which may reflect a more subdued income expansion relative to other areas.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing prices is 2.69%, situated in the 30th percentile, suggesting a moderate pace of appreciation. The number of building permits has declined by 5.7% year-over-year, ranking in the 42nd percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for properties have remained stable at 45 days, in the 74th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The weekly hours worked stand at 33.8 hours, in the 34th percentile, which may reflect a trend towards part-time or flexible work arrangements.

These data points collectively suggest a mixed but generally stable economic environment in Minneapolis, with strengths in labor market participation and housing market balance, offset by moderate earnings growth and a decline in new construction permits.
