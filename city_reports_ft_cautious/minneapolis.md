# Minneapolis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metropolitan area ranks 16th among its peers, with a weighted percentile score of 37.6, corresponding to a grade of C+. The unemployment rate is 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions.

The labour force participation rate of 69.03% is high, ranking in the 88th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. Earnings growth over the past year has been 3.0%, placing the region in the 10th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases relative to other areas.

The cost of living index is 7.29, relatively low, ranking in the 8th percentile, suggesting that living expenses in Minneapolis are more affordable compared to many other metropolitan areas. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture, with a year-over-year increase in home prices of 2.74%, placing the region in the 32nd percentile, and a decline in building permits of 22.23%, in the 18th percentile.

The data indicates a metropolitan area with a solid labour force participation rate but facing challenges in earnings growth and housing construction.
