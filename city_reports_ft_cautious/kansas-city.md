## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.98% further supports this, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is at 2.82%, situated in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 17.0% places Kansas City in the 82nd percentile, suggesting a strong appreciation in property values. The number of building permits issued has risen by 10.29% year-over-year, ranking in the 68th percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. Conversely, the median days on market for homes is 57 days, in the 48th percentile, which might suggest a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Kansas City's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be average, with strengths in employment and housing markets offset by more moderate earnings growth.
