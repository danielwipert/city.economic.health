# Kansas City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Kansas City's economic indicators show a mixed picture, according to recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile among metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 67.91% is high, ranking in the 82nd percentile. Earnings growth over the past year has been 6.8%, placing it in the 84th percentile.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 17.56% places Kansas City in the 78th percentile. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 5.01% year-over-year, ranking in the 44th percentile. The cost of living index of 4.64 is moderate, ranking in the 68th percentile. The weekly hours worked average of 33.6 hours is below the national average, ranking in the 37th percentile. The days on market for homes have remained stable at 57 days, in the 36th percentile.

Kansas City's economic performance is generally above average, with notable strengths in employment, labour force participation and earnings growth. However, some moderation is observed in housing construction.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture, according to the latest data. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.91% is notably high, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been solid at 6.8%, placing it in the 84th percentile, which may reflect a healthy demand for labor and potentially rising wages.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 17.56% places Kansas City in the 78th percentile, suggesting a strong housing market. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 5.01% year-over-year, ranking in the 44th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index of 4.64 is moderate, ranking in the 68th percentile, while the weekly hours worked average of 33.6 hours is below the national average, ranking in the 37th percentile. The days on market for homes have remained stable at 57 days, in the 36th percentile, which may reflect balanced conditions between supply and demand in the real estate market.

Overall, Kansas City's economic performance is above average, with strengths in employment, labor force participation, and earnings growth, though some moderation is observed in housing construction.
