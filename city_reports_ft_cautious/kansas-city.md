## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally stable picture, as reflected in its overall grade of B and a weighted percentile of 59.6. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 67.98% is also robust, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.82%, placing it in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases relative to other regions. The cost of living, measured at 5.08, is in the 44th percentile, suggesting moderate living expenses.

Housing market indicators show a more dynamic trend. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is significant at 17.0%, placing Kansas City in the 82nd percentile, which indicates a strong appreciation in property values. The number of building permits has risen by 10.29% year-over-year, in the 68th percentile, suggesting ongoing construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 57 days, in the 48th percentile, indicating a balanced market where properties are neither selling extremely quickly nor languishing. The price-to-square-foot ratio has increased by 1.06% year-over-year, in the 76th percentile, which may reflect rising demand or changes in the types of properties being sold. Weekly hours worked are at 33.5, in the 22nd percentile, which is lower than the national average and could indicate shorter workweeks or part-time employment patterns.

Overall, Kansas City's economic data suggests a balanced but not exceptional performance, with strengths in employment and housing market activity, but more moderate growth in earnings and work hours.
