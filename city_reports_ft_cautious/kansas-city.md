# Kansas City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 67.91% ranks higher, at the 82nd percentile. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.8% is notable, ranking in the 84th percentile. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.64%, aligning with broader national trends and placing it in the 68th percentile.

Housing and construction metrics show varied results. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 5.01%, placing it in the 44th percentile. However, the housing price index has increased by 17.56% year-over-year, placing it in the 78th percentile. The pace of sales, as indicated by days on market, is 57 days, in the 36th percentile. The year-over-year change in property sales frequency, at 1.38%, is in the 74th percentile.

Kansas City's economic performance is generally above average, with some strengths in labour market indicators and earnings growth. However, there are also some cautionary signs in housing construction.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, suggesting a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.91% ranks higher, at the 82nd percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.8% is notably high, ranking in the 84th percentile, which may reflect improvements in wage conditions or shifts in employment composition. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.64%, aligning with broader national trends and placing it in the 68th percentile.

Housing and construction metrics show more varied results. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 5.01%, placing it in the 44th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new residential construction. However, the housing price index has increased by 17.56% year-over-year, a significant rise that places it in the 78th percentile, suggesting continued demand for housing despite the decline in permits. The pace of sales, as indicated by days on market, is 57 days, in the 36th percentile, which may reflect a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The year-over-year change in property sales frequency, at 1.38%, is in the 74th percentile, indicating a modest increase in the number of transactions.

Overall, Kansas City's economic performance is above average, with strengths in labor market indicators and earnings growth, but with some cautionary signs in housing construction.
