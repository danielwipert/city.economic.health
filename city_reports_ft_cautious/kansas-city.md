However, earnings growth year-over-year is moderate at 3.67%, placing it in the 44th percentile, indicating that wage increases may not be outpacing inflation.

The days on market metric stands at 51 days, which is relatively average, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The cost of living index is 4.98, and the overall well-being ratio is 1.04, both of which are near the median, suggesting that living costs and quality of life are broadly in line with national averages.

These data points collectively suggest that Kansas City's economy is performing in line with national averages, with particular strengths in labor force participation and housing market activity.
