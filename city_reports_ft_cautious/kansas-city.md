# Kansas City

**Grade: A+ (Excellent) | 74.4th percentile | April 2026**

---

Kansas City earns an A+ grade, driven by its exceptional economic performance, with rapid job creation and a growing workforce. The city's labor market is characterized by nonfarm employment growth of +1.39% year-over-year, ranking in the 96th percentile, and labor force growth of +2.10% year-over-year. Wages are rising fast, with hourly earnings increasing by +6.97% year-over-year, indicating a competitive labor market.

The city's below-average cost of living, with a composite score of 1.45, is a significant advantage. However, the housing market is experiencing a surge in demand, with homes selling in an average of 45 days on market, ranked in the 4th percentile. This fast-moving market poses a relocation challenge for new hires, who face intense competition for housing. Despite above-average building permit growth of +5.41% year-over-year, demand outpaces housing supply, eroding the affordability advantage.

Kansas City's thriving labor market makes it an attractive location for expansion, with rapid job creation and a growing workforce. However, the fast-moving housing market poses a significant risk, as relocating employees may struggle to secure affordable housing, potentially impacting talent attraction and retention.
