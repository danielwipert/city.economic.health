## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Kansas City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and moderation. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, placing the metro area in the 48th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly tight nor slack. The labor force participation rate of 68.55% is notably high, ranking in the 86th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 2.82%, situating Kansas City in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a stable but not rapidly expanding income environment.

Housing market dynamics in Kansas City show a significant increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 17.0%, placing the region in the 82nd percentile. This strong price appreciation contrasts with a more moderate 10.29% increase in building permits, which ranks in the 68th percentile, suggesting a balanced but growing housing supply. The median days on market for homes is 57 days, a figure that aligns closely with the national average, indicating a steady pace of sales activity. The cost of living index of 5.08 and the weekly hours worked of 33.5 are both near the national median, further highlighting the region's alignment with broader economic trends.

Overall, Kansas City's economic profile suggests a market that is performing in line with national averages, with particular strengths in labor force participation and housing price appreciation.
