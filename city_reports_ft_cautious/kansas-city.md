# Kansas City

**Grade: A+ (Excellent) | 76.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Kansas City earns an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 76.0th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is largely defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score of 5.47, and its top-tier wage growth of 6.36% year-over-year. These metrics signal a city with a thriving job market and rising labor costs.

**Labor Demand**
Kansas City's employment growth rate of 0.57% year-over-year, combined with a weekly hours deviation of 0.372% above its own trend, indicates genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, suggesting a strong and growing economy. The labor demand composite score of 5.47, ranking in the 88th percentile, further reinforces this assessment.

**Unemployment**
The city's unemployment rate of 4.10% is relatively low, ranking in the 73rd percentile, indicating a tight labor market with some slack. For a business trying to hire in Kansas City, this means that while it may be slightly easier to find talent than in other cities, there is still competition for skilled workers and potential upward pressure on wages.

**Wage Growth**
With a year-over-year wage growth rate of 6.36%, Kansas City is experiencing fast wage growth, ranking in the 85th percentile. This rapid wage growth implies rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing purchasing power for workers, which can be beneficial for businesses relying on local consumer demand.

**Cost of Living**
Kansas City's cost of living, with a PSF of $200/sqft and an earnings ratio of 5.63, ranks in the 86th percentile, indicating a relatively affordable city. This affordability advantage can be a significant talent attraction factor, as businesses can offer competitive wages without needing to provide large premiums to offset high living costs.

**Labor Force Growth**
The city's civilian labor force is growing at a rate of 1.76% year-over-year, ranking in the 83rd percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth in the labor force implies that businesses in Kansas City will have a larger pool of potential employees to draw from, making it easier to hire and expand operations.

**Building Permits**
Kansas City has seen a 5.41% year-over-year increase in residential building permits, ranking in the 67th percentile, signaling an expansion in housing supply. This increase in building permits suggests that the city's housing market is becoming more accommodating, which can help attract and retain workers, and mitigate future affordability concerns.

**Days on Market**
The current median days on market in Kansas City is 45 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 11.8%, ranking in the 4th percentile. This fast-paced market means that homes are selling quickly, which can make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing, potentially impacting a business's ability to attract talent.

**Office Economy**
Kansas City's professional and office worker share, ranking in the 39th percentile, indicates a relatively shallow talent pool in these sectors. This suggests that the city is better suited for businesses that are not heavily reliant on professional or office workers, such as industrial or logistics companies, rather than tech, finance, or consulting firms.

In conclusion, Kansas City offers businesses a strong labor market with growing demand, rising wages, and an affordable cost of living, making it an attractive location for companies looking to expand or relocate. However, the city's fast-paced housing market and relatively shallow professional talent pool are potential constraints that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating Kansas City as a business location.
