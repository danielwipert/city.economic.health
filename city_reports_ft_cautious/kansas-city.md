# Kansas City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Kansas City's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile nationally. The labour force participation rate of 67.91% is high, ranking in the 82nd percentile. Earnings growth over the past year has been 6.8%, placing Kansas City in the 84th percentile.

In the housing market, home prices have increased by 17.56% annually, ranking in the 78th percentile. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 5.01% year-over-year, ranking in the 44th percentile. The cost of living index is 4.64, positioned in the 68th percentile. The average weekly hours worked is 33.6 hours, below the national median and ranking in the 36th percentile.

These data points provide a snapshot of Kansas City's current economic conditions, highlighting both strengths and areas for consideration. The overall weighted percentile score is 63.2%, and a grade of B+ suggests that Kansas City's economy is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas.
