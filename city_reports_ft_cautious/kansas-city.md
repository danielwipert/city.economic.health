# Kansas City

**Grade: B (Average) | 52.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Kansas City's economy earns a grade of B, or 52.6th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a low unemployment rate of 3.50% and a high labor force participation rate of 67.91%. However, this is juxtaposed with a concerning -0.31% year-over-year nonfarm employment growth, signaling a contraction in the job market.

The city's wage growth at +6.80% year-over-year is driven by a mix shift towards higher-paying industries, rather than broad labor market health. This, combined with a highly affordable cost of living, may indicate that the affordability advantage is eroding as demand outpaces housing supply. Homes are selling in fewer days than last year, averaging just 45 days on market, further exacerbating the issue.

Kansas City offers businesses a unique combination of a deep labor pool and an affordable cost of living, but the primary risk lies in the stagnant job market and rising labor costs. As a business location, Kansas City presents a trade-off between accessing a talented workforce at a lower cost of living and navigating the challenges of a slowing job market and competitive housing environment.
