# Milwaukee-Waukesha

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 38.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Milwaukee's economic character is defined by a tight labor market, with an unemployment rate of 3.10%, and a challenging employment growth story, with a -0.91% year-over-year change in total nonfarm employment. This tension is further complicated by a cost of living composite score of 4.27, indicating that Milwaukee is among the most expensive metros, which can constrain talent acquisition and consumer spending power. The city's overall grade of C+, reflecting a 38.7th percentile ranking out of 50 US metros, suggests a challenging economic environment.

The labor market and workforce profile present a conflicting story. While the low unemployment rate suggests a competitive job market, the labor force participation rate of 63.19% indicates a shallow labor pool. The office and professional worker share is below average, ranking in the 22nd percentile, which may pose constraints for specialized or senior roles. The housing market is also slowing, with days on market increasing by 6.5% year-over-year, at 33 days.

In summary, Milwaukee offers a business a highly competitive labor market, but with significant constraints, including a shallow labor pool and high cost of living. The primary risk is that the city's economic growth may be hindered by its inability to attract and retain talent, making it a challenging location for businesses to thrive.
