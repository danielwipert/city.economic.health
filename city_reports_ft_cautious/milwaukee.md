## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 86th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.19% is at the 34th percentile, indicating a lower engagement of the working-age population in the labor market compared to other regions. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 3.73%, ranking in the 56th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases that align with broader national trends.

Housing market indicators show significant dynamics. The year-over-year increase in home prices is 21.59%, placing the region in the 94th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply in the housing market. Conversely, building permits have declined by 26.15% year-over-year, ranking in the 10th percentile, potentially signaling a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index is 0.67, in the 90th percentile, indicating that Milwaukee-Waukesha remains relatively affordable compared to other metropolitan areas. Additionally, the median days on market for homes is 58 days, in the 78th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where properties are neither selling exceptionally quickly nor languishing unsold.

Overall, the data suggests a metropolitan area with a strong labor market and housing affordability, but with some indicators pointing to potential constraints in labor force participation and new housing development.
