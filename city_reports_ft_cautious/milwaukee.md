# Milwaukee — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.1% places the region in the 86th percentile among comparable areas, indicating relatively low joblessness. However, the labour force participation rate of 63.19% is in the 34th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labour market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.73% ranks the region in the 56th percentile, indicating moderate wage increases.

In the housing market, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 23.89% is high, ranking in the 94th percentile. The number of building permits has declined by 23.05% year-over-year, placing it in the 14th percentile. This contrast may reflect a combination of factors, including strong demand for existing homes and a slowdown in new construction. The median days on market for properties is 39 days, which is in the 86th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market. The cost of living index of 4.8 and the weekly hours worked of 34.2 are both within the middle range of national comparisons, at the 62nd and 57th percentiles, respectively.

The data suggests that Milwaukee-Waukesha performs well in some areas, such as low unemployment and robust home price appreciation, but also indicates areas where improvement could be made, including labour force participation and new construction permits.
