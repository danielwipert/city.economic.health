# Milwaukee — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.1% places it in the 86th percentile among similar regions, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.19% ranks at the 34th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.73% ranks in the 56th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.8% is at the 62nd percentile. The average weekly hours worked are 34.2 hours, which is around the median, at the 58th percentile.

In the housing sector, data shows a significant increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 23.89%, placing the region in the 94th percentile. This contrasts with a decline in building permits, down 10.47% year-over-year, at the 38th percentile, which may reflect a change in new construction activity. The pace of sales remains brisk, with properties averaging 39 days on the market, in the 86th percentile, and rental prices have increased by 4.37% year-over-year, at the 88th percentile.

The weighted score of 63, corresponding to a grade of B+, indicates that Milwaukee-Waukesha performs above average relative to other metropolitan areas.
