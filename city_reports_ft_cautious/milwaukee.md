# Milwaukee-Waukesha

**Grade: C (Very Poor) | 36.2th percentile | April 2026**

---

Milwaukee earns a B+ grade, with its exceptionally low unemployment rate of 3.10% being the most important takeaway about this city. The city's labor market is highly competitive, with a tight hiring environment. However, this is paired with a shrinking civilian labor force, which has contracted by 3.11% year-over-year, posing a structural headwind for businesses seeking to expand their workforce.

The city's nonfarm employment growth has stagnated, with a year-over-year decline of 0.75%, indicating a broader economic slowdown. Milwaukee's low office and professional worker concentration, ranking in the 10th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses. The city's housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 6.5% year-over-year, and homes selling at an average of 33 days.

Milwaukee offers a highly competitive labor market, but with significant constraints, including a shrinking labor force and limited professional talent pool. The primary risk for businesses is demand erosion, driven by weak employment growth and a slowing housing market, which limits the city's attractiveness as a location for expansion or relocation.
