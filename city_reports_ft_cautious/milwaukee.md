## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 86th percentile among similar regions, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.19% is in the 34th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.73% is in the 56th percentile, indicating moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators present a more varied picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices is robust at 23.89%, placing it in the 94th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. Conversely, building permits have declined by 23.05% year-over-year, ranking in the 14th percentile, potentially signaling a slowdown in new construction. The median days on market for homes is 39 days, in the 86th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market. Overall, the weighted percentile score of 60.6 and a grade of B+ indicate an above-average economic performance, though with notable areas of both strength and concern.

These data points provide a snapshot of the current economic conditions in Milwaukee-Waukesha, highlighting both resilience and challenges.
