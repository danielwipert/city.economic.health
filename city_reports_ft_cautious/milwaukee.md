# Milwaukee — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 86th percentile among comparable regions. This suggests a relatively low level of joblessness. However, the labour force participation rate of 63.19% is in the 34th percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labour market. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 3.73%, ranking in the 56th percentile, indicating moderate wage increases.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices is 23.89%, placing the region in the 94th percentile. The cost of living index is 0.67, in the 90th percentile, indicating that living costs are relatively low compared to other metropolitan areas. Building permits have declined by 23.05% year-over-year, ranking in the 14th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 58 days, in the 78th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests that Milwaukee-Waukesha has an above-average economic performance, with strengths in employment, cost of living and housing appreciation, but also some areas showing signs of moderation or decline.
