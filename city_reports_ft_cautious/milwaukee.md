The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing the region in the 94th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.2% is more moderate, ranking in the 32nd percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force. Housing market dynamics are particularly noteworthy. The median days on market for homes is 50 days, in the 98th percentile, suggesting a relatively slow-moving market despite rising prices.

Overall, the data indicates a labor market with low unemployment but moderate participation, alongside a housing market characterized by high price appreciation and a decline in new construction activity.
