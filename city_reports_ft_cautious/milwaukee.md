## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Milwaukee-Waukesha metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 64.09% is more moderate, ranking in the 42nd percentile, suggesting that while employment is robust, there may be room for further engagement of the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.34% is below average, with a percentile score of 26, indicating slower wage growth compared to other areas.

Housing market indicators present a more complex picture. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 21.59% is significantly higher than the national average, placing it in the 94th percentile. This strong appreciation in home values contrasts with a decline in building permits, down 16.64% year-over-year, which ranks in the 32nd percentile. This discrepancy may reflect a combination of high demand and constrained supply in the housing market. Additionally, the days-on-market metric of 32 days is in the 98th percentile, suggesting a rapid turnover in property sales, which aligns with the strong price growth observed.

Overall, the data indicates that Milwaukee-Waukesha has a resilient labor market and a robust housing sector, though wage growth remains a point of concern.
