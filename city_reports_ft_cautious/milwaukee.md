# Milwaukee-Waukesha

**Grade: C (Very Poor) | 34.2th percentile | May 2026**

The Milwaukee-Waukesha metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 34.2th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a labor demand composite score of 3.15, indicating a below-average performance. **Labor Demand**

The unemployment rate in Milwaukee-Waukesha is 4.40%, ranking 39th percentile, indicating a moderate level of slack in the labor market. While the market is not extremely tight, it is still relatively competitive, with some implications for businesses trying to hire in the area. However, the moderate unemployment rate suggests that businesses may not face extreme wage pressure or hiring difficulties.

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Milwaukee-Waukesha is +2.83%, ranking 32nd percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This suggests that the city is not well-suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting firms. The Milwaukee-Waukesha metro area offers businesses a relatively moderate labor market, with some challenges in terms of labor demand and labor force growth. However, the city's expanding housing supply and relatively slow housing market may make it an attractive location for workers and businesses looking for a more accessible and affordable place to live and work.
