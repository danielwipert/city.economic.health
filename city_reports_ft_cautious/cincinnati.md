# Cincinnati

**Grade: C (Very Poor) | 36.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Cincinnati's struggling labor market is the single most important takeaway, with a bottom-tier nonfarm employment growth of -0.52% year-over-year, indicating job losses and stagnation. The city's labor force participation rate of 63.08% is also below average, suggesting a shallow labor pool. This challenging environment is further exacerbated by sluggish wage growth of +1.53% year-over-year, making it difficult for businesses to attract and retain talent.

Despite the labor market woes, Cincinnati is experiencing an aggressive expansion in housing supply, with building permits increasing by 57.47% year-over-year. However, this growth is not translating to a strong housing market, as homes are taking longer to sell, with days on market rising by 21.6% year-over-year to an average of 45 days.

Cincinnati's business environment is marked by a low cost of living, but its struggling labor market and slowing housing market pose significant risks. The primary caveat for businesses considering location in Cincinnati is the potential for demand erosion and talent attraction challenges, which could outweigh the benefits of a relatively affordable cost of living.
