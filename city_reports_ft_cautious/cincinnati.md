## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Cincinnati's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but not one that is exceptionally robust. The labor force participation rate of 64.07% is in the 40th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, with a year-over-year change of -0.27%, ranking in the 4th percentile, which may reflect challenges in wage growth or employment composition shifts.

Housing market indicators show more positive trends. The housing price index has increased by 20.88% year-over-year, placing Cincinnati in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a strong demand for housing. Permits for new residential construction have risen by 5.64% year-over-year, in the 62nd percentile, which could indicate ongoing development and investment in the housing sector. The cost of living index is 1.2, in the 88th percentile, indicating that living costs in Cincinnati are relatively high compared to other metropolitan areas. The average days on market for homes is 38 days, in the 84th percentile, which may reflect a competitive market with relatively quick sales processes.

Overall, Cincinnati's economic performance appears to be average, with strengths in the housing market offset by weaker labor market conditions and earnings growth.
