Cincinnati's economic landscape, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture. The metropolitan area ranks 30th overall, with a weighted percentile score of 53.8, placing it in the "Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.1%, which is slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 63.68%, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Notably, earnings have decreased year-over-year by 1.51%, a decline that may reflect broader economic pressures or sector-specific challenges. In contrast, the cost of living index is 1.05, suggesting that living expenses are relatively stable compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show a more positive trend. The housing price index has increased by 20% year-over-year, a significant rise that places Cincinnati in the 92nd percentile for this metric. This growth is accompanied by a 5.64% increase in building permits, indicating ongoing construction activity. However, the pace of sales remains moderate, with properties taking an average of 43 days to sell, which is within the upper quartile but not indicative of a particularly fast-moving market. Additionally, the rental market appears stable, with a 2.7% year-over-year increase in rents, placing the city in the 84th percentile for this indicator.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not robust economic environment in Cincinnati, with strengths in the housing market offset by modest labor market performance.
