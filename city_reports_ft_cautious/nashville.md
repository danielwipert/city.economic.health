# Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin

**Grade: A- (Good) | 61.1th percentile | April 2026**

---

The Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin metro area has an overall grade of A- with a composite score ranking at the 61.1th percentile out of 50 US metros. **Labor Demand**

This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. This indicates a strong labor market with increasing demand for workers.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate is 3.10%, ranking at the 92nd percentile, indicating a very tight labor market with little slack. This means that businesses trying to hire in this city will face significant competition for talent, leading to potential wage pressure. With such a low unemployment rate, companies may need to offer competitive salaries to attract and retain workers.

**Wage Growth**

This slow wage growth means that employer labor costs are not rising rapidly, but it also implies weak bargaining power for workers and potentially limited purchasing power. **Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +1.87% YoY, ranking at the 89th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth in labor force supply is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire, as it suggests a increasing pool of potential workers. With a growing labor force, companies may find it easier to staff their operations.

This decline in building permits suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability concerns and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. The current median days on market is 53 days, with a year-over-year increase of +12.8%. This indicates a relatively slow market, ranking near the median at the 52nd percentile. For workers relocating to this city, the slower market may make it easier to find a home, but it also suggests a less competitive housing market.

**Office Economy**
The share of jobs in professional and office sectors is 3.57, ranking at the 89th percentile, indicating a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. This city is well-suited for businesses that require specialized knowledge-economy workers, but may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-dominant workforces.

The Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a tight labor market, expanding workforce supply, and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for future housing supply constraints due to declining building permits, which may impact workforce accommodation and affordability.
