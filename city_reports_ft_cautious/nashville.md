# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate is 3.1%, placing it in the 92nd percentile among comparable regions. This suggests a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.06% ranks in the 98th percentile, indicating a high level of workforce engagement. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect a stable income environment.

Housing market indicators show both growth and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile. The number of building permits has risen by 22.78% year-over-year, ranking in the 84th percentile. The days on market metric stands at 63.0 days, in the 26th percentile. These metrics suggest a balanced housing market.

The data indicates that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and moderate growth in other sectors.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 92nd percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.06% further supports this, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect a more stable but less dynamic income environment.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. In contrast, the number of building permits has risen by 22.78% year-over-year, ranking in the 84th percentile, which could indicate ongoing construction activity and potential growth in the housing supply. The days on market metric stands at 63.0 days, in the 26th percentile, which may suggest a balanced market with neither an excess of inventory nor a severe shortage.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and moderate growth in other sectors.
