# Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin

**Grade: A+ (Excellent) | 70.6th percentile | May 2026**

---

Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score ranking in the 70.6th percentile among 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score in the 90th percentile, and its top-tier labor force growth rate of 1.86% YoY. These metrics signal a city with a thriving job market and an expanding workforce.

**Labor Demand**
Nashville's employment growth rate is 0.84% YoY, combined with a weekly hours deviation of 0.874% above its own trend, indicating genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, a clear sign of a strong and growing economy. The labor demand composite score of 6.35 further reinforces this assessment, ranking in the 90th percentile.

**Unemployment**
The city's unemployment rate is 3.30%, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The tight market also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power.

**Wage Growth**
Nashville's wage growth rate is 3.94% YoY, ranking in the 54th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively so, and workers are seeing some improvement in their purchasing power. However, the moderate growth rate may not be sufficient to keep pace with the city's growing economy.

**Cost of Living**
With a cost of living ratio of 7.33, and a PSF of $262/sqft that is falling by 1.1% YoY relative to wages of $35.73/hr, Nashville ranks in the 69th percentile for affordability. This means that the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers, making it an attractive location for talent without requiring significant wage premiums. The falling PSF is a key driver of this affordability.

**Labor Force Growth**
The city's labor force is growing at a rate of 1.86% YoY, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth rate suggests that businesses will have access to a increasing pool of potential workers, making it easier to hire and staff their operations. The growing labor force is a significant advantage for companies looking to locate in Nashville.

**Building Permits**
The city has seen a decline in building permits of 31.30% YoY, indicating a tightening of the housing supply. This decline suggests that the city may face future affordability challenges and workforce accommodation constraints, as the supply of new housing is not keeping pace with demand. This could be a concern for businesses looking to attract and retain talent.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market is 50 days, with a YoY increase of 6.4%, indicating a slightly slower market. This means that workers relocating to Nashville may find a more accessible and less competitive housing market, with more time to find a suitable home. However, the relatively fast pace of sales still suggests a strong demand for housing in the city.

**Office Economy**
Nashville has a deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 90th percentile, making it an attractive location for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. The city's strong office economy suggests that it is well-suited for knowledge-based industries, but may be less suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

In conclusion, Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin offers businesses a thriving job market, an expanding workforce, and a relatively affordable cost of living, making it an attractive location for companies looking to grow and hire talent. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the decline in building permits, which could lead to future affordability challenges and workforce accommodation constraints, potentially limiting the city's ability to support long-term business growth.
