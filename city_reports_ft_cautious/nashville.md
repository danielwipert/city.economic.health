# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area's economic data shows a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 94th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 75.77% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of workforce engagement. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, which may indicate a slower wage growth environment compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show both positive and negative trends. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. Building permits have risen by 22.78% year-over-year, a significant increase that ranks in the 84th percentile, indicating active construction activity. Meanwhile, the supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 0.13% year-over-year, a minimal change that places the region in the 60th percentile. The median days on market for homes is 63 days, which is in the 26th percentile, suggesting a relatively balanced market.

The data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and active housing construction, although wage growth and the housing market balance out these positive trends.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 94th percentile nationally, which suggests a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 75.77% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect a slower wage growth environment compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. Building permits have risen by 22.78% year-over-year, a significant increase that ranks in the 84th percentile, indicating strong construction activity. Conversely, the supply of for-sale homes has slightly decreased by 0.13% year-over-year, though this change is minimal and places the region in the 60th percentile. The median days on market for homes is 63 days, which is in the 26th percentile, suggesting a relatively balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and active housing construction, despite moderate wage growth and a balanced housing market.
