# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.1% places it in the 94th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a strong labour market. The labour force participation rate of 75.77% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 2.8%, placing it in the 34th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate pace of income expansion.

Housing market indicators show both positive and cautionary signs. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 7.88%, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating steady appreciation. Building permits have risen by 22.78% year-over-year, a notable increase that ranks in the 84th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction. The days on market metric stands at 61 days, in the 22nd percentile, which may indicate a balanced market.

The weighted score of 67 and the grade of B+ suggest that Nashville's economy is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and labour force participation, but with more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing market dynamics.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 94th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 75.77% further supports this, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.8%, placing it in the 34th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate pace of income expansion.

Housing market indicators show both strength and caution. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 7.88%, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating steady appreciation. Building permits have risen by 22.78% year-over-year, a significant increase that ranks in the 84th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction. Conversely, the days on market metric stands at 61 days, in the 22nd percentile, which might indicate a more balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers dominate.

Overall, the weighted score of 67 and the grade of B+ suggest that Nashville's economy is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and labor force participation, but with more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing market dynamics.
