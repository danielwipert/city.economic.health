# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across several key indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.0% places it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of workforce engagement. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate expansion in income levels.

Housing market indicators show both positive and negative trends. The home price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 62nd percentile, indicating continued appreciation in property values. Conversely, building permits have declined by 8.94% year-over-year, in the 44th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction activity. The rental market appears stable, with a slight decrease in the number of days properties remain on the market to 60 days, and a minor decline in the per-square-foot rental rate of -0.13% year-over-year.

The data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market metrics offset by more moderate growth in earnings and some signs of cooling in the housing sector.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate expansion in income levels.

Housing market indicators show both strength and caution. The home price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 62nd percentile, indicating continued appreciation in property values. Conversely, building permits have declined by 8.94% year-over-year, in the 44th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction activity. The rental market appears stable, with a slight decrease in the number of days properties remain on the market, down to 60 days, and a minor decline in the per-square-foot rental rate of -0.13% year-over-year.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market metrics offset by more moderate growth in earnings and some signs of cooling in the housing sector.
