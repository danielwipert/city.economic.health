# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may be influenced by broader economic conditions or sector-specific trends.

Housing market indicators show both growth and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, indicating continued demand and value appreciation, with a slower pace of increase compared to previous years. The number of building permits issued has risen by 6.15% year-over-year, suggesting ongoing construction activity at a moderate pace. The supply of for-sale homes, as measured by days on market, remains relatively low at 60 days, which could indicate a balanced market where demand is meeting supply without significant excesses.

The data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and a stable housing market. However, wage growth and cost of living pressures remain areas of potential concern.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect broader economic conditions or sector-specific trends.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, indicating continued demand and value appreciation, though the pace of increase has slowed compared to previous years. The number of building permits issued has risen by 6.15% year-over-year, suggesting ongoing construction activity, albeit at a moderate pace. The supply of for-sale homes, as measured by days on market, remains relatively low at 60 days, which could indicate a balanced market where demand is meeting supply without significant excesses.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and a stable housing market, though wage growth and cost of living pressures remain areas of potential concern.
