# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of workforce engagement. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may indicate a stable but less dynamic income environment.

Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 10.88%, placing it in the 62nd percentile. Single-family housing permits have declined by 0.13% year-over-year, ranking in the 60th percentile, suggesting continued but moderating construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 60 days, in the 28th percentile, which could indicate a balanced market.

Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, in the 80th percentile, suggesting a relatively full workweek for employed individuals. Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strengths in employment and labor force participation, but with more moderate growth in earnings and housing permits.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which suggests a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% further supports this, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a more stable but less dynamic income environment.

Housing market indicators show a strong appreciation in home prices, with a year-over-year increase of 10.88%, placing it in the 62nd percentile. This contrasts with a slight decline in single-family housing permits, down 0.13% year-over-year, though this still ranks in the 60th percentile, suggesting continued but moderating construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 60 days, in the 28th percentile, which could indicate a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, in the 80th percentile, suggesting a relatively full workweek for employed individuals.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strengths in employment and labor force participation, but with more moderate growth in earnings and housing permits.
