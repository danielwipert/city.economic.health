## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 92nd percentile among comparable regions, suggesting a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.06% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, which may reflect a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, indicating that wage increases have been limited compared to other areas.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 7.88%, situated in the 54th percentile, suggesting steady appreciation without the rapid escalation seen in some other markets. Building permits have increased by 22.78% year-over-year, ranking in the 84th percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. The supply of homes for sale has slightly decreased by 0.13% year-over-year, placing it in the 60th percentile, while the average days on market is 63 days, in the 26th percentile, suggesting a balanced market with neither a surplus nor a shortage of available properties.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and moderate growth in housing and earnings.
