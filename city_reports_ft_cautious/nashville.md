# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing a positive performance. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may indicate a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show a combination of strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 62nd percentile, indicating a relatively strong appreciation in home values. The number of building permits has grown by 6.15% year-over-year, which is in the 60th percentile, suggesting a steady expansion in construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 60 days, in the 28th percentile, which may indicate a balanced market.

The data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and moderate growth in earnings and housing.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 62nd percentile, indicating a relatively strong appreciation in home values. In contrast, the number of building permits has grown by 6.15% year-over-year, which is in the 60th percentile, suggesting a steady but not overly aggressive expansion in construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 60 days, in the 28th percentile, which may indicate a balanced market with neither a significant buyer's nor seller's advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market indicators and moderate growth in earnings and housing.
