# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed but generally positive trends across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a strong labour market. The labour force participation rate of 76.27% further supports this, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement in the workforce.

However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate income environment. Housing market indicators show both strength and caution. The housing price index has increased by 10.88% year-over-year, indicating a strong appreciation in home values. The number of building permits has risen by 6.15% year-over-year, suggesting continued investment in residential construction.

Conversely, the days on market metric stands at 71.0 days, which is relatively high and may indicate some softening in the pace of sales. The overall weighted percentile score of 66.5 and a grade of B+ suggest that Nashville's economy is performing above average compared to other metropolitan areas, with notable strengths in employment and labour force participation.

These data points collectively indicate that Nashville's economy has solid fundamentals in the labour market, balanced by more moderate growth in earnings and some signs of cooling in the housing market.
