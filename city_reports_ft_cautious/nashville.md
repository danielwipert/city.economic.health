# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across several key indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.0% places it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a strong labour market. The labour force participation rate of 76.27% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect broader economic conditions or sector-specific trends.

Housing market indicators show both growth and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating steady appreciation. Building permits have declined by 6.96% year-over-year, ranking in the 40th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction or a shift in development priorities. The days on market metric stands at 71 days, in the 42nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market.

The weighted score of 64 and a grade of B+ indicate that Nashville's economy is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and labour force participation offset by more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing construction.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.27% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect broader economic conditions or sector-specific trends.

Housing market indicators show both strength and moderation. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating steady appreciation. In contrast, building permits have declined by 6.96% year-over-year, ranking in the 40th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction or a shift in development priorities. The days on market metric stands at 71 days, in the 42nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers dominate.

Overall, the weighted score of 64 and a grade of B+ indicate that Nashville's economy is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and labor force participation offset by more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing construction.
