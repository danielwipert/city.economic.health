# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 94th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 75.77% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement in the workforce.

However, earnings growth year-over-year is 2.8%, placing it in the 34th percentile, which may reflect a more moderate expansion in income levels. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in housing prices of 7.88%, placing the region in the 54th percentile, while building permits have risen by 22.78%, a notable increase that places the area in the 84th percentile.

The cost of living index of 5.68, in the 14th percentile, and the overall wage ratio of 3.72, in the 86th percentile, indicate that living costs are relatively low, while wages are competitive compared to other regions. The average weekly hours worked, at 35.0 hours, rank in the 82nd percentile, indicating a balanced workweek.

The overall weighted score of 67 and a grade of B+ suggest that Nashville's economy is performing above average relative to its peers, with strengths in employment and labor force participation, but with more moderate performance in earnings growth and cost of living.
