# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 92nd percentile, indicating a strong labour market. The labour force participation rate of 76.06% is higher, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting a high level of workforce engagement. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.15%, placing it in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect a more moderate income environment. The cost of living index of 7.83 is at the bottom of the distribution (0th percentile), indicating that living costs in Nashville are relatively low compared to other metropolitan areas. The average weekly hours worked, at 35.4, is in the 86th percentile, suggesting that workers are maintaining a relatively high level of employment activity.

Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in the housing price index of 7.88%, placing it in the 54th percentile, indicating a steady increase in home prices. The number of building permits has risen by 22.78% year-over-year, ranking in the 84th percentile, suggesting ongoing construction activity. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 0.13% year-over-year, placing it in the 60th percentile, which may reflect a balanced market with stable inventory levels. Homes are taking an average of 63 days to sell, in the 26th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of sales. The weighted percentile score of 64.7 and a grade of B+ suggest that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strengths in employment and labour force participation, but with more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing supply.

The data suggests that Nashville's economic environment is balanced, with no clear indication of rapid growth or significant weakness.
