# Nashville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a strong labour market. The labour force participation rate of 76.17% ranks in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a stable but less dynamic wage environment.

Housing market indicators show a nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, indicating continued appreciation in home values. Building permits have declined by 6.96% year-over-year, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 71 days, which is relatively high and may indicate a balanced market between buyers and sellers. The cost of living index of 7.82 is low, suggesting that living costs in Nashville are below the national average.

The data suggests that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labour market metrics and moderate housing market activity.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 76.17% is even more impressive, ranking in the 98th percentile, suggesting high engagement among the working-age population. However, earnings growth year-over-year is relatively modest at 1.62%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a more stable but less dynamic wage environment.

Housing market indicators present a nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 7.88% year-over-year, indicating continued appreciation in home values, although this is not as pronounced as in some other markets. Building permits have declined by 6.96% year-over-year, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 71 days, which is relatively high and may indicate a more balanced market between buyers and sellers. Additionally, the cost of living index of 7.82, while low, suggests that living costs in Nashville are below the national average, potentially enhancing the region's attractiveness for both residents and businesses.

Overall, the data indicates that Nashville's economy is performing above average, with strong labor market metrics and moderate housing market activity.
