# Miami — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area ranks sixth in the dataset, with a weighted percentile score of 45.8, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, a relatively low level, and the labour force participation rate is 62.53%, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is 7.12%, placing it in the 86th percentile, suggesting strong income gains for those employed.

However, the cost of living has increased by 5.72% over the same period. Housing market indicators present a mixed picture. Building permits have increased by 20.94% year-over-year, a notable rise that could indicate ongoing construction activity. The housing price index has declined by 1.47% year-over-year, while the price-to-rent ratio has decreased by 2.69%, suggesting a softening in the housing market. Properties are taking longer to sell, with an average of 88 days on the market, which is higher than usual and may reflect a slowdown in demand or an oversupply of homes.

The data suggests a metropolitan area with strong employment and earnings growth but facing challenges in the housing sector and rising living costs.
