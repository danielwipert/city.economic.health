# Miami — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area has been ranked sixth in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 45.3, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, which is relatively low, ranking in the 68th percentile among comparable regions. However, the labour force participation rate of 62.53% is lower, ranking in the 26th percentile, indicating a mixed picture of labour market conditions.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.12%, placing Miami in the 86th percentile, indicating strong income gains. In contrast, the cost of living has risen by 5.79%, ranking in the 22nd percentile, which may reflect broader inflationary pressures affecting the region. Housing market indicators show a decline in home prices, with a year-over-year decrease of 1.47%, and a reduction in single-family home prices by 2.43%. Building permits have increased by 20.94% year-over-year, suggesting ongoing construction activity despite the softening in prices.

The data presents a complex economic landscape in Miami, with strong earnings growth and continued construction activity, but also challenges in labour force participation and rising costs of living.
