# Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 49.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Miami earns a below-average overall grade of 49.7th percentile, with its most notable feature being rapid wage growth amidst stagnant employment. The city's nonfarm employment growth is in contraction, with a -0.70% year-over-year change, indicating job losses or near-stagnation. Hourly earnings are rising by 8.39% year-over-year, a top-tier performance.

The labor force is growing at a moderate pace of 1.40% year-over-year, which is a positive sign for workforce supply. However, the housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 10.4% year-over-year to 74 days, indicating demand erosion. Building permits are up 41.11% year-over-year, suggesting that housing supply is expanding aggressively, which could help alleviate affordability pressures.

Miami's combination of rapid wage growth and expanding housing supply could attract businesses looking to tap into a skilled workforce. However, the city's stagnant employment growth, with a -0.70% year-over-year change, poses a primary risk, potentially limiting the pool of available talent and making it challenging to scale operations.
