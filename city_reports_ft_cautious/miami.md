# Miami — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area ranks sixth in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 46, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, which is lower than the national average, suggesting a relatively strong labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.53% is below the national average, indicating that a portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is 7.12%, placing it in the 86th percentile, suggesting strong wage increases for those employed.

The housing market presents a mixed picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.47%, while the price-to-square-foot ratio has decreased by 2.43%. These declines may reflect changes in demand or supply in the housing market. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 83 days, a notable increase from previous periods, which could indicate a slowdown in sales activity. Construction activity, as measured by building permits, has seen a year-over-year increase of 41.11%, suggesting ongoing investment in the real estate sector despite the softening in prices.

The data suggests a complex economic landscape in Miami, with strong labor market conditions and wage growth contrasting with signs of a moderating housing market.
