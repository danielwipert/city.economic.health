# Miami — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area ranks sixth in the dataset, with a weighted percentile score of 45.4, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The area's unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, which is relatively low and suggests a stable labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 62.53% is below the national average, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labour market.

Earnings growth year-over-year is 7.12%, placing Miami in the 86th percentile for this metric. This may reflect a strong demand for labour or rising productivity. In contrast, the cost of living has increased by 5.79%, suggesting that wage gains might be partially offset by higher living costs. Housing indicators show mixed trends: building permits have increased by 20.94% year-over-year, indicating continued investment in residential construction. However, home prices have declined by 1.47% over the same period, and rental prices have decreased by 2.43%. Properties are also taking longer to sell, with an average of 83 days on the market.

These data points indicate a complex economic landscape in Miami, characterised by strong labour market conditions and earnings growth, but also by challenges in housing affordability and labour force engagement.
