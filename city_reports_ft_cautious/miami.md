# Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach

**Grade: B+ (Above Average) | 55.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Miami's economy earns a B+ grade, driven by its strong performance, with a low unemployment rate of 3.50% and rapid wage growth of 7.12% year-over-year, indicating a constructive labor market. However, the city's labor force participation rate of 62.53% suggests a shallow labor pool, which may limit its ability to attract and retain talent.

The housing market presents a mixed picture, with building permits surging 41.11% year-over-year, but the days on market have increased by 10.4% year-over-year, with homes taking an average of 74 days to sell. This signals a slowing market, despite strong employment growth and rising permits. The cost of living, at 3.31, is a consideration for businesses looking to attract talent, particularly given the rapid wage growth.

Miami offers businesses a dynamic labor market with rising wages and an expanding housing supply, making it an attractive location for talent and investment. However, the primary risk lies in the city's ability to balance its above-average cost of living with the need to attract and retain top talent, potentially requiring wage premiums to remain competitive.
