# Miami — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area ranks sixth in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 43.6, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, a relatively low level, and the labor force participation rate is 62.53%, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is 7.12%, suggesting an improvement in income levels. However, the cost of living has increased by 5.72% over the same period.

Housing market indicators show a mixed picture. Building permits have risen by 7.44% year-over-year, indicating ongoing construction activity. The housing price index has declined by 1.03% over the same period, and the price-to-square-foot ratio has decreased by 2.69%, which may reflect a softening in the real estate market. Properties are taking longer to sell, with an average of 88 days on the market, a notable increase from previous periods. Weekly hours worked remain steady at 35.2 hours, in line with national averages.

The data presents a complex economic landscape in Miami, with strong employment and earnings growth tempered by rising costs and a cooling housing market.
