# Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach

**Grade: B+ (Above Average) | 55.8th percentile | April 2026**

---

Miami's economy earns a B+ grade, ranking in the 55.8th percentile among 50 US metros, with a single most important takeaway being its dynamic labor market, characterized by a 3.50% unemployment rate and 7.12% year-over-year wage growth. The city's labor force participation rate of 62.53% indicates a relatively shallow labor pool, but strong wage growth is a significant draw, albeit with increased labor costs for employers.

The high cost of living in Miami, with a cost of living composite score of 3.36, limits real purchasing power gains, despite significant nominal wage increases. The housing market is characterized by a slowing pace of sales, with days on market increasing by 12.2% year-over-year to an average of 83 days, despite a 41.11% year-over-year increase in building permits. This complex landscape presents challenges for businesses, particularly in terms of labor costs and housing supply.

Miami offers businesses a unique blend of a dynamic labor market and expanding housing supply, making it an attractive location for companies willing to adapt to its high cost of living. However, the primary risk for businesses is the potential for labor costs to outpace productivity gains, making it essential to carefully consider compensation strategies to attract and retain top talent.
