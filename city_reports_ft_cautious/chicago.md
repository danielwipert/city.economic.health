# Chicago-Naperville-Elgin

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 39.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Chicago's economic character is defined by a rapidly tightening housing market and labor market slack, with an unemployment rate of 4.50%. This labor market slack could make hiring easier, but also potentially weaken local consumer demand. Meanwhile, the housing market is extremely fast, with homes selling in just 35 days on average, a pace that is accelerating with a -2.8% year-over-year change.

The sharp decline in building permits, down 27.41% year-over-year, raises concerns about the sustainability of this housing market strength. This contraction in housing supply, combined with the rapid pace of home sales, may be creating a thin-supply illusion rather than genuine demand strength. The labor force participation rate, at 64.75%, is a mild positive, but it is not enough to offset the concerns about housing supply and labor market slack.

Chicago offers a business a labor market with some slack, which could facilitate hiring, but also poses significant risks in terms of housing affordability and workforce attraction. The rapidly tightening housing market could become a major obstacle to attracting and retaining talent, making it essential for businesses to carefully consider the potential implications of this dynamic on their operations and growth prospects.
