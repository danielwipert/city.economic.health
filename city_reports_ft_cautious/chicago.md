## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable economic environment. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, which is relatively low and suggests a labor market that continues to support employment. The labor force participation rate of 65.11% is above the median, indicating a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.12% suggests modest income gains, while the cost of living increase of 4.97% is in line with this trend, potentially reflecting a balanced relationship between income and expenses.

In the housing market, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 5.46% and a 11.46% rise in building permits suggest ongoing demand and investment in residential construction. However, the slight decline in single-family home sales of -0.16% may indicate some softening in the market. The average days on market for homes is 38 days, which is relatively low and could suggest a competitive market for buyers. Weekly hours worked have decreased to 33.3 hours, which is significantly below the median, possibly indicating a shift in work patterns or reduced demand for labor hours.

Overall, the data suggests a moderate economic performance for Chicago, with some areas showing strength and others indicating potential areas for concern.
