## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metropolitan area ranks third in its region, with an overall weighted percentile score of 40.7, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, which is relatively low but places the region in the 20th percentile nationally, suggesting that labor market conditions may be tighter than in many other areas. The labor force participation rate of 64.75% is more in line with national averages, ranking in the 60th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 2.84%, placing the region in the 42nd percentile, while consumer price inflation stands at 5.07%, slightly above the earnings growth rate. This suggests that real wage growth may be limited. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture: the housing price index has increased by 5.37% year-over-year, placing the region in the 44th percentile, while the supply of existing homes for sale has grown by only 1.63%, ranking in the 76th percentile. The number of building permits issued has declined by 27.01% year-over-year, a significant drop that places the region in the 4th percentile, potentially reflecting a slowdown in new construction activity. Properties are taking an average of 40 days to sell, which is relatively quick and places the region in the 80th percentile, indicating a strong demand for existing homes.

Overall, these data points suggest a metropolitan area with a stable but not robust economic environment, characterized by moderate employment and wage growth, and a housing market that remains active despite a decline in new construction.
