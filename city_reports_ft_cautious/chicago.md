## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metropolitan area suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 20th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively tight labor market but one that lags behind many other regions. The labor force participation rate of 64.75% is more favorable, ranking in the 60th percentile, suggesting a higher level of engagement in the workforce compared to other areas. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.84% and consumer price inflation of 4.99% indicate modest income growth that is slightly outpaced by inflation, potentially eroding purchasing power.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 5.73% and a 1.3% rise in rental prices suggest a steady but not overly robust market. The number of building permits has declined by 18.25% year-over-year, which may reflect a slowdown in construction activity or a shift in development priorities. Properties are taking an average of 60 days to sell, which is in line with national trends and suggests a balanced market without significant buyer or seller advantages.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 42.4 and the grade of B- indicate that Chicago's economic performance is below average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in labor force participation and housing prices, but challenges in employment and construction activity.
