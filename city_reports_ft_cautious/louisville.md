# Louisville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Louisville-Jefferson County metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.1% ranks in the 86th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. However, the labour force participation rate of 62.74% is in the 28th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labour market.

Year-over-year earnings growth is 3.37%, ranking in the 52nd percentile, indicating moderate wage increases. The cost of living index of 0.75 places Louisville below the national average, which may contribute to the relative affordability of living in the area. Housing market indicators show a strong performance, with housing prices increasing by 16.45% year-over-year and days on market at 58 days, both in the 78th percentile. Building permits have risen by 11.25% year-over-year, reflecting ongoing construction activity.

The weighted score of 63 and a grade of B+ suggest that Louisville's economy is performing above average, with strengths in employment, housing and cost of living, though labour force participation remains a point of consideration.

The cost of living index of 0.75 places Louisville well below the national average, which may contribute to the relative affordability of living in the area. Housing market indicators show a robust performance, with housing prices increasing by 16.45% year-over-year and days on market at 58 days, both in the 78th percentile. Building permits have also risen by 11.25% year-over-year, reflecting ongoing construction activity.

Overall, the weighted score of 63 and a grade of B+ suggest that Louisville's economy is performing above average, with strengths in employment, housing, and cost of living, though labor force participation remains a point of consideration.
