# Louisville-Jefferson County

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.3th percentile | April 2026**

---

Louisville's economy earns a grade of B, or 53rd percentile out of 50 US metros, with a tight labor market and limited availability of workers. The city's exceptionally low unemployment rate of 3.10% indicates significant hiring competition and worker leverage. However, this is paired with a below-average labor force participation rate of 62.74%, suggesting a shallow labor pool.

The interplay between these metrics reveals key tensions in the market. The low unemployment rate is offset by weak labor force participation and slow nonfarm employment growth of -0.06%, signaling economic deceleration. The housing market also shows signs of slowing demand, with days on market increasing by 9.8% year-over-year to an average of 45 days.

In summary, Louisville presents businesses with a unique challenge, with a tight labor market and slow employment growth. The primary risk is demand erosion, driven by weak employment growth and a slowing housing market, which may impact the city's long-term economic viability.
