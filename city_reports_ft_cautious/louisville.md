# Louisville-Jefferson County

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 47.4th percentile | April 2026**

---

Louisville earns a B+ grade, with its exceptionally low unemployment rate of 3.10% being the most important takeaway about this city. The city's labor market is characterized by a tight hiring environment where workers have leverage, but this is paired with below-average labor force growth of 0.38% YoY and sluggish nonfarm employment growth of -0.24% YoY, indicating a shallow pool of available workers and a decelerating economy.

The city's low office and professional worker concentration, ranking in the 18th percentile, constrains its appeal to knowledge-economy businesses. The slow labor force growth and weak employment growth signal a potential shortage of skilled workers, making it challenging for businesses to find and retain talent. The housing market is moderating, with above-average days on market of 45 days and a 9.8% YoY increase, which is a sign of softening demand given the weak employment growth.

Louisville's below-average cost of living composite of 3.38 means that businesses may need to offer wage premiums to attract talent, complicating the hiring landscape. The city's shallow labor pool, slow employment growth, and low professional worker concentration pose significant risks for businesses, and the potential for talent shortages and hiring challenges may outweigh the benefits of the city's low cost of living and moderating housing market.
