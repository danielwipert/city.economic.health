## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Louisville-Jefferson County metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it slightly above the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, with a percentile score of 52. The labor force participation rate is at 63.25%, which is below the median, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment. Earnings growth year-over-year is reported at 2.68%, also below the median, suggesting moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators show more robust activity. The year-over-year increase in housing permits is 18.48%, significantly above the median, reflecting a strong level of new construction activity. The home price index has risen by 16.45% year-over-year, also well above the median, which may indicate a strong demand for housing in the region. The days on market for properties have decreased to 43 days, which is among the lower values, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market. The cost of living index is 0.76, indicating that Louisville remains more affordable than many other metropolitan areas, which could be attracting both residents and businesses.

Overall, the data suggests that while some aspects of the labor market are performing modestly, the housing sector is showing signs of strength and growth.
