# Riverside — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength and others pointing to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, placing it in the 4th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate is 60.37%, ranking in the 14th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.54%, ranking in the 88th percentile, which may reflect a strong demand for labor or rising costs of living. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.55%, placing it in the 30th percentile, suggesting moderate price pressures.

The housing market dynamics are complex. The housing price index has increased by 10.03% year-over-year, placing it in the 56th percentile, indicating a steady appreciation in home values. However, the number of building permits has declined by 8.31% year-over-year, ranking in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The rental market appears stable, with a 1.3% increase in rents, in the 16th percentile, and a slight decrease in the supply of rental properties, down 0.59% year-over-year, in the 52nd percentile. The average days on market for homes is 60 days, in the 28th percentile, suggesting a balanced market.

The overall economic conditions in the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metro area suggest a range of opportunities and challenges, with a weighted score of 37 out of 100, corresponding to a grade of C+.
