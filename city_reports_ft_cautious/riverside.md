The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area, with Riverside as its primary city, reveals a mixed picture of labor market conditions and housing dynamics. The unemployment rate stands at 5.9%, placing it in the 2nd percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively high level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 60.25% is in the 6th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market compared to other areas. However, earnings growth over the past year has been robust, with a 7.71% increase, ranking in the 88th percentile, which may reflect strong wage pressures or improvements in the quality of employment.

Housing market indicators show both positive and negative trends. The year-over-year change in house prices has increased by 7.22%, placing the region in the 52nd percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. The supply of existing homes, as measured by the percentage change in the number of properties for sale, has decreased by 0.88%, in the 48th percentile, while the average days on market remains at 61 days, in the 32nd percentile, indicating a stable but not particularly dynamic market.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 31.2 and the grade of C+ suggest that Riverside's economic performance is below average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in earnings growth offset by weaknesses in employment and housing market activity.
