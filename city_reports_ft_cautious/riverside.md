## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area, centered around Riverside, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 6.1%, placing it in the lower 2nd percentile when compared to other regions, which suggests a relatively high level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 60.02% is also below average, ranking in the 6th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment. On a more positive note, earnings growth over the past year has been robust at 7.71%, placing it in the 88th percentile, suggesting that those who are employed are experiencing significant wage increases. However, the cost of living index at 5.61% and the weekly hours worked at 33.9 hours per week, both in the 24th and 38th percentiles respectively, indicate moderate pressures on household budgets and work schedules.

In the housing market, the annual increase in home prices of 7.22% places Riverside in the 52nd percentile, reflecting a steady but not overly rapid appreciation in property values. The supply of single-family homes, however, has decreased by 0.88% year-over-year, ranking in the 48th percentile, which may reflect a tightening market. The number of building permits issued has declined by 18.53% year-over-year, placing it in the 30th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. Additionally, the median days on market for homes is 61 days, in the 32nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests a region with some areas of strength, particularly in earnings growth, but also facing challenges in employment and housing market dynamics.
