# Riverside — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, ranking in the 4th percentile nationally, indicating a higher unemployment level compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate is 60.37%, placing it in the 14th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of workforce engagement. Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.54%, ranking in the 88th percentile, which is above average. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is 5.55%, placing it in the 30th percentile, indicating moderate price pressures.

Housing market indicators show a complex picture. The housing price index has increased by 10.03% year-over-year, placing it in the 56th percentile, reflecting steady growth. Permits for new residential construction have risen by 18.04% year-over-year, ranking in the 74th percentile. The supply of existing homes appears constrained, with a 0.59% decrease in the number of properties for sale year-over-year and an average of 60 days on the market, both metrics falling in the middle percentiles. These figures suggest a balanced but tight housing market.

The economic performance of the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metro area is characterized by strong earnings growth and a moderately tight labor market, alongside a housing sector with both demand and supply constraints.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, placing it in the 4th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 60.37% is in the 14th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, with a 7.54% increase, ranking in the 88th percentile, which is significantly above average. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.55%, placing it in the 30th percentile, indicating moderate price pressures.

Housing market indicators show a complex picture. The housing price index has increased by 10.03% year-over-year, placing it in the 56th percentile, reflecting steady but not exceptional growth. Permits for new residential construction have risen by 18.04% year-over-year, ranking in the 74th percentile, which may indicate a growing demand for new housing. However, the supply of existing homes appears constrained, with a 0.59% decrease in the number of properties for sale year-over-year, and an average of 60 days on the market, both metrics falling in the middle percentiles. These figures suggest a balanced but tight housing market.

Overall, the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metro area's economic performance is characterized by strong earnings growth and a moderately tight labor market, alongside a housing sector that shows signs of both demand and supply constraints.
