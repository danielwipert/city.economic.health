# Riverside — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Riverside's labour market is exhibiting signs of strain, with the unemployment rate rising to 5.1%, a 0.2 percentage point increase year-on-year. This modest deterioration in labour market conditions is accompanied by a 1.15% expansion in the civilian labour force over the past year, indicating ongoing workforce supply growth. However, employment growth has been sluggish, with nonfarm payrolls increasing by just 0.18% year-on-year. The labour demand composite score of 3.68, ranking in the 18th percentile nationally, highlights weak job creation momentum. In contrast, average hourly earnings growth remains robust, at 7.55% year-on-year, placing it in the 88th percentile. This suggests that wage pressures are being driven by sectoral or inflationary factors, rather than broad-based labour scarcity.

The housing market in Riverside is characterised by supply constraints and slowing activity. While residential building permits have surged 18.04% year-on-year, ranking in the 76th percentile, home sales are struggling. The median days-on-market has increased by 5.88% to 54 days, and the days-on-market composite score ranks in the 32nd percentile. Affordability remains a moderate constraint, with a cost of living score of 2.82, ranking in the 44th percentile. This is particularly notable given the stagnant employment growth. Furthermore, the office worker presence is weak, with a composite score of 1.3, ranking in the 12th percentile, which aligns with the broader trend of hybrid work in Southern California and may dampen commercial real estate and downtown economic activity.

Looking ahead, Riverside's economy faces near-term challenges in job creation and housing turnover, despite strong wage growth and construction activity. The key to improving affordability and market fluidity lies in stronger employment momentum, which is currently lacking. The sustainability of wage gains amid softening labour demand will be crucial in determining the trajectory of the economy.

## Original analysis draft (for reference)

Riverside’s labour market shows signs of strain amid a cooling demand for work. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, up 0.2 percentage points year-on-year, reflecting a modest deterioration in labour market conditions. While the civilian labour force expanded 1.15% over the past year—indicating ongoing workforce supply growth—employment growth has lagged, with nonfarm payrolls rising just 0.18% YoY. The labour demand composite score of 3.68, in the 18th percentile nationally, underscores weak job creation momentum. Average hourly earnings growth remains robust at 7.55% YoY (88th percentile), suggesting wage pressures persist, likely driven by sectoral or inflationary factors rather than broad-based labour scarcity.

Housing market indicators point to a supply-constrained, slowing market. Residential building permits surged 18.04% YoY (76th percentile), signaling builder response to long-term demand, yet home sales are languishing: homes take a median of 54 days to sell, up 5.88% from a year ago, and the days-on-market composite score ranks in the 32nd percentile. With a cost of living score of 2.82 (44th percentile), affordability remains a moderate constraint, particularly given stagnant employment growth. Office worker presence is notably weak, at a composite score of 1.3 (12th percentile), aligning with broader Southern California’s hybrid work trends and potentially dampening commercial real estate and downtown economic activity.

**Outlook:** Riverside’s economy faces near-term headwinds in job creation and housing turnover, despite strong wage growth and construction activity. Without stronger employment momentum, rising supply may not translate into improved affordability or market fluidity. The trajectory hinges on whether wage gains can be sustained amid softening labour demand.
