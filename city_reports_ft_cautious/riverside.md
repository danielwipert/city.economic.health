# Riverside — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 5.1%, placing it in the 4th percentile, indicating that joblessness remains a concern. The labor force participation rate of 60.37% is relatively low, ranking in the 14th percentile, suggesting potential underutilization of the workforce.

Earnings growth has been notable, with a year-over-year increase of 7.54%, placing the region in the 88th percentile. This rise in earnings may be related to a tightening labor market or other factors. The housing market shows mixed signals, with a year-over-year increase in home prices of 10.03% placing the region in the 56th percentile, indicating moderate appreciation. However, the number of building permits has declined by 8.31% year-over-year, ranking in the 40th percentile, which may suggest a slowdown in new construction activity.

The weighted percentile score of 37.2 and the grade of C+ indicate that the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario area exhibits some areas of strength, particularly in earnings growth, but also faces challenges in employment and labor force participation.

The year-over-year increase in home prices of 10.03% places the region in the 56th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. In contrast, the number of building permits has declined by 8.31% year-over-year, ranking in the 40th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 37.2 and the grade of C+ suggest that while the Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario area exhibits some areas of strength, particularly in earnings growth, it faces challenges in employment and labor force participation.
