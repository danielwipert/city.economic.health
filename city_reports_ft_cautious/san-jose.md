## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is performing moderately well but not exceptionally so. Labor force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, indicating a stable engagement level among the working-age population.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, increasing by 0.59%, which places San Jose in the 16th percentile, suggesting slower wage growth compared to many other regions. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is 7.33%, ranking in the 6th percentile, which may reflect higher living costs in the area. Housing market dynamics show a significant increase in building permits, up 211.79% year-over-year, placing the region in the 98th percentile, indicative of robust construction activity. However, the housing price index has grown by 9.58% year-over-year, in the 62nd percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in home values. The days on market for properties have increased to 40 days, also in the 98th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in the pace of sales.

Overall, the data suggests a regional economy with areas of strength and areas needing attention, with a weighted score of 46 and a grade of B-, described as "Below Average."
