## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which may reflect a relatively tight labor market. Labor force participation is at 64.77%, ranking in the 54th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth has slowed, with a year-over-year decrease of 1.3%, placing the region in the 4th percentile, a significant decline compared to broader trends.

Housing market dynamics show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 9.58% year-over-year, placing the region in the 62nd percentile, suggesting continued demand despite broader economic uncertainties. However, the pace of new construction permits has surged by 90.23% year-over-year, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may indicate efforts to meet housing demand. On the other hand, the days-on-market metric has risen to 43 days, in the 86th percentile, potentially signaling a cooling in the pace of sales.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 41.4 and the grade of B- suggest that while San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara maintains some economic strengths, it also faces challenges that warrant attention.
