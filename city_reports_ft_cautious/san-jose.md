# San Jose — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara's economic landscape presents a mixed picture, according to recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile nationally, indicating a labor market that is performing moderately well. The labor force participation rate of 64.34% is at the median, suggesting stable workforce engagement. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, with a year-over-year increase of 0.59%, ranking in the lower 16th percentile.

Housing and construction indicators show varying trends. Building permits have increased by 157.99% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 98th percentile, suggesting robust construction activity. However, the housing price index has grown by 9.98% year-over-year, ranking in the 54th percentile. Sales have slowed, with a 2.86% decrease in properties sold per square foot, placing this indicator in the 14th percentile. The average days on market for homes has remained at 40 days, also in the 98th percentile.

These data points suggest a regional economy with areas of strength and caution, reflecting both opportunities and challenges in the labor and housing markets.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is performing moderately well but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 64.34% is at the median, indicating stable engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, with a year-over-year increase of 0.59%, ranking in the lower 16th percentile, which may reflect subdued wage pressures or broader economic conditions affecting income levels.

Housing and construction indicators show more dynamic trends. Building permits have increased by 157.99% year-over-year, a significant rise that places this metric in the 98th percentile, suggesting robust activity in the construction sector. However, the housing price index has grown by 9.98% year-over-year, which is above average but not at the extreme end of the spectrum, ranking in the 54th percentile. This contrasts with a decline in the pace of sales, as indicated by a 2.86% decrease in the number of properties sold per square foot, placing this indicator in the 14th percentile. Additionally, the average days on market for homes has remained relatively high at 40 days, also in the 98th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in the rate of transactions despite rising prices.

Overall, these data points suggest a regional economy with areas of strength and caution, reflecting both opportunities and challenges in the labor and housing markets.
