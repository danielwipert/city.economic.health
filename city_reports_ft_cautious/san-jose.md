# San Jose — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, ranking in the 34th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is performing moderately well. Labour force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, as indicated by its 50th percentile ranking. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, increasing by 0.59%, placing the region in the lower 16th percentile.

Housing market indicators show a more varied picture. The housing price index has risen by 9.98% year-over-year, placing the region in the 54th percentile. The number of building permits has surged by 157.99% year-over-year, a significant increase that ranks in the 98th percentile. In contrast, the pace of sales appears to have slowed, with days on market increasing to 23 days, also in the 98th percentile.

The data suggests a regional economy with diverse trends across different sectors, with notable trends in construction and housing prices, but more moderate performance in employment and earnings.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national averages. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is performing moderately well but not exceptionally so. Labor force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, as indicated by its 50th percentile ranking. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, increasing by 0.59%, which places the region in the lower 16th percentile, suggesting slower wage growth compared to many other areas.

Housing market indicators present a more dynamic scenario. The housing price index has risen by 9.98% year-over-year, placing the region in the 54th percentile, indicating a steady appreciation in home values. However, the number of building permits has surged by 157.99% year-over-year, a significant increase that ranks in the 98th percentile, possibly reflecting robust construction activity or a response to high demand. In contrast, the pace of sales appears to have slowed, with days on market increasing to 23 days, also in the 98th percentile, which may indicate a cooling in buyer interest or an imbalance between supply and demand.

Overall, the data suggests a regional economy with diverse trends across different sectors, with notable strengths in construction and housing prices, but more moderate performance in employment and earnings.
