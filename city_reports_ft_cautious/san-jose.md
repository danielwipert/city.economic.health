# San Jose — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. Labour force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, suggesting a stable labour market engagement. Earnings growth over the year is 0.59%, ranking in the 16th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

Housing and construction metrics show varying trends. Building permits have increased by 157.99% year-over-year, placing this indicator at the 98th percentile, indicating strong construction activity. The housing price index has grown by 9.98% year-over-year, ranking in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate home value appreciation. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 2.36% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could indicate tightening inventory. The average days on market for properties is 23 days, placing it at the 98th percentile, suggesting a brisk pace of sales.

The data suggests a complex economic landscape in San Jose, with strong construction activity and rapid property sales, but with more moderate performance in employment and earnings growth.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness but not exceptionally so. Labor force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, suggesting a stable but not particularly robust labor market engagement. Earnings growth over the year is modest at 0.59%, ranking in the 16th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

Housing and construction metrics show more dynamic trends. Building permits have increased by 157.99% year-over-year, a significant rise that places this indicator at the 98th percentile, indicating strong activity in the construction sector. However, the housing price index has grown by 9.98% year-over-year, also in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in home values. In contrast, the supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 2.36% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could indicate tightening inventory. The average days on market for properties is 23 days, placing it at the 98th percentile, suggesting a brisk pace of sales.

Overall, the data suggests a complex economic landscape in San Jose, with strong construction activity and rapid property sales, but with more moderate performance in employment and earnings growth.
