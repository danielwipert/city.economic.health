## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, placing it slightly above the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, with a percentile score of 38. Labor force participation is at 64.44%, which is also near the middle of the distribution, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 0.59%, ranking in the lower 16th percentile, indicating slower wage growth relative to peers. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is 7.38%, which is relatively high, placing it in the 4th percentile. This may reflect broader economic pressures affecting the region.

Housing market indicators show a more dynamic trend. Building permits have increased by 211.79% year-over-year, a significant rise that places this metric in the 98th percentile, suggesting robust construction activity. The housing price index has grown by 9.58% over the same period, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which indicates a steady appreciation in property values. However, the pace of sales appears to be moderating, with days on market increasing to 55 days, in the 78th percentile, and the number of properties sold falling by 2.21%, in the 24th percentile. These figures suggest a cooling in the rate of transactions despite rising prices.

Overall, the data suggests a region experiencing moderate economic conditions with notable activity in the housing sector.
