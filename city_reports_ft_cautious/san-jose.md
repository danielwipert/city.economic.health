# San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara

**Grade: B (Average) | 51.4th percentile | April 2026**

---

San Jose earns a top-tier grade due to its rapid job creation, with nonfarm employment growth at +1.69% year-over-year. The city's deep professional talent pool, ranked in the 88th percentile for office and professional worker share, supports this momentum. However, wage growth remains stagnant at +0.65% year-over-year, a bottom-tier performance.

The city's cost of living is a significant constraint, with a composite score of 5.76, making it one of the most expensive metros. The housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 9.1% year-over-year to 24 days. This slowdown is a healthy normalization, given the strong employment growth and a 195.09% year-over-year increase in building permits.

San Jose offers businesses a unique combination of a deep professional talent pool and rapid job creation, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses. The high cost of living constrains talent acquisition and real consumer spending power, making it essential for businesses to carefully consider compensation packages and relocation support.
