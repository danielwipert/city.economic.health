# San Jose — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, indicating relatively stable job market conditions. Labor force participation is at 64.34%, closely aligned with the national median, suggesting a balanced workforce engagement. Earnings growth over the past year has been 0.59%, ranking in the 16th percentile, indicating a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

Housing market activity is notable, with building permits up 211.79% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 98th percentile. This increase in permits suggests robust construction activity, which may be driven by demand for new housing units. The housing price index has risen by 9.58% year-over-year, in the 62nd percentile, indicating a steady appreciation in property values. The supply of existing homes for sale has decreased by 2.86% year-over-year, which may reflect tight inventory conditions. The average days on market for properties is 40 days, in the 98th percentile, suggesting a brisk pace of sales.

The data suggests a region with strong housing market activity and relatively stable employment, though earnings growth remains subdued.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators performing well above average while others lag behind. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, suggesting that job market conditions are relatively stable but not exceptionally strong. Labor force participation is at 64.34%, aligning closely with the national median, which may indicate a balanced engagement of the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 0.59%, ranking in the 16th percentile, indicating a slower pace of income expansion compared to many other regions.

Housing market dynamics show significant activity, with building permits up 211.79% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 98th percentile. This substantial increase in permits suggests robust construction activity, potentially driven by demand for new housing units. The housing price index has risen by 9.58% year-over-year, also in the 62nd percentile, indicating a steady appreciation in property values. However, the supply of existing homes for sale has decreased by 2.86% year-over-year, which may reflect tight inventory conditions. The average days on market for properties is 40 days, in the 98th percentile, suggesting a brisk pace of sales.

Overall, the data indicates a region with strong housing market activity and stable employment, though earnings growth remains subdued.
