## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 26th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but not exceptionally so. Labor force participation is at 64.66%, ranking in the 52nd percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Weekly hours worked average 34.3 hours, which is in the 60th percentile, suggesting a stable but not particularly robust workweek duration.

Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 1.3%, placing the region in the 4th percentile, which may reflect challenges in wage growth or employment composition shifts. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is 7.48%, in the 2nd percentile, indicating higher living costs relative to income growth. Housing prices have increased by 9.58% year-over-year, placing the region in the 62nd percentile, while the number of building permits has surged by 90.23%, in the 96th percentile, suggesting strong residential construction activity. However, the pace of sales for existing homes has slowed, with days on market increasing to 43 days, in the 86th percentile, which may indicate a cooling in the housing market.

Overall, the data suggests a complex economic environment in San Jose, with some indicators pointing to strength and others to potential vulnerabilities.
