## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of San Jose-Sunnyvale-Santa Clara, as of the latest available data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 26th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively stable but not particularly robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 64.66% is in the 52nd percentile, indicating moderate engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.21%, ranking in the 8th percentile, which may reflect subdued wage pressures or broader economic conditions affecting income levels.

Housing market indicators show significant activity, with a 90.23% increase in building permits year-over-year, placing this metric in the 96th percentile. This surge in permits could indicate strong demand for new housing or commercial development. The home price index has risen by 9.58% year-over-year, in the 62nd percentile, suggesting continued appreciation in property values. However, the pace of sales appears to be slowing, as evidenced by a 3.06% decline in the number of properties sold year-over-year, which is in the 12th percentile. Additionally, the average days on market have increased to 43 days, in the 86th percentile, which may reflect a cooling in buyer interest or a shift in market dynamics.

Overall, the data suggests that while San Jose's economy exhibits areas of strength, particularly in housing development, other indicators point to more moderate performance in employment and earnings growth.
