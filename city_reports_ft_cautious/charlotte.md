# Charlotte — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, which is at the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, suggesting a stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.43% is above average, indicating a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth over the past year has been negative at -1.52%, which may reflect broader economic pressures or specific local conditions affecting income levels.

Housing and construction metrics also show varied trends. The cost of living index is 1.25, significantly higher than the national average, placing Charlotte in the 86th percentile for this measure. This suggests that living costs in Charlotte are relatively high compared to other regions. Housing prices have increased by 0.64% year-over-year, a modest gain that places the region in the 24th percentile. Building permits have declined by 26.68% year-over-year, a notable decrease that could indicate a slowdown in residential construction activity. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 69 days, which may suggest a cooling in the housing market.

The data indicates a regional economy with stable employment and labor force participation, but facing challenges in terms of earnings growth and housing market dynamics. The weighted score of 42 and a grade of B- suggest that Charlotte's economic performance is below average relative to its peers.
