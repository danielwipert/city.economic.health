# Charlotte — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally. The labour force participation rate of 65.43% is relatively high, ranking in the 66th percentile. However, earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 1.52%.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices is modest, increasing by 0.64%. The number of building permits has declined by 26.68% year-over-year, indicating a slowdown in residential construction activity. The days-on-market metric for homes is 69 days, which is relatively high. The cost of living index is 1.25 times the national average, placing it in the 86th percentile.

The data points suggest that Charlotte's economic performance is below average compared to other metropolitan areas, with an overall weighted score of 42 and a grade of B-.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators suggesting stability while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which indicates a moderate level of labor market health. The labor force participation rate of 65.43% is relatively high, ranking in the 66th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labor market. However, earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 1.52%, which may reflect challenges in wage growth or employment composition shifts.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices is modest, increasing by 0.64%, while the number of building permits has declined sharply by 26.68% year-over-year, indicating a slowdown in residential construction activity. The days-on-market metric for homes stands at 69 days, which is relatively high and may suggest a cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the cost of living index is 1.25 times the national average, placing it in the 86th percentile, which could impact the region's attractiveness for both residents and businesses. The overall weighted score of 42 and a grade of B- suggest that Charlotte's economic performance is below average compared to other metropolitan areas.

These data points highlight a range of economic conditions in Charlotte, with some strengths and several areas of potential concern.
