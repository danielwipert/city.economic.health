# Charlotte — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 65.43% ranks higher, at the 66th percentile, suggesting a relatively active workforce.

However, earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 1.52%, which is in the 2nd percentile, indicating a challenge in wage growth. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 5.19%, placing it in the 40th percentile, which is moderate. Owner-occupied housing costs have increased by 4.14% year-over-year, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a relatively high burden for homeowners.

The housing market shows signs of cooling, with a 0.64% increase in home prices year-over-year, placing it in the 24th percentile, and a 26.68% decline in building permits, which is in the 6th percentile. The average days on market for homes has risen to 78 days, in the 18th percentile, suggesting a less dynamic housing market.

Overall, the data suggests a metropolitan area with a stable but not robust labour market, challenges in wage growth, and a cooling housing sector. The weighted percentile score of 37 and a grade of C+ indicate that Charlotte's economic performance is below average relative to other metropolitan areas.
