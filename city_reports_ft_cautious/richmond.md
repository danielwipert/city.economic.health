## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 88th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate is at 64.58%, aligning with the national average, as reflected by its 48th percentile ranking. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, with a 7.81% increase, placing Richmond in the 90th percentile, suggesting strong wage gains. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living, has risen by 4.51%, which is in line with broader economic trends, and the overall wage rate has increased by 3.08%, placing it in the 78th percentile, indicating that wages are keeping pace with inflation.

Housing market indicators present a more complex picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has risen by 19.13%, placing Richmond in the 88th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. However, the number of building permits has declined by 38.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction. The days on market for homes has remained steady at 49 days, in the 62nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market where properties are neither selling exceptionally quickly nor languishing. The price-to-square-foot ratio has increased by 1.35%, placing it in the 78th percentile, indicating that home prices are rising, albeit modestly, relative to size. Weekly hours worked have remained stable at 34.1 hours, in the 48th percentile, suggesting consistent employment levels but not significant changes in work hours.

Overall, Richmond's economic performance appears to be above average, with strong labor market indicators and wage growth, but with some signs of moderation in the housing sector.
