# Richmond — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Richmond's economic data shows a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate is 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, indicating relatively low joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate is 64.24%, ranking in the 48th percentile, suggesting a somewhat average level of engagement in the labour market. Earnings growth year-over-year is 5.12%, scoring in the 74th percentile, which may reflect a healthy increase in wages.

Housing market indicators show both strong and softening trends. The home price index has risen by 19.85% year-over-year, placing Richmond in the 88th percentile, suggesting robust appreciation in property values. However, building permits have declined by 20.27% year-over-year, ranking in the 18th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes is 48 days, in the 68th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

Richmond's economic profile appears to be above average, with strengths in employment and housing prices, but with some cautionary signs in labour force participation and new construction.
