# Richmond — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Richmond's economic data reveals a mixed but generally positive picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate is at 64.24%, ranking in the 48th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is reported at 5.12%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which reflects a steady increase in income levels.

Housing market indicators also show strength. The year-over-year change in housing prices is 19.13%, ranking in the 88th percentile, suggesting a significant appreciation in property values. Building permits have increased by 16.01% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 76th percentile, which indicates ongoing construction activity. The days on market for properties is 59 days, in the 74th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the real estate market.

Richmond's economic performance appears to be above average, with particular strengths in employment, earnings growth, and the housing market.
