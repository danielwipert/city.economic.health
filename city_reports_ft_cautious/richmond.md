## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.37% is slightly below average, ranking in the 62nd percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 6.34%, ranking in the 78th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening job market or rising wages.

Housing market indicators show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 19.13% year-over-year, placing it in the 88th percentile, suggesting a strong appreciation in home values. However, building permits have declined sharply by 38.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes remain at 45 days, in the 64th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Richmond's economic profile appears to be characterized by solid employment and wage growth, alongside a robust but potentially moderating housing market.
