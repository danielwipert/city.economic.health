# Richmond — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Richmond metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, indicating relatively low joblessness compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 64.24% is more moderate, ranking in the 48th percentile, suggesting a typical level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.12% is notable, placing Richmond in the 74th percentile, while consumer price inflation at 4.87% is in line with broader trends, ranking in the 54th percentile.

Housing market dynamics show a significant increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 19.85%, placing Richmond in the 88th percentile. However, the number of building permits has decreased by 10.9% year-over-year, ranking in the 34th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 48 days, a moderate figure that places the region in the 68th percentile, suggesting a balanced market.

The weighted percentile score of 63.1% and a grade of B+ suggest Richmond's economic performance is above average relative to other metropolitan areas. These data points provide a snapshot of Richmond's current economic conditions without drawing conclusions about underlying causes or future trends.
