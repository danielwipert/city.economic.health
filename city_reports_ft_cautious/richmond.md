# Richmond — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Richmond metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing positive trends. The unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, indicating relatively low joblessness. The labour force participation rate is 64.24%, ranking in the 48th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is 5.12%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which may reflect a steady increase in income levels.

Housing market indicators show a significant year-over-year increase in home prices of 19.85%, placing this metric in the 88th percentile. However, building permits have declined by 10.9% year-over-year, ranking in the 34th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes is 59 days, in the 74th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market. Inflation measures, such as the cost of living index, are at 4.86%, in the 58th percentile, and wage growth relative to inflation is 3.02%, in the 68th percentile, suggesting that wages are keeping pace with living costs.

Richmond's economic profile appears to be above average, with some strengths in employment, earnings growth and the housing market, although there are also some signs of moderation in construction activity.
