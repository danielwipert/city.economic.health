## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.37% is slightly below average, ranking in the 62nd percentile. Earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 6.34%, placing Richmond in the 78th percentile, indicating a strong increase in wages. However, the cost of living has also risen, with an annual increase of 4.87%, ranking in the 52nd percentile, which may reflect broader inflationary pressures.

Housing market dynamics show a significant divergence. The housing price index has increased by 19.13% year-over-year, placing Richmond in the 88th percentile, suggesting a strong appreciation in home values. Conversely, building permits have declined sharply by 38.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes remain moderate at 45 days, in the 64th percentile, while the percentage of homes selling above list price has increased by 0.75%, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicating continued buyer interest despite higher prices.

Overall, Richmond's economic profile suggests a balanced but not exceptional performance, with strengths in employment and wage growth offset by challenges in the housing market and cost of living.
