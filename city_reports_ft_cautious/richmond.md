## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.37% is slightly below average, ranking in the 62nd percentile. Earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 6.34%, placing Richmond in the 78th percentile, indicating a strong increase in wages. However, the cost of living index at 4.87 is moderate, ranking in the 52nd percentile, suggesting that wage gains may be partially offset by higher living costs.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 19.64% year-over-year, a significant rise that places Richmond in the 88th percentile. This contrasts with a decline in building permits, which have fallen by 38.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile. This disparity may reflect a tightening supply of new housing units despite rising demand. The days on market metric stands at 45 days, which is average, ranking in the 64th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Richmond's economic profile suggests a region experiencing moderate growth with some areas of strength and others of concern.
