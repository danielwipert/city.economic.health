## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 68th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 64.18% is at the 40th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 9.3%, ranking in the 88th percentile, which may reflect a strong demand for labor or rising productivity.

Housing market indicators also show strength. The housing price index has increased by 19.13% year-over-year, placing it in the 88th percentile, suggesting a significant appreciation in home values. However, the days on market for homes is 59 days, in the 64th percentile, which might suggest a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a pronounced advantage.

Overall, Richmond's economic profile appears to be above average, with particular strengths in earnings growth and housing prices.
