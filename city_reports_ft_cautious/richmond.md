The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 88th percentile, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate is at 64.58%, ranking in the 48th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is reported at 7.81%, a figure that places Richmond in the 90th percentile, suggesting strong wage increases. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.51%, which is in the 66th percentile, indicating moderate price pressures.

Housing market indicators show a more nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 19.13% year-over-year, placing Richmond in the 88th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. However, building permits have declined by 38.63% year-over-year, a significant drop that ranks in the 4th percentile, potentially signaling a slowdown in new construction. The days on market for properties is 49, in the 62nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a clear advantage. Rent growth is modest at 1.35% year-over-year, placing it in the 78th percentile, which may indicate stable rental conditions.

Overall, Richmond's economic profile suggests a resilient labor market with strong wage growth, but the housing sector shows signs of both strength and potential challenges.
