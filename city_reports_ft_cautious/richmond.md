## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Richmond indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 68th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. Labor force participation is at 64.18%, ranking in the 40th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is robust at 5.12%, placing it in the 74th percentile, while the cost of living increase is more modest at 4.99%, in the 48th percentile, potentially suggesting some wage growth outpacing inflation.

Housing market indicators show significant activity. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 19.13%, placing it in the 88th percentile, which is a notably high level of appreciation. Building permits have increased by 16.01% year-over-year, ranking in the 76th percentile, indicating a strong construction sector. However, the days on market for properties is 59 days, in the 64th percentile, which may reflect a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The year-over-year change in property sales frequency is 1.35%, in the 80th percentile, suggesting a steady pace of transactions.

Overall, Richmond's economic indicators suggest a resilient and growing local economy, with particular strengths in employment and housing markets.
