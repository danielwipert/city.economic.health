# Boston-Cambridge-Newton

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 46.8th percentile | April 2026**

---

Boston's economy is marked by a mix of strengths and weaknesses, earning a below-average overall grade of 46.8th percentile. The city's labor force participation rate of 68.47% stands out as a top-tier advantage, indicating a deep and engaged labor pool. However, this is offset by a cost of living composite score of 5.25, making it one of the most expensive metros in the country.

The city's housing market is complex, with days on market increasing by 20% year-over-year to an average of 30 days, suggesting a slowing market. Below-average building permit growth of -14.97% year-over-year indicates a potential tightening of housing supply. Sluggish wage growth of +1.42% year-over-year and a high cost of living raise concerns about the sustainability of demand in the housing market.

Boston offers businesses a highly engaged and skilled labor force, but at a significant cost. The primary risk for businesses considering location in Boston is the high cost of living, which may erode real consumer spending power and talent acquisition capabilities.
