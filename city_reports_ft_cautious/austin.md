## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 86th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 74.47% further supports this, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 5.67% is more moderate, sitting in the 60th percentile, which may reflect a broader trend in wage growth that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak.

In the housing sector, the data reveals a decline in several key metrics. Building permits have decreased by 19.54% year-over-year, and the housing price index has fallen by 10.3%, both of which are at the lower end of the national spectrum, with percentiles of 18 and 0, respectively. This suggests a cooling in the construction and real estate markets. Additionally, the days on market for properties have increased to 88 days, also at the 0th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in property sales activity. Despite these declines, the average weekly hours worked remain steady at 35.5 hours, aligning with the 86th percentile, suggesting stability in employment duration.

Overall, the data paints a picture of an economy with strong labor market fundamentals but facing challenges in the housing sector.
