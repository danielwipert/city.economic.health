# Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos

**Grade: A- (Good) | 62.3th percentile | April 2026**

---

Austin's economy earns an A- grade with a 62.3rd percentile ranking out of 50 US metros, driven by its exceptionally tight labor market, with an unemployment rate of 3.20%, and a deep labor pool, as evidenced by a labor force participation rate of 73.99%. This highly competitive hiring environment gives workers significant leverage. Austin also boasts one of the most affordable cost of living composites, with a score of 1.44, suggesting lower real-wage requirements and higher consumer purchasing power.

However, the city's housing market is experiencing a sharp decline, with building permits down 29.44% year-over-year, and homes taking 20.4% more days to sell than last year, with an average of 53 days on market. This slowdown, combined with weak employment growth, suggests demand erosion rather than a healthy normalization of the market.

Austin offers businesses a unique blend of a tight labor market, deep labor pool, and affordable cost of living, making it an attractive location for talent attraction and retention. However, the primary risk lies in the city's housing supply constraints and potential demand erosion, which may ultimately impact workforce availability and affordability, making it crucial for businesses to carefully consider these factors in their location decision.
