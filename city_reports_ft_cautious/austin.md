# Austin — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing the region in the 84th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 73.99% is high, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. Earnings growth over the year has been moderate, at 3.18%, aligning with the national median as reflected by its 50th percentile score.

In the housing sector, the data shows a downturn. Housing prices have decreased by 5.6% year-over-year, and the number of single-family home permits issued has fallen by 22.9% compared to the previous year, both of which are below the national average, as indicated by their respective percentile scores of 0 and 16. The days-on-market metric for homes has increased to 76 days, which may indicate a cooling in the housing market. The weekly hours worked remain above the national average, at 34.9 hours, scoring in the 79.6th percentile.

The weighted percentile and score of 51.8 and 52, respectively, place Austin in the 'Average' category, suggesting a balanced but not exceptional economic performance.

Additionally, the days-on-market metric for homes has increased to 76 days, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market. Despite these challenges, the weekly hours worked remain above the national average, at 34.9 hours, scoring in the 79.6th percentile.

Overall, the weighted percentile and score of 51.8 and 52, respectively, place Austin in the 'Average' category, suggesting a balanced but not exceptional economic performance.
