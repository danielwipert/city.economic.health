# Austin — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area's economic data shows a mixed picture across various indicators. The labour force participation rate of 73.99% is high, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting a strong engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. Earnings growth year-over-year is 3.18%, aligning with the median performance as reflected by its 50th percentile score.

In the housing sector, data reveals a decline in both home prices and permits. The days-on-market metric stands at 76 days, which is relatively high and suggests a slower pace of sales in the real estate market. The overall weighted percentile score is 51.9, and the grade of "B" indicates average economic performance, with strengths in employment and labour force participation offset by weaknesses in the housing sector.

The data suggests a balanced economic environment in Austin, with no clear indication of exceptional performance.
