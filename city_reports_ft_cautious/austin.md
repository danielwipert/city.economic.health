## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 64th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate is notably high at 73.89%, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Weekly hours worked average 35.2 hours, a figure that ranks in the 84th percentile, further suggesting strong labor market conditions.

In terms of earnings and cost of living, the year-over-year growth in earnings is 2.84%, placing it in the 42nd percentile, while the cost of living index is 4.97, in the 52nd percentile, indicating moderate growth in both areas. However, the housing market shows signs of cooling, with a significant decline in building permits (-37.08% year-over-year) and a decrease in home prices (-10.3% year-over-year), both of which rank in the lower percentiles (6th and 0th, respectively). Days on market for homes have increased to 81 days, up from previous levels, which may reflect a shift towards a more balanced market.

Overall, the data suggests a labor market that remains strong but a housing sector that is experiencing a downturn. The weighted score of 46 and a grade of B- indicate that Austin's economic performance is below average relative to other metropolitan areas.
