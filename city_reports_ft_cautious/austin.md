## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators suggesting strength while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 64th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 73.89% is notably high, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 2.84%, placing the region in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a stable but not particularly dynamic wage environment.

In the housing sector, the data reveals a significant decline in both home prices and permits for new construction. The home price index has fallen by 10.3% year-over-year, placing Austin in the lowest percentile, indicating a substantial correction in the housing market. Similarly, building permits have decreased by 37.08% year-over-year, also ranking in the bottom percentiles, which may reflect a slowdown in residential construction activity. The average days on market for homes has increased to 81 days, up from previous levels, which could suggest a softening demand in the real estate market.

Overall, the data suggests that while Austin's labor market remains strong, the housing sector is experiencing a notable downturn.
