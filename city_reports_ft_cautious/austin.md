# Austin — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate is high at 73.99%, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may reflect a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. Earnings growth year-over-year is moderate at 3.18%, aligning with the national median as indicated by its 50th percentile score.

In contrast, housing and construction metrics show a different trend. Building permits have declined by 22.9% year-over-year, placing the region in the 16th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in new residential construction. Home price appreciation has reversed, with a year-over-year decrease of 5.6%, and the price-to-rent ratio has also declined by 6.22%. These figures, both in the lowest percentiles, indicate a cooling in the housing market. The average days on market for properties have increased to 76 days, up from previous levels, suggesting a less dynamic real estate environment.

Overall, the data suggests that while the labour market in Austin remains strong, the housing sector is experiencing a downturn. The weighted percentile score of 51.8 and the grade of B indicate an average economic performance relative to other metropolitan areas.
