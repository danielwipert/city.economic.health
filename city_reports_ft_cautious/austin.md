## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it in the 70th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.46% ranks even higher at the 96th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 5.02%, situating the region in the 62nd percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.64% is slightly above the national average, placing it in the 68th percentile.

On the housing front, the data reveals significant headwinds. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined sharply by 37.08%, ranking in the 6th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in construction activity. Similarly, the home price index has decreased by 10.3% year-over-year, placing the region at the bottom of the percentile rankings. The supply of homes for sale has also contracted, with a 4.23% decrease in the number of properties for sale, ranking in the 2nd percentile. Additionally, the average days on market have increased to 80 days, suggesting a less dynamic real estate market.

Overall, these figures indicate a complex economic landscape in Austin, with strong labor market indicators contrasting with weakening housing metrics.
