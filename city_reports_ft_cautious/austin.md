# Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos

**Grade: A- (Good) | 61.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Austin's economy earns an A- grade with a 61.9th percentile ranking out of 50 US metros, driven by its exceptionally tight labor market, with an unemployment rate of 3.20%. This tight labor market gives workers considerable leverage, but is mitigated by a deep labor pool, as evidenced by a labor force participation rate of 73.99%. Additionally, Austin's cost of living composite score of 1.44 makes it among the most affordable metros, offering a substantial advantage in terms of lower real-wage requirements and higher consumer purchasing power.

However, the data also reveals notable tensions. The sharp decline in building permits, down 29.44% year-over-year, signals a contraction in housing supply, which could lead to future affordability stress and workforce attraction difficulties. The days on market have increased by 20.4% year-over-year, with homes now taking an average of 53 days to sell, indicating a slowing market.

Austin's unique combination of a tight labor market, deep labor pool, and affordable cost of living makes it an attractive location for talent attraction and retention. However, the primary risk lies in the potential demand erosion signaled by the decline in building permits and slowing housing market, which could impact the city's long-term economic momentum and affordability.
