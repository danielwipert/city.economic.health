# Austin — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.99% is high, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is 3.18%, which is average, placing it in the 50th percentile. The cost of living has increased by 4.68% year-over-year, a figure that is slightly above the median, indicating moderate inflationary pressures.

Housing indicators, however, show a different trend. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 19.54%, and the housing price index has decreased by 10.3% year-over-year, both of which are at the lower end of the spectrum, ranking in the 18th and 0th percentiles, respectively. The year-over-year change in the price per square foot has fallen by 5.54%, and the average days on market for properties is 91 days, both of which are at the lower end of the percentile rankings. This suggests a slowdown in the housing market.

The data indicates a labor market that remains strong, while the housing sector shows signs of moderation.

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile, which suggests a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.99% is notably high, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 3.18%, which is average, placing it in the 50th percentile. The cost of living has increased by 4.68% year-over-year, a figure that is slightly above the median, suggesting moderate inflationary pressures.

Housing indicators, however, show a different trend. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 19.54%, and the housing price index has decreased by 10.3% year-over-year, both of which are at the lower end of the spectrum, ranking in the 18th and 0th percentiles, respectively. This decline in housing activity and prices may reflect a cooling in the real estate market. Additionally, the year-over-year change in the price per square foot has fallen by 5.54%, and the average days on market for properties is 91 days, both of which are at the lower end of the percentile rankings, indicating a slowdown in the housing market.

Overall, the data suggests a labor market that remains strong, but with signs of moderation in the housing sector.
