## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators performing well above national averages while others lag behind. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile nationally, suggesting a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.99% is even more impressive, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may reflect a strong attachment of the working-age population to the labor market. Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, a figure that is also above the median, indicating stable employment conditions.

On the earnings front, year-over-year growth stands at 3.18%, aligning closely with the national median, as indicated by its 50th percentile score. However, the housing market appears to be under pressure, with a significant year-over-year decline in home prices of 10.3% and a 19.54% decrease in building permits. These figures, both in the lowest percentiles, suggest a cooling in the real estate sector. Additionally, the days on market for properties have increased to 91 days, further indicating a slowdown in housing activity.

Overall, the data suggests that while Austin's labor market remains strong, the housing sector faces challenges. The city's economic performance is average when considering all indicators, with a weighted percentile of 50.7 and a grade of B.
