# Austin — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively robust labour market. The labour force participation rate of 73.99% ranks in the 96th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population. Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, which is above the national median.

However, other indicators point to areas of concern. Year-over-year earnings growth is 3.18%, in line with the national median. Consumer price inflation and owner's equivalent rent have risen by 4.68% and 2.44% respectively. The housing market appears to be cooling, with a decline in building permits (-22.9%) and home prices (-5.6%) year-over-year. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 5.54%, and the average days on market have increased to 91 days.

The data suggests that while Austin's labour market remains strong, there are signs of weakening in the housing sector.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 84th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.99% is even more impressive, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement among the working-age population. Weekly hours worked average 34.9 hours, which is above the national median, further supporting the notion of a strong labor market.

However, other indicators point to areas of concern. Year-over-year earnings growth is moderate at 3.18%, aligning with the national median, while both consumer price inflation and owner's equivalent rent have risen, albeit modestly, by 4.68% and 2.44% respectively. The housing market appears to be cooling, with a significant decline in building permits (-22.9%) and home prices (-5.6%) year-over-year. Similarly, the supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 5.54%, and the average days on market have increased to 91 days, suggesting a slowdown in real estate transactions.

Overall, the data indicates that while Austin's labor market remains strong, there are signs of weakening in the housing sector.
