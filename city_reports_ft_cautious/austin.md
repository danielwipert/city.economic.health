## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it in the 70th percentile among comparable regions, suggesting a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.46% ranks even higher at the 96th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.84%, ranking in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in wage increases.

Housing and construction metrics show significant declines. Building permits have decreased by 37.08% year-over-year, placing the region in the 6th percentile, a stark contrast to the labor market indicators. Similarly, the housing price index has fallen by 10.3% year-over-year, ranking at the bottom percentile, and the price-to-rent ratio has dropped by 4.52%, also in the lower percentiles. These figures suggest a cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the average days on market for properties has increased to 81 days, further indicating a less dynamic real estate sector.

Overall, the data suggests that while Austin's labor market remains strong, the housing sector faces notable challenges. The weighted percentile score of 46.5 and the grade of B- reflect a below-average performance across the board.
