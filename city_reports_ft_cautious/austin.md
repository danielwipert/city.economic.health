# Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos

**Grade: A (Very Good) | 65.2th percentile | June 2026**

---

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A, ranking in the 65.2th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven significantly by its top-tier wage growth and cost of living metrics. Specifically, the city's wage growth of +6.75% year-over-year and its cost of living ratio of $240/sqft to $37.61/hr, which has decreased by 8.1% year-over-year, stand out as defining characteristics. These metrics suggest a city with a strong economy and attractive affordability.

**Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is +0.63% year-over-year, combined with a -0.498% deviation in weekly hours from its own trend, indicating a near median labor demand composite score of 4.68. This combination signals a moderate expansion in job numbers but with hours worked slightly below the city's own trend, suggesting some level of genuine demand but not at an accelerated pace. The labor market is thus characterized by steady, albeit not spectacular, growth.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos stands at 3.40%, placing it in the 66th percentile, which indicates a tighter labor market. This tightness implies that businesses may face challenges in hiring due to the lower availability of workers, potentially leading to upward pressure on wages. For a business trying to hire in this market, being competitive with salary offerings will be crucial.

**Wage Growth**
With a year-over-year wage growth rate of +6.75%, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos experiences fast wage growth, ranking it in the 86th percentile. This rapid wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, which can be a challenge for businesses but is beneficial for workers' purchasing power. Employers will need to factor in these increasing labor costs when considering operations in this metro area.

**Cost of Living**
The cost of living in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos, with a PSF of $240/sqft versus $37.61/hr earnings, resulting in a ratio of 6.38, and an 8.1% year-over-year decrease in PSF, places it in the top tier for affordability, ranking in the 94th percentile. This high affordability ranking means the city has a significant talent attraction advantage without needing wage premiums, making it an attractive location for businesses looking to relocate or expand.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has grown by +0.54% year-over-year, ranking in the 78th percentile. This positive growth rate indicates that the workforce supply is expanding, which is a favorable condition for businesses looking to hire, as it suggests a growing pool of potential employees.

**Building Permits**
The year-over-year change in residential building permits is -22.85%, placing it in the bottom tier. This significant decline suggests that the housing supply is tightening, which could lead to future affordability issues and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. This trend may signal potential risks for businesses reliant on a readily available and affordable housing stock for their employees.

**Days on Market**
Homes in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos currently sit on the market for a median of 56 days, with a year-over-year increase of 21.7%. This increase in days on market, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicates a slowing market, which can be beneficial for workers relocating to the area as it becomes less competitive. However, in the context of a strong job market, this slowing could also signal a healthy normalization rather than demand erosion.

**Office Economy**
With an office and professional worker share composite score of 4.28, ranking in the 92nd percentile, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos boasts a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. This makes the city an ideal location for businesses in these sectors but less suited for those in industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

In conclusion, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, high affordability, and a deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for certain industries. However, the significant decline in building permits and its implications for future housing affordability and workforce accommodation pose a considerable risk that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating this metro area for business relocation or expansion.
