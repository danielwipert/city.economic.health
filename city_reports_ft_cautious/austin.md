# Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos

**Grade: A (Very Good) | 64.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos earns an overall grade of A, driven by its strong economic character. The city's low unemployment rate of 3.20% and solid wage growth of 5.84% year-over-year indicate a healthy labor market, conducive to consumer spending and business growth. Its cost of living composite score of 1.04 makes it one of the most affordable metros, offering a strong cost advantage for businesses and residents.

The city's office and professional worker share is exceptionally high, ranking in the 96th percentile. However, the sharp decline in building permits of -29.44% year-over-year raises concerns about future affordability and workforce attraction. The days on market have increased by 20.4% year-over-year, with homes now taking an average of 53 days to sell, indicating a slowing market.

Austin offers businesses a unique combination of a skilled workforce, affordable cost of living, and solid wage growth, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses. The primary risk lies in the city's constrained housing supply, which will lead to future affordability stress and workforce attraction difficulties, potentially undermining its economic advantages.
