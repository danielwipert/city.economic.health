## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 86th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 74.47% is notably high, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is moderate at 3.18%, aligning with the national average as reflected by its 50th percentile score.

In the housing sector, the data reveals some challenges. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 10.3%, placing it at the bottom of the distribution (0th percentile). Similarly, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has decreased by 19.54%, also in the lower percentiles (18th percentile), which may reflect a slowdown in the housing market. The days on market for properties have increased to 88 days, another indicator of a less dynamic real estate environment. These trends contrast with the positive labor market indicators, suggesting divergent dynamics between different sectors of the local economy.

Overall, the data indicates that while Austin's labor market remains strong, the housing sector faces significant headwinds.
