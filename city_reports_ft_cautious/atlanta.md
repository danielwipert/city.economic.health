## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metropolitan area ranks eighth in the dataset, with an overall grade of B+, indicating above-average performance across a range of economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 64.57% is more moderate, ranking in the 56th percentile, indicating that while employment is strong, there may be some segments of the population not actively participating in the workforce.

Earnings growth over the past year has been significant, with a year-over-year increase of 8.27%, placing Atlanta in the 94th percentile. This strong earnings growth is complemented by a cost of living increase of 4.43%, which, while lower than earnings growth, still places the metro area in the 76th percentile. Housing market indicators show a more mixed picture. The housing price index has increased by only 0.16% year-over-year, placing it in the 20th percentile, which may reflect a stabilization or cooling in the housing market. Meanwhile, the number of building permits has declined by 17.49% year-over-year, ranking in the 28th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in construction activity.

Overall, the data indicates a resilient labor market with strong earnings growth, but also points to potential moderating trends in the housing sector.
