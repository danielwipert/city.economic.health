# Atlanta — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metropolitan area ranks eighth among major US metros, with an overall grade of B+, indicating above-average performance across key economic indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, placing it in the 80th percentile, suggesting a relatively robust labour market. The labour force participation rate of 64.57% ranks in the 56th percentile, indicating that while employment is strong, there may be some underutilization of the workforce.

Year-over-year earnings growth of 8.27% is high, placing the metro in the 94th percentile, which may reflect a strong demand for labour or rising productivity. In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing prices is a modest 0.16%, placing it in the 20th percentile, suggesting a stable market. The number of building permits has declined by 17.49% year-over-year, ranking in the 28th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity.

The median days on market for homes is 71 days, in the 42nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market. Weekly hours worked average 34.6 hours, in the 72nd percentile, indicating a standard workweek that aligns with national norms. Overall, the data suggests a mixed economic environment in Atlanta, with strengths in employment and earnings growth, but more moderate performance in labour force participation and housing dynamics.
