## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it in the 58th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a labor market that remains relatively tight but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 64.36% is slightly below the median, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, increasing by 8.27%, which ranks in the 94th percentile, a clear highlight in the data set.

Housing and construction metrics offer a more nuanced view. Building permits have decreased by 6.61% year-over-year, placing this indicator in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new residential construction. The housing price index has remained stable, with no change over the past year, while the supply of homes for sale has slightly decreased by 0.68%, both indicators sitting around the median. The average days on market for properties is 70 days, which is relatively high and suggests a less dynamic housing market compared to other regions.

Overall, Atlanta's economic performance appears to be average, with strengths in earnings growth and employment, but with signs of moderation in the housing sector.
