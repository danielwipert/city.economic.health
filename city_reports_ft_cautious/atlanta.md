# Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell

**Grade: A- (Good) | 59.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Atlanta earns an overall grade of A- and ranks in the 59.5th percentile among 50 US metros, with its low unemployment rate of 3.30% and top-tier wage growth of 6.47% driving a healthy labor market. The labor force is growing slowly, with a year-over-year growth rate of only 0.51%, which constrains hiring capacity. This slow growth, combined with a tight unemployment rate, indicates a shrinking workforce rather than just low layoffs.

The city's housing supply is a concern, with building permits declining sharply by 30.90% year-over-year, indicating a contracting housing supply. This decline, combined with a moderate days-on-market increase of 8.5% year-over-year, indicates a potential demand erosion scenario. Atlanta has a cost of living composite score of 1.53, ranking in the 76th percentile, and an above-average professional workforce, ranking in the 68th percentile.

Atlanta offers businesses a talented and affordable workforce, with a low unemployment rate and high wage growth. However, the city's constrained housing supply and potential demand erosion pose a primary risk, impacting workforce attraction and affordability in the long run. The city's slow labor force growth and tight unemployment rate also pose challenges for businesses looking to hire and expand.
