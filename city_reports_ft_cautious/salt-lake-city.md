## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.38% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 3.68%, situating the city in the 36th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.17% is slightly above the national average, reflecting a modest rise in living expenses.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 6.26% places Salt Lake City in the 50th percentile, suggesting stable but not overly speculative real estate market conditions. The number of building permits issued has increased by 9.65% year-over-year, indicating ongoing construction activity and potential growth in the housing stock. However, the decrease in single-family home sales of -1.65% may reflect a cooling in demand or a shift in buyer preferences. The average days on market for properties is 72 days, which is relatively high and could indicate a more balanced market between buyers and sellers.

Overall, the data suggests that Salt Lake City's economy is performing above average with strong labor market participation and moderate growth in earnings and housing prices.
