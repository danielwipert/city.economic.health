## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The city's unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.38% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 3.15%, situated in the 48th percentile, reflecting moderate wage increases that align with broader national trends.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 6.26% places Salt Lake City in the 50th percentile, suggesting stable but not overly speculative growth in property values. The number of building permits issued has risen by 9.65% year-over-year, in the 62nd percentile, which may reflect ongoing demand for new housing and commercial developments. However, the supply of existing homes for sale has decreased by 1.65% year-over-year, in the 36th percentile, potentially indicating a tightening market. The average days on market for properties is 72 days, in the 20th percentile, which could suggest a competitive buyer's market where properties are taking longer to sell compared to other regions.

Overall, Salt Lake City's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market participation and housing demand, though wage growth and housing supply dynamics suggest areas for continued monitoring.
