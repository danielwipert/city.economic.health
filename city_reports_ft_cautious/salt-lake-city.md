## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City-Murray's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 76th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 72.77% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 3.15%, situated in the 48th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.2% is in the 36th percentile, potentially squeezing household budgets.

In the housing sector, the annual home price index growth of 9.89% is in the 52nd percentile, suggesting steady appreciation in property values. However, the decline in single-family home sales of -1.89% over the year, in the 32nd percentile, may reflect cooling demand or supply constraints. The number of building permits issued has increased by 41.99% year-over-year, a significant rise that ranks in the 86th percentile, pointing to ongoing construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 49 days, in the 64th percentile, indicating a balanced market with neither an excess of inventory nor a severe shortage.

Overall, Salt Lake City-Murray's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market participation and housing construction, though challenges in earnings growth and cost of living remain evident.
