## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 70th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 72.66% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Weekly hours worked average 35.7 hours, also in the 94th percentile, which may reflect a well-utilized workforce.

In terms of housing, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 6.26% is moderate, placing Salt Lake City in the 50th percentile, while the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 63 days, in the 26th percentile, suggesting a potential slowdown in the pace of sales. Construction activity, as measured by building permits, has seen a significant year-over-year increase of 36.31%, ranking in the 86th percentile, which indicates ongoing growth in the construction sector. However, the year-over-year change in earnings is modest at 1.0%, in the 20th percentile, and the cost of living index of 5.51 is in the 26th percentile, suggesting that wage growth may not be keeping pace with living costs.

Overall, Salt Lake City's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market participation and construction activity, but with more moderate indicators in earnings growth and housing market dynamics.
