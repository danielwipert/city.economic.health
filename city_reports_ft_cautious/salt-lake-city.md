## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City-Murray's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 70th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 72.66% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 3.68%, situating the region in the 36th percentile, which reflects moderate wage increases. The cost of living index is 5.16, and the overall well-being rank is 4.28, both in the lower mid-range percentiles (34th and 96th, respectively), suggesting that while living costs are moderate, the quality of life remains high.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in building permits is 36.31%, placing the city in the 86th percentile, which indicates a significant level of construction activity. The home price index has risen by 6.26% year-over-year, aligning with the 50th percentile, suggesting stable but not overly speculative growth in property values. However, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales is down by 1.27%, in the 38th percentile, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market. The average days on market for homes is 63 days, in the 26th percentile, indicating a relatively slower pace of sales compared to other markets.

Overall, Salt Lake City-Murray exhibits a balanced economic profile with strengths in labor market engagement and quality of life, alongside moderate wage growth and stable housing dynamics.
