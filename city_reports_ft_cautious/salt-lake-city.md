Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The labor force participation rate is notably high at 72.66%, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Housing market indicators show both strength and caution. The housing price index has risen by 6.26% year-over-year, placing the city in the 50th percentile, reflecting moderate appreciation in home values. On the other hand, the days on market metric stands at 63 days, in the 26th percentile, suggesting that properties are taking longer to sell compared to many other markets. The overall weighted score of 62, corresponding to a grade of B+, indicates that Salt Lake City's economy is performing above average relative to its peers.

These data points suggest a balanced but dynamic economic environment in Salt Lake City.
