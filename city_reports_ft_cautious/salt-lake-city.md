## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 74th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 72.53% ranks even higher at the 94th percentile, indicating a high level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 3.22%, situated in the 40th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.31% is in the 30th percentile, potentially squeezing household budgets.

Housing market dynamics show both strength and moderation. The year-over-year increase in housing prices of 6.26% is at the median, reflecting stable appreciation. In contrast, the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has risen sharply by 36.31%, placing it in the 86th percentile, which may indicate a growing demand for new housing. The average days on market for homes is 59 days, in the 30th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Salt Lake City's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market participation and housing construction, though wage growth and cost of living pressures suggest ongoing challenges for residents.
