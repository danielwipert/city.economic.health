# Salt Lake City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Salt Lake City's economic indicators show a mixed picture, with some positive trends. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 76th percentile among metropolitan areas. This suggests a relatively robust labour market. The labour force participation rate of 72.77% is high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Year-over-year earnings growth is 3.15%, situated in the 48th percentile, reflecting moderate wage increases. The cost of living index is 5.2, placing it in the 36th percentile, which may indicate that living costs are relatively moderate compared to other cities.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices is 9.89%, ranking in the 52nd percentile. This suggests a steady appreciation in property values. However, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales is down by 1.89%, in the 32nd percentile, which could indicate a cooling in the housing market. Building permits have increased by 14.73% year-over-year, placing the city in the 64th percentile, suggesting continued investment in residential construction. The average days on market for homes is 49 days, also in the 64th percentile, indicating a balanced market.

Overall, Salt Lake City's economic performance appears to be above average, with some strengths in labour market engagement and housing construction, and some moderation in housing sales activity.
