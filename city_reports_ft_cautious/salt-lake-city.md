## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City's economic indicators present a mixed but largely positive picture. The city boasts a relatively low unemployment rate of 3.8%, placing it in the 74th percentile nationally, which suggests a robust labor market. Labor force participation stands at 72.53%, a high figure that ranks in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Weekly hours worked average 35.7 hours, also in the 94th percentile, further supporting the notion of a well-utilized workforce. However, year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 1.0%, ranking in the 20th percentile, which may reflect a more stable but less dynamic wage environment.

In the housing sector, Salt Lake City's home price index has increased by 6.26% year-over-year, a moderate rise that places the city in the 50th percentile. This growth is accompanied by a 36.31% increase in building permits issued year-over-year, a significant rise that ranks in the 86th percentile, suggesting continued investment in residential construction. Despite this, the number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 63 days, in the 26th percentile, which could indicate a slight cooling in the pace of sales. The cost of living index stands at 5.51, in the 26th percentile, and the owner-occupied housing rate is 4.22, in the 94th percentile, both of which suggest affordability and a strong preference for homeownership.

Overall, Salt Lake City's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market engagement and housing construction, though wage growth remains a point of consideration.
