## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Salt Lake City-Murray's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 73.38% is notably high, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 3.68%, situated in the 36th percentile, reflecting moderate wage increases. The cost of living index at 5.17, in the 34th percentile, and the overall well-being rank at 4.28, in the 96th percentile, suggest that while the cost of living is moderate, the quality of life remains high.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in housing permits is 34.71%, placing it in the 84th percentile, which may reflect ongoing construction activity and demand for new housing. The home price index has grown by 6.26% year-over-year, in the 50th percentile, indicating stable but not overly speculative price movements. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 1.65% year-over-year, in the 36th percentile, suggesting a slight tightening in the housing market. The average days on market for properties is 72 days, in the 20th percentile, which could indicate a competitive market where properties are selling relatively quickly.

Overall, Salt Lake City-Murray's economic performance appears to be above average, with strengths in labor market engagement and quality of life, balanced by moderate earnings growth and stable housing dynamics.
