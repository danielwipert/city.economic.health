## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 90th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is in the 2nd percentile, indicating a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 1.36%, ranking in the 10th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, Cleveland's home price index has increased by 4.0% year-over-year, placing it in the 38th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in property values. The number of building permits issued has risen by 14.96% year-over-year, a figure in the 78th percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. The days on market metric stands at 55 days, in the 48th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Cleveland's economic performance appears to be average, with some indicators pointing to strengths in employment and housing construction, while others highlight challenges in labor force participation and wage growth.
