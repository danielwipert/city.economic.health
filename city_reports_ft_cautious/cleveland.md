# Cleveland — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The city's unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 76th percentile among its peers. This suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% ranks in the 2nd percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings have declined by 1.79%, placing Cleveland at the bottom of the percentile rankings in this category.

In the housing market, Cleveland has seen a 4.05% increase in home prices over the past year, placing it in the 36th percentile. Building permits have risen by 14.96% year-over-year, a positive sign that ranks the city in the 72nd percentile. The pace of sales, as measured by days on market, stands at 55 days, which is in the middle of the distribution.

The data points suggest a complex economic landscape in Cleveland, with some indicators showing resilience and others pointing to areas requiring attention. The overall economic grade for Cleveland is a B-, with a weighted percentile of 49.6, indicating performance that is below average relative to other metropolitan areas.
