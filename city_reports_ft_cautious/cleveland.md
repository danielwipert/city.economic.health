## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 80th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is in the 2nd percentile, indicating a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Earnings have declined year-over-year by 1.24%, a figure that ranks in the 6th percentile, suggesting a weakening in wage growth. Weekly hours worked average 34.5 hours, placing Cleveland in the 66th percentile, which may reflect stable employment conditions despite the earnings decline.

In the housing sector, Cleveland shows signs of moderate growth and activity. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 4.1%, placing it in the 40th percentile, while the number of building permits has risen by 14.96% year-over-year, ranking in the 78th percentile. This suggests a modest expansion in the construction sector. The pace of sales, as indicated by the 55 days on market, is in the 54th percentile, which may reflect a balanced market with neither excessive supply nor demand pressures. The rental market appears to be experiencing stronger growth, with a 12.86% year-over-year increase in rents, placing Cleveland in the 98th percentile, indicating a tight rental market.

Overall, Cleveland's economic performance is characterized by average conditions across most indicators, with some notable strengths in the housing and rental markets.
