Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 90th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is in the 2nd percentile, indicating a lower engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. The days-on-market metric of 55.0 days is in the 48th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. Additionally, the year-over-year increase in permits for single-family homes of 12.86% is in the 98th percentile, highlighting a strong interest in residential development.

Overall, Cleveland's economic performance appears to be average, with some indicators pointing to positive trends in employment and housing, while others suggest ongoing challenges in labor force participation and wage growth.
