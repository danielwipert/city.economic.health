# Cleveland

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 48.8th percentile | June 2026**

---

Cleveland has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 48.8th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its stagnant wage growth, with a year-over-year change of -0.55%, and its strong labor force growth, with a 1.80% year-over-year increase. These two metrics signal a mixed economic environment, with a growing workforce but limited upward pressure on wages.

**Labor Demand**
Cleveland's employment growth rate is 0.57% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -0.363% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a moderate labor demand expansion, with some genuine demand but also a slight contraction in hours worked. The labor demand composite score of 4.73 ranks near the median at the 52nd percentile.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Cleveland is 3.40%, ranking near the median at the 57th percentile. This indicates a relatively balanced labor market, neither too tight nor too slack. For a business trying to hire in Cleveland, this means that while it may not be extremely difficult to find workers, there is still some competition for talent.

**Wage Growth**
Cleveland's year-over-year wage growth rate is -0.55%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 2nd percentile. This stagnant wage growth signals limited upward pressure on labor costs for employers, but also weak bargaining power for workers. As a result, worker purchasing power is not increasing significantly.

**Cost of Living**
Cleveland has a cost of living score with a PSF of $143/sqft and an earnings ratio of $33.75/hr, resulting in a ratio of 4.24, which ranks near the median at the 50th percentile. This means that the city is neither extremely affordable nor expensive relative to peers. The 13.5% year-over-year increase in PSF, however, may put upward pressure on housing costs. For talent attraction, this neutral affordability score means that businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to compensate for the cost of living.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Cleveland is growing at a rate of 1.80% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 94th percentile. This expanding labor force supply signals a positive environment for hiring, with a growing pool of potential workers. Businesses can expect to find a increasing number of candidates for open positions.

**Building Permits**
The number of building permits in Cleveland is decreasing by 7.43% year-over-year, ranking below average at the 38th percentile. This decline in permits signals a tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future. This could pose a risk to workforce accommodation and attraction.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market in Cleveland is 55 days, with a year-over-year increase of 7.8%. This ranks near the median at the 54th percentile, indicating a relatively balanced housing market. For a worker relocating to Cleveland, this means that the housing market is neither extremely competitive nor extremely accessible.

**Office Economy**
Cleveland's office economy has a professional and office worker share of 3.28, ranking above average at the 74th percentile. This indicates a deep talent pool suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. However, the city may be less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

Cleveland offers businesses a growing labor force and a relatively balanced labor market, but also poses risks due to stagnant wage growth and a tightening housing supply. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker to factor in is the potential for decreased affordability and increased competition for housing, which could impact workforce accommodation and attraction.
