# Cleveland — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 76th percentile among metropolitan areas, indicating a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is low, ranking in the 2nd percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is not actively engaged in the labor market. Earnings have declined year-over-year by 1.79%, which may reflect broader economic challenges or sector-specific issues within the region.

In the housing market, Cleveland has seen a 3.37% increase in home prices over the past year, placing it in the 36th percentile, suggesting moderate growth. The number of building permits has risen by 14.96% year-over-year, a positive development for construction activity. The median days on market for homes is 55 days, which is relatively high but within a range that suggests a balanced market. Single-family home sales have increased by 12.86% year-over-year, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating strong demand in this segment of the housing market.

Overall, Cleveland's economic performance appears to be average, with some indicators pointing to stability and others highlighting areas that may require attention.
