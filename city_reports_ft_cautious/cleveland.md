# Cleveland

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 48.2th percentile | May 2026**

---

Cleveland earns an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 48.2th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its near-median labor demand and above-average unemployment rate, with the latter standing at 3.40%. Specifically, the labor demand composite score of 4.01 and the unemployment rate of 3.40% suggest a mixed economic environment.

**Labor Demand**
Cleveland's employment growth rate is +0.57% year-over-year, while weekly hours are -1.088% below trend. This combination signals a lack of genuine demand expansion, as hours are not running above trend despite job growth. The labor demand composite score of 4.01, near the median, further supports this assessment.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Cleveland is 3.40%, ranking it 73rd percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This tightness implies that businesses may face challenges in hiring, with potential upward pressure on wages. However, the low unemployment rate also suggests a skilled and employed workforce.

**Wage Growth**
Wage growth in Cleveland is -0.25% year-over-year, ranking it in the bottom tier at the 5th percentile. This stagnant wage growth signals flat labor costs for employers but weak bargaining power for workers. The slow wage growth may also impact worker purchasing power.

**Cost of Living**
Cleveland's cost of living is near median, with a percentile rank of 50th, and a PSF to earnings ratio of $143/sqft to $33.94/hr, resulting in a ratio of 4.21. The city's affordability is not particularly advantageous, as the PSF is rising 13.5% year-over-year. This may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract talent.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Cleveland is growing at a rate of +1.80% year-over-year, ranking it in the top tier at the 84th percentile. This expanding labor force supply is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire, as it indicates a growing pool of potential employees.

**Building Permits**
Building permits in Cleveland are decreasing by -18.19% year-over-year, ranking it below average at the 29th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability issues and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market in Cleveland is 55 days, with a year-over-year increase of +7.8%. This rising days on market indicates a slower, more buyer-friendly market. For workers relocating to the city, this may mean a more accessible and less competitive housing market.

**Office Economy**
Cleveland's professional and office worker share is above average, ranking it 72nd percentile. This deep talent pool makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but less ideal for industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

In conclusion, Cleveland offers businesses a mixed economic environment with a tight labor market and stagnant wage growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for future affordability issues and housing supply constraints, driven by the decline in building permits.
