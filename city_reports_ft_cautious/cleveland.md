Cleveland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.4%, placing it in the 88th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. However, the labor force participation rate of 58.01% is in the 2nd percentile, indicating a lower share of the working-age population engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is negative at -1.84%, ranking at the bottom of the scale, which may reflect challenges in wage growth or employment composition.

On the housing front, Cleveland's housing market shows some positive signs. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 4.1%, placing it in the 40th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. The year-over-year growth in permits for new housing units is 14.96%, in the 70th percentile, indicating a healthy level of new construction activity. The days on market for homes is 55 days, in the 78th percentile, which suggests a balanced market where properties are neither selling extremely quickly nor languishing unsold. Weekly hours worked are at 35.2 hours, in the 80th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of full-time employment.

Overall, Cleveland's economic performance appears to be average, with strengths in employment and housing market indicators offset by challenges in labor force participation and earnings growth.
