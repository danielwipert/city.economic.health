# Cleveland

**Grade: B (Average) | 52.0th percentile | May 2026**

---

Cleveland earns an overall grade of B, ranking 52.0th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its labor force growth and office economy. The city's labor force is expanding at a rate of 1.80% year-over-year, and its professional worker share is above average, ranking in the 74th percentile. These metrics suggest a city with a growing workforce and a deepening talent pool in professional sectors.

**Labor Demand**
Cleveland's employment growth rate is 0.57% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -1.207% from the city's own 12-month baseline, resulting in a labor demand composite score of 3.89, near the median. This combination signals a moderate expansion of genuine demand, as hours are not significantly above trend during job growth. The labor market is neither strongly expanding nor contracting.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Cleveland is 3.40%, ranking in the 75th percentile, indicating a tight market with relatively low unemployment. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests upward pressure on wages.

**Wage Growth**
Wage growth in Cleveland is -0.47% year-over-year, ranking in the bottom tier, 2nd percentile. This stagnant wage growth environment means that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly, but worker purchasing power is also not increasing. This may be a concern for businesses relying on local consumer demand.

**Cost of Living**
Cleveland's cost of living is near the median, with a percentile rank of 50th, and a price-to-salary ratio of $143/sqft to $33.79/hr, or 4.23. The city's affordability is average, neither particularly attractive nor deterrent, as the PSF is rising 13.5% year-over-year relative to wages. This means that businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to attract talent, but the city is not a standout in terms of affordability either.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Cleveland is growing at a rate of 1.80% year-over-year, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This positive growth rate suggests that businesses will have a growing pool of potential workers to draw from, making it easier to hire and staff operations.

**Building Permits**
Residential building permits in Cleveland are increasing by 11.14% year-over-year, signaling an expansion of future housing supply. This rise in permits suggests that affordability may improve, and the city may become more attractive to workers, as the supply of housing is increasing to meet demand.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market for homes in Cleveland is 55 days, with a year-over-year increase of 7.8%. This indicates a relatively balanced market, neither extremely competitive nor accessible, for workers relocating to the city. The rising days on market may signal a healthy normalization of the housing market.

**Office Economy**
Cleveland's professional worker share is above average, ranking in the 74th percentile, indicating a deep talent pool in office sectors. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in tech, finance, consulting, or headquarters operations, but less ideal for industries with primarily industrial or logistics roles.

In conclusion, Cleveland offers businesses a growing labor force, an above-average professional worker share, and an expanding housing supply, making it an attractive location for companies in office sectors. However, the city's stagnant wage growth and average affordability may pose challenges for businesses relying on local consumer demand or seeking to attract talent without offering significant wage premiums.
