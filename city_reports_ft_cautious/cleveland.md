# Cleveland

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 49.7th percentile | May 2026**

---

Cleveland earns an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it at the 49.7th percentile among 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its near-median labor demand composite score of 4.01 and its bottom-tier wage growth of -0.25% year-over-year. These metrics suggest a mixed economic environment, with labor demand neither strongly expanding nor contracting, but with notable downward pressure on wages.

**Labor Demand**
Cleveland's employment growth rate is +0.57% year-over-year, while weekly hours are -1.088% below the city's own 12-month trend. This combination signals a lack of genuine demand expansion, as hours are not running above trend despite modest job growth. The labor market is not experiencing a significant contraction, but rather a period of stabilization.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Cleveland is 3.40%, ranking it at the 78th percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This suggests that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, leading to potential wage pressure. However, the low unemployment rate also indicates a skilled and employed workforce.

**Wage Growth**
Wage growth in Cleveland is -0.25% year-over-year, placing it in the bottom tier of US metros. This stagnant wage growth environment means that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly, but it also implies weak bargaining power for workers and potentially limited purchasing power. This could impact the overall attractiveness of the city for businesses relying on consumer spending.

**Cost of Living**
Cleveland's cost of living, with a PSF of $143/sqft and average hourly earnings of $33.94, results in a ratio of 4.21, ranking it near the median at the 50th percentile. This indicates that the city is neither particularly affordable nor expensive relative to its peers. The 13.5% year-over-year increase in PSF suggests that affordability may be decreasing over time. This neutral cost of living environment means that businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to attract talent, but it also does not provide a strong competitive advantage.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Cleveland is growing at a rate of +1.80% year-over-year, placing it in the top tier at the 86th percentile. This expanding labor force supply is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire, as it indicates a growing pool of potential workers. This growth can help support hiring capacity and reduce the risk of labor shortages.

**Building Permits**
The number of residential building permits in Cleveland has decreased by -18.19% year-over-year, ranking it below average at the 26th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that housing supply is tightening, which could lead to future affordability challenges and make it more difficult for workers to find accommodation. This is a concern for businesses relying on a mobile workforce.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market for homes in Cleveland is 55 days, with a year-over-year increase of +7.8%. This indicates a slowing market, which can make it easier for relocating workers to find housing. However, the relatively moderate days on market suggests that the housing market is not overly competitive, providing a balanced environment for both buyers and sellers.

**Office Economy**
Cleveland's professional and office worker share is above average, ranking it at the 70th percentile. This deep talent pool makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. However, it may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented economies.

In conclusion, Cleveland offers businesses a mixed economic environment with a tight labor market and stagnant wage growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for future affordability challenges due to tightening housing supply, which could impact workforce attraction and accommodation. Despite this, the city's growing labor force and deep professional talent pool provide a solid foundation for businesses looking to establish or expand operations.
