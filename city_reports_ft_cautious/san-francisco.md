## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing resilience while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 64.93% is near the national median, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.29% is slightly above average, reflecting modest income gains for workers.

Housing and construction metrics, however, paint a more complex picture. Building permits have declined by 39.96% year-over-year, a significant drop that may reflect reduced investment in new housing projects. The housing price index has decreased by 0.83% over the same period, suggesting a cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the year-over-year change in the price of single-family homes has fallen by 4.62%, further indicating a slowdown in real estate activity. The average days on market for properties has increased to 50 days, up from previous levels, which may suggest a softer demand for housing.

Overall, the data indicates a region with a stable but not robust labor market, alongside a housing sector that appears to be experiencing a downturn. The weighted percentile score of 27.5 and the grade of "C" reflect these mixed conditions.
