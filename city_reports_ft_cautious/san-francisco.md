# San Francisco — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 4.1%, placing the region in the 32nd percentile nationally. This suggests a labor market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak compared to other regions. Labor force participation is at 64.45%, which is slightly above the median when considering national percentiles, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is 0.49%, ranking in the 14th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in wage increases relative to other areas.

Housing market indicators show a nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 0.06%, placing the region in the 18th percentile. This suggests a slight cooling in the housing market. The year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has surged by 142.07%, a significant increase that ranks in the 94th percentile, indicating robust activity in new housing development. However, the year-over-year change in office space fundamentals has decreased by 3.63%, ranking in the 10th percentile, which may reflect ongoing challenges in the commercial real estate sector. The average days on market for homes is 30 days, a figure that places the region in the 96th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests a metropolitan area with a stable but not particularly dynamic labor market, a housing market experiencing both price declines and strong new construction, and a commercial real estate sector facing challenges. The overall weighted score of 39 and a grade of C+ indicate that San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is performing below average relative to other metropolitan areas.
