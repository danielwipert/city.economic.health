## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of the San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metropolitan area, as of the latest data, presents a mixed picture with several indicators suggesting both strengths and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 20th percentile nationally, which may reflect a relatively stable but not robust labor market. Labor force participation is at 64.89%, ranking in the 56th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.29%, situated in the 62nd percentile, suggesting modest income growth that keeps pace with, but does not significantly outstrip, inflation.

Housing and construction metrics show more pronounced challenges. Building permits have declined by 39.96% year-over-year, placing the region in the 2nd percentile, which could indicate a significant slowdown in new housing development. The housing price index has decreased by 0.83% over the same period, ranking in the 10th percentile, while the price-to-square-foot ratio has fallen by 4.62%, in the lowest percentile. These trends suggest a cooling in the housing market. Additionally, properties are taking an average of 50 days to sell, which is in the 60th percentile, possibly reflecting a more balanced market between buyers and sellers.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 26.6 and the grade of "C" assigned to the region highlight a set of economic conditions that are below average compared to other metropolitan areas.
