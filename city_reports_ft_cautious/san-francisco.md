# San Francisco — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.1%, placing the region in the 32nd percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labor market. Labour force participation is at 64.45%, ranking in the 52nd percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is 0.49%, a low figure that places the region in the 14th percentile, indicating limited wage expansion.

The housing market shows a year-over-year increase in the housing price index of 0.47%, placing the region in the 26th percentile. This may reflect a cautious recovery in home values. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 10.83%, ranking in the 66th percentile, indicating a more robust construction sector. The year-over-year change in the price of square footage for existing homes has decreased by 4.68%, placing the region in the 4th percentile, which could signal a softening in the resale market. The average days on market for homes is 55 days, a high figure in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a slower pace of sales.

The data suggests a region with a stable but not particularly dynamic economy, with varying trends across different sectors.
