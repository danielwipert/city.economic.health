# San Francisco — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength and others pointing to challenges. The unemployment rate of 4.1% places it in the 32nd percentile among comparable regions, indicating a labour market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak. The labour force participation rate of 64.45% is in the 52nd percentile, suggesting moderate engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 0.49% ranks in the 14th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in income gains compared to other areas.

Housing and construction metrics show contrasting trends. Building permits have increased by 142.07% year-over-year, placing this indicator in the 94th percentile, suggesting robust activity in the construction sector. However, the housing price index has declined by 0.06% over the same period, ranking in the 18th percentile, which could indicate cooling demand or supply adjustments in the housing market. Office space vacancy rates have declined by 3.63% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 10th percentile, potentially reflecting changes in commercial real estate utilization. The median days on market for homes is 30 days, a figure in the 96th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the residential property market.

These data points suggest that the metropolitan area is experiencing varied economic conditions across different sectors, with notable shifts in construction and real estate markets.

Office space vacancy rates have also seen a decline of 3.63% year-over-year, placing this metric in the 10th percentile, potentially reflecting changes in commercial real estate utilization. The median days on market for homes is 30 days, a figure in the 96th percentile, which suggests a relatively quick turnover in the residential property market.
