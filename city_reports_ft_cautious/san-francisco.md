# San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont

**Grade: C- (Critical) | 27.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

San Francisco's economy earns a C- grade, marked by a declining labor market and a highly competitive housing market, with nonfarm employment growth declining by 0.52% year-over-year and wage growth stagnating at 0.49% year-over-year. The city's cost of living composite score of 5.79 indicates it is among the most expensive metros, constraining talent acquisition and real consumer spending power.

The housing market is extremely competitive, with homes selling in just 29 days on average, and the market speeding up by 6.5% year-over-year. This fast-paced market poses a relocation challenge for new hires. However, building permits are increasing by 86.01% year-over-year, potentially signaling a expansion of the housing supply and alleviating some affordability pressures.

San Francisco offers a highly competitive and expensive market, with a declining labor market and a fast-paced housing market. The primary risk for businesses is the potential difficulty in attracting and retaining talent due to the high cost of living and competitive housing market, which could outweigh the benefits of locating in this city.
