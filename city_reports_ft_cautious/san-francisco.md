# San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont

**Grade: B (Average) | 50.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

San Francisco earns an overall grade of B, with its strong wage growth of 5.54% year-over-year being the most important takeaway. The city's above-average nonfarm employment growth and solid wage growth indicate a healthy expansion, with a strong demand for labor. San Francisco's cost of living composite score is 4.53, making it one of the most expensive metros, which constrains talent acquisition and real consumer spending power.

The housing market is extremely competitive, with homes selling in just 29 days on average, a pace that is speeding up by 6.5% year-over-year. This fast-moving market, combined with the high cost of living, poses a significant relocation challenge for new hires. The city's below-average office and professional worker share raises concerns about the availability of specialized or senior talent.

San Francisco offers businesses a strong and growing economy with a highly paid workforce, but the primary risk is the high cost of living and extremely competitive housing market, which can make it difficult to attract and retain talent. Companies must carefully weigh the benefits of San Francisco's economic growth against the challenges of operating in one of the most expensive cities in the country, with a cost of living composite score of 4.53.
