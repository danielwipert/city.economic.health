# Orlando — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area ranks 20th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 36, corresponding to a grade of C+. The unemployment rate is 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 63.98% is in the 44th percentile, suggesting moderate engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 0.37% is in the 12th percentile, indicating minimal wage growth relative to other areas.

Housing indicators show mixed trends. The year-over-year increase in housing prices of 10.12% places Orlando in the 64th percentile, indicating relatively strong appreciation. The year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 9.6%, ranking in the 60th percentile. In contrast, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales is down by 2.44%, in the 20th percentile, suggesting a decline in the pace of sales. The median days on market for homes is 88 days, in the 2nd percentile, indicating a relatively slow market for property sales.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Orlando, with some indicators showing strength and others indicating areas of concern.
