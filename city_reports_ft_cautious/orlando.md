# Orlando — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area ranks 20th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 36.5, placing it in the lower half of the evaluated regions. The unemployment rate is  stands at 4.4%, slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 63.98%, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is 0.37%, suggesting a stable job market.

Housing market indicators show mixed trends. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 10.79%, while the number of building permits has risen by 9.83%, indicating continued investment in residential construction. However, the year-over-year change in the price-to-rent ratio is -2.31%, and properties are taking an average of 82 days to sell, which may reflect a shift in buyer preferences. The cost of living index is 5.6, and the overall wage replacement rate is 3.0, both within moderate ranges.

The data collectively indicate a region with steady but unremarkable economic conditions, with some sectors showing growth and others indicating potential areas of concern.

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area ranks 20th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 36.5, placing it in the lower half of the evaluated regions. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, which is slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 63.98%, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 0.37%, suggesting a stable but not rapidly expanding job market.

Housing market indicators show mixed trends. The year-over-year increase in housing prices is 10.79%, which is relatively robust, while the number of building permits has risen by 9.83%, suggesting continued investment in residential construction. However, the year-over-year change in the price-to-rent ratio is negative at -2.31%, and properties are taking an average of 82 days to sell, which may reflect a cooling in demand or a shift in buyer preferences. The cost of living index is 5.6, and the overall wage replacement rate is 3.0, both of which are within moderate ranges.

These data points collectively suggest a region experiencing steady but unspectacular economic conditions, with some sectors showing growth while others indicate potential areas of concern.
