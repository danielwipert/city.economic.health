The economic data for the Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, placing it near the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, as indicated by its percentile score of 48. Labor force participation is also at 64.44%, aligning closely with the unemployment rate's position. Earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.81%, ranking in the lower third of all metro areas with a percentile score of 36. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is 5.26%, which is slightly below the median, suggesting that wage growth may not be keeping pace with price increases.

The housing market in Orlando exhibits contrasting dynamics. Home prices have increased by 16.16% year-over-year, a significant rise that places the region in the 74th percentile, indicating a robust housing market. However, this growth is accompanied by a decline in building permits, down 3.79% year-over-year, and an increase in days on market to 81 days, both of which suggest a potential slowdown in new construction and a less dynamic sales environment. The overall weighted percentile score of 41.4 and the grade of B- reflect a below-average performance across the board, integrating these various economic indicators.

These data points suggest a complex economic landscape in Orlando, with strengths in the housing market offset by more moderate performance in employment and earnings.
