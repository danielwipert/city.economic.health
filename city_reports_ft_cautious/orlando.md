## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth while others indicate potential areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing the region in the 18th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 64.05% is slightly above the median, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.63% is in line with broader national trends, though it does not stand out significantly.

Housing market dynamics are noteworthy. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 10.12% places Orlando in the 64th percentile, suggesting a robust but not overheated market. However, the decline in single-family housing permits by 1.96% year-over-year may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric of 83 days is notably high, placing the region in the 6th percentile, which could indicate challenges in selling properties or a shift in buyer behavior. Additionally, the year-over-year decrease in permits for multifamily structures by 2.58% further underscores a potential cooling in the housing sector.

Overall, the data suggests that Orlando's economy is experiencing moderate growth with some signs of softening in the housing market.
