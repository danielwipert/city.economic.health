# Orlando — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area ranks 20th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 36.3, corresponding to a grade of C+. The unemployment rate is 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile, indicating a labour market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate is 63.98%, in the 44th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 0.37%, ranking in the 12th percentile. The cost of living index is 5.6, in the 28th percentile, suggesting that living expenses are relatively moderate. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture: the housing price index has increased by 10.79% year-over-year, placing it in the 60th percentile, while the number of single-family home permits issued has risen by 9.83%, in the 66th percentile. However, the days on market metric is 82 days, in the 4th percentile, indicating a relatively slow turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests that the region's economic performance is moderate across various indicators, with some areas showing signs of growth and others lagging behind national trends.
