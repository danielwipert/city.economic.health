## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.1%, which is slightly above the national average, placing it in the 44th percentile among similar regions. Labor force participation is also at 64.5%, aligning with the same percentile rank, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is reported at 4.63%, which is relatively stable and places the region in the 48th percentile, suggesting modest income growth.

Housing market indicators show a more dynamic picture. The year-over-year increase in home prices is 10.12%, placing the region in the 64th percentile, which may reflect continued demand despite other economic pressures. However, building permits have decreased by 3.79% year-over-year, ranking in the 44th percentile, potentially indicating a slowdown in new construction activity. The rental market appears to be experiencing some softening, with a 2.56% decrease in per square foot rental prices, placing the region in the 20th percentile. Additionally, the median days on market for homes has increased to 80 days, significantly higher than many other markets, which could suggest a less competitive selling environment.

Overall, the data indicates that while Orlando's economy shows some areas of strength, particularly in housing prices, there are also signs of moderation in labor market dynamics and construction activity.
