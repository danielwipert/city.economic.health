The economic data for the Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing modest growth while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively stable labor market but not one that is particularly robust compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 63.98% is in the 44th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 0.37% is in the 12th percentile, indicating a slight increase in wages, though this is among the lower ranks nationally.

Housing market indicators show more dynamic trends. The year-over-year change in housing prices is 10.79%, placing Orlando in the 60th percentile, which suggests a continued appreciation in home values. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction is 9.83%, in the 66th percentile, indicating a steady but not explosive pace of new housing development. The days on market metric stands at 88 days, in the 2nd percentile, which may reflect a competitive market where properties are taking longer to sell. The year-over-year change in the price-to-square-foot ratio is -2.44%, in the 20th percentile, suggesting a slight moderation in the cost per square foot of housing.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 36.2 and the grade of C+ assigned to Orlando's economic performance indicate a below-average standing relative to other metropolitan areas, with both strengths and weaknesses evident across various economic indicators.
