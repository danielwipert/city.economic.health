# Memphis

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Memphis earns an overall grade of B, with its stagnant job market, at -0.35% year-over-year nonfarm employment growth, being the most significant concern. The city's labor force growth is 1.36% and wage growth is 6.12%, indicating that the existing workforce is experiencing wage increases. The combination of these factors shows a market where the workforce is growing and earning more, but the job market is not expanding.

The city's cost of living is highly affordable, ranked in the 98th percentile, but its housing market is slow, with days on market increasing by 6.9% year-over-year to an average of 62 days. This slowdown is consistent with the weak employment growth. The city's low concentration of office and professional workers, ranked in the 0th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses.

Memphis offers businesses an affordable cost of living and a growing labor force, with above-average building permit growth of 19.69% year-over-year. However, its stagnant job market and limited professional workforce pose significant risks. The city's softening demand and limited access to skilled workers could hinder long-term growth and success for businesses considering locating in Memphis.
