## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 34th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to peers. The labor force participation rate of 60.17% is relatively low, ranking in the 8th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 6.16%, placing Memphis in the 78th percentile, which may reflect improvements in wage levels or employment composition.

Housing and construction metrics present a more challenging picture. Building permits have declined by 32.45% year-over-year, a significant drop that places Memphis in the 2nd percentile. This decline is mirrored in the housing price index, which has increased by 5.21% year-over-year, a moderate pace that ranks in the 44th percentile. However, the pace of sales of existing homes has slowed, with a year-over-year decrease of 3.53%, placing the city in the 8th percentile. Additionally, the average number of days on the market for homes has risen to 78 days, suggesting a less dynamic housing market.

Overall, the data suggests a labor market with moderate employment and strong earnings growth, but a housing sector facing challenges in both construction and sales activity.
