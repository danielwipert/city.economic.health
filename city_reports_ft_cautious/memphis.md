# Memphis

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.2th percentile | April 2026**

---

Memphis has an overall grade of B, ranking 53.2th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its low labor demand and high cost of living affordability. The city's labor demand composite score of 2.93, combining a -0.35% employment growth rate and +0.882% weekly hours deviation, and its top-tier cost of living ratio of 0.51, ranking 98th percentile, are the two metrics that most define its current economic character. With a wage growth rate of +6.12%, Memphis also offers a relatively strong labor market.

**Labor Demand**
Memphis has a labor demand composite score of 2.93, resulting from a -0.35% employment growth rate and +0.882% weekly hours deviation. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, as the city is experiencing a decline in employment growth despite an increase in weekly hours worked. This suggests a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Memphis is 4.00%, ranking near the median at the 60th percentile. This indicates a relatively balanced labor market, neither too tight nor too slack. For a business trying to hire in Memphis, this means that while it may not be extremely difficult to find talent, there is still some competition for workers, and wages may be subject to moderate pressure.

**Wage Growth**
Memphis has a year-over-year wage growth rate of +6.12%, ranking above average at the 74th percentile. This signals fast wage growth, which implies rising labor costs for employers but also increased worker purchasing power. As a result, businesses in Memphis may face higher labor costs, but workers will have more disposable income to spend in the local economy.

**Cost of Living**
With a cost of living ratio of 0.51, ranking at the 98th percentile, Memphis is one of the most affordable cities relative to its peers. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset high housing costs. As a result, Memphis has a talent attraction advantage, with a lower real-wage floor for employers.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Memphis is growing at a rate of +1.36% year-over-year, ranking above average at the 74th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire in the city. With a growing labor force, Memphis is likely to have a more robust hiring capacity, making it easier for companies to find the talent they need.

**Building Permits**
Memphis has seen a +19.69% year-over-year increase in residential building permits, ranking above average at the 77th percentile. This signals that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodation for the workforce in the future. As a result, businesses in Memphis can expect a more stable and affordable housing market, making it easier to attract and retain talent.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market for homes in Memphis is currently 62 days, with a +6.9% year-over-year increase. This indicates a relatively slow market, with homes taking longer to sell. For a worker relocating to Memphis, this means that the housing market is more accessible, with less competition for homes, making it easier to find and purchase a property.

**Office Economy**
With an office/professional worker share of 0.31, ranking at the 2nd percentile, Memphis has a relatively shallow professional talent pool. This makes the city less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting firms. However, Memphis may be more suitable for businesses with industrial or logistics-dominated economies.

In conclusion, Memphis offers businesses a unique combination of affordability, growing labor force, and expanding housing supply, making it an attractive location for companies looking to establish or expand operations. However, the city's low labor demand and shallow professional talent pool are significant constraints that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating Memphis as a potential business location.
