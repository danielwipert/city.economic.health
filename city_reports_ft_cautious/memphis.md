# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength and others lagging behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among metropolitan areas. This suggests a labor market that is neither exceptionally robust nor deeply troubled. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% is in the 18th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of workforce engagement compared to other regions.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile. This may reflect a strengthening income environment for those employed. Housing market indicators show a moderate increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 5.23%, placing Memphis in the 42nd percentile. This suggests a stable but not overly dynamic housing market.

The number of single-family building permits has increased by 19.69% year-over-year, a figure in the 76th percentile. This could indicate growing demand for new housing. However, the pace of sales for existing homes appears to be slowing, with a decline of 5.54% in the number of properties sold year-over-year, placing this metric in the 2nd percentile. The average days on market for homes has risen to 79 days, in the 8th percentile, suggesting a less competitive market for sellers.

The data suggests a Memphis economy with pockets of strength, including earnings growth and housing construction, but also areas of concern, such as lower labor force participation and a slowdown in home sales.

Additionally, the average days on market for homes has risen to 79 days, in the 8th percentile, suggesting a less competitive market for sellers.

Overall, the data indicates a Memphis economy with pockets of strength, particularly in earnings growth and housing construction, but also areas of concern, such as lower labor force participation and a slowdown in home sales.
