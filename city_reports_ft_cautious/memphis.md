# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas. This suggests a moderate level of labor market activity. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% ranks lower, at the 18th percentile, which may indicate some workforce engagement challenges. Earnings growth year-over-year is 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile, indicating a relatively strong income environment for those employed.

Housing market indicators present a complex picture. The year-over-year increase in building permits is 14.06%, placing it in the 70th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction. However, the housing price index has grown by 5.23% year-over-year, ranking in the 42nd percentile, which is less pronounced compared to other markets. The rental vacancy rate remains low at 0.61%, in the 2nd percentile, potentially indicating tight rental conditions. The days on market for property sales have increased to 79 days, in the 8th percentile, which may suggest a slowdown in the pace of home sales.

The data suggests that Memphis exhibits both areas of strength and challenges. Employment earnings and construction activity appear to be performing relatively well, while labor force engagement and housing market dynamics present some difficulties.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it at the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, suggesting a moderate level of labor market activity. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% ranks lower, at the 18th percentile, which may reflect challenges in engaging the workforce. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile, indicating a relatively strong income environment for those employed.

Housing market indicators show a complex picture. The year-over-year increase in building permits is 14.06%, placing it in the 70th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction. However, the housing price index has grown by only 5.23% year-over-year, ranking in the 42nd percentile, which is less pronounced compared to other markets. The rental vacancy rate remains low at 0.61%, in the 2nd percentile, potentially indicating tight rental conditions. Conversely, the days on market for property sales have increased to 79 days, in the 8th percentile, which may suggest a slowdown in the pace of home sales.

Overall, the data suggests that while Memphis exhibits some areas of strength, particularly in employment earnings and construction activity, it also faces challenges in labor force engagement and housing market dynamics.
