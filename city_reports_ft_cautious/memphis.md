# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic data, as of the latest available figures, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas. This suggests a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak relative to peers. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% ranks in the 18th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year has been 6.16%, a figure that ranks in the 78th percentile, suggesting above-average wage increases. However, the cost of living increase of 4.12% ranks in the 80th percentile, indicating higher living costs compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 5.23% places Memphis in the 42nd percentile, reflecting moderate appreciation. Single-family home sales have declined by 5.54% year-over-year, ranking in the 2nd percentile, indicating a significant slowdown in this segment of the market. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 79 days, a figure in the 8th percentile, which may reflect a less active housing market. Building permits have risen by 14.06% year-over-year, a positive sign for construction activity, ranking in the 70th percentile.

The data suggests a Memphis economy with areas of strength and weakness, with particular challenges in labor market engagement and housing market dynamics.

The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 79 days, a figure in the 8th percentile, which may reflect a less active housing market. Building permits have risen by 14.06% year-over-year, a positive sign for construction activity, ranking in the 70th percentile.

Overall, the data suggests a Memphis economy with areas of strength and weakness, with particular challenges in labor market engagement and housing market dynamics.
