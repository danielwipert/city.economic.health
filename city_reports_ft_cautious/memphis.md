# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic performance is characterised by a mixed set of indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it ranks in the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, suggesting a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak relative to peers. The labour force participation rate of 60.48% is in the 18th percentile, indicating a lower level of engagement in the workforce compared to other regions. Earnings growth year-over-year is 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening income environment for those employed.

Housing market indicators show a moderate increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 5.23%, placing it in the 42nd percentile. This suggests a stable but not overly dynamic housing market. The number of building permits has increased by 14.06% year-over-year, a figure in the 70th percentile, indicating a growing construction sector. However, the number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 81 days, in the 14th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in property sales. The overall weighted percentile score of 40.4 places Memphis in the "Below Average" category, with a grade of B-.

Conversely, the number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 81 days, in the 14th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in property sales. The overall weighted percentile score of 40.4 places Memphis in the "Below Average" category, with a grade of B-.
