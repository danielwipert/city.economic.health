# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas. This suggests a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak relative to peers. The labour force participation rate of 60.48% is relatively low, ranking in the 18th percentile, which may reflect underlying issues in workforce engagement or broader demographic trends.

On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is 6.16%, placing Memphis in the 78th percentile. This indicates a significant improvement in income levels for those employed. In contrast, housing market indicators present a less favourable picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices is 5.21%, a moderate increase that places the city in the 44th percentile. However, the number of building permits has declined sharply by 32.45% year-over-year, ranking in the 2nd percentile. This could signal a slowdown in residential construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 81 days, a relatively high figure in the 14th percentile, suggesting a less dynamic real estate market.

The data also shows a decline in permits for single-family homes, down by 4.35% year-over-year. This further indicates a cooling trend in the housing sector. Overall, the data suggests a Memphis economy with some strengths in employment earnings but facing challenges in labour force participation and housing market dynamics.
