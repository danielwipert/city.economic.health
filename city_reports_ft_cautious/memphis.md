## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 32nd percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness but not among the lowest. The labor force participation rate of 60.45% is in the 12th percentile, suggesting a lower engagement of the working-age population in the labor market compared to other metropolitan areas. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust at 9.43%, ranking in the 94th percentile, which may reflect strong wage pressures or improvements in employment quality.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined sharply by 34.38%, placing it in the 10th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in residential construction activity. In contrast, the home price index has increased by 5.21% year-over-year, in the 42nd percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in property values. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 68 days, in the 14th percentile, indicating a less competitive market for homebuyers. Additionally, the cost of living has increased by 4.12% year-over-year, in the 82nd percentile, which may be putting pressure on household budgets.

Overall, the data suggests that while Memphis is experiencing some positive economic trends, particularly in earnings growth, there are also signs of challenges in labor market participation and housing market dynamics.
