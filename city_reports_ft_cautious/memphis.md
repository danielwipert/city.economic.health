## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 32nd percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which may reflect a relatively stable but not robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 60.45% is in the 12th percentile, indicating a lower level of engagement in the workforce compared to other regions. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.95%, ranking in the 84th percentile, suggesting that those employed are experiencing significant wage increases. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.15%, which is in the 78th percentile, indicating higher living costs relative to income growth.

Housing and construction indicators present a contrasting picture. Building permits have declined by 34.38% year-over-year, placing Memphis in the 10th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in residential construction activity. However, the housing price index has increased by 5.21% over the same period, in the 42nd percentile, suggesting some resilience in property values despite the decline in new construction. The days on market metric, at 71 days, is in the 12th percentile, indicating a longer time to sell properties, which could be a sign of weakening demand or an oversupply in the market.

Overall, the data indicates a labor market with moderate employment levels and strong wage growth, but with challenges in labor force participation and housing market dynamics.
