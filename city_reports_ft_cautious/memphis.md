**Grade: B+ (Above Average) | 56.1th percentile | April 2026**

However, beneath the surface, tensions emerge. The city's housing market is experiencing a slowdown, with days on market increasing by +6.9% YoY to 62 days, indicating a stabilizing force rather than a warning sign, given the strong employment growth and rising building permits.

Memphis offers businesses an attractive combination of affordability, solid wage growth, and a healthy labor market expansion. However, the primary risk lies in the city's low labor force participation rate and limited professional workforce, which may constrain talent attraction and business growth.
