## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which indicates a relatively stable labor market but not one that is particularly robust. The labor force participation rate of 60.48% is in the lower end of the spectrum, at the 18th percentile, potentially reflecting challenges in engaging a larger portion of the population in the workforce. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile, suggesting that those employed are experiencing moderate wage increases.

Housing and construction metrics present a more complex picture. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined sharply by 32.45%, placing Memphis in the 2nd percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in residential construction activity. Conversely, the housing price index has increased by 5.21% year-over-year, in the 44th percentile, indicating some stability in property values despite the decline in building permits. The rental market appears to be under pressure, with a 4.35% decrease in the number of single-family rentals year-over-year, placing the city in the 6th percentile. Additionally, the average days on market for properties has risen to 81 days, in the 14th percentile, which could suggest a less dynamic real estate market.

Overall, the data suggests that while Memphis is experiencing some positive trends in employment and wages, there are significant challenges in labor force engagement and housing market dynamics.
