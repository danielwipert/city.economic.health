## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Memphis, as of the latest available figures, presents a mixed picture with some positive indicators alongside areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, which suggests that while employment conditions are not among the best, they are also not among the worst. The labor force participation rate of 60.17% is relatively low, ranking in the 8th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market compared to other metropolitan areas.

Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, increasing by 8.34%, which places Memphis in the 84th percentile, suggesting that those employed are experiencing significant wage gains. However, the cost of living has also risen by 4.12%, which may offset some of the benefits of higher earnings. Housing market dynamics show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 5.21%, but this is accompanied by a decline in housing permits by 32.34%, indicating a potential slowdown in new construction activity. Additionally, the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 78 days, which may reflect a cooling in demand or an oversupply of homes.

Overall, the weighted score of 36 and a grade of C+ suggest that Memphis's economic performance is below average when compared to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in earnings growth and cost of living, but challenges in labor force participation and housing market activity.
