# Memphis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Memphis's economic performance is characterised by a mixed set of indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, ranking in the 34th percentile among comparable metro areas. This suggests a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak relative to peers. The labour force participation rate of 60.48% is in the lower 18th percentile, indicating relatively low engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. Earnings growth over the year has been 6.16%, ranking in the 78th percentile, which may reflect some underlying strength in the local economy.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in housing prices of 5.23% places Memphis in the 42nd percentile, indicating moderate appreciation. However, single-family home sales have declined by 5.54% year-over-year, ranking in the 2nd percentile, suggesting a weakening in this segment of the market. The number of days on market for homes has increased to 79 days, in the 8th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in the pace of sales. Building permits have seen a 14.06% year-over-year increase, placing it in the 70th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction.

The data for Memphis suggests a mixed picture, with some areas of growth and others showing signs of softening. This is consistent with the city's overall economic grade.
