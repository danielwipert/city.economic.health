# Jacksonville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 16th percentile, indicating a tighter labour market than in many other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 60.38% also ranks in the 16th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year is reported at 5.0%, a figure that places Jacksonville in the 70th percentile, indicating a relatively robust increase in wages compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in home prices is 5.54%, ranking in the 46th percentile, indicating a steady appreciation in property values. However, the number of building permits issued has decreased by 2.57% year-over-year, placing the city in the 48th percentile. The days-on-market metric for real estate sales stands at 81 days, in the 14th percentile, which may indicate a less dynamic housing market. The year-over-year change in the price-to-square-foot ratio for homes is down by 2.73%, ranking in the 16th percentile, suggesting a slight cooling in the housing market.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be stable but not particularly dynamic, with some positive signs in wage growth and housing prices offset by slower construction activity and longer property listing durations.
