## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, placing it in the 30th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 60.83% is lower, ranking in the 20th percentile, indicating a potential underutilization of the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust at 9.59%, placing Jacksonville in the 96th percentile, a strong performance that may reflect a tightening labor market or other local economic factors.

Housing market indicators show moderate changes. The year-over-year increase in the housing price index is 5.54%, placing the city in the 48th percentile, suggesting a steady but not overly heated market. However, the number of building permits has decreased by 2.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 46th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction. The days on market for properties have increased to 78 days, in the 6th percentile, suggesting a less competitive market for homebuyers. Additionally, the cost of living index of 4.4% and the owner-occupied housing rate of 1.54% are in the 72nd and 18th percentiles, respectively, indicating moderate living costs and a relatively low rate of homeownership.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic profile suggests a balanced but not exceptional performance, with strengths in earnings growth and housing prices, but challenges in labor force participation and housing market dynamics.
