# Jacksonville

**Grade: D (Emergency) | 18.9th percentile | June 2026**

---

The city of Jacksonville has an overall grade of D, ranking in the 18.9th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a labor demand composite score of 2.55, placing it in the bottom tier. The city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand and high unemployment rate, with employment growth at -0.42% year-over-year and an unemployment rate of 4.70%. These metrics signal significant challenges for businesses looking to expand or relocate in the area.

**Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate in Jacksonville is -0.42% year-over-year, combined with a weekly hours deviation of +1.167% above its own trend. This signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The labor demand composite score of 2.55 is in the bottom tier, indicating a weak job market.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Jacksonville is 4.70%, placing it in the bottom tier. This indicates a relatively loose labor market, with some slack, making it easier for businesses to hire workers. However, this also suggests weaker local consumer demand, which could impact businesses reliant on local spending.

**Wage Growth**
The year-over-year wage growth in Jacksonville is +1.23%, which is relatively slow. This moderate wage growth rate implies that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly, but worker purchasing power is also not increasing significantly. This could make it easier for businesses to manage labor costs, but may also limit worker demand for goods and services.

**Cost of Living**
Jacksonville has a cost of living score with a PSF of $218/sqft, which is decreasing by -2.2% year-over-year, compared to average hourly earnings of $36.76/hr, resulting in a ratio of 5.93. With a percentile rank of 35th, the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. This makes it easier for businesses to attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Jacksonville is growing at a rate of -0.05% year-over-year, indicating a slight contraction in the labor pool. This implies that the supply of workers is not expanding, which could create structural headwinds for hiring and business growth.

**Building Permits**
The number of building permits in Jacksonville has decreased by -34.14% year-over-year, indicating a significant tightening of the housing supply. This suggests that future affordability and workforce accommodation may be at risk, as the supply of new housing is not keeping pace with demand.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market for homes in Jacksonville is 58 days, with a year-over-year increase of +1.8%. This indicates a relatively slow market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, this also suggests that the housing market may be normalizing, rather than being highly competitive.

**Office Economy**
Jacksonville has an office economy score with a percentile rank of 50th, indicating a relatively deep professional talent pool. This makes the city suited for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but less suited for those in industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The city of Jacksonville presents a challenging environment for businesses, with low labor demand and high unemployment. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the limited supply of workers and the tightening housing market, which could create significant headwinds for growth and expansion. Despite some advantages in terms of affordability, the overall economic character of the city suggests that businesses may need to carefully consider these challenges before making a location decision.
