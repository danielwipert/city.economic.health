# Jacksonville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 16th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly robust nor weak compared to other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 60.38% also ranks in the 16th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.0%, ranking in the 70th percentile, which may reflect a relatively healthy income environment for those employed.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 7.27% places Jacksonville in the 46th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. However, the decline in building permits by 25.81% year-over-year, ranking in the 8th percentile, indicates a significant slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric for home sales stands at 81 days, in the 14th percentile, which could suggest a less dynamic housing market. Additionally, the weekly hours worked average of 33.7 hours, in the 40th percentile, and the cost of living index of 4.65, in the 66th percentile, both indicate conditions that are somewhat below or near the national average.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be characterised by moderate growth and stability, with some areas of concern.
