# Jacksonville

**Grade: C- (Critical) | 31.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

The Jacksonville metro area has an overall grade of C- with a composite score ranking it 31.9th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score in the bottom tier, and its strong wage growth, which ranks in the 77th percentile. Specifically, the labor demand composite score of 2.35 and the wage growth rate of 6.38% year-over-year are key metrics that shape the city's economic landscape.

**Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate in Jacksonville is -0.49% year-over-year, and weekly hours are 1.267% above the city's own trend. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, as the city is experiencing job losses while hours are above trend, indicating a survivor squeeze where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles. This scenario suggests that businesses may face challenges in finding new talent.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Jacksonville is 4.60%, which is in the bottom tier, ranking 6th percentile. This indicates that the market has some slack, making it relatively easier for businesses to hire compared to cities with tighter labor markets. However, the practical implication is that local consumer demand may be weaker due to the higher unemployment rate.

**Wage Growth**
The year-over-year wage growth rate in Jacksonville is 6.38%, which is above average, ranking in the 77th percentile. This signals fast wage growth, implying rising labor costs for employers and increasing worker purchasing power. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to attract and retain talent.

**Cost of Living**
Jacksonville has a cost of living ratio of 1.29, which ranks in the top tier, 85th percentile, indicating that the city is more affordable relative to its peers. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums, providing a talent attraction advantage. The lower cost of living also translates to a lower real-wage floor, making it easier for businesses to operate.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Jacksonville is growing at a rate of 0.79% year-over-year, which is near the median, ranking 53rd percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, albeit slowly, providing a moderate hiring capacity for businesses. However, the growth rate is not strong enough to drive significant labor market tightening.

**Building Permits**
The year-over-year change in building permits in Jacksonville is -32.70%, which is in the bottom tier, ranking 8th percentile. This signals a tightening of housing supply, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation. As a result, businesses may face difficulties in attracting talent due to limited housing options.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market in Jacksonville is 58 days, with a year-over-year increase of 1.8%. This indicates a slower market, ranking below average, 29th percentile. For a worker relocating to this city, the market is relatively accessible, with homes taking longer to sell. However, this may also signal a normalization of the market, rather than a demand erosion.

**Office Economy**
The share of professional and office workers in Jacksonville is 0.90, which ranks in the bottom tier, 11th percentile. This indicates a relatively shallow talent pool, making the city less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting. However, the city may be more suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Jacksonville metro area offers businesses a relatively affordable cost of living and a growing labor force, but the city's low labor demand and tightening housing supply pose significant risks. The single biggest constraint for a decision-maker is the limited labor demand, which may hinder businesses' ability to find and attract new talent, making it essential to carefully consider these factors when making a location decision.
