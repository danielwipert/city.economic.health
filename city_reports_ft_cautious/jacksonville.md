# Jacksonville

**Grade: C (Very Poor) | 32.3th percentile | April 2026**

---

Jacksonville earns a grade of C due to its severe labor market weakness, with an unemployment rate of 4.60% and nonfarm employment growth of -0.49% year-over-year. The city's low professional and office worker concentration, ranking in the 12th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses. Jacksonville boasts a strong cost advantage, with a cost of living composite score of 1.30, making it one of the most affordable metros in the dataset.

Wage growth is solid at +6.38% year-over-year, but this is likely due to a mix shift towards higher-paying survivors rather than broad labor market health. The housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 1.8% year-over-year to an average of 58 days, and building permits declining sharply by -32.70% year-over-year. This slowdown in the housing market, combined with the city's affordability advantage, indicates that demand for housing is softening.

Jacksonville's cost advantage is a significant benefit for businesses, but the city's struggling labor market poses a primary risk, hindering workforce attraction and retention. The city's economic weaknesses, including its high unemployment rate and low professional worker concentration, pose significant challenges for businesses looking to establish or expand operations in the area, with a nonfarm employment growth of -0.49% year-over-year.
