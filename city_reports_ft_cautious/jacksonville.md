## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 32nd percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a labor market that is neither exceptionally tight nor particularly weak. The labor force participation rate of 60.61% is relatively low, ranking in the 14th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market compared to other regions. On a more positive note, earnings growth over the past year has been robust at 9.66%, placing Jacksonville in the 98th percentile, which is a significant strength in the current economic environment.

Housing market indicators show moderate trends. The housing price index has increased by 5.54% year-over-year, placing the city in the 48th percentile, suggesting stable but not overly exuberant growth in home values. However, the number of building permits has declined by 2.63% year-over-year, ranking in the 46th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 76 days, well above the national average, and in the 8th percentile, indicating a less competitive market for home sales. Additionally, the cost of living index is 4.4, in the 76th percentile, suggesting that living costs in Jacksonville are higher than in many other metropolitan areas.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic performance, as indicated by these metrics, appears to be below average, with strengths in earnings growth offset by challenges in labor market engagement and housing market dynamics.
