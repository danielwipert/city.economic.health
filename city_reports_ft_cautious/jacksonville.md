# Jacksonville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 16th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 60.38% also ranks in the 16th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of workforce engagement. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.0%, placing it in the 70th percentile, which is a positive sign for income stability and growth. However, the cost of living index of 4.66, in the 66th percentile, suggests that living expenses are relatively high compared to other metropolitan areas.

In the housing market, the annual home price index increase of 7.27% places Jacksonville in the 46th percentile, indicating moderate appreciation. Building permits have declined by 25.81% year-over-year, ranking in the 8th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The property sales forecast is down 2.58% year-over-year, in the 14th percentile, suggesting a cooling in the real estate market. The average days on market for homes is 72 days, in the 16th percentile, indicating a slower pace of sales compared to the national average.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be stable but with some challenges.
