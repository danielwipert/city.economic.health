# Jacksonville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas outperforming national trends while others trail behind. The city's unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, ranking in the 16th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly robust nor severely strained. The labour force participation rate of 60.38% also ranks in the 16th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.0%, placing Jacksonville in the 70th percentile, a relatively strong performance compared to other metropolitan areas.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices is 7.27%, ranking in the 46th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 25.81% year-over-year, ranking in the 8th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in construction activity. The supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 2.58% year-over-year, placing the city in the 14th percentile, while the average days on market for properties is 72 days, in the 16th percentile, indicating a relatively stable market.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be characterised by moderate growth and stability, with some sectors showing stronger resilience than others.
