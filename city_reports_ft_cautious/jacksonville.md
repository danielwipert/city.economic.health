# Jacksonville — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Jacksonville's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 16th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly tight nor loose compared to other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 60.38% also ranks in the 16th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.0%, placing Jacksonville in the 70th percentile, a positive sign for income levels and consumer spending potential.

The housing market shows both gains and declines. The home price index has risen by 7.27% year-over-year, placing the city in the 46th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation in property values. However, the number of building permits has declined by 25.81% year-over-year, ranking in the 8th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric stands at 72 days, in the 16th percentile, indicating a relatively balanced market. The cost of living index is 4.66, and the overall wage rate is 2.24, both in the middle range of national comparisons, suggesting that Jacksonville remains affordable relative to many other U.S. cities.

Overall, Jacksonville's economic profile suggests a region with moderate growth, some areas of strength and others facing challenges.
