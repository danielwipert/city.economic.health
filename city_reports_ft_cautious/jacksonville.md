# Jacksonville

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 45.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Jacksonville's economy lags, with a below-average overall grade of 45.0th percentile, driven by severe labor market weakness, with an unemployment rate of 4.60% and a labor force participation rate of 60.38%. Nonfarm employment growth, at 0.68%, is a bright spot, signaling a healthy expansion, while wage growth is solid, with hourly earnings rising 5.00% year-over-year.

Despite these positive trends, the city's housing market is a concern. Building permits are declining sharply, with a year-over-year drop of 32.70%, indicating a contraction in housing supply. Homes are taking 1.8% more days to sell, averaging 58 days, suggesting a slowing market and potentially eroding demand.

In summary, Jacksonville offers businesses an affordable cost of living, with a composite score of 1.44, and solid wage growth, but the primary risk is a tightening labor market and a slowing housing market. The city's severe labor market weakness and declining housing supply pose significant challenges for businesses, making it essential to carefully consider these factors in any location decision.
