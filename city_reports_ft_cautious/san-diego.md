# San Diego — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable regions. The labour force participation rate of 62.01% also ranks in the 24th percentile, indicating a relatively stable level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.43% places the region in the 36th percentile, suggesting modest income growth.

Housing market indicators show a relatively stable but slow-growing real estate market. The year-over-year change in housing prices is a modest 0.78%, ranking in the 26th percentile. However, the year-over-year decline in building permits of 23.13% suggests a slowdown in new construction activity. The year-over-year decrease in the price-to-square-foot ratio of 3.33% may reflect a softening demand for housing. The average days on market of 56.0 days, in the 88th percentile, indicates a longer than average time for properties to sell.

The data suggests that the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area is experiencing a period of moderate economic activity with some signs of slowing in key sectors.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate performance while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak. The labor force participation rate of 62.01% also ranks in the 24th percentile, indicating a relatively stable but not robust level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.43% places the region in the 36th percentile, suggesting modest income growth that may reflect broader economic trends.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced view. The year-over-year change in housing prices is a modest 0.78%, ranking in the 26th percentile, which indicates a relatively stable but slow-growing real estate market. However, the year-over-year decline in building permits of 23.13%, placing the region in the 12th percentile, suggests a significant slowdown in new construction activity. Additionally, the year-over-year decrease in the price-to-square-foot ratio of 3.33%, in the 12th percentile, may reflect a softening demand for housing. The average days on market of 56.0 days, in the 88th percentile, indicates a longer than average time for properties to sell, which could be a sign of reduced buyer interest or overpriced listings.

Overall, the data suggests that the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area is experiencing a period of moderate economic activity with some signs of slowing in key sectors.
