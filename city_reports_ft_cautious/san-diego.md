## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 14th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is performing moderately well but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 62.18% is in the 26th percentile, indicating a somewhat lower engagement of the working-age population compared to other regions. Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 3.03%, ranking in the 46th percentile, suggesting moderate wage pressure.

Housing market indicators show contrasting trends. Building permits have increased by 61.67% year-over-year, placing San Diego in the 90th percentile, which may reflect a significant level of construction activity. However, the housing price index has declined by 0.55% over the same period, ranking in the 16th percentile, and the price-to-rent ratio has decreased by 2.73%, in the 18th percentile, indicating a softening in the housing market. The average days on market for properties is 48 days, in the 68th percentile, suggesting a relatively balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers hold a strong advantage.

Overall, the data suggests a regional economy with areas of both strength and weakness, without clear indications of broad economic momentum or decline.
