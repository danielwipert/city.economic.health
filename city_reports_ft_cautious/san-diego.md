The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 14th percentile nationally, which suggests that the local labor market is performing moderately well but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 62.18% is also below the median, indicating a somewhat lower engagement of the working-age population in the labor market compared to other regions.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 3.35%, ranking in the 30th percentile, which may reflect a stable but not particularly dynamic wage environment. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is relatively high at 7.13%, placing it in the 6th percentile, suggesting that residents face significant price pressures. Housing market indicators show a decline in home prices year-over-year by 0.55%, and a decrease in permits for new housing units by 2.73%, both of which are in the lower percentiles. These figures indicate a cooling in the housing sector, which may be a response to broader economic conditions or local factors.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 26.1 and the grade of "C" suggest that San Diego's economic performance is below average when compared to other metropolitan areas.
