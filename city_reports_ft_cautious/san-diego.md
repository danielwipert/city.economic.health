## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 16th percentile among comparable regions, suggesting a labor market that is performing below average but not in crisis. Labor force participation is at 62.1%, which is slightly above the median but still indicates room for improvement in workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 2.04%, reflecting a stable but not particularly dynamic income environment.

Housing market indicators show contrasting trends. Building permits have increased by 61.67% year-over-year, a significant rise that may reflect growing demand or investment in construction. However, the housing price index has declined by 0.82% over the same period, and the price-to-square-foot ratio has decreased by 3.13%, indicating a softening in home prices. The average days on market for properties is 47 days, which is relatively high and suggests a slowdown in property sales. These housing metrics, combined with a weighted percentile score of 24 and a grade of "C," indicate an overall economic performance that is below average but not severely distressed.

In summary, the data suggests a cautiously stable economic environment in San Diego, with some sectors showing resilience and others facing challenges.
