## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 38 days, a relatively high figure that ranks in the 88th percentile.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with several indicators suggesting moderate performance relative to other U.S. metropolitan areas. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile among similar regions, which indicates a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak. The labor force participation rate of 62.01% also ranks in the 24th percentile, suggesting a level of engagement in the workforce that is consistent with broader national trends but not exceptional.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, at 2.43%, ranking in the 36th percentile, while the cost of living index of 7.14 places the region in the 14th percentile, indicating higher living costs compared to many other areas. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 0.78%, placing the region in the 26th percentile, and a decline in single-family home sales of 3.69%, in the 8th percentile, which may reflect cooling demand or supply constraints. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 38 days, a relatively high figure that ranks in the 88th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in property transactions.

Overall, the data suggests a regional economy that is performing in line with national averages in some areas, while facing challenges in others, particularly in housing and cost of living.
