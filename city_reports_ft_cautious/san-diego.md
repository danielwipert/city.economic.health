# San Diego — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate is 62.01%, also in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is recorded at 2.43%, ranking in the 36th percentile, indicating a slight improvement in income levels.

Housing and construction metrics, however, show a less positive trend. Building permits have declined by 25.87% year-over-year, placing the region in the 12th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in new residential and commercial development. The housing price index has increased by only 0.35% over the same period, ranking in the 24th percentile, indicating a relatively flat housing market. The days on market metric stands at 56 days, in the 88th percentile, which may reflect a less active real estate market where properties take longer to sell. Additionally, the cost of living index is 7.09, in the 14th percentile, and the overall wage rate index is 1.34, in the 18th percentile, both suggesting that living costs and wages are relatively low compared to other regions.

These data points collectively indicate a mixed economic picture for San Diego, with some indicators showing stability and others suggesting areas of concern.
