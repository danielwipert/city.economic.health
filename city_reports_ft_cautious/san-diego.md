## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate performance while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively stable labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 62.01% also ranks in the 24th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 2.43%, ranking in the 36th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 7.14% places the region in the 14th percentile, potentially squeezing household budgets.

Housing market indicators present a more challenging outlook. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined by 23.13%, indicating a slowdown in construction activity. The home price index has increased by only 0.78% over the same period, ranking in the 26th percentile, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market. The year-over-year change in permits for single-family homes has decreased by 3.69%, further suggesting a reduction in new housing supply. Despite these declines, the average days on market for homes is 38 days, placing the region in the 88th percentile, which could indicate a relatively balanced market where properties are still selling at a steady pace.

Overall, the data suggests that while San Diego's economy exhibits some stability, there are signs of moderation in key sectors such as housing and employment.
