## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate performance while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak. The labor force participation rate of 62.42% is similarly positioned in the 26th percentile, indicating a level of engagement that is below average but not critically low.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 2.43%, ranking in the 36th percentile, which may reflect a stable but not robust income environment. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is relatively high at 7.12%, placing it in the 10th percentile, suggesting that residents are facing significant price pressures. Housing market indicators show a slight increase in home prices of 0.35% year-over-year, while the number of building permits has declined sharply by 25.87% compared to the previous year, both figures ranking in the lower percentiles. This decline in permits may indicate a slowdown in construction activity, although the days on market for homes remain at a moderate 57 days, which is in the 74th percentile, suggesting a relatively balanced market.

Overall, the data indicates a region with a stable but not dynamic economy, facing challenges in housing and cost of living, with a labor market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak.
