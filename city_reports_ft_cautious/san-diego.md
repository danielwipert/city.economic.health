## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative stability while others point to potential areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly robust nor distressed. The labor force participation rate of 62.01% also ranks in the 24th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.43% places the region in the 36th percentile, suggesting modest income growth that may reflect broader economic conditions.

Housing and construction metrics show more variability. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined by 25.87%, ranking in the 12th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in residential construction activity. The housing price index has increased by 0.35% over the same period, placing it in the 24th percentile, suggesting a relatively stable but not rapidly appreciating housing market. The days on market metric, at 56.0 days, ranks in the 88th percentile, indicating a longer time for properties to sell, which could be a sign of reduced demand or an oversupply of homes.

Overall, the data suggests a cautiously balanced economic environment in San Diego, with some indicators pointing to stability and others to potential softening.
