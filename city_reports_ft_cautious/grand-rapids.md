# Grand Rapids — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 4.0% ranks in the 34th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 63.89% is slightly above the national median, suggesting a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.13% is in the 46th percentile, consistent with modest wage increases that may be influenced by broader economic conditions.

Housing market indicators show notable activity. The year-over-year increase in housing permits is 39.86%, ranking in the 82nd percentile, which suggests robust construction activity. The home price index has risen by 21.27% year-over-year, placing it in the 90th percentile, indicating strong property value appreciation. However, the days on market metric at 64 days is in the 26th percentile, suggesting a more balanced market where properties take a relatively longer time to sell compared to other regions. The cost of living index of 0.63, in the 94th percentile, indicates that Grand Rapids remains an affordable place to live relative to many other metropolitan areas.

The data suggests that the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area is performing above average, with a balanced mix of labor market strength, wage growth, and housing market activity.
