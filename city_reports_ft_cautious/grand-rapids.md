## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with several indicators suggesting a stable but not robust economic environment. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 20th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of unemployment compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.6% is more favorable, ranking in the 66th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the labor market. Earnings growth over the past year is reported at 4.35%, placing it in the 54th percentile, which is a moderate increase but not particularly strong.

In the housing market, the data shows a year-over-year increase in the housing price index of 20.55%, which is a significant rise and places the area in the 90th percentile. This contrasts with a more modest 5.0% increase in the price of single-family homes, which is in the 94th percentile. The number of building permits has decreased by 8.64% year-over-year, placing the area in the 38th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction. The average days on market for homes is 42 days, in the 72nd percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

Overall, the data suggests a stable economic environment with moderate growth in key areas, but with some indicators of potential softening in the housing market.
