## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 63.89% is also below the national median, ranking in the 40th percentile. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.13% is slightly above the midpoint, with a percentile score of 46, suggesting moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators show more robust activity. Building permits have increased by 19.91% year-over-year, placing the region in the 84th percentile, indicating a strong pace of new construction. The housing price index has risen by 19.4% over the same period, ranking in the 90th percentile, which may reflect a tight supply of homes and strong demand. The pace of sales, as measured by the percentage change in existing home sales, has grown by 7.35%, placing the area in the 94th percentile, further suggesting a vibrant real estate market. However, the average days on market for homes is 68 days, which is in the 48th percentile, indicating that while sales are brisk, they are not exceptionally fast compared to other markets.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 64.3 and the grade of B+ suggest that Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas.
