# Grand Rapids — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate of 63.89% is in the middle range, at the 40th percentile, suggesting moderate engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.13% places the region in the 46th percentile, indicating modest income growth.

Housing market indicators show stronger performance. The year-over-year increase in housing prices of 21.27% is in the 90th percentile, reflecting significant appreciation in property values. Building permits have risen by 39.86% year-over-year, placing the region in the 82nd percentile, which may indicate robust construction activity. However, the days on market for properties has increased to 64 days, in the 26th percentile, suggesting a potential slowdown in the pace of sales. The cost of living index of 0.63, in the 94th percentile, indicates that the region remains relatively affordable compared to other metropolitan areas.

The data suggests a balanced economic environment in Grand Rapids, with above-average performance in housing and affordability, and more moderate performance in employment and earnings.
