# Grand Rapids — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, ranking in the 34th percentile among comparable regions, indicating a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate of 63.89% is slightly above average, ranking in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.13% is in the 46th percentile, indicating modest wage increases.

In the housing sector, the data shows a significant year-over-year increase in home prices of 21.27%, placing it in the 90th percentile. The number of building permits issued has also risen by 39.86% year-over-year, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating ongoing construction activity. However, the median days on market for homes is 64 days, in the 26th percentile, suggesting a balanced market.

The weighted score of 63 and a grade of B+ indicate that Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is performing above average relative to its peers, with strengths in housing and construction offsetting more moderate performance in employment and earnings.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, placing it in the 34th percentile among comparable regions, suggesting a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 63.89% is slightly above average, ranking in the 40th percentile, which may reflect a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.13% is in the 46th percentile, indicating modest wage increases that are neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other areas.

In the housing sector, the data shows a robust year-over-year increase in home prices of 21.27%, placing it in the 90th percentile, which suggests a strong demand for housing in the region. The number of building permits issued has also risen by 39.86% year-over-year, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating ongoing construction activity and potential growth in the housing market. However, the median days on market for homes is 64 days, in the 26th percentile, which may reflect a more balanced market where properties take a reasonable amount of time to sell.

Overall, the weighted score of 63 and a grade of B+ suggest that Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is performing above average relative to its peers, with strengths in housing and construction offsetting more moderate performance in employment and earnings.
