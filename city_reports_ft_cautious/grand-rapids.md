# Grand Rapids — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Grand Rapids' economic indicators present a nuanced picture, with both encouraging and cautionary signals. The labor market has shown a modest improvement, with the unemployment rate decreasing by 0.3 percentage points year-over-year. However, this progress is tempered by a 4.96% contraction in the civilian labor force over the same period, a trend that warrants attention. Average hourly earnings have risen by 3.1% year-over-year, a relatively modest gain that may struggle to keep pace with inflation.

In contrast, business activity in Grand Rapids has been buoyed by a surge in residential building permits, which have grown by 55.17% year-over-year. This suggests robust demand for housing, a notion supported by the median days on market composite score. While the labor demand composite score is more subdued, indicating potential slowing in employment growth, the office worker ratio composite score remains high, underscoring the city's strong presence of office-based employment. Furthermore, Grand Rapids' relatively low cost of living composite score makes it an attractive location for businesses and individuals seeking a more affordable base.

The housing market in Grand Rapids is characterized by a significant decrease in median days on market and a high composite score, indicating a strong and competitive market. However, this rapid pace of activity also poses challenges, including rapid price appreciation and dwindling inventory, which can hinder buyers' ability to enter the market. Overall, Grand Rapids' economic indicators suggest a city experiencing moderate growth, albeit with notable challenges related to labor force contraction and slowing employment growth.

Looking ahead, Grand Rapids' economy is likely to maintain a moderate growth trajectory, driven by robust housing demand and a low cost of living. To sustain long-term growth, however, the city must address its labor force challenges and focus on attracting new businesses and talent.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic indicators for Grand Rapids suggest a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labor market front, the city's unemployment rate has decreased by 0.3 percentage points year-over-year, indicating a modest improvement in job market conditions. However, the civilian labor force has contracted by 4.96% over the same period, which may be a cause for concern. Average hourly earnings have grown by 3.1% year-over-year, a relatively modest increase that may not be sufficient to keep pace with inflation.

In terms of business activity, Grand Rapids has seen a significant increase in residential building permits, with a year-over-year growth rate of 55.17%. This suggests a strong demand for housing in the city, which is further supported by the median days on market composite score. However, the labor demand composite score is more muted, indicating that employment growth may be slowing. The office worker ratio composite score is relatively high, suggesting a strong presence of office-based employment in the city. The cost of living composite score is relatively low, indicating that Grand Rapids may be an attractive location for businesses and individuals looking for a lower cost of living.

The housing market in Grand Rapids appears to be strong, with a significant decrease in median days on market and a high composite score. However, this may also be a source of stress, as rapid price appreciation and decreasing inventory can make it difficult for buyers to enter the market. Overall, Grand Rapids' economic indicators suggest a city that is experiencing moderate growth, but may be facing challenges in terms of labor force contraction and slowing employment growth. 

Closing outlook: Grand Rapids' economy is likely to continue growing at a moderate pace, driven by strong demand for housing and a low cost of living. However, the city will need to address its labor force challenges and work to attract new businesses and talent to sustain long-term growth.
