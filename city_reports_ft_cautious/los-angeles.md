## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate growth while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.3%, placing it in the 6th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively high level of joblessness compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 64.28% is closer to the national median, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.5% is slightly above the midpoint in national rankings, indicating modest wage increases.

Housing and construction metrics offer a more nuanced view. Building permits have increased by 8.63% year-over-year, placing the region in the 58th percentile, which may reflect ongoing demand for new housing units. However, the housing price index has grown by only 0.13% over the same period, ranking in the 22nd percentile, suggesting a relatively stable but not rapidly appreciating real estate market. The days on market metric stands at 67 days, near the median, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. Commercial space fundamentals, as indicated by the year-over-year change in per square foot rates, have declined by 1.98%, placing the region in the 28th percentile, which may signal challenges in the commercial real estate sector.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 28.4 and the grade of "C" suggest that the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area faces several economic challenges, though it also shows signs of stability and moderate growth in certain sectors.
