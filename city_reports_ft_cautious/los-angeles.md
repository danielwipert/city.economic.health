# Los Angeles — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 63.61% is in the 38th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.33% is in the 76th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening income environment for those employed.

Housing and construction metrics show varied trends. Building permits have increased by 10.45% year-over-year, placing the region in the 68th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential and commercial construction. In contrast, the housing price index has grown by only 0.86% over the same period, in the 28th percentile, which could indicate a stabilization or moderation in the housing market. The pace of sales, as measured by days on market, is 67 days, in the 52nd percentile, indicating a balanced market.

The weighted percentile score of 31.6 and the grade of C+ suggest that the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metropolitan area performs well in certain areas but also has significant areas for improvement.

The economic data for the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 63.61% is in the 38th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.33% is robust, ranking in the 76th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening income environment for those employed.

Housing and construction metrics show varied trends. Building permits have increased by 10.45% year-over-year, placing the region in the 68th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential and commercial construction. In contrast, the housing price index has grown by only 0.86% over the same period, in the 28th percentile, which could indicate a stabilization or moderation in the housing market. The pace of sales, as measured by days on market, is 67 days, in the 52nd percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers dominate.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 31.6 and the grade of C+ suggest that while Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim performs well in certain areas, there are also significant areas for improvement.
