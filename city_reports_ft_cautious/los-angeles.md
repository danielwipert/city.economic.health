# Los Angeles — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metropolitan area's economic landscape, as of the latest available data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 63.61% is at the 38th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labour force.

Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.33% is robust, ranking in the 76th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 7.17% is lower, at the 12th percentile, which may ease financial pressures on households. The average weekly hours worked of 32.9 hours is among the lowest, at the 4th percentile, which could reflect part-time employment trends or flexible work arrangements.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in the housing price index of 0.86% is in the 28th percentile, suggesting stable but modest growth in home values. The decline in the price per square foot of -2.37%, at the 18th percentile, may indicate a softening in the residential real estate market. Building permits have increased by 10.45% year-over-year, placing the region in the 68th percentile, which could reflect ongoing construction activity.

The median days on market for homes is 44 days, a relatively high figure at the 74th percentile, possibly indicating a slowdown in the pace of sales. The weighted percentile score of 31.9 and the grade of C+ suggest that while some sectors show strength, others lag behind national averages.
