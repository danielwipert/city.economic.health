# Hartford — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area presents a mixed economic picture, with some indicators outperforming national trends while others trail behind. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing the region in the 94th percentile, indicating a relatively strong labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 64.7% ranks in the 58th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labour force.

Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 0.36%, which places the region in the 6th percentile. This decline may be related to broader economic challenges or sector-specific issues within the local economy. In contrast, the housing market appears to be performing well, with a home price index increase of 28.29% year-over-year, placing the region in the 98th percentile. The number of permits for new residential construction has increased by 16.13%, a figure above the median but not as pronounced as the rise in home prices.

The overall weighted score of 57 and a grade of "B" suggest that the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area is performing at a level comparable to other regions.

The economic data for the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing the region in the 94th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 64.7% is more modest, ranking in the 58th percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force.

Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 0.36%, which places the region in the 6th percentile. This decline in earnings may reflect broader economic challenges or sector-specific issues within the local economy. In contrast, the housing market appears to be performing well, with a home price index increase of 28.29% year-over-year, placing the region in the 98th percentile. Despite this strong performance in home prices, the number of permits for new residential construction has increased by only 16.13%, a figure that is still above the median but not as pronounced as the rise in home prices.

The overall weighted score of 57 and a grade of "B" suggest that the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area is performing at an average level compared to other regions.
