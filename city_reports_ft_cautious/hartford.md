The economic data for the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing it in the 98th percentile nationally, which indicates a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 64.7% is more moderate, ranking in the 54th percentile, suggesting that while employment is strong, there may be segments of the population not actively participating in the workforce. Earnings growth has been slightly negative year-over-year at -0.36%, placing it in the 2nd percentile, which may reflect broader economic pressures or specific local factors.

In the housing sector, the Hartford area has seen significant appreciation, with home prices increasing by 26.71% year-over-year, placing it in the 98th percentile. This strong growth contrasts with a modest decline in permits for new residential construction, up 16.13% year-over-year, which ranks in the 80th percentile. The median days on market for homes is 39 days, a relatively low figure that places it in the 96th percentile, indicating a competitive market where properties are selling quickly. The cost of living index of 3.51, in the 84th percentile, and the overall wage replacement ratio of 2.24, in the 44th percentile, suggest that while living costs are relatively high, they are somewhat offset by the strong job market.

Overall, the data indicates that the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area is performing above average, with strengths in employment and housing markets, though some areas like earnings growth show room for improvement.
