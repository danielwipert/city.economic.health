# Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford

**Grade: A (Very Good) | 65.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Hartford's economy boasts a remarkably tight labor market, with an unemployment rate of 2.70% and rapid job creation, as evidenced by 1.88% year-over-year nonfarm employment growth, earning it an overall grade of A, ranking in the 65.6th percentile.

The city's highly competitive job market, where workers have leverage and businesses face hiring challenges, drives this momentum story. However, wage growth has been stagnant, with a -0.36% year-over-year change, and the cost of living is above average, with a composite score of 3.37, which could necessitate wage premiums to attract talent.

Hartford offers a business a highly competitive and expanding economy, with a talented workforce and a stabilizing housing market, with homes taking 30% more days to sell than last year, resulting in an average of 39 days on market. However, the primary risk is the potential for wage pressure, given the tight labor market and above-average cost of living, which could impact businesses' ability to attract and retain talent.
