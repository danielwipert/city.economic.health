# Hartford — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing it in the 94th percentile, indicating a relatively strong labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 64.7% ranks in the 58th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force.

Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decline of 0.36%, placing the region in the 6th percentile for this metric. The reasons for this decline are unclear, but it may be related to broader economic pressures or local factors affecting wage levels. Housing market indicators show a more dynamic trend, with housing prices increasing by 26.71% year-over-year, a figure that ranks in the 98th percentile. Despite this strong price growth, the number of single-family homes sold has decreased by 0.54%, which may indicate a potential cooling in sales activity. The median days on market for properties is 54 days, a relatively low figure that places the region in the 96th percentile, indicating a brisk pace of sales.

The data suggests a complex economic environment in Hartford, with some indicators showing relative strength and others lagging behind national trends.
