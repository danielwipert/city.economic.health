# Hartford — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area exhibits a mixed economic performance across various indicators. The labor force participation rate of 64.7% ranks in the 58th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of labor market engagement.

Earnings growth has been negative over the past year, declining by 0.36%, placing the region in the 6th percentile. This indicates a contraction in income levels that could impact consumer spending and economic activity. On the housing front, the Hartford area has seen a significant increase in home prices, with a year-over-year growth of 28.29%, ranking in the 98th percentile. However, single-family home sales have declined by 0.94%, and building permits have increased by 16.13%, placing the region in the 46th and 70th percentiles, respectively. These figures suggest a complex housing market with rising prices and mixed signals on sales and construction activity.

The data indicates that Hartford's labor market is performing relatively well in terms of unemployment, but there are challenges in earnings growth and housing dynamics. The region's economic performance is average, as reflected by its weighted percentile score of 54.5 and a grade of B.
