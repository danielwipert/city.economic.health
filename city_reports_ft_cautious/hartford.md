## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing the region in the 96th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 64.7% is more moderate, ranking in the 52nd percentile, indicating that a significant portion of the working-age population remains outside the labor force.

Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decline of 0.36%, placing the region in the 6th percentile. This contrasts with the housing market, where the home price index has increased by 26.71% year-over-year, a figure in the 98th percentile, suggesting strong demand for residential properties. Construction activity also appears healthy, with building permits up 16.13% year-over-year, ranking in the 78th percentile. Despite these positive signs in the housing sector, the number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 51 days, a level in the 92nd percentile, which may reflect a cooling in buyer interest or an increase in inventory.

Overall, the Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area's economic performance is average, with strengths in employment and housing markets offset by weaker earnings growth and labor force participation.
