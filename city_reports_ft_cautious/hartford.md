# Hartford — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area received a grade of B in a recent economic assessment, ranking 50th overall. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing it in the 94th percentile, indicating a relatively strong labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 64.7% is at the 58th percentile, suggesting that a portion of the population remains outside the labour force.

Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decline of 0.36%, ranking in the 6th percentile. In contrast, the home price index has risen by 28.29% year-over-year, placing it in the 98th percentile. The cost of living index is relatively high at 7.56, in the 2nd percentile, which may be influenced by broader economic factors. Building permits have increased by 16.13% year-over-year, indicating ongoing investment in the local real estate sector.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Hartford, with some indicators showing strength and others indicating challenges.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area ranks 50th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing it in the 94th percentile, which suggests a robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 64.7% is more moderate, at the 58th percentile, indicating that while employment is strong, a significant portion of the population remains outside the labor force.

Earnings growth has been modest, with a year-over-year decline of 0.36%, ranking in the 6th percentile. This contrasts with the housing market, where the home price index has risen by 28.29% year-over-year, placing it in the 98th percentile. Despite this, the cost of living index is relatively high at 7.56, in the 2nd percentile, which may reflect broader economic pressures. Construction activity, as measured by building permits, has increased by 16.13% year-over-year, suggesting ongoing investment in the local real estate sector.

Overall, the data indicates a mixed economic picture for Hartford, with strengths in employment and housing prices, but challenges in earnings growth and cost of living.
