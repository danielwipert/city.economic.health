# Hartford — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metropolitan area's economic data reveals a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 2.7%, placing it in the 94th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of unemployment compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate is 64.7%, ranking in the 58th percentile, suggesting that while employment is robust, a significant portion of the population remains outside the labour market.

Earnings growth has been negative, with a year-over-year decrease of 0.36%, placing the region in the 6th percentile. In contrast, housing market indicators show more positive trends. The housing price index has increased by 28.29% year-over-year, placing it in the 98th percentile, reflecting strong appreciation in home values. Building permits have risen by 16.13% year-over-year, in the 74th percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in residential construction. The days on market for homes is 46 days, in the 70th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests that Hartford's labour market is performing well in terms of unemployment, but faces challenges in earnings growth and labour force participation. The housing market appears to be experiencing robust activity and value appreciation.
