## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate is notably high at 70.73%, ranking in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is reported at 6.2%, a figure that falls within the 66th percentile, indicating moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.32%, placing the region in the 8th percentile, which may reflect cooling demand or other factors affecting property values. Similarly, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales has decreased by 3.27%, ranking in the 10th percentile, further suggesting a slowdown in this segment of the market. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 79 days, a significant rise that places the region in the 12th percentile, potentially indicating a softer market for sellers.

Overall, the data suggests that while Denver's labor market remains strong, the housing sector is experiencing some challenges. The region's weighted score of 54 and grade of B indicate an average performance relative to other metropolitan areas.
