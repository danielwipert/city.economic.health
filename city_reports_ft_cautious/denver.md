The economic landscape of the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture of performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. Housing and construction metrics show contrasting trends. Additionally, the average days on market for homes is 72 days, at the 38th percentile, which may indicate a more balanced market compared to periods of rapid appreciation.

Overall, the data indicates a Denver-Aurora-Centennial economy with strengths in labor market participation and earnings growth, but with signs of moderation in the housing sector.
