## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it in the 54th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness but not exceptionally so. The labor force participation rate of 71.33% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.2% places the region in the 66th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases that may reflect broader economic conditions.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 1.32%, placing the region in the 8th percentile, which indicates a cooling in the housing market compared to previous periods. Similarly, the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 13.55%, ranking in the 74th percentile, suggesting continued investment in residential construction despite the decline in home prices. The average days on market for homes is 65 days, which is in the 16th percentile, indicating a slower pace of sales that may reflect softer demand or a shift in buyer behavior.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not outstanding economic environment in Denver, with strengths in labor market participation and construction activity, offset by a cooling in the housing market.
