## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators performing above average while others lag behind. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it in the 54th percentile nationally, suggesting a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 71.33% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, which may reflect a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 4.35%, situated in the 66th percentile, indicating a steady but not exceptional increase in income levels.

Housing market indicators present a more challenging scenario. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.32%, placing Denver in the 8th percentile, which suggests a cooling trend in the housing market. Similarly, the year-over-year change in single-family home sales has decreased by 3.48%, ranking in the 10th percentile, further indicating a slowdown in this sector. The number of days properties remain on the market has increased to 65 days, in the 16th percentile, which may reflect a softer demand for housing. Construction activity, however, appears to be more resilient, with building permits up 13.55% year-over-year, ranking in the 74th percentile.

Overall, the data suggests that while Denver's labor market remains strong, the housing sector faces headwinds, contributing to an overall below-average economic performance as indicated by its weighted percentile score of 49.2.
