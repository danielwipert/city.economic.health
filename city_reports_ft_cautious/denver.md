## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable economic environment. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 71.28% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.09% and a cost of living increase of 4.69% indicate that wages are growing, albeit at a pace slightly above inflation, which may reflect a tightening labor market.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 0.64%, while the supply of homes for sale has decreased by 3.57% year-over-year. These figures, combined with an average of 63 days on the market for properties, suggest a cooling in the housing market, though not a dramatic one. Construction activity remains active, with building permits up 13.55% year-over-year, indicating ongoing investment in residential and commercial development. The overall weighted score of 55 out of 100 and a grade of B further suggest that Denver's economy is performing at an average level compared to other metropolitan areas.

These data points provide a snapshot of Denver's current economic conditions without pointing to any clear trends or definitive shifts.
