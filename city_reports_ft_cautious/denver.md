# Denver — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile, indicating a moderate level of labor market tightness. The labor force participation rate of 70.39% ranks in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.57% is solid, placing the area in the 80th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.64% and the overall wage rate growth of 2.71% are more moderate, at the 70th and 57th percentiles, respectively.

Housing and construction indicators show some softening. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 6.75%, placing it in the 42nd percentile. The year-over-year change in the housing price index is -2.07%, and the year-over-year change in the price of single-family homes is -3.36%, both in the lower percentiles (4th and 12th, respectively). The average days on market for homes is 38 days, ranking in the 88th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market.

The data suggests that the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area has a strong labor market and moderate wage growth, but also shows signs of cooling in the housing sector.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable economic environment. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile, which suggests a moderate level of labor market tightness. The labor force participation rate of 70.39% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.57% is solid, placing the area in the 80th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.64% and the overall wage rate growth of 2.71% are more moderate, at the 70th and 57th percentiles, respectively.

Housing and construction indicators show some softening. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 6.75%, placing it in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new residential construction. The year-over-year change in the housing price index is -2.07%, and the year-over-year change in the price of single-family homes is -3.36%, both in the lower percentiles (4th and 12th, respectively), suggesting a cooling in the housing market. The average days on market for homes is 38 days, which is relatively low and ranks in the 88th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in the housing market despite the price declines.

Overall, the data suggests a Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area with a strong labor market and moderate wage growth, but with signs of cooling in the housing sector.
