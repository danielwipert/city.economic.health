## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable economic environment. The unemployment rate stands at 3.9%, placing it slightly above the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, which may suggest a moderate level of labor market tightness. The labor force participation rate of 71.33% ranks notably high, in the 92nd percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is recorded at 4.35%, which is moderate and aligns with broader national trends, while the cost of living increase of 4.92% suggests that wage growth is keeping pace with inflationary pressures.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 0.64%, and the year-over-year change in single-family home sales has decreased by 3.48%. These figures, both in the lower percentiles, indicate a cooling in the housing market, which may reflect broader economic conditions or specific local factors. The number of days on market for homes is 65 days, which is also relatively low, suggesting that while sales volumes have decreased, properties are still selling within a reasonable timeframe. Construction activity, as indicated by the 13.55% year-over-year increase in building permits, remains robust, placing this indicator in the 74th percentile and suggesting continued investment in residential and commercial development.

Overall, the data suggests that Denver's economy is experiencing a period of moderate growth with some signs of cooling in the housing market.
