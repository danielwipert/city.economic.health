## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of unemployment compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate is notably high at 71.28%, ranking in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.09%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which is a positive sign for income levels in the region.

Housing indicators, however, present a different story. The year-over-year change in the housing price index is -0.72%, and the year-over-year change in the price of single-family homes is -3.57%, both ranking in the lower percentiles (12th and 8th, respectively). This suggests a decline in housing prices, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market. The number of days on the market for homes is 63 days, which is in the 18th percentile, indicating a slower pace of home sales. Construction activity, as measured by the year-over-year change in building permits, is up 13.55%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which suggests ongoing investment in new housing and commercial development.

Overall, the data indicates a balanced but not uniformly strong economic environment in Denver, with strong labor market indicators offset by weaker housing market performance.
