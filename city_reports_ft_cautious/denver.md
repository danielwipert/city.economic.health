# Denver — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labour market. The labour force participation rate of 70.39% ranks higher, at the 92nd percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is 6.57%, placing the region in the 80th percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market.

Housing market indicators show a nuanced trend. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.28%, placing Denver in the 8th percentile, indicating a cooling market compared to other regions. The year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has increased by 14.99%, ranking in the 72nd percentile, suggesting ongoing investment in housing. The days on market metric stands at 72 days, which is in the 38th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of sales.

The data suggests a balanced economic environment in Denver, with strong labour force participation and earnings growth offset by a cooling housing market.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with several indicators suggesting both strengths and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively stable labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 70.39% ranks significantly higher, at the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.57%, placing the region in the 80th percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market and potentially rising living costs.

Housing market indicators show a more nuanced trend. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.28%, placing Denver in the 8th percentile, indicating a cooling market compared to other regions. Similarly, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has increased by 14.99%, ranking in the 72nd percentile, which suggests ongoing investment in housing despite the price declines. The days on market metric stands at 72 days, which is in the 38th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of sales that neither reflects a seller's nor a buyer's market definitively.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not overly dynamic economic environment in Denver, with strong labor force participation and earnings growth offset by a cooling housing market.
