# Denver-Aurora-Centennial

**Grade: B (Average) | 54.8th percentile | April 2026**

---

Denver earns an overall grade of B, with its exceptionally low unemployment rate of 3.60% being the single most important takeaway about this city. The city's tight labor market gives workers considerable leverage, but businesses face significant hiring competition. The civilian labor force has contracted by 1.50% year-over-year, indicating a structural headwind for businesses seeking to expand their headcount.

The city's housing market is tightening, with building permits declining by 15.96% year-over-year. Homes are taking longer to sell, with days on market increasing by 8.6% year-over-year to an average of 38 days. Denver's cost of living is below average, with a composite score of 1.69. The housing market slowdown is a sign of demand erosion, particularly given the weak employment growth and declining labor force.

Denver offers businesses a unique combination of a tight labor market and a below-average cost of living, but the primary risk is the shrinking workforce and tightening housing supply. The city's economic character is defined by its exceptionally low unemployment rate and below-average cost of living, making it an attractive location for businesses. However, the declining labor force and slowing housing market pose significant challenges for companies looking to expand their operations, with the city ranking in the 54.8th percentile out of 50 US metros.
