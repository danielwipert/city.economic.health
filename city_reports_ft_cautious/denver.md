The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The labor force participation rate of 71.33% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.35% places the region in the 66th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, has risen by 4.92% year-over-year, aligning with broader national trends.

Housing market indicators present a more complex picture. This may reflect a cooling in the housing market, although the median days on market remains at 65 days, which is in the 16th percentile, suggesting a relatively stable but slower sales environment. Construction activity, as indicated by building permits, has increased by 13.55% year-over-year, placing the region in the 74th percentile, which could suggest continued investment in residential and commercial development.

Overall, the data suggests that Denver's economy is performing averagely, with strengths in labor market participation and construction activity, but facing challenges in the housing sector.
