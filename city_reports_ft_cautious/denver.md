## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 70.73% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.57% and consumer price inflation of 4.57% indicate that wage increases are outpacing inflation, potentially improving real incomes for workers.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.28%, while the supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 3.27%, and properties are taking an average of 79 days to sell. These figures, particularly the decline in home prices and the extended time on market, may reflect a cooling in the housing sector. Construction activity, as measured by building permits, has increased by 14.99% year-over-year, which could suggest continued investment in residential and commercial development despite the softer housing market conditions.

Overall, the data indicates a balanced but not overly dynamic economic environment in Denver, with strengths in employment and labor force participation offset by signs of moderation in the housing sector.
