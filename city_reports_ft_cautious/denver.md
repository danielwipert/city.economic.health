# Denver — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area's economic data shows a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 3.6% places it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 70.39% ranks in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is 6.57%, which is in the 80th percentile, indicating robust wage increases relative to other metro areas.

Housing market indicators suggest some softening. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 6.75%, placing it in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a moderation in construction activity. The home price index has declined by 2.07% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, and the price-to-rent ratio has decreased by 3.36%, in the 12th percentile. The average days on market for homes is 38 days, in the 88th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in property sales.

The data indicates that Denver's economy exhibits both areas of strength and signs of a moderating housing market.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area indicates a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, suggesting a labor market that is neither exceptionally strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 70.39% ranks in the 92nd percentile, indicating a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.57%, which is in the 80th percentile, suggesting robust wage increases relative to other metro areas.

Housing market indicators show some softening. The year-over-year change in housing permits is down by 6.75%, placing it in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect a moderation in construction activity. The home price index has declined by 2.07% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, and the price-to-rent ratio has decreased by 3.36%, in the 12th percentile, both of which suggest a cooling in the housing market. The average days on market for homes is 38 days, in the 88th percentile, indicating a relatively quick turnover in property sales.

Overall, the data suggests that while Denver's economy exhibits some areas of strength, particularly in labor force participation and earnings growth, there are also signs of a moderating housing market.
