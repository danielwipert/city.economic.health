## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 76th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of unemployment compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 71.28% is notably high, ranking in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.09% is solid, though not exceptional, placing it in the 74th percentile. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, has increased by 4.69%, which may reflect broader economic pressures affecting consumer purchasing power.

On the housing front, the year-over-year change in the housing price index is slightly negative at -0.64%, indicating a modest decline in home prices. This is in contrast to the 13.55% increase in building permits, which suggests continued investment in residential construction despite the slight cooling in home prices. The days-on-market metric stands at 63 days, which is relatively high, placing it in the 18th percentile, potentially indicating a slowdown in the pace of home sales. The year-over-year change in the price-to-square-foot ratio is down by 3.57%, further suggesting a softening in the housing market.

Overall, the data suggests that while Denver's labor market remains strong, there are signs of moderation in the housing sector.
