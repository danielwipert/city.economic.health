# Denver — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength and others suggesting areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 70.39% ranks in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a higher level of engagement in the workforce relative to other metro areas. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 6.57%, placing it in the 80th percentile, which indicates a relatively robust increase in wages.

On the housing front, the year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 2.07%, ranking in the 4th percentile, reflecting a cooling in the housing market. The year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has decreased by 6.75%, placing it in the 42nd percentile. The days on market for homes have increased to 38 days, ranking in the 88th percentile. The overall weighted percentile score of 52 for Denver-Aurora-Centennial suggests an average economic performance, with strengths in labour force participation and earnings growth offset by weaker housing market indicators.

These data points collectively suggest a regional economy with stable employment and wage growth, but facing challenges in the housing sector.
