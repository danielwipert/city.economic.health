## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 18th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is tighter than in many other regions but still above the national average. The labor force participation rate of 66.84% ranks in the 74th percentile, indicating a relatively high engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.05% is positioned in the 40th percentile, suggesting moderate wage growth that aligns with broader national trends.

Housing and construction metrics show a more subdued outlook. Building permits have declined by 17.1% year-over-year, placing the region in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in residential construction activity. The housing price index has decreased by 0.59% over the same period, ranking in the 14th percentile, indicating a slight cooling in the housing market. Additionally, the days on market metric has risen to 86 days, significantly higher than the national average and placing the region in the 2nd percentile, which could suggest a less active real estate market.

Overall, the data suggests a regional economy with stable employment and labor force participation, but facing headwinds in the housing and construction sectors.
