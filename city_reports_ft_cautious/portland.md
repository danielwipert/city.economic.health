## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 12th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively higher unemployment level. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.5% ranks in the 70th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, at 1.59%, ranking in the 24th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.68% places the region in the 22nd percentile, potentially squeezing household budgets. The housing market shows signs of cooling, with a year-over-year decline in home prices of 0.24% and a significant drop in building permits, down 20.27% compared to the previous year. Additionally, properties are taking longer to sell, with an average of 74 days on the market, which is in the 10th percentile, suggesting a less dynamic real estate sector.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 31.4 and the grade of C+ indicate that Portland's economic performance is below average when compared to other metropolitan areas, reflecting a balance of moderate strengths and notable weaknesses.
