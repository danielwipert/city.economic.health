# Portland — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate of 4.9% ranks in the 8th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 66.17% is higher, ranking in the 72nd percentile. Earnings growth year-over-year is 4.78%, placing it in the 64th percentile. However, the cost of living index is 5.21, in the 38th percentile, suggesting a modest increase in living expenses.

In the housing sector, data indicates a cooling trend. Housing prices have decreased by 0.62% year-over-year, placing the region in the 12th percentile. The number of building permits has declined by 17.1% year-over-year, in the 24th percentile. The days on market metric is 87 days, in the 6th percentile. The overall weighted percentile score is 34.3, and the grade is C+, suggesting both strengths and weaknesses in the economy.

These data points provide a snapshot of the current economic conditions in Portland, highlighting both positive and negative trends.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 8th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 66.17% is more favorable, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.78%, positioned in the 64th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases. However, the cost of living index at 5.21, in the 38th percentile, may reflect a modest increase in living expenses, potentially offsetting some of the gains in earnings.

In the housing sector, the data indicates a cooling trend. Housing prices have decreased by 0.62% year-over-year, placing the region in the 12th percentile, which could be a sign of a softening market. Similarly, the number of building permits has declined by 17.1% year-over-year, in the 24th percentile, possibly indicating reduced construction activity. The days on market metric at 87 days, in the 6th percentile, further supports the notion of a slower housing market, as properties are taking longer to sell. The overall weighted percentile score of 34.3 and a grade of C+ suggest that while some aspects of the economy are performing reasonably well, there are significant areas of weakness that warrant attention.

These data points provide a snapshot of the current economic conditions in Portland, highlighting both strengths and challenges.
