# Portland — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Portland's economic landscape presents a nuanced picture, with both short-term and long-term trends warranting scrutiny. While the labour market is not in crisis, it shows signs of deceleration. The unemployment rate has risen to 4.9%, an increase of 0.8 percentage points year-over-year, and civilian labour force growth has been modest, at 0.04% year-over-year. However, average hourly earnings have grown at a respectable 3.94% year-over-year, indicating that workers continue to experience wage gains.

In contrast, the housing market is experiencing a downturn, with residential building permits declining 18.14% year-over-year. This decrease in construction activity may be a response to the city's relatively high cost of living, as reflected in its cost of living composite score of 2.02. Although the median days on market for homes has decreased by 3.92% year-over-year, it remains at 49 days, suggesting a relatively slow sales pace. Business activity, as measured by the labour demand composite score, has been weak, with nonfarm payroll employment declining 0.69% year-over-year. The office worker ratio composite score is also low, at 1.3, indicating potential underutilization of the city's office market.

Despite these challenges, Portland's economy retains strengths, including positive earnings growth and a cost of living that, while high, is not excessively so. The key areas of concern are the slowing labour market and declining business activity. To revitalise its economy, Portland may need to focus on attracting new businesses and investments, particularly in sectors that can leverage its highly educated workforce. The short-term economic outlook is cautious, with potential for further labour market and housing sector slowing. Nevertheless, with targeted policies and investments, Portland can address its weaknesses and build on its strengths to achieve more sustainable economic growth. Ultimately, the city's policymakers must navigate the challenges ahead to restore momentum and ensure a brighter economic future.

Portland's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both short-term and longer-term trends warranting attention. The labour market, while not in crisis, shows signs of slowing. The unemployment rate, at 4.9%, has increased by 0.8 percentage points year-over-year, and the civilian labor force growth has been modest, at 0.04% year-over-year. Average hourly earnings, however, have grown at a respectable 3.94% year-over-year, suggesting that workers are still experiencing some wage gains.

The housing market in Portland is experiencing a downturn, with residential building permits declining by 18.14% year-over-year. This decrease in construction activity may be a response to the city's relatively high cost of living, as reflected in its cost of living composite score of 2.02. The median days on market for homes has decreased by 3.92% year-over-year, but still stands at 49 days, indicating a relatively slow pace of sales. Business activity, as measured by the labor demand composite score, has been weak, with nonfarm payroll employment declining by 0.69% year-over-year. The office worker ratio composite score is also low, at 1.3, suggesting that the city's office market may be underutilized.

Despite these challenges, Portland's economy is not without strengths. The city's earnings growth, while not spectacular, is still positive, and the cost of living, while high, is not excessively so. The key stress points for the city's economy are the slowing labour market and the decline in business activity. To revitalize its economy, Portland may need to focus on attracting new businesses and investments, particularly in sectors that can take advantage of its highly educated workforce. In the short term, the city's economic outlook is cautious, with the potential for further slowing in the labour market and housing sector. However, with targeted policies and investments, Portland can work to address its weaknesses and build on its strengths to achieve more sustainable economic growth. Closing outlook: Portland's economy is at a crossroads, and the city's policymakers must navigate the challenges ahead to restore momentum and ensure a brighter economic future.
