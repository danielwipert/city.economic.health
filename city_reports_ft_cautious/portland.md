# Portland — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing moderate strength and others suggesting areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 8th percentile among comparable regions, which may indicate a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 66.17% is more robust, ranking in the 72nd percentile, suggesting a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.78%, situated in the 64th percentile, indicating modest wage increases that keep pace with inflation, which is reported at 5.21%.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing permits has declined by 16.6%, placing it in the 30th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in construction activity. The home price index has decreased by 1.1% over the same period, ranking in the 12th percentile, potentially signaling a cooling in the real estate market. The days on market for properties have increased to 87 days, in the 6th percentile, which may reflect a softer demand for housing. The overall weighted percentile score of 34.9 and a grade of C+ suggest that Portland's economy exhibits some resilience but also faces challenges.
