## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 12th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is less robust than many peers. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.5% ranks in the 70th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.05% is moderate, sitting in the 40th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.36% is slightly above average, at the 24th percentile. These figures suggest that wage growth may not be keeping pace with the rising cost of living, potentially affecting household financial stability.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 0.59%, placing the region in the 14th percentile, which indicates a cooling trend in property values. Similarly, the number of building permits has decreased by 20.27% year-over-year, ranking in the 28th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in construction activity. The median days on market for homes has increased to 74 days, in the 10th percentile, which may reflect a softer demand for housing. Additionally, the year-over-year change in rental prices has fallen by 1.8%, in the 32nd percentile, further indicating a less dynamic real estate sector.

Overall, the data suggests a metropolitan area experiencing moderate economic conditions with signs of softening in both the labor and housing markets.
