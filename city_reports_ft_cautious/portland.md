## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, placing it in the 12th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively higher unemployment level. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.5% is more favorable, ranking in the 70th percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, at 1.59%, placing the region in the 24th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.68% is slightly above the national average, ranking in the 22nd percentile. This disparity may reflect challenges in maintaining purchasing power for local residents. Housing market indicators show a decline in both home prices and permits, with home prices down 0.59% year-over-year and building permits decreasing by 20.27% compared to the previous year. These trends, coupled with an increase in days on market to 74 days, suggest a cooling in the housing sector.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 30.8 and the grade of C+ indicate that while Portland's economy exhibits some resilience, particularly in labor force participation, it faces significant challenges in employment, earnings growth, and housing market dynamics.
