# Portland — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 4.9%, placing it in the 8th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 66.17% ranks in the 72nd percentile, suggesting a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is 4.78%, situating the region in the 64th percentile, which may reflect moderate wage pressures.

In contrast, housing and construction metrics show a different trend. Building permits have declined by 16.6% year-over-year, placing the region in the 30th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in new residential construction. The housing price index has decreased by 1.1% over the same period, also in the 12th percentile, which could indicate a cooling in the real estate market. The days on market metric stands at 68 days, in the 22nd percentile, potentially signalling a less dynamic housing market compared to other regions.

The data suggests that while the labor market in Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro remains relatively strong, there are signs of moderation in the housing sector.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others point to areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.9%, placing it in the 8th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.17% is more robust, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.78%, situating the region in the 64th percentile, which may reflect moderate wage pressures.

Housing and construction metrics present a different narrative. Building permits have declined by 16.6% year-over-year, placing the region in the 30th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in new residential construction. The housing price index has decreased by 1.1% over the same period, also in the 12th percentile, which could indicate a cooling in the real estate market. Additionally, the days on market metric stands at 68 days, in the 22nd percentile, potentially signaling a less dynamic housing market compared to other regions.

Overall, the data suggests that while the labor market in Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro remains relatively strong, there are signs of moderation in the housing sector.
