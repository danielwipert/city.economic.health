The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others indicate areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 18th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a labor market that is performing below average in terms of job availability. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.84% ranks in the 74th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.05%, situated in the 40th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases.

Housing and construction metrics present a more challenging outlook. Additionally, the cost of living index is at 5.33% above the national average, in the 26th percentile, and weekly hours worked stand at 34.6 hours, in the 58th percentile, both of which suggest moderate pressures on household budgets and work schedules.

Overall, the data indicates a regional economy with strengths in labor force participation but facing challenges in housing and construction activity.
