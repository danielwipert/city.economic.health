## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Portland-Vancouver-Hillsboro metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others indicate areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 5.3%, placing it in the 10th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively higher unemployment level compared to other metropolitan areas. However, the labor force participation rate of 66.84% is more robust, ranking in the 74th percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been modest, at 1.43%, placing the region in the 14th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases. The cost of living index, at 5.66, is slightly above the national average, ranking in the 18th percentile. Housing market indicators show a decline in both home prices and permits issued, with home prices falling by 0.24% year-over-year and building permits down by 20.27% year-over-year, suggesting a cooling in the housing sector. The median days on market for homes is 71 days, which is relatively high and may indicate a less active real estate market.

Overall, the data suggests that while Portland's labor market engagement remains strong, there are signs of economic softness in terms of employment, earnings growth, and the housing market.
