# New York — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The New York-Newark-Jersey City metropolitan area's economic data presents a mixed picture, with some indicators outperforming national trends while others trail others. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 20th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is below average compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 63.3% is similarly positioned in the 36th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of workforce engagement.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.84%, ranking in the 66th percentile, which may reflect a relatively robust income environment for those employed. However, the cost of living index is 6.87, in the 16th percentile, suggesting that living expenses remain high relative to other areas, potentially offsetting some of the gains in earnings. Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 5.14%, in the 40th percentile, and a modest rise in building permits of 4.77%, in the 56th percentile, both of which indicate a stable but not particularly dynamic housing sector.

The overall weighted score of 35 and grade of C+ for the New York-Newark-Jersey City metro area suggest an economy that is performing moderately, with no clear signs of either significant distress or exceptional strength.
