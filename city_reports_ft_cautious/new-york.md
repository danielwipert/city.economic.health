The economic data for the New York-Newark-Jersey City metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 20th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but not one that outperforms most other regions. The labor force participation rate of 63.3% is also below the national median, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.84% is above the national average, as reflected in its 66th percentile ranking, suggesting some improvement in income levels for those employed.

Housing market indicators show both strengths and weaknesses. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 5.07% is slightly above the national median, while the decline in building permits of -6.39% may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The rental market appears stable, with a modest decrease in the price-to-square-foot ratio of -2.44%. Properties are taking an average of 76 days to sell, which is longer than the national median, possibly indicating a less dynamic housing market. The overall weighted percentile score of 32.2 places the region in the lower third of all metropolitan areas, earning it a grade of C+, described as "Poor" according to the grading scale.

These data points suggest a cautiously balanced economic environment in the New York-Newark-Jersey City area, with some positive trends in employment and earnings offset by challenges in labor force participation and housing market dynamics.
