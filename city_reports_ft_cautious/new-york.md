# New York — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The New York-Newark-Jersey City metropolitan area, centred around New York City, displays a mixed economic profile based on recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 20th percentile nationally. This suggests a relatively tight labour market, but one that lags behind some other regions. The labour force participation rate of 63.3% is below the national median, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.84% places the region in the 66th percentile, suggesting a moderate improvement in wages. The cost of living increase of 6.57% is in the lower quartile, potentially affecting household budgets.

Housing and construction indicators show a nuanced picture. The year-over-year increase in housing permits of 4.77% and a home price index growth of 5.14% both fall within the middle range of national performance, indicating steady growth in the housing sector. However, the year-over-year decline in permits for single-family homes by 2.44% and an average of 76 days on the market for properties suggest a cooling in the single-family housing market. These figures may reflect shifts in demand or supply dynamics within the housing sector.

The New York-Newark-Jersey City metro area's economic indicators suggest a mixed performance, with moderate growth in some areas and potential challenges in others.
