# New York — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The New York-Newark-Jersey City metropolitan area, centred around New York City, presents a mixed economic picture based on recent data. The unemployment rate of 4.5% places it in the 20th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labour market. The labour force participation rate of 63.3% is in the 36th percentile, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.84% is in the 66th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 6.57% is in the 18th percentile.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year change in housing prices is 5.07%, placing it in the 42nd percentile, indicating moderate appreciation. Building permits have declined by 6.39% year-over-year, in the 44th percentile, suggesting a slowdown in construction activity. Property sales frequency has decreased by 2.44% year-over-year, in the 20th percentile, and the average days on market is 76 days, in the 24th percentile. Weekly hours worked average 32.9 hours, in the 4th percentile.

The New York-Newark-Jersey City metro area's economic indicators suggest a balanced but not robust economic environment, with some sectors showing signs of moderation.
