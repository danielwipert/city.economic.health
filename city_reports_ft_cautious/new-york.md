**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 48.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

New York's economy earns a below-average overall grade of 48.0th percentile, with a 4.50% unemployment rate indicating labor market slack, making it easier for businesses to hire. However, the city's +0.48% YoY nonfarm employment growth and +4.84% YoY wage growth suggest a healthy expansion and positive consumer spending outlook.

Despite these positives, New York's cost of living composite score of 5.01 places it in the bottom tier, making it one of the most expensive metros. This high cost of living is a significant constraint on talent acquisition and real consumer spending power. The labor force participation rate of 63.30% is below average, suggesting a shallow labor pool.

New York offers businesses a unique combination of a growing economy and a highly skilled workforce, but at a high cost. The primary risk for businesses is the city's extremely high cost of living, which may limit their ability to attract and retain top talent, and ultimately impact their bottom line.
