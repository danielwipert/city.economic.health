The economic data for the New York-Newark-Jersey City metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, placing it in the 18th percentile nationally, which may reflect a labor market that is neither particularly robust nor distressed. Labor force participation is at 63.63%, ranking in the 36th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 7.0%, a figure that ranks in the 72nd percentile, suggesting a relatively healthy increase in income levels. However, the cost of living index at 6.27 places the region in the 12th percentile, indicating that living expenses remain high compared to other areas.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices is 4.97%, which is in the 42nd percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. The supply of for-sale homes has declined by 2.88% over the same period, placing the region in the 12th percentile, potentially reflecting tight inventory conditions. Properties are taking an average of 71 days to sell, a figure in the 28th percentile, which suggests a relatively balanced market with neither excessive buyer nor seller pressure.

Overall, the New York-Newark-Jersey City metro area's economic performance, as indicated by these metrics, appears to be characterized by moderate growth and stability, with some indicators pointing to challenges in affordability and construction activity.
