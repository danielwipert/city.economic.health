# Pittsburgh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Pittsburgh's economic data presents a mixed picture, with various indicators pointing to both strengths and potential challenges. The city's unemployment rate of 3.6% is in line with national averages, placing it at the 50th percentile among metropolitan areas. Labour force participation, however, is relatively low at 60.33%, ranking in the 10th percentile, which may be influenced by broader demographic or regional labour market factors.

Earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 8.65%, significantly outpacing many other regions and placing Pittsburgh in the 96th percentile. This strong earnings growth is accompanied by moderate consumer price inflation at 4.17% and a slightly higher owner-occupied housing cost increase of 3.38%.

The housing market in Pittsburgh exhibits a complex pattern. Home prices have increased by 17.94% year-over-year, a significant rise that places the city in the 80th percentile. However, this growth is tempered by a decline in building permits, down 13.83% compared to the previous year, suggesting a slowdown in new residential construction. The days-on-market metric, at 85 days, is notably high, ranking in the 8th percentile, which could indicate a less dynamic housing market or a shift in buyer behaviour. The rental market appears stable, with a modest 3.76% increase in rents year-over-year, placing the city in the 86th percentile.

In summary, Pittsburgh's economic indicators suggest a region with strong earnings growth and a robust housing market, but also facing challenges in labour force participation and new construction activity.
