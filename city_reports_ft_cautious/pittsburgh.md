# Pittsburgh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Pittsburgh's economic data presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 3.6%, placing it at the median among metropolitan areas. This suggests a stable labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 60.33% is lower than many other areas, ranking in the bottom 10th percentile. This may indicate underutilization of the workforce.

Earnings growth year-over-year is 8.65%, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating strong income gains for those employed. Housing market trends are also mixed. The home price index has increased by 15.05% year-over-year, placing Pittsburgh in the 76th percentile. However, building permits have declined by 18.72% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile. The days on market metric stands at 85 days, ranking in the 8th percentile, indicating a slower pace of sales.

Pittsburgh's economic performance appears to be average, with some strengths and challenges. Earnings growth and housing prices are relatively strong, but labour force participation and new construction activity are weaker.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Pittsburgh's economic landscape, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture with some positive indicators and others that suggest caution. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the median when compared to other metropolitan areas, which may indicate a stable labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.33% is notably lower, ranking in the bottom 10th percentile, suggesting a potential underutilization of the workforce. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 8.65%, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating strong income gains for those employed.

Housing market dynamics show contrasting trends. The home price index has increased by 15.05% year-over-year, placing Pittsburgh in the 76th percentile, which suggests a relatively strong housing market. However, building permits have declined by 18.72% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. Additionally, the days on market metric stands at 85 days, ranking in the 8th percentile, indicating a slower pace of sales compared to other markets.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic performance appears to be average, with strengths in earnings growth and housing prices, but challenges in labor force participation and new construction.
