# Pittsburgh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Pittsburgh's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly robust nor weak compared to other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 60.33% is lower, ranking in the 10th percentile, suggesting a relatively low share of the working-age population engaged in the labour market. 

On the positive side, earnings growth year-over-year is 8.65%, placing Pittsburgh in the 96th percentile, which may reflect a competitive job market or rising productivity. Housing market dynamics show contrasting trends. The year-over-year increase in home prices is 17.94%, placing the city in the 80th percentile, suggesting a strong demand for housing. However, the number of building permits has declined by 13.83% year-over-year, ranking in the 32nd percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction or a shift in development priorities. The days on market for properties have increased to 90 days, the lowest percentile score, which may reflect a more balanced or buyer-friendly market.

Pittsburgh's economic performance appears to be average, with earnings growth and housing appreciation offset by lower labour force participation and a decline in building permits.
