# Pittsburgh

**Grade: B+ (Above Average) | 57.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Pittsburgh earns an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking at the 57.9th percentile among 50 US metros. **Labor Demand**

This combination signals a near-median labor demand, with neither strong expansion nor contraction. The labor demand composite score of 4.54 also falls near the median, at the 51st percentile.

**Unemployment**
The city's unemployment rate is 3.60%, ranking near the median at the 55th percentile. This indicates a relatively balanced labor market, neither too tight nor too slack. For a business trying to hire in Pittsburgh, this means a moderate level of competition for talent, with some wage pressure but not extreme.

**Wage Growth**
With a year-over-year wage growth rate of 7.00%, Pittsburgh is experiencing strong wage growth, ranking at the 87th percentile. This signals rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing worker purchasing power. Businesses should factor in higher labor costs, but also consider the benefits of a workforce with growing disposable income.

**Cost of Living**
Pittsburgh's cost of living is relatively affordable, with a price-to-wage ratio of 1.42, ranking at the 78th percentile. This means the city offers a talent attraction advantage, as workers can maintain a high standard of living without requiring excessive wage premiums. Businesses can leverage this affordability to attract and retain talent.

The city's civilian labor force is growing at a rate of 0.73% year-over-year, indicating a slowly expanding workforce supply. While this growth is near the median, at the 49th percentile, it suggests a moderate level of hiring capacity, with some room for business expansion.

The current median days on market in Pittsburgh is 65 days, with a year-over-year increase of 4.8%. This ranks below average, at the 20th percentile, indicating a relatively fast-paced market. For workers relocating to the city, this means a competitive housing market, where homes sell quickly, and buyers must be prepared to act fast.

**Office Economy**
Pittsburgh's professional talent pool is relatively deep, with an office economy ranking at the 62nd percentile. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require specialized knowledge workers. However, it may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented workforces.

In conclusion, Pittsburgh offers businesses a growing economy with strong wage growth and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the tightening housing supply, signaled by the decline in building permits, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation.
