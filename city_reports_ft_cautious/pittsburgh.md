## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Pittsburgh's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing it in the 24th percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a labor market that is performing below average compared to its peers. The labor force participation rate of 60.7% further indicates a moderate level of engagement in the workforce, ranking in the 20th percentile. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 4.92%, placing Pittsburgh in the 60th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening job market or rising wages.

Housing indicators show a complex dynamic. The housing price index has increased by 15.05% year-over-year, a significant rise that places Pittsburgh in the 76th percentile, suggesting a strong housing market. However, building permits have declined by 24.33% over the same period, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction or a shift in housing demand. The pace of sales, as measured by days on market, is 53 days, a figure that is near the median, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a clear advantage.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic performance appears to be characterized by moderate growth in key areas, with notable strengths in earnings and housing prices, but challenges in employment and construction activity.
