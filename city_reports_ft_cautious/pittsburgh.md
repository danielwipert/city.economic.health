## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Pittsburgh's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing the city in the 24th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but one that lags behind many other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 60.7% is similarly positioned in the 20th percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 4.92%, ranking in the 60th percentile, suggesting some improvement in income levels for those employed.

Housing market dynamics show contrasting trends. The year-over-year increase in home prices is significant at 16.85%, placing Pittsburgh in the 78th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. However, the decline in building permits by 24.33% year-over-year, situated in the 20th percentile, indicates a slowdown in new construction activity. Additionally, the cost of living has increased by 4.34%, ranking in the 80th percentile, and the overall wage replacement ratio stands at 3.38%, also in the 80th percentile, suggesting that while incomes are growing, so too are living costs. The average number of days on the market for homes is 53, in the 52nd percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic performance, as reflected in these indicators, appears to be below average, with strengths in earnings growth and housing appreciation offset by challenges in employment and cost of living.
