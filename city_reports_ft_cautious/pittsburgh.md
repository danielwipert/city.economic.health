## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Pittsburgh's economic landscape, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing it in the 24th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but one that lags behind many other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 60.7% is also below the national median, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. On a more positive note, earnings growth year-over-year is robust at 4.92%, ranking in the 60th percentile, which may reflect a strengthening job market with better compensation for workers. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.34%, placing it in the 80th percentile, suggesting that living costs are rising faster than in most other regions.

Housing dynamics in Pittsburgh show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 15.05% year-over-year, a significant rise that places the city in the 76th percentile, indicating a strong housing market. However, building permits have declined by 24.33% over the same period, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction or a shift in development priorities. The days on market metric, at 53 days, is near the median, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic indicators suggest a region experiencing moderate growth with some areas of strength and others of concern.
