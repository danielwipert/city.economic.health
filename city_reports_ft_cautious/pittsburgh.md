# Pittsburgh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Pittsburgh's economic indicators display a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of relative strength and challenges. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, in line with national averages, suggesting a stable labour market. However, the labour force participation rate of 60.33% is lower than in many other metropolitan areas, indicating potential underutilization of the workforce. Earnings growth year-over-year is 8.65%, placing Pittsburgh in the 96th percentile among similar regions, suggesting significant wage increases for those employed.

Housing market dynamics show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 17.94% year-over-year, a strong figure that places Pittsburgh in the 80th percentile. This growth may be influenced by low inventory and high demand. Conversely, building permits have declined by 13.83% over the same period, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 90 days, the lowest percentile score, suggesting a less dynamic housing market compared to other regions.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic performance appears to be average, with some strengths in employment earnings and housing prices, but challenges in labour force participation and housing market liquidity.
