## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Pittsburgh's economic landscape, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture with several indicators showing both strengths and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 60.61% is in the 14th percentile, indicating a lower engagement of the working-age population in the labor market compared to other areas. Earnings growth year-over-year is strong at 8.65%, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may reflect a tightening labor market or shifts in industry composition. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.17%, slightly above the national average, and the overall wage growth appears to be outpacing inflation, potentially benefiting workers.

Housing market dynamics show a complex trend. The housing price index has increased by 15.05% year-over-year, placing Pittsburgh in the 76th percentile, suggesting a strong demand for housing. However, the number of building permits has declined by 18.72% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction or a shift in housing market priorities. The days on market metric stands at 71 days, in the 28th percentile, indicating a moderate pace of home sales, neither particularly fast nor slow.

Overall, Pittsburgh's economic performance is average, with strengths in earnings growth and housing prices, but challenges in labor force participation and new construction activity.
