# Columbus

**Grade: A- (Good) | 60.0th percentile | April 2026**

---

Columbus earns an A- grade, driven by its strong economic momentum, with nonfarm employment growth of +1.17% YoY, ranking in the 84th percentile, and a deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 90th percentile for office and professional worker share. The city's labor force participation rate of 65.61% indicates a readily available workforce.

Wage growth, at +0.86% YoY, ranks in the 20th percentile, suggesting weak worker bargaining power or softening demand. The housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 10.8% YoY to an average of 41 days, a relatively fast market. This slowdown may be a healthy normalization, given the strong employment growth and above-average building permits, up 8.67% YoY.

Columbus offers businesses a dynamic and expanding market with access to a deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses. However, the primary risk is the potential for labor market tightening, which could lead to increased competition for workers and upward pressure on wages, potentially eroding the current cost advantage.
