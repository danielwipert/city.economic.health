## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Columbus suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.94% is also above average, ranking in the 68th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.91%, placing it in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a more stable but less dynamic wage environment.

Housing indicators show a strong market, with a home price index increase of 16.76% year-over-year, ranking in the 80th percentile, indicating significant appreciation in property values. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units is more moderate at 4.74%, in the 66th percentile, which could suggest a balanced approach to new construction. The days on market metric stands at 61.0 days, in the 60th percentile, indicating a reasonably active but not overheated real estate market. The overall weighted score of 61 and a grade of B+ further suggest that Columbus's economy is performing above average, though not exceptionally so.

These data points collectively indicate a stable and moderately growing economy in Columbus, with strengths in employment and housing markets.
