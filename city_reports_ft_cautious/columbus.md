# Columbus

**Grade: A (Very Good) | 63.6th percentile | May 2026**

---

Columbus, with an overall grade of A and a composite score ranking at the 63.6th percentile, is characterized by its low unemployment rate of 4.10% and a labor demand composite score of 4.24, which is near the median at the 59th percentile. The city's economic character is also defined by its moderate cost of living, with a PSF to wages ratio of 6.12, and its above-average labor force growth rate of 1.69% YoY. These metrics suggest a city with a relatively stable labor market and moderate growth prospects.

**Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate in Columbus is -0.04% YoY, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -1.374%, indicating a contraction in labor demand. This combination signals a slight contraction in the labor market, rather than genuine demand expansion or survivor squeeze. The labor demand composite score of 4.24, near the median, further supports this assessment.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Columbus is 4.10%, ranking at the 89th percentile, indicating a tight labor market with low unemployment. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the market has limited slack, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests that workers have more bargaining power.

**Wage Growth**
The year-over-year wage growth rate in Columbus is +2.51%, which is below average, ranking at the 30th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate implies that labor costs for employers are rising, but at a slower pace, and worker purchasing power is increasing, albeit not rapidly. The relatively slow wage growth may help keep labor costs in check for businesses.

**Cost of Living**
Columbus has a cost of living score ranking at the 52nd percentile, with a PSF to wages ratio of 6.12, and a PSF of $206/sqft that is decreasing by 1.4% YoY. This suggests that the city is moderately affordable relative to its peers. The moderate affordability means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset the cost of living.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in Columbus is growing at a rate of 1.69% YoY, which is above average, ranking at the 77th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, providing a positive implication for hiring capacity, as businesses can tap into a growing pool of potential workers.

**Building Permits**
The number of residential building permits in Columbus is increasing by 8.67% YoY, which is above average, ranking at the 64th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is expanding, which may improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce in the future. The increasing permits signal a positive outlook for future workforce accommodation and affordability.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market in Columbus is 36 days, with a year-over-year increase of 16.1%, ranking at the 96th percentile. This indicates a slower market, where homes are sitting longer before being sold. For workers relocating to this city, the slower market may make it easier to find a home, as there is less competition and more time to make a decision.

**Office Economy**
Columbus has an office economy score ranking at the 46th percentile, with a share of professional and office workers of 2.46. This suggests that the city has a moderate depth of professional talent pool, making it suited for businesses that require a mix of office and non-office workers. The city may be less suited for businesses that require a highly specialized or deep knowledge-economy talent pool.

In conclusion, Columbus offers businesses a relatively stable labor market, moderate growth prospects, and a moderately affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in Columbus is the tight labor market, which may lead to challenges in hiring and upward pressure on wages.
