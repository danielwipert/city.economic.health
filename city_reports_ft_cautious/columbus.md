The economic data for Columbus suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.94% is also above average, ranking in the 68th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 2.91%, placing it in the 24th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases relative to other regions.

In the housing market, the year-over-year increase in home prices is robust at 16.76%, ranking in the 80th percentile, indicating a strong appreciation in property values. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units is more moderate at 2.83%, in the 52nd percentile, which could suggest a balanced approach to new construction. The median number of days on the market for homes is 61 days, in the 60th percentile, indicating a reasonably active but not overheated market. The cost of living index is 5.12, in the 36th percentile, and the overall well-being rank is 4.24, in the 94th percentile, both of which suggest that Columbus remains relatively affordable while offering a high quality of life.

Overall, the data indicates that Columbus maintains a balanced economic profile with strengths in employment and housing markets, though wage growth is more subdued.
