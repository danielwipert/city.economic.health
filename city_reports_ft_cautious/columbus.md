# Columbus

**Grade: A- (Good) | 59.8th percentile | April 2026**

---

Columbus earns an A- grade, driven by its strong economic momentum, with nonfarm employment growth of +1.17% YoY, ranking in the 84th percentile, and a deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 90th percentile for office and professional worker share. This combination positions the city as an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses and headquarters.

However, wage growth, at +0.86% YoY, lags, ranking in the 20th percentile, indicating weak worker bargaining power or softening demand. The housing market is also slowing, with days on market increasing by 10.8% YoY to an average of 41 days, a relatively fast market. This slowdown can be seen as a stabilizing force, given the strong employment growth and above-average building permits, suggesting that supply is finally catching up to demand.

Columbus offers businesses a dynamic and expanding market with access to a deep professional talent pool, making it an ideal location for knowledge-economy companies. However, the primary risk lies in the sluggish wage growth, which may signal underlying weaknesses in the labor market and potentially impact the city's long-term economic sustainability.
