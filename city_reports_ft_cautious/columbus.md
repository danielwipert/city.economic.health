## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for Columbus suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile, which indicates a relatively low level of unemployment compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.94% is also above average, ranking in the 68th percentile, suggesting a robust engagement of the workforce. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 0.86%, placing it in the 20th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases relative to other regions.

Housing market indicators show a more dynamic picture. The housing price index has increased by 16.76% year-over-year, placing it in the 80th percentile, indicating a strong appreciation in home values. In contrast, the number of building permits issued has grown by only 2.83% year-over-year, ranking in the 52nd percentile, which might suggest a moderate expansion in construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 61.0 days, in the 60th percentile, suggesting that properties are selling at a moderate pace, neither exceptionally fast nor slow.

Overall, the data for Columbus indicates a balanced economic environment with strengths in employment and housing prices, but more subdued performance in earnings growth and construction activity.
