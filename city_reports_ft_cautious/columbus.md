# Columbus

**Grade: B (Average) | 50.1th percentile | April 2026**

---

Columbus earns an overall grade of B, with its low unemployment rate of 3.60% being the single most important takeaway about this city. The city's labor force growth is 0.35% YoY and nonfarm employment growth is 0.29% YoY, indicating a constructive but constrained market. The cost of living is affordable, with a composite score of 1.85.

The days on market for homes has increased by 10.8% YoY, with an average of 41 days, placing it in the top tier. This indicates a slowing housing market, likely due to softening demand rather than a healthy normalization, given the weak employment growth. Columbus has an above-average office and professional worker share, ranking in the 74th percentile.

Columbus offers a business an affordable cost of living and a skilled workforce, but the primary risk lies in its slowing economic growth and potential demand erosion. The city's sluggish job growth and increasing days on market for homes signal a cautionary note for businesses considering location decisions, as they may face challenges in attracting and retaining talent in a stagnant labor market.
