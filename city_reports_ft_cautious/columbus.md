## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Columbus's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and moderation. The unemployment rate stands at 4.7%, placing it in the 24th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively tight labor market but not one that is exceptionally robust. The labor force participation rate of 66.13% ranks in the 70th percentile, indicating a higher level of engagement in the workforce compared to many other regions. Year-over-year earnings growth of 3.89% is situated in the 46th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases that align with broader national trends.

In the housing sector, Columbus has experienced a 16.76% year-over-year increase in home prices, placing it in the 80th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply conditions. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction is only 1.32%, near the median, indicating a more stable pace of new housing development. The days-on-market metric stands at 47 days, in the 60th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where homes are selling at a steady pace but not at an accelerated rate. The weekly hours worked average of 33.8 hours is in the 34th percentile, indicating a moderate level of work intensity.

Overall, Columbus's economic performance appears to be average, with some indicators showing relative strength and others indicating more moderate conditions.
