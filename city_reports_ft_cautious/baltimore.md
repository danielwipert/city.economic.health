The Baltimore-Columbia-Towson metropolitan area ranks 22nd in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 44.8, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.3%, which is slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 64.9%, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. The median days on market for homes is 45 days, a figure that suggests a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data indicates a region with stable employment and housing markets, though with some signs of moderation in construction activity.
