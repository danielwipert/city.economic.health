# Baltimore-Columbia-Towson

**Grade: C- (Critical) | 30.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Baltimore earns a bottom-tier grade due to its significant structural headwinds, with nonfarm employment growth contracting by -1.46% YoY and a shrinking civilian labor force, down 0.62% YoY. The city's unemployment rate is 3.60%, elevated compared to its peers, indicating labor market slack. This combination of factors defines Baltimore as a market with weakened local consumer demand and limited opportunities for business growth.

The housing market is slowing, with homes taking 37 days to sell on average, but with a significant increase of 27.6% YoY, and declining building permits, down 11.14% YoY, indicating demand erosion. The cost of living is above average at a score of 1.86, but this is not enough to offset the labor market challenges. The professional talent pool ranks in the 24th percentile, posing constraints for specialized or senior roles.

Baltimore's struggling labor market, characterized by job losses and a shrinking workforce, poses significant challenges for businesses seeking to grow and expand in the city. The city's cost advantage due to its low cost of living is not enough to offset these challenges, making it a less attractive location for investment and development. With a thinner professional talent pool and weakened local consumer demand, businesses may find it difficult to thrive in this market.
