# Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 46.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Tampa's economic character is defined by its labor market weaknesses, with a 4.60% unemployment rate and a critically low labor force participation rate of 59.95%, making it a challenging location for businesses. However, the city's above-average nonfarm employment growth of +0.92% YoY and solid wage growth of +4.86% YoY suggest a healthy expansion, which could help mitigate some of the labor market concerns.

Despite these challenges, Tampa's cost of living composite score of 1.87 indicates a below-average cost of living, making it an attractive location for businesses and residents alike. The housing market, however, is slowing, with days on market increasing by 21.2% YoY to an average of 80 days, signaling a decline in the market.

In summary, Tampa offers businesses an affordable cost of living and a growing economy, but the primary risk is the city's labor market weaknesses, which could constrain business expansion and relocation plans. The slowing housing market and low labor force participation rate are key caveats that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating Tampa as a potential business location.
