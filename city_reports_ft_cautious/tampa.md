# Tampa — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area ranks 17th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 41.8, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate is 4.6%, slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 59.95%, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.86%, a positive trend, but one that remains within a moderate range compared to other metropolitan areas.

Housing market indicators show a mixed picture. Building permits have increased by 71.38% year-over-year, a notable rise that may reflect ongoing demand for new housing or commercial developments. However, the home price index has declined by 3.84% over the same period, which could indicate a cooling in the housing market or a correction from previous gains. The days on market metric is 85.0 days, a relatively high figure suggesting a slower pace of sales. Rental prices have seen a modest increase of 0.41% year-over-year, indicating stable but not robust growth in the rental sector.

The data suggests a region with moderate economic activity, featuring some areas of strength and others showing signs of softening.
