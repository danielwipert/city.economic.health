# Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 43.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Tampa earns a C+ grade, with its high unemployment rate of 4.60% being the most critical concern. This reflects a labor market characterized by severe weakness and potential consumer spending risk. The area's nonfarm employment growth is below average, with a -0.22% year-over-year change, indicating economic deceleration.

The cost of living composite ranks in the 84th percentile, making Tampa one of the most affordable metros. However, the housing market tells a different story, with days on market increasing by 15.8% year-over-year and homes taking an average of 66 days to sell, indicating a slowing market. This slowdown, combined with a below-average office and professional worker share, ranking in the 32th percentile, poses constraints for specialized or senior roles.

Tampa offers strong wage growth, with an 8.81% year-over-year increase, but decision-makers should weigh the benefits of affordability against the challenges of a slowing economy and constrained professional talent pool. The labor market's weakness and potential demand erosion in the housing market are primary risks that require careful consideration.
