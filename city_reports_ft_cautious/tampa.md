# Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 43.3th percentile | April 2026**

---

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 43.3th percentile out of 50 US metros. **Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 4.60%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 6th percentile. This indicates a very tight labor market with little slack, making it harder for businesses to hire and potentially leading to upward wage pressure. Companies looking to staff up in this city may face significant challenges in finding available talent.

This fast wage growth signals rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing purchasing power for workers. Businesses operating in this city should be prepared for higher labor costs and potentially increased consumer demand.

This means the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers, making it easier for businesses to attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums. This indicates a slower market, ranking in the bottom tier at the 10th percentile. For workers relocating to this city, the slower market may make it easier to find a home, but it also suggests a less competitive and less dynamic housing market.

**Office Economy**
The share of professional and office workers in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 1.94, ranking below average at the 34th percentile. This suggests a relatively shallow talent pool for businesses requiring specialized knowledge-economy workers, making the city less suited for tech, finance, or consulting headquarters. The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area offers businesses a unique combination of high wage growth and relatively affordable cost of living, but the tight labor market and slowing housing supply growth pose significant risks. The single biggest constraint for decision-makers to factor in is the challenging hiring environment, which may require creative talent attraction and retention strategies to overcome.
