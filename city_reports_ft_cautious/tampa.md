## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area ranks 17th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 41.6, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, which is relatively low but places the region in the 16th percentile nationally, suggesting a labor market that is performing modestly compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 59.95% is also below average, ranking in the 6th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is actively engaged in the labor market.

Earnings growth over the past year has been positive at 4.86%, placing the region in the 68th percentile, which suggests a moderate improvement in income levels. However, the cost of living increase of 5.25% may be outpacing earnings growth, potentially squeezing household budgets. Housing market indicators show mixed results: while building permits have increased by 66.26% year-over-year, placing the region in the 92nd percentile, the home price index has declined by 2.83% over the same period, ranking in the 2nd percentile. This combination suggests a robust construction sector but a softening in home prices. The median days on market for homes is 85 days, which is relatively high and places the region in the 8th percentile, indicating a slower pace of sales.

Overall, the data indicates a mixed economic performance in Tampa, with strengths in employment growth and construction activity, but challenges in labor force engagement and housing market dynamics.
