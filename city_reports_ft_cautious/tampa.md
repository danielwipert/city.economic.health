# Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater

**Grade: B (Average) | 50.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Tampa's economic character is defined by its above-average job growth, with nonfarm employment increasing by 0.92% year-over-year, and solid wage growth, with hourly earnings rising by 6.07% year-over-year, despite a high unemployment rate of 4.60%. This suggests that while the city is creating new jobs, it still struggles with putting its workforce to full use.

The city's cost of living is a notable advantage, with a composite score of 1.74 indicating a below-average cost of living. This, combined with a deep professional talent pool, as evidenced by its 84th percentile ranking for office and professional worker share, makes Tampa an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses. The housing market, however, tells a conflicting story, with days on market increasing by 15.8% year-over-year to 66 days, indicating a slowing market.

Tampa offers businesses a unique combination of affordable living, a talented workforce, and growing job market. However, the primary risk is the city's labor market weakness, which could pose a challenge for businesses looking to tap into the local workforce.
