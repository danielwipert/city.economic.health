# Tampa — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area has a weighted percentile score of 36.1 in a recent economic assessment, corresponding to a grade of C+, described as "Poor". The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, placing it in the 16th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 59.95% is in the 6th percentile, suggesting a lower level of engagement in the workforce compared to other regions.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.86%, ranking in the 68th percentile, indicating modest income gains for workers in the Tampa area. The cost of living index is 5.1, placing it in the 42nd percentile, which may reflect moderate living costs relative to other metropolitan areas. Housing market indicators show a decline, with housing prices falling by 2.83% year-over-year and building permits down by 8.56%, both in the lower percentiles. The average days on market for properties is 80 days, in the 6th percentile, suggesting a slower pace of sales.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Tampa, with some positive signs in employment and earnings, but challenges in labor force participation and the housing market.
