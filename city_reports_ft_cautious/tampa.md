# Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 46.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Tampa's economic character is defined by its severe labor market weaknesses, with an unemployment rate of 4.60% and a critically low labor force participation rate of 59.95%, making it a challenging location for businesses and individuals. However, the city's cost of living composite score of 1.83 indicates a below-average cost of living, making it an attractive option for those seeking affordable living.

The city's nonfarm employment growth of +0.92% YoY and solid wage growth of +4.86% YoY suggest a healthy expansion, but the labor market challenges and slowing housing market, with days on market increasing by 15.8% YoY, raise concerns about the city's ability to sustain economic momentum. The affordable cost of living and above-average professional workforce concentration, ranking in the 76th percentile, are notable strengths that could attract businesses.

In summary, Tampa offers businesses an affordable cost of living and a growing economy, but the primary risk is the city's labor market weaknesses, including high unemployment and low labor force participation, which could constrain business expansion and relocation plans.
