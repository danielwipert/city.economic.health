## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area ranks 17th in the dataset, with a weighted percentile score of 40.6, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.6%, which is relatively low compared to historical averages but places the region in the 16th percentile nationally. The labor force participation rate of 59.95% is also below the national median, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement.

Earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.86%, placing Tampa in the 68th percentile, suggesting a steady increase in income levels. The cost of living index is 5.1, slightly above the national average, and the overall wage replacement ratio stands at 3.08, indicating that workers in the region are maintaining a reasonable standard of living relative to their earnings. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture, with housing prices declining by 2.83% year-over-year and a slight decrease in the price-to-rent ratio of 0.41%. Despite these declines, the number of building permits issued has increased by 66.26% year-over-year, reflecting robust construction activity. Properties are taking an average of 80 days to sell, which is relatively high and may indicate a slowdown in the housing market.

These data points suggest a stable but not particularly dynamic economic environment in Tampa, with some positive trends in employment and construction offset by a cooling housing market.
