## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area ranks 17th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 47.2, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.4%, which is slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 60.12%, indicating a relatively low engagement in the workforce compared to other metropolitan areas. Earnings growth over the past year has been robust, increasing by 8.64%, which places the region in the 88th percentile for this metric. However, the cost of living has also risen by 4.68%, suggesting that wage gains may be partially offset by higher living costs.

Housing market indicators present a mixed picture. Building permits have increased by 10.06% year-over-year, reflecting continued interest in new construction. However, the housing price index has declined by 4.14% over the same period, which may indicate a cooling in the housing market. The days-on-market metric, at 79 days, is significantly higher than the national average, further suggesting a slowdown in home sales activity. Additionally, the median weekly hours worked is 34.8, which is in line with national trends but does not suggest any particular strength or weakness in the labor market.

Overall, the data suggests a moderate economic performance in the Tampa area, with some positive signs in earnings growth and construction activity, balanced by challenges in the labor market and housing sector.
