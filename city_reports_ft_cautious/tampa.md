# Tampa — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metropolitan area ranks 17th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 40.6, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate is 4.6%, slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 59.95%. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.86%, outpacing inflation, which is 5.1%. This suggests that real wages may be improving, albeit modestly.

Housing market indicators show a mixed picture. Building permits have increased by 66.26% year-over-year, indicating construction activity. However, the housing price index has declined by 2.83% over the same period, which may reflect a cooling in the housing market. The days-on-market metric is 80 days, indicating a relatively slow pace of home sales. The overall weighted score of 41 further underscores the region's below-average performance across multiple economic indicators.

The data points suggest a stable economic environment in Tampa, with some areas showing strength and others indicating potential softness.
