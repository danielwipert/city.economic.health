# Phoenix — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area ranks 10th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other regions. The unemployment rate is 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, suggesting a relatively low level of joblessness compared to peers. The labour force participation rate of 66.34% is in the 74th percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment. Year-over-year earnings growth is 4.52%, ranking in the 60th percentile, suggesting moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators present a mixed picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.53%, placing the region in the 6th percentile. The year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 16.78%, in the 76th percentile, indicating continued construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 55 days, in the 44th percentile, suggesting that properties are taking longer to sell than in some other markets. The cost of living index is 5.18% above the national average, in the 38th percentile, indicating moderate living costs relative to other U.S. cities.

The data collectively show a metropolitan area with stable employment conditions and moderate wage growth, alongside signs of a moderating housing market.
