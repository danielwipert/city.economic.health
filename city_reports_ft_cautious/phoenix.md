# Phoenix — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area received a B grade in a recent economic assessment, ranking 10th among metropolitan areas. The unemployment rate is 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate is 66.34%, scoring in the 74th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population in the labour market. Year-over-year earnings growth is 4.52%, ranking in the 60th percentile, while the cost of living increase is 5.18%, in the 38th percentile.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 1.53%, placing Phoenix in the 6th percentile, reflecting a cooling trend in property values. The year-over-year change in single-family rental prices has decreased by 2.18%, in the 28th percentile. Building permits have increased by 16.78% year-over-year, scoring in the 76th percentile, indicating continued investment in residential construction. The average number of days on the market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile.

The data points suggest a mixed economic environment in Phoenix, with some indicators showing strength and others indicating moderation or decline.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area ranks 10th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other metropolitan areas. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate is 66.34%, scoring in the 74th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.52%, ranking in the 60th percentile, while the cost of living increase is 5.18%, in the 38th percentile, suggesting that wage growth may not be fully keeping pace with living costs.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices has declined by 1.53%, placing Phoenix in the 6th percentile, reflecting a cooling trend in property values. Similarly, the year-over-year change in single-family rental prices has decreased by 2.18%, in the 28th percentile, which may indicate a softening demand in the rental market. Building permits have increased by 16.78% year-over-year, scoring in the 76th percentile, suggesting continued investment in residential construction despite the decline in home prices. The average number of days on the market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile, which is consistent with a moderate pace of sales activity.

These data points collectively suggest a mixed but generally stable economic environment in Phoenix, with some indicators showing strength and others indicating moderation or decline.
