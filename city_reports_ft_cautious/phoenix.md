# Phoenix — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area received a B grade in a recent economic assessment, ranking 10th overall. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate is 66.34%, ranking in the 74th percentile, suggesting a higher proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 4.52%, placing it in the 60th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth.

Housing market indicators present a mixed picture. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.53%, placing it in the 6th percentile. The year-over-year change in single-family home sales has decreased by 2.18%, ranking in the 28th percentile. However, building permits have increased by 16.86% year-over-year, placing it in the 72nd percentile. The median days on market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile.

The data suggests that the Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area maintains a relatively strong labour market, while the housing sector shows signs of moderation.
