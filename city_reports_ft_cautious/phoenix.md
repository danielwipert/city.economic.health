# Phoenix — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area ranks 10th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other regions. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 66.34% is in the 74th percentile, suggesting a higher proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment compared to many other areas.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.52%, ranking in the 60th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.19% is in the 42nd percentile. This suggests that wage gains may not be fully keeping pace with rising costs. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture: the year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.53%, placing it in the 6th percentile, and the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 16.78%, in the 76th percentile. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 75 days, in the 26th percentile.

The data suggests a balanced economic environment in Phoenix, with some indicators showing strength and others pointing to potential areas of concern.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metropolitan area ranks 10th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance relative to other regions. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 66.34% is in the 74th percentile, indicating a higher proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment compared to many other areas.

Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate, at 4.52%, ranking in the 60th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 5.19% is in the 42nd percentile, suggesting that wage gains may not be fully keeping pace with rising costs. Housing market indicators show a mixed picture: the year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 1.53%, placing it in the 6th percentile, and the year-over-year change in permits for new private housing units has increased by 16.78%, in the 76th percentile. This combination may reflect a cooling in home prices but continued robust construction activity. The number of days properties remain on the market has risen to 75 days, in the 26th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in the pace of sales.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not exceptional economic environment in Phoenix, with some indicators showing strength and others pointing to potential areas of concern.
