# St. Louis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic performance of the St. Louis metropolitan area, as indicated by recent data, exhibits a mixed profile across various metrics. The unemployment rate of 3.5% places it in the 68th percentile nationally, suggesting a relatively low level of joblessness. The labour force participation rate of 65.27% is above average, indicating a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the labour market. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 0.66%, ranking in the 18th percentile, which may indicate a slower pace of wage increases compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, data shows a significant year-over-year increase in home prices of 18.78%, placing St. Louis in the 84th percentile. This contrasts with a decline in building permits, down 6.99% year-over-year, suggesting a potential slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living in St. Louis is relatively low, at 73% of the national average, which could be an attractive factor for both residents and businesses. The average days on market for homes is 55 days, in line with national averages, indicating a balanced market without significant over- or undersupply.

The weighted percentile score of 57.9 and the grade of B suggest that St. Louis's economy is performing at an average level relative to other metropolitan areas.

This contrasts with a decline in building permits, down 6.99% year-over-year, suggesting a potential slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living in St. Louis is relatively low, at 73% of the national average, which could be an attractive factor for both residents and businesses. Additionally, the average days on market for homes is 55 days, which is in line with national averages, indicating a balanced market without significant over- or undersupply.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 57.9 and the grade of B suggest that St. Louis's economy is performing at an average level relative to other metropolitan areas.
