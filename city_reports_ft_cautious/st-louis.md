## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the St. Louis metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 68th percentile among similar regions, suggesting a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 65.2% is also above average, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which may reflect a stable engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. However, earnings growth over the past year has been modest, with a 0.22% increase, placing it in the 12th percentile, indicating slower wage growth compared to other areas.

In the housing sector, the annual change in home prices has been robust, increasing by 18.79%, which ranks in the 86th percentile, suggesting a strong demand for residential properties. Conversely, building permits have declined by 15.43% year-over-year, placing this indicator in the 34th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living in St. Louis is relatively low, with an index of 0.64, ranking in the 96th percentile, making it more affordable compared to many other metropolitan areas. The average number of days a property remains on the market is 49 days, in the 62nd percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers dominate.

Overall, the data suggests that while St. Louis maintains a low unemployment rate and affordable living costs, it faces challenges in wage growth and new housing development.
