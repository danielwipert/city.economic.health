The economic data for the St. Louis metropolitan area reveals a mixed picture, with some indicators performing above average while others lag behind. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a relatively robust labor market. However, the year-over-year growth in earnings is modest at 0.66%, ranking in the 18th percentile, indicating limited wage growth. In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices is significant at 18.79%, placing St. Louis in the 86th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply. Conversely, the decline in building permits by 7.66% year-over-year, at the 36th percentile, could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index is notably low at 0.56, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting that St. Louis remains an affordable place to live relative to other metropolitan areas.

Overall, the data indicates a balanced but not exceptional economic environment in St. Louis, with strengths in housing affordability and price appreciation, but with more subdued performance in wage growth and labor market dynamics.
