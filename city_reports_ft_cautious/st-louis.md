# St. Louis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

St. Louis's economic profile, as indicated by recent data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness. However, the labour force participation rate of 65.27% is slightly below the national average, ranking in the 64th percentile, which may indicate a moderate level of workforce engagement.

Earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 0.66%, placing St. Louis in the 18th percentile, suggesting limited wage increases compared to other metropolitan areas. In the housing market, the annual increase in home prices of 18.78% places St. Louis in the 84th percentile, indicating a robust appreciation in property values. However, the decline in building permits by 18.7% year-over-year, in the 26th percentile, suggests a slowdown in new construction activity.

The cost of living index of 0.73, in the 92nd percentile, highlights that St. Louis remains more affordable relative to other U.S. cities. The average days on market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile, which is neither particularly fast nor slow, indicating a balanced market.

Overall, the data suggests that St. Louis maintains a stable economic position, with notable strengths in housing affordability and price appreciation, alongside more moderate performance in employment and earnings growth.
