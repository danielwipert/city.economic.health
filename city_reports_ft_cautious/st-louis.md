# St. Louis

**Grade: B- (Below Average) | 48.3th percentile | June 2026**

---

St. Louis has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 48.3th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its below-average labor demand, with a composite score of 3.63, and its above-average unemployment rate of 3.60%, which ranks in the 70th percentile. These metrics suggest a city with a relatively tight labor market but struggling to create new job opportunities.

**Labor Demand**
The employment growth rate in St. Louis is -0.48% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.025% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. Instead, it may be facing a period of job losses or stagnation.

**Unemployment**
The unemployment rate in St. Louis is 3.60%, ranking in the 70th percentile, which means the market is relatively tight. This tightness implies that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and wage pressure may be higher. As a result, companies may need to offer competitive salaries to attract top talent.

**Wage Growth**
The year-over-year wage growth rate in St. Louis is +1.80%, which is below average, ranking in the 24th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not rapidly, and worker purchasing power is increasing, albeit slowly. This may impact businesses' ability to retain and attract workers, as they may need to adjust their compensation packages accordingly.

**Cost of Living**
St. Louis has a cost of living ratio of $166/sqft to $37.52/hr, with a percentile rank of 59th, indicating that the city is near the median in terms of affordability. This means that St. Louis is neither extremely affordable nor expensive, and businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to attract workers. However, the city's affordability may not be a major talent attraction advantage.

**Labor Force Growth**
The civilian labor force in St. Louis is growing at a rate of +0.01% year-over-year, which is above average, ranking in the 60th percentile. This slow but positive growth rate suggests that the workforce supply is expanding, albeit slowly, which may help businesses find the talent they need. However, the slow growth rate may not be enough to meet the demands of rapidly expanding companies.

**Building Permits**
The number of building permits in St. Louis is increasing by +4.06% year-over-year, which is near the median, ranking in the 56th percentile. This moderate growth rate suggests that housing supply is expanding, which may improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. However, the growth rate is not rapid enough to significantly impact the city's affordability in the short term.

**Days on Market**
The median days on market in St. Louis is 42 days, with a year-over-year increase of +7.7%, ranking in the 82nd percentile. This means that homes are sitting on the market for a relatively long time, indicating a slower and more buyer-friendly market. For workers relocating to St. Louis, this may be a competitive advantage, as they may have more time to find and purchase a home.

**Office Economy**
St. Louis has a relatively shallow professional talent pool, ranking in the 14th percentile. This suggests that the city is not well-suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting companies. Instead, St. Louis may be more suitable for industries with fewer specialized roles, such as logistics or manufacturing.

In conclusion, St. Louis offers businesses a relatively tight labor market with moderate wage growth, but its below-average labor demand and slow labor force growth may pose challenges for companies looking to expand. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the city's limited professional talent pool, which may make it difficult to attract and retain top talent in certain industries.
