The economic data for the St. Louis metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing relative strength while others lag behind national trends. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 40th percentile, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly tight nor loose compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.69% is in the 68th percentile, indicating a relatively active labor market. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 1.2% is in the 8th percentile, suggesting that wage growth in St. Louis is lagging behind many other regions.

Housing market indicators show a more nuanced story. The year-over-year increase in the housing price index of 17.21% is in the 82nd percentile, indicating a robust housing market in terms of price appreciation. However, the year-over-year change in single-family housing permits is -15.43%, placing it in the 34th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living in St. Louis, as measured by the cost of living index, is 0.68 times the national average, placing it in the 96th percentile, suggesting that St. Louis remains a relatively affordable place to live. The median days on market for homes is 45 days, in the 64th percentile, indicating a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that St. Louis is experiencing average economic conditions, with strengths in housing price appreciation and cost of living, but challenges in wage growth and new housing construction.
