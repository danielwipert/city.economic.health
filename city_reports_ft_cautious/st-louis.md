# St. Louis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

St. Louis's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some metrics suggesting strengths and others areas of concern. The unemployment rate of 3.5% ranks in the 68th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, indicating a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 65.27% is slightly below the national average, ranking in the 64th percentile. Year-over-year earnings growth of 0.66% places St. Louis in the 18th percentile, suggesting a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 18.78% ranks in the 84th percentile, indicating a strong appreciation in property values. This contrasts with a decline in building permits of 18.7%, which ranks in the 26th percentile. The cost of living index of 0.73 is above the median, suggesting that St. Louis remains relatively affordable compared to other major cities. The average days on market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile, which may indicate a balanced market.

The data suggests that St. Louis maintains a stable economic position, with some notable strengths in housing value appreciation and affordability, balanced against moderate labor market conditions and a slowdown in new residential construction.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of St. Louis, as reflected in recent data, presents a mixed picture with several indicators suggesting both strengths and areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile among comparable metropolitan areas, which indicates a relatively robust labor market. However, the labor force participation rate of 65.27% is slightly below the national average, ranking in the 64th percentile, potentially signaling some underutilization of the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth is modest at 0.66%, placing it in the 18th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 18.78% places St. Louis in the 84th percentile, indicating a strong appreciation in property values. This contrasts with a decline in building permits of 18.7%, which ranks in the 26th percentile, possibly suggesting a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index of 0.73, well above the median, suggests that St. Louis remains relatively affordable compared to other major cities. Additionally, the average days on market for homes is 55 days, in the 44th percentile, which may indicate a balanced market with neither a significant buyer's nor seller's advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that St. Louis maintains a stable but not exceptional economic position, with notable strengths in housing value appreciation and affordability, balanced against moderate labor market conditions and a slowdown in new residential construction.
