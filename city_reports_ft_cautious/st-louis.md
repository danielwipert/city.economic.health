## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the St. Louis metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 4.5%, placing it in the 40th percentile among comparable regions, which suggests a moderate level of labor market activity. The labor force participation rate of 65.69% is relatively high, ranking in the 68th percentile, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 1.2%, placing it in the 8th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases compared to other areas.

Housing market indicators show contrasting trends. The housing price index has increased by 18.79% year-over-year, a significant rise that places St. Louis in the 86th percentile, suggesting robust demand for housing. Conversely, building permits have declined by 15.43% year-over-year, ranking in the 34th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction or a shift in development priorities. The cost of living in St. Louis is relatively low, with an index of 0.68, placing it in the 96th percentile, making it more affordable compared to many other metropolitan areas.

Overall, the data suggests that while St. Louis exhibits some areas of strength, particularly in housing prices and affordability, other indicators such as earnings growth and building permits point to more moderate or declining trends.
