# St. Louis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

St. Louis ranks 23rd among metropolitan areas, with a mixed economic profile according to recent data. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 68th percentile, indicating relatively low joblessness compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 65.27% is above average, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population in the labour market. However, earnings growth over the past year has been modest at 0.66%, ranking in the 18th percentile, which may reflect slower wage increases relative to other metro areas.

In terms of housing, the annual increase in home prices of 18.78% places St. Louis in the 84th percentile, suggesting a strong appreciation in property values. This contrasts with a decline in building permits of 18.7% year-over-year, which ranks in the 26th percentile, potentially indicating a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index of 0.73 is well above the 92nd percentile, indicating that St. Louis remains more affordable than many other metropolitan areas. The median days on market for homes is 55 days, a figure that is neither particularly high nor low, sitting in the 44th percentile.

The data suggests a balanced economic performance for St. Louis, with some indicators pointing to strengths in employment and housing affordability, while others suggest moderation in other areas.
