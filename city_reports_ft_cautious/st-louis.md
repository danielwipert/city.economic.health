## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic landscape of St. Louis, as reflected in recent data, suggests a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.7%, placing it in the 68th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 65.2% is also above average, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which may reflect a stable engagement of the workforce in the economy. However, earnings growth year-over-year is modest at 0.22%, placing it in the 12th percentile, suggesting limited wage expansion.

In the housing market, the annual increase in home prices of 18.79% places St. Louis in the 86th percentile, indicating a robust appreciation in property values. Conversely, building permits have declined by 15.43% year-over-year, ranking in the 34th percentile, which could suggest a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living index of 0.64, in the 96th percentile, indicates that St. Louis remains relatively affordable compared to other metropolitan areas. Additionally, the average days on market for homes is 49 days, in the 62nd percentile, suggesting a balanced market with neither an excess of supply nor demand.

Overall, the data suggests that St. Louis maintains a stable but not exceptional economic position, with strengths in housing affordability and price appreciation, but with more moderate performance in employment and wage growth.
