# Providence-Warwick

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 43.9th percentile | April 2026**

---

Providence-Warwick earns a C+ overall grade, reflecting a labor market with significant structural constraints, with a critically low labor force participation rate of 50.77% hindering business expansion or relocation. The city's cost of living composite score of 7.35 places it among the most expensive metros, constraining talent acquisition and real consumer spending power. An above-average share of office and professional workers could be a mild positive for office-dependent businesses.

The housing market presents conflicting signals, with building permits up 144.37% year-over-year, suggesting an aggressive expansion of housing supply, but days on market have increased by 33.3% to 48 days, indicating a slowing market. Strong employment growth and rising permits suggest normalization, but the high cost of living and low labor force participation rate remain concerns.

Providence offers an above-average professional workforce and an expanding housing supply, but the primary risk is the city's high cost of living and low labor force participation rate, which could limit its appeal to businesses seeking to relocate or expand. Decision-makers must carefully weigh these factors when considering Providence as a potential location.
