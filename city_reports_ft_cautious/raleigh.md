The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile, which indicates a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.67% is also strong, ranking in the 80th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population. However, the average weekly hours worked, at 32.9 hours, is notably low, ranking in the 4th percentile, which may reflect either part-time employment or reduced work schedules.

In terms of housing, the year-over-year change in the housing price index is 8.65%, indicating continued appreciation in home values, though this growth has slowed compared to previous years. The days on market for homes have increased to 74 days, up from previous levels, which may reflect a more balanced market between buyers and sellers. Earnings growth, at 4.51% year-over-year, is moderate, ranking in the 58th percentile, while cost of living and wage growth relative to inflation are both around the median, suggesting stable but not exceptional economic conditions.

Overall, the data suggests that Raleigh-Cary's economy is performing averagely, with strengths in employment and housing prices offset by concerns in labor market intensity and construction activity.
