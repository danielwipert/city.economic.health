# Raleigh-Cary

**Grade: A+ (Excellent) | 72.8th percentile | April 2026**

---

Raleigh earns an A+ overall grade, driven by its exceptionally tight labor market with a 3.00% unemployment rate and 1.70% year-over-year nonfarm employment growth. The city's highly affordable cost of living, with a composite index score of 1.43, indicates a strong cost advantage. Raleigh ranks in the 98th percentile for professional workforce concentration, making it attractive for knowledge-economy businesses and headquarters.

The labor force is growing slowly, at 0.53% year-over-year, which will constrain hiring capacity and exacerbate the tight labor market. The housing market is stabilizing, with days on market increasing by 4.5% year-over-year to an average of 46 days, a sign of healthy normalization given the strong employment growth and 38.82% year-over-year increase in building permits.

Raleigh offers a unique combination of a highly skilled workforce, rapid job growth, and an affordable cost of living, making it an excellent location for companies. The primary risk is labor availability becoming a significant constraint due to the tight labor market and slow labor force growth, which could limit the city's ability to sustain its current pace of economic expansion.
