# Raleigh — Economic Overview (FT Style)

## Polished FT-style column

Raleigh's economic indicators point to a robust labour market, with an unemployment rate of 3.0% and a modest year-over-year increase of 0.2 percentage points. The civilian labour force has expanded by 0.53% over the past year, indicating a steady supply of workers. Notably, average hourly earnings have risen by 5.28% year-over-year, outpacing many other cities. The office worker ratio composite score of 4.6 also suggests a high demand for office space and a strong presence of white-collar jobs.

The city's housing market is experiencing significant growth, with residential building permits surging 38.82% year-over-year. However, this rapid expansion has contributed to a slight increase in the median days on market, which has risen by 4.55% to 46 days. Raleigh's cost of living composite score of 1.41 remains relatively low, making it an attractive option for individuals and businesses. The labour demand composite score of 6.28 and nonfarm payroll employment growth of 1.25% year-over-year further indicate a strong and growing economy.

While these trends are positive, there are potential stress points to consider. The weekly hours deviation from the city's 12-month baseline is 0.226%, which may signal some labour market volatility. Moreover, the rapid growth in residential building permits could put pressure on the city's infrastructure and affordability. Nevertheless, Raleigh's economic indicators suggest a strong and diverse economy, driven by a talented workforce and high quality of life.

Looking ahead, Raleigh's economic prospects appear favourable, with a strong labour market, growing business activity, and a high quality of life. Although there are potential stress points to monitor, the city's overall economic indicators suggest a positive outlook. To ensure Raleigh remains a desirable and sustainable place to live and work, it will be crucial to balance development with affordability and infrastructure investments as the city continues to grow and evolve.

However, this rapid expansion may be contributing to a slight increase in the median days on market, which has risen by 4.55% year-over-year to 46 days. The cost of living composite score of 1.41 is relatively low, suggesting that Raleigh remains an attractive option for individuals and businesses alike. The labor demand composite score of 6.28 and nonfarm payroll employment growth of 1.25% year-over-year also indicate a strong and growing economy.

Despite these positive trends, there are some potential stress points to consider. Additionally, the rapid growth in residential building permits may lead to increased pressure on the city's infrastructure and affordability. Overall, however, Raleigh's economic indicators suggest a city with a strong and diverse economy, driven by a talented workforce and a high quality of life.

Closing outlook: Raleigh's economic prospects appear bright, with a strong labour market, growing business activity, and a high quality of life. While there are some potential stress points to monitor, the city's overall economic indicators suggest a positive outlook for the future.
