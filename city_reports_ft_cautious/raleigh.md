## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 88th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 67.86% is also above average, ranking in the 76th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 2.94% is more modest, falling in the 44th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of wage increases relative to other areas.

Housing market indicators present a contrasting picture. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.65% places the region in the 58th percentile, suggesting moderate appreciation. Conversely, the decline in building permits by 28.25% year-over-year, situated in the 16th percentile, points to a significant reduction in new construction activity. Additionally, the days on market metric of 65 days, also in the 16th percentile, indicates that properties are taking longer to sell, which may reflect a cooling in demand or an oversupply of homes.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not particularly dynamic economic environment in Raleigh-Cary, with strengths in employment and labor force participation offset by more subdued indicators in housing and earnings growth.
