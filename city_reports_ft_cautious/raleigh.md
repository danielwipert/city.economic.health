## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.5%, placing it in the 86th percentile, which suggests a relatively low level of joblessness compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 67.45% ranks in the 76th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 2.94% is positioned in the 44th percentile, suggesting moderate wage growth that may not be outpacing inflation.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 8.65% year-over-year, placing it in the 58th percentile, which may reflect continued demand for housing despite broader economic uncertainties. Conversely, building permits have declined by 28.25% year-over-year, ranking in the 16th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes have remained stable at 65 days, in the 16th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that Raleigh-Cary's economy is performing averagely with some strengths in employment and labor force participation, but with more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing market dynamics.
