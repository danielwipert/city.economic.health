# Raleigh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Raleigh-Cary's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some areas outperforming national trends while others trail behind. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.67% is also relatively high, ranking in the 80th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement from the working-age population in the economy. However, earnings growth year-over-year is moderate at 4.51%, situated in the 58th percentile, which may reflect a stable wage environment.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 10.4% places the region in the 58th percentile, suggesting a steady appreciation in property values. The number of building permits issued has grown by 9.02% year-over-year, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. The physical vacancy rate for rental properties has decreased by 0.91% year-over-year, placing it in the 48th percentile, potentially signalling a tightening rental market. The average days on market for homes is 60 days, in the 28th percentile, which may suggest a balanced market.

Overall, the data suggests that Raleigh-Cary maintains a solid economic foundation, with particular strengths in employment and labor force participation, while other metrics indicate a more moderate pace of growth.
