## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally stable performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile nationally, which indicates a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 67.53% is robust, ranking in the 80th percentile, suggesting a high engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 6.56% is moderate, positioned in the 68th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.7% and the overall wage rate growth of 4.46% are both within the middle range of national comparisons, at the 56th and 98th percentiles, respectively.

Housing market indicators present a more varied picture. The year-over-year change in housing permits has declined by 25.29%, placing this metric in the 14th percentile, which may reflect a slowdown in construction activity. In contrast, the home price index has increased by 8.65% over the same period, ranking in the 58th percentile, indicating continued appreciation in property values despite the decline in new construction. The days on market for homes have increased to 72 days, in the 20th percentile, which could suggest a softening in demand or an oversupply of properties. The year-over-year change in the number of single-family homes for sale has decreased by 1.06%, in the 40th percentile, further complicating the housing market dynamics.

Overall, the weighted percentile score of 54.2 and the grade of B for Raleigh-Cary indicate an average economic performance relative to other metropolitan areas.
