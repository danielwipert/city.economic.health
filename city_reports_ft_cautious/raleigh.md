## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area presents a mixed picture, with some indicators showing strength while others suggest areas of concern. The unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, placing it in the 88th percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a relatively robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.86% is also above average, ranking in the 76th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement in the workforce. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 2.94% is more modest, falling in the 44th percentile, which may reflect a slower pace of income expansion compared to other regions.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.65% places the area in the 58th percentile, indicating a steady but not overly rapid appreciation in property values. Conversely, the decline in building permits by 28.25% year-over-year, situated in the 16th percentile, suggests a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market metric of 65 days, also in the 16th percentile, points to a less dynamic housing market, possibly reflecting softer demand or a shift in buyer behavior.

Overall, the data suggests that Raleigh-Cary's economy is performing at an average level, with strengths in employment and labor force participation offset by more moderate performance in earnings growth and housing market dynamics.
