The economic data for Raleigh-Cary indicates a mixed performance across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it in the 74th percentile among metropolitan areas, suggesting a relatively low level of joblessness. The labor force participation rate of 67.53% ranks in the 80th percentile, indicating a high engagement of the working-age population in the labor market. Earnings growth over the year has been moderate at 4.51%, which is in the 58th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.95% is near the median, at the 50th percentile. Notably, the wage growth relative to the cost of living is positive, with a score of 4.4, ranking in the 96th percentile, suggesting that wages are outpacing living costs.

In the housing market, the annual home price index increase of 8.65% places Raleigh-Cary in the 58th percentile, indicating steady but not exceptional appreciation in property values. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 25.29% year-over-year, a significant drop that ranks in the 14th percentile, potentially reflecting a slowdown in new construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 72 days, which is in the 20th percentile, suggesting a relatively slower pace of sales compared to other markets. The weekly hours worked, at 32.9 hours, is in the 4th percentile, indicating a below-average workweek length.

Overall, the data suggests a balanced but not outstanding economic environment in Raleigh-Cary, with strengths in employment and wage growth relative to living costs, but challenges in the housing market and labor supply.
