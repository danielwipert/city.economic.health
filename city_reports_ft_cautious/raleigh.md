# Raleigh — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Raleigh-Cary metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.0%, placing it in the 90th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively strong labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.67% is also relatively high, ranking in the 80th percentile, suggesting a high level of engagement of the working-age population in the economy. However, the year-over-year earnings growth of 4.51% is more moderate, sitting in the 58th percentile, which may reflect broader economic conditions or sector-specific trends.

In the housing market, the year-over-year increase in the housing price index of 10.4% is notable, though it ranks only in the 58th percentile, suggesting that while prices are rising, the pace is not among the fastest in the country. The number of building permits issued has increased by 9.02% year-over-year, placing the region in the 62nd percentile, which could indicate ongoing investment in residential construction. Conversely, the supply of for-sale homes has decreased by 1.06% year-over-year, ranking in the 44th percentile, which may contribute to upward pressure on home prices. The average days on market for homes is 74 days, a relatively long period that places the region in the 30th percentile, possibly reflecting a balance between supply and demand in the housing market.

The data suggests that Raleigh-Cary maintains a solid economic foundation with some areas of strength and others showing more moderate performance.
