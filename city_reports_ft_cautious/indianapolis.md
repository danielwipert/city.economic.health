# Indianapolis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 2.5%, placing it in the 96th percentile, indicating a low joblessness rate. The labour force participation rate of 66.86% ranks in the 76th percentile, suggesting a significant portion of the working-age population is engaged in the labour force. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.1%, placing it in the 72nd percentile, while the cost of living index of 0.17 ranks in the 96th percentile, indicating that wages may be keeping pace with living costs.

Housing market indicators show a more complex picture. The housing price index has increased by 16.78% year-over-year, placing it in the 76th percentile, indicating a strong appreciation in home values. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 21.04% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market metric is 73 days, placing it in the 14th percentile, suggesting that homes are taking longer to sell compared to other markets. The price-to-square-foot ratio has risen by 6.24% year-over-year, placing it in the 92nd percentile, which could indicate increasing demand or rising construction costs.

The data suggests that Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and affordability, but with some signs of moderation in the housing sector.
