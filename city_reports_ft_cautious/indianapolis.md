# Indianapolis — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across several key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 2.5%, placing it in the 96th percentile, indicating a low joblessness rate. The labour force participation rate of 66.86% is also favourable, ranking in the 76th percentile, suggesting a significant portion of the working-age population is engaged in the labour market. Earnings growth over the past year has been 5.1%, placing it in the 72nd percentile, indicating moderate wage increases.

Housing market indicators show a more nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 16.78% year-over-year, placing it in the 76th percentile, which may reflect strong demand or limited supply in the housing sector. However, building permits have declined by 21.04% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The cost of living in Indianapolis is relatively low, at 17% below the national average, which may contribute to its attractiveness as a place to live and work. Weekly hours worked are at 33.1 hours, ranking in the 12th percentile, which is lower than the national average and may suggest part-time employment or flexible work arrangements.

The data suggests that Indianapolis is performing above average in many economic metrics, though some areas, such as new construction, show signs of moderation.
