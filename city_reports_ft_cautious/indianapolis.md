## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic data for the Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across key indicators. The unemployment rate stands at 3.1%, placing it in the 92nd percentile among comparable regions, which indicates a robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 67.42% is also relatively strong, ranking in the 76th percentile. Earnings growth over the past year has been moderate at 5.1%, aligning with broader national trends and placing the region in the 72nd percentile. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, is at 4.17%, slightly above the national average, and the overall wage replacement rate is 3.54%, suggesting that wages are keeping pace with living costs to some extent.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The housing price index has increased by 14.37% year-over-year, a significant rise that places the region in the 74th percentile. However, the number of building permits issued has declined by 5.87% compared to the previous year, indicating a potential slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market for homes have remained stable at 64 days, which is within the 54th percentile, suggesting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The weekly hours worked, at 33.1 hours, are notably lower than the national average, ranking in the 12th percentile, which may reflect structural changes in the local economy or differences in industry composition.

Overall, the data suggests that Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is performing above average relative to other metropolitan areas, with strengths in employment and housing prices, but with some cautionary signs in construction activity and work hours.
