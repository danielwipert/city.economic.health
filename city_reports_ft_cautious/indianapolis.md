The economic data for the Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metropolitan area suggests a mixed but generally positive performance across various indicators. The labor force participation rate of 66.86% is also relatively high, ranking in the 76th percentile, suggesting that a significant portion of the working-age population is engaged in the labor market. Year-over-year earnings growth of 5.1% is solid, though it ranks slightly lower in the 72nd percentile, indicating moderate wage growth relative to other areas.

Housing market indicators present a more nuanced picture. The year-over-year increase in home prices of 16.78% is strong, placing the region in the 76th percentile, which may reflect continued demand for housing. However, the number of building permits has declined by 21.04% year-over-year, ranking in the 20th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 74 days, in the 30th percentile, suggesting that while homes are selling, the process is not particularly swift compared to other markets.

Overall, the weighted score of 68 and a grade of B+ suggest that Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is performing above average relative to its peers, with strengths in employment and housing prices, but with some cautionary signs in construction activity and labor market engagement metrics.
