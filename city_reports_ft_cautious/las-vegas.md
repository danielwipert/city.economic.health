## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metropolitan area ranks 29th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 33.5, corresponding to a grade of C+, described as "Poor." The unemployment rate stands at 5.5%, placing it in the 2nd percentile, which suggests a relatively high level of joblessness compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.4% is at the 62nd percentile, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 7.52% places the region in the 76th percentile, suggesting a robust increase in wages.

Housing market indicators show mixed trends. The year-over-year change in housing prices has declined by 0.66%, placing it in the 12th percentile, while the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has dropped by 24.63%, also in the 12th percentile. This decline in both housing prices and construction permits may reflect a cooling in the housing market. The average number of days a property remains on the market is 72 days, in the 20th percentile, which could indicate a slowdown in sales activity. Additionally, the year-over-year change in the price-to-square-foot ratio has decreased by 1.86%, in the 30th percentile, further suggesting a moderation in property value appreciation.

Overall, the data indicates a mixed economic performance in Las Vegas, with some positive signs in wage growth and labor force participation, but challenges in the housing market and employment.
