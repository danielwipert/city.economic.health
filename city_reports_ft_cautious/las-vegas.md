# Las Vegas — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metropolitan area ranks 29th in a recent economic assessment, with a weighted percentile score of 45.5, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 5.2%, which is relatively low compared to historical averages and places the region in the 2nd percentile nationally, indicating a tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.02% is near the national median, suggesting a moderate level of engagement in the workforce.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 6.71%, placing the region in the 82nd percentile. This may reflect strong demand for labor or rising costs of living. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, has risen by 5.08%, placing it in the 44th percentile. This suggests that wage growth is outpacing inflation, potentially benefiting workers. Housing market indicators show mixed trends; while housing prices have declined slightly by 0.88% year-over-year, placing the region in the 14th percentile, the number of building permits has surged by 40.95%, in the 84th percentile, indicating a significant increase in construction activity. The days on market for homes have increased to 53 days, in the 54th percentile, which may indicate a more balanced market compared to the recent past.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Las Vegas, with strong labor market dynamics and construction activity offset by modest declines in housing prices and a below-average overall economic grade.
