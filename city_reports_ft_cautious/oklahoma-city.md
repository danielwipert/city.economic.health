# Oklahoma City

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 43.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Oklahoma City earns a grade of C+ due to its struggling labor market, with a nonfarm employment growth rate of -0.54% year-over-year. The city's labor force growth rate is only 0.23% year-over-year, indicating a slow expansion of the workforce. This contraction in jobs is a significant concern for businesses considering the city as a potential location.

The city's cost of living composite score of 1.13 places it among the most affordable metros, with a 90th percentile ranking. However, the low office and professional worker share, ranked in the 16th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses. The housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 17.0% year-over-year to 55 days, despite aggressive expansion in housing supply, with building permits up 20.14% year-over-year.

Oklahoma City offers an extremely affordable cost of living and a growing housing supply, but the primary risk is the city's struggling labor market. The weak job growth and low professional worker concentration make it a challenging location for businesses reliant on a skilled workforce. Decision-makers should carefully weigh these factors when considering Oklahoma City as a potential location, taking into account the city's unique combination of affordability and labor market challenges.
