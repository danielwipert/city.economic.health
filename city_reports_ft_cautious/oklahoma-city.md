# Oklahoma City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture. The unemployment rate is 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% is higher than the national median, suggesting a high level of engagement among the working-age population. Earnings growth year-over-year is 4.7%, a solid rate that is not exceptional compared to other metropolitan areas. The cost of living index is 4.88 and the owner-occupied housing rate is 2.6%, both of which fall within the middle range of national comparisons.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in building permits is 23.95%, a notable rise that may reflect growing demand for new housing or commercial properties. The home price index has risen by 10.86% year-over-year, which is above average. However, the pace of sales, as measured by days on market, has increased to 67 days, suggesting a slight slowing in the speed of transactions. Weekly hours worked stand at 35.8 hours, significantly above the national average.

The data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with notable strengths in labor force participation and weekly hours worked.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% is higher than the national median, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.7%, which, while solid, is not exceptional compared to other metropolitan areas. The cost of living index of 4.88 and the owner-occupied housing rate of 2.6% both fall within the middle range of national comparisons, suggesting moderate affordability and homeownership levels.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in building permits is 23.95%, a significant rise that may reflect growing demand for new housing or commercial properties. The home price index has risen by 10.86% year-over-year, which is above average and could indicate a strengthening real estate market. However, the pace of sales, as measured by days on market, has increased to 67 days, which might suggest a slight cooling in the speed of transactions. Weekly hours worked stand at 35.8 hours, significantly above the national average, which could point to high productivity or longer working hours among employed individuals.

Overall, the data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with particular strengths in labor force participation and weekly hours worked.
