# Oklahoma City

**Grade: A+ (Excellent) | 69.7th percentile | April 2026**

---

Oklahoma City earns an A+ grade, driven by its exceptional economic performance, with a strong labor market and increasing consumer spending power. The city's nonfarm employment growth of +0.52% YoY and solid wage growth of +4.70% YoY indicate a healthy expansion.

The city's cost of living composite score of 1.27 makes it one of the most affordable metros, offering a significant cost advantage for businesses and residents. Meanwhile, the housing market is stabilizing, with building permits growing at an above-average rate of +20.14% YoY, and days on market increasing by 17.0% YoY, now averaging 55 days. This suggests a slowing market, which could ease affordability pressure and give buyers negotiating room.

Oklahoma City presents a unique combination of a strong labor market, affordable cost of living, and a stabilizing housing market, making it an attractive location for businesses. However, the primary risk lies in the potential for the housing market to continue slowing, which could impact demand and affordability if not managed carefully.
