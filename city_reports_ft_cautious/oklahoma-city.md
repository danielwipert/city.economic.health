# Oklahoma City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some indicators pointing to a relatively stable labour market. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally. The labour force participation rate of 65.65% ranks higher, at the 70th percentile, indicating a relatively high engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is reported at 4.7%, situating the city at the 62nd percentile, a moderate increase that may reflect broader economic conditions. The cost of living index of 4.82 and the overall wage rate of 2.55 both fall around the 60th percentile, suggesting that living costs and wages are in line with national averages.

Housing and construction activity show notable trends. Building permits have increased by 22.35% year-over-year, placing Oklahoma City at the 80th percentile. The home price index has risen by 12.7% over the same period, ranking at the 66th percentile. However, the days on market metric of 58 days is at the 34th percentile, suggesting that the market is active but not among the fastest moving in the country. Weekly hours worked average 35.8 hours, a high 96th percentile score.

These data points suggest that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with particular strengths in labour force participation and housing market activity.

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% ranks higher, at the 70th percentile, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is reported at 4.7%, situating the city at the 62nd percentile, a moderate increase that may reflect broader economic conditions. The cost of living index of 4.82 and the overall wage rate of 2.55 both fall around the 60th percentile, suggesting that living costs and wages are in line with national averages.

Housing and construction activity show notable trends. Building permits have increased by 22.35% year-over-year, placing Oklahoma City at the 80th percentile, which could indicate a growing demand for housing or commercial space. The home price index has risen by 12.7% over the same period, ranking at the 66th percentile, a significant increase that may reflect strong local real estate dynamics. However, the days on market metric of 58 days is at the 34th percentile, suggesting that while the market is active, it is not among the fastest moving in the country. Weekly hours worked average 35.8 hours, a high 96th percentile score, which might point to a well-utilized workforce.

Overall, these data points suggest that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with particular strengths in labor force participation and housing market activity.
