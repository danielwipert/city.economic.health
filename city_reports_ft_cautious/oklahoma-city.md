# Oklahoma City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some indicators pointing to a positive trend. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither exceptionally tight nor loose. The labour force participation rate of 65.65% is above average, ranking in the 70th percentile, which may reflect a relatively high level of engagement among the working-age population. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.7%, placing the city in the 62nd percentile, indicating moderate wage increases that align with broader national trends.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 12.7% places Oklahoma City in the 66th percentile, suggesting a steady appreciation in property values. The year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has risen by 22.35%, a notable increase that ranks in the 80th percentile. The median days on market for homes is 67 days, which is slightly above the national median, possibly reflecting a balanced market.

The data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average in some areas, with strengths in labour force participation and housing construction, while maintaining stable conditions in employment and housing markets.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, suggesting a labor market that is neither exceptionally tight nor loose. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% is above average, ranking in the 70th percentile, which may reflect a relatively high level of engagement among the working-age population. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.7%, placing the city in the 62nd percentile, indicating moderate wage increases that align with broader national trends.

In the housing sector, the annual increase in home prices of 12.7% places Oklahoma City in the 66th percentile, suggesting a steady appreciation in property values. However, the year-over-year change in permits for new residential construction has risen by 22.35%, a significant increase that ranks in the 80th percentile, potentially indicating robust housing demand and development activity. The median days on market for homes is 67 days, which is slightly above the national median, possibly reflecting a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a pronounced advantage.

Overall, the data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with strengths in labor force participation and housing construction, while maintaining stable conditions in employment and housing markets.
