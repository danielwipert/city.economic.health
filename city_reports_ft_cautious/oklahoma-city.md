# Oklahoma City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with some indicators pointing to a relatively stable labour market. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally. The labour force participation rate of 65.65% is higher than the national median, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is 4.7%, slightly above the national average, while the cost of living index of 4.82 suggests that living expenses are modestly higher than the national baseline. The average weekly hours worked of 35.8 hours is high, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may reflect robust demand for labour or longer working hours in the region.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in housing permits is 22.35%, placing Oklahoma City in the 80th percentile. The home price index has grown by 12.7% year-over-year, indicating a healthy appreciation in property values. However, the days on market metric is 58 days, below the national average and ranking in the 34th percentile, which could suggest a more balanced market. The vacancy rate remains unchanged year-over-year, which may reflect a stable rental market.

The data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average in some areas, with strengths in labour market engagement and housing market activity.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which suggests a relatively stable labor market. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% is higher than the national median, indicating a strong engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Earnings growth year-over-year is at 4.7%, slightly above the national average, and the cost of living index of 4.82 suggests that living expenses are modestly higher than the national baseline. The weekly hours worked average of 35.8 hours is notably high, ranking in the 96th percentile, which may reflect a robust demand for labor or longer working hours in the region.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in housing permits is 22.35%, a significant rise that places Oklahoma City in the 80th percentile, suggesting a strong construction activity. The home price index has grown by 12.7% year-over-year, indicating a healthy appreciation in property values. However, the days on market metric at 58 days is below the national average, ranking in the 34th percentile, which could suggest a more balanced market with neither an excess of supply nor demand. The vacancy rate remains unchanged year-over-year, which may reflect a stable rental market.

Overall, the data indicates that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with strengths in labor market engagement and housing market activity.
