# Oklahoma City — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% ranks higher, at the 70th percentile. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.7%, situating the city at the 62nd percentile. The cost of living increase of 4.82% is slightly above the national median, at the 60th percentile.

In the housing sector, the annual home price index (HPI) growth of 12.7% places Oklahoma City at the 66th percentile. The number of building permits issued has increased by 22.35% year-over-year, a significant rise that ranks the city at the 80th percentile. The days on market metric of 58 days, at the 34th percentile, suggests that homes are selling at a moderate pace. The weekly hours worked, at 35.8 hours, is high, ranking at the 96th percentile.

These data points indicate that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average in some areas, including labor force participation, earnings growth and construction activity, while unemployment and housing market dynamics are moderate.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, which suggests a labor market that is neither particularly tight nor loose compared to other metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 65.65% ranks higher, at the 70th percentile, indicating a relatively active workforce. Earnings growth over the past year has been 4.7%, situating the city at the 62nd percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.82% is slightly above the national median, at the 60th percentile. These figures suggest a labor market with moderate wage growth and a cost of living that is keeping pace with national trends.

In the housing sector, the annual home price index (HPI) growth of 12.7% places Oklahoma City at the 66th percentile, reflecting a robust but not overheated real estate market. The number of building permits issued has increased by 22.35% year-over-year, a significant rise that ranks the city at the 80th percentile, indicating strong construction activity. However, the days on market metric of 58 days, at the 34th percentile, suggests that while homes are selling, the market is not experiencing the rapid turnover seen in some other cities. The weekly hours worked, at 35.8 hours, is notably high, ranking at the 96th percentile, which may reflect a combination of full-time employment and part-time work patterns.

Overall, these data points indicate an economy that is performing above average, with strengths in labor force participation, earnings growth, and construction activity, balanced by moderate unemployment and housing market dynamics.
