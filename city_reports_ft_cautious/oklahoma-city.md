Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed but generally positive picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.8%, placing it in the 62nd percentile among metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively tight labor market. The labor force participation rate of 66.19% is above average, indicating a robust engagement of the working-age population in the economy. Year-over-year earnings growth of 4.7% aligns with broader national trends, though it is not exceptional, ranking in the 62nd percentile. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, has increased by 4.8% year-over-year, which is slightly below the national average, suggesting that the purchasing power of residents may be holding steady.

Housing market dynamics in Oklahoma City show a significant increase in building permits, up 23.95% year-over-year, which ranks in the 86th percentile and indicates strong construction activity. The housing price index has risen by 10.86% over the same period, placing it in the 66th percentile, reflecting a healthy but not overheated market. The supply of homes for sale has remained stable, with only a 0.2% change year-over-year, while the average days on market stand at 65 days, which is moderate and suggests a balanced market between buyers and sellers.

Overall, the data suggests that Oklahoma City's economy is performing above average, with strengths in labor market participation and housing construction, though inflation and earnings growth are more in line with national trends.
