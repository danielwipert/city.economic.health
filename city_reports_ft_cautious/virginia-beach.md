# Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk

**Grade: C (Very Poor) | 36.2th percentile | April 2026**

---

Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area earns a C grade, ranking 36.2nd out of 50 US metros, with a labor market characterized by critically low participation (58.99% of working-age adults employed or actively looking for work) and a -1.34% year-over-year contraction in total nonfarm employment. Despite this, hourly earnings have risen by 10.22% year-over-year, driven by a shift toward higher-paying jobs rather than broad labor market health.

The housing market is marked by scarcity, with building permits declining sharply by -46.79% year-over-year and homes selling quickly, with an average of 36 days on market and a -5.3% year-over-year change. This rapid pace of sales, combined with a below-average cost of living, suggests that the affordability advantage is eroding as demand outpaces housing supply.

The primary risk for businesses in this market is the scarcity of labor and housing, which may drive up costs and make it difficult to attract and retain talent. With the affordability advantage likely to be short-lived, businesses must carefully consider the long-term implications of locating in this market.
