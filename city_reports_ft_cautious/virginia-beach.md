# Virginia Beach — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metropolitan area's economic landscape, as of the latest available data, presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, placing it at the 50th percentile nationally, indicating a labour market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labour force participation rate of 58.99% is lower, ranking in the 4th percentile, which may reflect broader demographic or regional trends affecting workforce engagement.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 10.22%, placing the region in the 98th percentile, indicating significant wage increases for those employed. Housing market indicators show a complex dynamic. The housing price index has increased by 18.41% year-over-year, placing the region in the 82nd percentile, suggesting a strong appreciation in home values. However, building permits have declined sharply by 42.41% year-over-year, ranking in the 2nd percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity.

The days on market metric stands at 40 days, in the 80th percentile, suggesting a relatively quick turnover in the housing market despite the decline in new permits. The cost of living has risen by 4.53%, and weekly hours worked average 33.1 hours, both metrics being in the middle to lower percentiles, which could suggest a moderate impact on household budgets and work schedules.

Overall, the Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area exhibits a range of economic indicators that highlight areas of strength and concern, with a weighted score of 49 and a grade of B-, indicating a below-average performance relative to other U.S. metropolitan areas.
