# Virginia Beach — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metropolitan area presents a mixed picture. The unemployment rate of 3.6% is in line with national averages, placing it at the 50th percentile among similar regions. Labour force participation is relatively low at 58.99%, ranking in the 4th percentile, indicating a lower proportion of the working-age population is either employed or actively seeking employment.

Earnings growth over the past year has been 10.22%, placing the region in the 98th percentile, suggesting significant wage gains for those who are employed. However, this growth may be partially offset by inflation, as the cost of living has risen by 4.52% over the same period. The housing market is characterised by rising home prices, increasing by 18.41% year-over-year, placing the region in the 82nd percentile. Notably, the number of building permits has declined sharply by 42.41% year-over-year, ranking in the 2nd percentile, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity.

The data suggests a labour market with strong earnings growth but low participation, alongside a housing market characterised by rising prices and reduced new construction. The weighted percentile score of 48.5 and a grade of B- indicate that the Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area is performing below average relative to its peers.
