The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metropolitan area ranks 37th in a recent economic assessment, with an overall grade of B, indicating average performance. The unemployment rate stands at 4.0%, which is slightly above the median percentile score of 48.0, suggesting a labor market that is neither particularly strong nor weak compared to other regions. The labor force participation rate of 58.9% is notably low, placing it in the 4th percentile, which may reflect challenges in engaging the local workforce or structural issues within the labor market.

The days on market for homes have increased to 51 days, in the 92nd percentile, suggesting a cooling in the pace of home sales.

These data points suggest a regional economy with strong wage growth and stable employment, but with some signs of cooling in the housing market and low labor force participation.
