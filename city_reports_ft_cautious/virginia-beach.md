# Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk

**Grade: C+ (Poor) | 38.6th percentile | April 2026**

---

Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area earns a C+ grade, ranking 38.6th out of 50 US metros, with a concerning economic landscape characterized by critically low labor market participation, at 58.99% of working-age adults employed or actively seeking work. The area's nonfarm employment growth has declined by 1.34% year-over-year, and building permits have plummeted by 46.79%, indicating a contracting housing supply.

Despite these challenges, the metro area boasts top-tier wage growth, with hourly earnings rising 10.22% year-over-year. However, this strong wage growth may be masking underlying labor market weaknesses, such as a shift toward higher-paying jobs rather than broad labor market health. The days on market for homes have increased by 2.6% year-over-year, to an average of 40 days, suggesting a slowing housing market that may be indicative of demand erosion rather than a healthy normalization.

In summary, Virginia Beach offers a cost advantage, with above-average affordability, but this comes with significant labor market and housing supply risks. Businesses considering relocation or expansion in this area should be aware of the potential for workforce constraints and affordability stress, driven by the contraction in employment and housing supply, which may outweigh any cost advantages and pose a significant challenge to long-term growth and success.
