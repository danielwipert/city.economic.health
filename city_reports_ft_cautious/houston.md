# Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands

**Grade: B (Average) | 51.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Houston's economy earns a grade of B, ranking 51.5th out of 50 US metros, with a single most important takeaway being its above-average nonfarm employment growth of +0.43% YoY, driven by a growing number of jobs across all sectors. However, this growth is accompanied by an elevated unemployment rate of 4.20%, indicating labor market slack and potentially weakened local consumer demand. Houston's cost advantage, with a cost of living composite score of 1.17, ranking it among the most affordable metros in the dataset, provides a significant offset.

Despite these positive trends, the city's office and professional worker share is remarkably low, ranking in the 6th percentile, posing a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses. The sharp decline in building permits, down 12.89% YoY, raises concerns about a potential housing supply squeeze, which could deteriorate affordability and workforce attraction.

Houston offers businesses a unique combination of affordability and job growth, making it an attractive location for companies looking to expand. However, the primary risk is a potential housing supply squeeze, which could undermine the city's affordability advantage and create challenges for workforce attraction, making it essential for businesses to carefully consider these factors in their location decision.
