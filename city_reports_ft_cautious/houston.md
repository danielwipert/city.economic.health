# Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands

**Grade: B (Average) | 53.5th percentile | April 2026**

---

Houston earns a B grade, with its cost of living composite score of 1.02 ranking in the 96th percentile, making it one of the most affordable metros in the dataset. This affordability offers a strong cost advantage with lower real-wage requirements and higher consumer purchasing power. The city's unemployment rate is 4.20%, signaling labor market slack and weakened local consumer demand.

The housing market shows signs of slowing, with days on market increasing by 11.1% year-over-year, and homes now taking an average of 50 days to sell, ranking in the 60th percentile. Building permit growth is -12.89%, below average, indicating demand erosion rather than a healthy normalization. This is consistent with the city's average employment growth, which fails to offset the declining permit activity.

Houston offers businesses an attractive cost structure, with its high affordability ranking being a major draw. However, the potential demand erosion in the housing market poses a risk, as it could signal broader economic softness and impact local consumer demand. Decision-makers must carefully weigh these factors in their location decision, considering the city's overall grade of B, or 53.5th percentile out of 50 US metros.
