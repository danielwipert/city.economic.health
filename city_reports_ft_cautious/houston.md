# Houston — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

Houston's economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, placing it in the 30th percentile among major metropolitan areas. The labour force participation rate of 64.52% is slightly above average. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.61% is modest, ranking in the 40th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.72% is higher. The occupancy-weighted rent ratio of 0.82 is very low, placing it in the 6th percentile.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.71% is average. However, building permits have declined by 10.61% year-over-year, and the average square footage per new home has decreased by 2.29%. The average days on market for homes is 54 days, which is relatively high. Overall, Houston's weighted percentile score of 46.7% and its grade of B- suggest a below-average performance across key economic indicators.

These data points highlight a range of economic conditions in Houston, with some indicators showing stability and others pointing to potential challenges.

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Houston's recent economic indicators present a mixed picture, reflecting both areas of strength and concern. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, placing it in the 30th percentile among major metropolitan areas, which suggests a relatively stable but not particularly robust labor market. The labor force participation rate of 64.52% is slightly above average, indicating a moderate level of engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.61% is modest, ranking in the 40th percentile, while the cost of living increase of 4.72% is higher, potentially squeezing household budgets. The occupancy-weighted rent ratio of 0.82 is very low, placing it in the 6th percentile, which may reflect either affordability or a soft rental market.

In the housing sector, the year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.71% is average, suggesting steady but not overheated appreciation. However, the decline in building permits by 10.61% year-over-year and the decrease in square footage per new home by 2.29% indicate a slowdown in construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 54 days, which is relatively high, possibly signaling a more balanced or buyer-favoring market. Overall, Houston's weighted percentile score of 46.7% and its grade of B- suggest a below-average performance across key economic indicators.

These data points highlight a range of economic conditions in Houston, with some indicators showing stability and others pointing to potential challenges.
