# Houston — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands metropolitan area ranks fifth in its economic profile, with a weighted percentile score of 46.7, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, slightly above the national average, and the labor force participation rate is 64.52%, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is 2.61%, a modest increase consistent with broader economic trends. Inflation, as measured by the cost of living index, has risen by 4.72% over the past year, which may reflect broader inflationary pressures.

Housing market indicators show mixed signals. The housing price index has increased by 8.71% year-over-year, suggesting continued demand for residential properties. However, the number of building permits has declined by 10.61% compared to the previous year, which could indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days-on-market metric is 54 days, a relatively stable figure suggesting a balanced market. The weekly hours worked average 36.5 hours, near the top of the percentile range, indicating a high level of employment intensity.

The data presents a picture of a cautiously stable economic environment in Houston, with some indicators showing moderate growth and others indicating potential areas of concern.

The days-on-market metric stands at 54 days, which is relatively stable and suggests a balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers have a significant advantage. The weekly hours worked average 36.5 hours, a figure that is near the top of the percentile range, indicating a high level of employment intensity.

Overall, the data suggests a cautiously stable economic environment in Houston, with some indicators showing moderate growth and others indicating potential areas of concern.
