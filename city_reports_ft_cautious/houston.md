# Houston — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The economic data for the Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands metropolitan area presents a mixed picture across various indicators. The unemployment rate of 4.2% places the area in the 30th percentile nationally, indicating a relatively tight labour market but not among the best in the country. The labour force participation rate of 64.52% is in the 54th percentile, suggesting moderate engagement in the workforce. Year-over-year earnings growth of 2.61% is in the 40th percentile, aligning with broader national trends.

Housing market indicators show a year-over-year increase in home prices of 8.71%, placing Houston in the 50th percentile, reflecting stable appreciation. However, the number of building permits has declined by 10.61% year-over-year, which may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The days on market metric stands at 66 days, in the 58th percentile, suggesting a balanced market. Industrial space fundamentals, as indicated by the year-over-year change in square feet leased, have decreased by 2.29%, placing Houston in the 24th percentile, which could indicate a softening in demand for commercial real estate.

Overall, the data suggests that Houston's economy is performing in line with national averages, with some sectors showing signs of moderation.
