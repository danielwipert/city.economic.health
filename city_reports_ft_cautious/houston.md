# Houston — Economic Overview (Cautious FT Style)

## Polished, cautious FT-style column

The Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands metropolitan area ranks fifth in economic performance, with a weighted percentile score of 46.6, placing it in the "Below Average" category. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, which is relatively low but ranks the region in the 30th percentile nationally. This suggests that while joblessness is not a major issue, it may be higher than in some other leading metropolitan areas. The labor force participation rate of 64.52% is slightly above the median, indicating a moderate level of workforce engagement. Year-over-year earnings growth is at 2.61%, a modest increase that aligns with the 40th percentile, reflecting stable wage growth.

In the housing market, the year-over-year change in home prices is 8.48%, placing the region in the 56th percentile. This suggests a steady appreciation in property values. However, the number of building permits has declined by 14.81% year-over-year, placing the region in the 28th percentile. This may indicate a slowdown in new construction activity. The average days on market for homes is 66 days, a figure significantly higher than the national average, placing the region in the 98th percentile. This could suggest a less dynamic housing market or a shift in buyer behavior. The year-over-year change in office space vacancy rates is down by 2.29%, placing the region in the 24th percentile, indicating a slight improvement in commercial real estate utilization.

The data presents a mixed economic picture for Houston, with some indicators showing stability and others pointing to potential areas of concern.
