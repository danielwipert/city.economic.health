Economic Analysis

Cincinnati's economic character is defined by its struggling labor market, earning it a grade of C and ranking 37th out of 50 US metros. The city's nonfarm employment growth is a concerning -0.52% year-over-year, placing it in the bottom tier, and wage growth is a lackluster +1.53% year-over-year, below average. This contraction story is further complicated by a labor force participation rate of 63.08%, indicating a shallow labor pool.

The housing market tells a conflicting story, with building permits surging +57.47% year-over-year, a top-tier performance, and days on market increasing by +21.6% year-over-year, with homes taking an average of 45 days to sell. However, this increase in days on market, combined with weak employment growth, suggests a demand erosion scenario, where fewer qualified buyers are in the market due to economic softness.

Cincinnati offers a business a relatively affordable cost of living composite of 3.72, but the primary risk is a weakening labor market, which may constrain growth and talent acquisition. The city's economic softness, particularly in employment growth, is a significant caveat that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating Cincinnati as a business location.
