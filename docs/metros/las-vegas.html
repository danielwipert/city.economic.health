Economic Analysis

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metro area has an overall grade of A- with a composite score ranking at the 60.7th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score in the top tier at the 86th percentile, and its high wage growth rate of 6.16% year-over-year. The combination of these metrics signals a city with a thriving job market and increasing labor costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is 1.46% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -0.319%, still indicating genuine demand expansion despite the slight decrease in hours. This combination signals that the city is experiencing job growth with hours running slightly below trend, but still above the national average. The labor demand composite score of 6.03 is in the top tier, confirming the city's strong job market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is 5.80%, which is below average, ranking at the 20th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. The practical implication for a business trying to hire here is that they may face some competition for talent, but it's still possible to find qualified candidates.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is 6.16%, which is relatively fast. This signals rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing worker purchasing power. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their budgets to accommodate higher labor costs, but they can also benefit from a more affluent local consumer base.

Cost of Living

Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas has a cost of living percentile rank of 61, indicating that it is slightly above average in terms of affordability. The city's PSF is $265/sqft, with a -2.2% year-over-year change, and the ratio of PSF to hourly earnings is 7.93. This means that the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers, making it an attractive location for talent without requiring significant wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is growing at a rate of 1.52% year-over-year, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire, as it suggests that there will be a growing pool of potential candidates in the future.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas has decreased by 25.89% year-over-year, indicating a tightening of the housing supply. This signals that future affordability and workforce accommodation may be at risk, as the demand for housing may outstrip the available supply.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is 51 days, with a 15.9% year-over-year increase. This indicates a relatively slow market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, the increasing days on market may also signal a normalization of the market after a period of rapid growth.

Office Economy

Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas has a deep professional talent pool, with an office economy percentile rank of 88. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require a high concentration of specialized knowledge workers. On the other hand, the city may be less suitable for businesses in industrial or logistics-dominant sectors.

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with growing demand and increasing wages, making it an attractive location for companies looking to expand. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the tightening housing supply, which may lead to future affordability and workforce accommodation challenges.
