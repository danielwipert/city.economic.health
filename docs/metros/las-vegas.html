Economic Analysis

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metro area has earned an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking at the 57.9th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong wage growth, at 6.16% year-over-year, and its labor demand composite score of 4.68, which ranks at the 63rd percentile. These metrics suggest a city with a growing economy and increasing demand for labor.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate is not available, but the weekly hours deviation from the city's own 12-month baseline is -0.319%, indicating that hours are slightly below trend. However, the labor demand composite score of 4.68 still signals genuine demand expansion, as the combination of employment growth and hours worked suggests a growing economy. This indicates that businesses can expect to find a growing pool of workers, but may need to offer competitive wages to attract top talent.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is 5.80%, ranking at the 23rd percentile, indicating a relatively loose labor market. This means that businesses may find it easier to hire workers, as there is a larger pool of available labor. However, this also suggests that local consumer demand may be weaker due to the higher unemployment rate.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in this city is 6.16%, ranking at the 77th percentile, indicating fast wage growth. This means that employer labor costs are rising, but worker purchasing power is also increasing. Businesses should be prepared to offer competitive wages to attract and retain top talent in this market.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas is relatively affordable, with a percentile rank of 64th, and a price-to-salary ratio of $265/sqft to $33.40/hr, or 7.93. The fact that PSF is falling by 2.2% year-over-year also contributes to the city's affordability. This makes the city an attractive location for businesses looking to recruit talent without having to offer large wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in this city is growing at a rate of 1.52% year-over-year, ranking at the 75th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This means that businesses can expect to find a growing pool of available workers, making it easier to hire and expand operations.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is not available, but the city's ranking at the 50th percentile suggests a near-median level of developer activity. This indicates that housing supply is likely to remain stable, neither expanding nor tightening significantly. As a result, future affordability and workforce accommodation are not likely to be major concerns.

Days on Market

The median days on market is 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of 15.9%, ranking at the 86th percentile. This suggests a slower market, where homes are taking longer to sell. For workers relocating to this city, this means a more accessible and less competitive housing market, making it easier to find a home.

Office Economy

The share of jobs in professional and office sectors is not available, but the city's ranking at the 50th percentile suggests a near-median level of depth in the professional talent pool. This makes the city suitable for a variety of businesses, but perhaps not ideal for those requiring a highly specialized or deep knowledge-economy talent pool.

The Las Vegas-Henderson-North Las Vegas metro area offers businesses a growing economy with increasing demand for labor, a relatively loose labor market, and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this city is the potential for rising labor costs due to fast wage growth, which could impact profitability and competitiveness.
