## Original analysis draft (for reference)

Columbus exhibits a resilient and structurally sound labor market, supported by steady employment growth and low unemployment. The metro’s 3.6% unemployment rate (101A_unemployment), down 0.3 percentage points year-over-year (101A_unemp_yoy_pp), reflects a tightening labor market, while nonfarm payroll growth of 1.17% (107E_employment_growth_yoy) and a strong labor demand composite score (84th percentile) indicate sustained business hiring. However, workforce expansion remains muted, with labor force growth at just 0.35% (102A_clf_yoy), suggesting potential constraints on future output. Wage growth is modest at 1.57% (103B_earnings_yoy), lagging behind national trends and limiting household purchasing power despite relatively stable labor conditions.

Housing market dynamics point to softening demand amid rising inventory duration and construction activity. Residential building permits are up 8.67% (200B_permits_yoy), signaling builder confidence, but homes are taking longer to sell—median days on market has increased 10.81% year-over-year to 41 days (204A_dom_level_days, 204A_dom_yoy_pct), a trend reflected in the 84th percentile DOM composite score. This divergence suggests a rebalancing, possibly due to higher mortgage rates or affordability constraints. Meanwhile, Columbus maintains a moderate cost of living (104C_col: 2.53) and ranks highly in office worker presence (105C_owr: 88th percentile), underpinning commercial real estate demand. A key strength lies in labor demand resilience; a positive weekly hours deviation (0.609%) indicates stable workloads. The main stress point is sluggish wage growth amid rising housing supply and slowing turnover.

**Outlook:** Columbus’ economy remains on a stable trajectory, supported by strong labor demand and moderate living costs. Near-term housing market softness may persist, but solid employment fundamentals and office sector strength provide a buffer. Absent a pickup in wage growth or labor force expansion, long-term growth may moderate.
