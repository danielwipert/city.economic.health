Economic Analysis

Columbus boasts a robust labor market, underpinned by steady employment growth and low unemployment. With a 3.6% unemployment rate, down 0.3 percentage points year-over-year, the metro's labor market is tightening. Nonfarm payroll growth of 1.17% and a strong labor demand composite score, ranking in the 84th percentile, indicate sustained business hiring. However, labor force growth remains subdued at 0.35%, suggesting potential constraints on future output. Modest wage growth of 1.57% lags behind national trends, limiting household purchasing power despite stable labor conditions.

The housing market is showing signs of softening demand, with rising inventory duration and construction activity. Although residential building permits are up 8.67%, homes are taking longer to sell, with median days on market increasing 10.81% year-over-year to 41 days. This trend is reflected in the 84th percentile DOM composite score, suggesting a rebalancing possibly due to higher mortgage rates or affordability constraints. Meanwhile, Columbus maintains a moderate cost of living, with a score of 2.53, and ranks highly in office worker presence, with an 88th percentile ranking. A key strength lies in labor demand resilience, with a positive weekly hours deviation of 0.609% indicating stable workloads. The main challenge is sluggish wage growth, which is exacerbated by rising housing supply and slowing turnover.

**Outlook:** Columbus' economy is expected to remain on a stable trajectory, supported by strong labor demand and moderate living costs. While near-term housing market softness may persist, solid employment fundamentals and office sector strength provide a buffer. However, without a pickup in wage growth or labor force expansion, long-term growth may moderate.
