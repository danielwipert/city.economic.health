Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Columbus's economic indicators suggest a stable labour market, with an unemployment rate of 3.6%, which has decreased by 0.3 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labor force has grown at a modest pace of 0.35% year-over-year, indicating a steady supply of workers. Average hourly earnings have increased by 1.57% year-over-year, although this growth is relatively subdued. The labour demand composite score of 6.55, combined with nonfarm payroll employment growth of 1.17% year-over-year, points to a positive, albeit slow, expansion in job opportunities.

The housing market in Columbus appears to be experiencing some upward pressure, with residential building permits increasing by 8.67% year-over-year. However, the median days on market has risen by 10.81% year-over-year, reaching 41 days, which may indicate a slight slowdown in sales activity. The cost of living composite score of 2.53 suggests that Columbus remains relatively affordable, which could support longer-term economic growth. The office worker ratio composite score of 3.56 is a notable strength, indicating a high proportion of office workers in the city, which can contribute to a vibrant business environment.

Business activity in Columbus is characterized by a strong office sector, as evidenced by the high office worker ratio composite score. The labour demand composite score and nonfarm payroll employment growth also point to a positive outlook for the city's economy. However, the slow growth in average hourly earnings and the modest expansion in the civilian labor force may be areas to watch in the short term. Overall, Columbus's economic indicators suggest a stable and growing economy, with some potential for longer-term expansion driven by its affordable cost of living and strong business activity.

Closing outlook: Columbus is likely to continue experiencing steady economic growth, driven by its stable labour market, affordable cost of living, and strong business environment. While some short-term indicators, such as earnings growth, may be subdued, the city's longer-term prospects appear positive. As the housing market continues to evolve, it will be important to monitor the balance between supply and demand to ensure that affordability is maintained.
