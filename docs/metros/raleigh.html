Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Raleigh's economic indicators suggest a city with a strong labour market, characterized by a low unemployment rate of 3.0% and a modest year-over-year increase of 0.2 percentage points. The civilian labor force has grown at a rate of 0.53% year-over-year, indicating a steady supply of workers. Average hourly earnings have risen by 5.28% year-over-year, outpacing many other cities. The office worker ratio composite score of 4.6 is also notable, suggesting a high demand for office space and a strong presence of white-collar jobs.

The housing market in Raleigh is experiencing significant growth, with residential building permits increasing by 38.82% year-over-year. However, this rapid expansion may be contributing to a slight increase in the median days on market, which has risen by 4.55% year-over-year to 46 days. The cost of living composite score of 1.41 is relatively low, suggesting that Raleigh remains an attractive option for individuals and businesses alike. The labor demand composite score of 6.28 and nonfarm payroll employment growth of 1.25% year-over-year also indicate a strong and growing economy.

Despite these positive trends, there are some potential stress points to consider. The weekly hours deviation from the city's own 12-month baseline is 0.226%, which may indicate some volatility in the labour market. Additionally, the rapid growth in residential building permits may lead to increased pressure on the city's infrastructure and affordability. Overall, however, Raleigh's economic indicators suggest a city with a strong and diverse economy, driven by a talented workforce and a high quality of life.

Closing outlook: Raleigh's economic prospects appear bright, with a strong labour market, growing business activity, and a high quality of life. While there are some potential stress points to monitor, the city's overall economic indicators suggest a positive outlook for the future. As the city continues to grow and evolve, it will be important to balance development with affordability and infrastructure investments to ensure that Raleigh remains a desirable and sustainable place to live and work.
