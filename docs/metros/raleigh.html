Economic Analysis

The Raleigh-Cary metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score ranking in the 78.8th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a top-tier composite score of 94th percentile, and its deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 98th percentile for office and professional worker share. The labor demand composite score of 6.58, driven by a 1.77% employment growth rate and a -0.200% weekly hours deviation, signals genuine demand expansion.

Labor Demand

The Raleigh-Cary metro area has seen a 1.77% employment growth rate year-over-year, combined with a -0.200% deviation in weekly hours from its own trend. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as the city is adding jobs while hours worked are slightly below trend, indicating a healthy and sustainable pace of growth. The labor demand composite score of 6.58 further reinforces this signal, ranking in the top tier at the 94th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Raleigh-Cary stands at 3.50%, ranking in the 77th percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The tight market also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power, potentially driving up labor costs.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Raleigh-Cary is 3.63%, ranking near the median at the 51st percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not at an alarming rate. At the same time, workers are seeing their purchasing power increase, which can have a positive impact on local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Raleigh-Cary has a cost of living percentile rank of 81, indicating that it is more affordable than many of its peer cities. The city's PSF of $218/sqft, which has decreased by 1.4% year-over-year, combined with an average hourly wage of $37.93, results in a ratio of 5.75. This affordability advantage can be a significant talent attraction factor, as workers may not require wage premiums to maintain their standard of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Raleigh-Cary has grown by 1.83% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 87th percentile. This expansion in labor force supply suggests that the city has a growing pool of potential workers, making it easier for businesses to hire and expand their operations. The increasing labor force can also contribute to a more dynamic and diverse local economy.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Raleigh-Cary has increased by 38.82% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 88th percentile. This surge in building permits signals that housing supply is expanding, which can help improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. The increasing supply of housing can also reduce the pressure on the local real estate market, making it more accessible to relocating workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Raleigh-Cary is currently 46 days, with a year-over-year increase of 4.5%. This suggests that the housing market is normalizing, with homes taking slightly longer to sell. While this may not be as competitive as some other markets, it still indicates a relatively fast-paced market, which can be challenging for relocating workers to find suitable housing.

Office Economy

Raleigh-Cary has a deep professional talent pool, with an office and professional worker share ranking in the 98th percentile. This makes the city an attractive location for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require a high concentration of specialized knowledge workers. On the other hand, the city may be less suited for industries that rely heavily on industrial or logistics workers.

The Raleigh-Cary metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong labor demand, a deep professional talent pool, and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the tight labor market and moderate wage growth rate may pose challenges for businesses looking to hire and expand their operations, making it essential for decision-makers to factor in these constraints when considering a location in this city.
