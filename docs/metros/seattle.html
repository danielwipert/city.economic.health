Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Seattle's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 5.0%, which, although relatively high, has increased by 1.4 percentage points year-over-year. This uptick is somewhat mitigated by a robust growth in average hourly earnings, which have risen by 8.83% over the same period. However, the civilian labour force has contracted by 0.65% year-over-year, suggesting a decrease in workforce supply growth.

In terms of business activity, Seattle's office worker ratio composite score is 3.26, indicating a relatively strong presence of office workers. The labour demand composite score is 3.73, which, although not exceptionally high, suggests a stable employment market. However, nonfarm payroll employment has declined by 0.15% year-over-year, which may be a cause for concern. The weekly hours deviation from the city's own 12-month baseline is 0.359%, indicating a slight increase in working hours. On the housing front, residential building permits have decreased by 0.7% year-over-year, while the median days on market have increased by 9.68% year-over-year to 34 days, suggesting a slowing housing market.

The cost of living composite score is 2.8, indicating a relatively high cost of living in Seattle. This, combined with a slowing housing market, may put pressure on affordability and potentially impact the city's attractiveness to businesses and workers. Despite these challenges, Seattle's strong earnings growth and stable labour demand suggest that the city's economy remains resilient. The office worker ratio composite score of 3.26 and labour demand composite score of 3.73 also indicate a strong presence of office workers and a stable employment market.

Closing outlook: Seattle's economy is navigating a period of slowing growth, with rising unemployment and declining workforce supply growth. However, the city's strong earnings growth and stable labour demand suggest that it can weather these challenges. Policymakers and businesses will need to monitor the housing market and cost of living closely to ensure that Seattle remains an attractive destination for workers and businesses. With its strong fundamentals, Seattle is likely to continue to be a major economic hub, but it will need to address its affordability and housing challenges to sustain long-term growth.
