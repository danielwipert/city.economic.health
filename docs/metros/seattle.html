Economic Analysis

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking in the 54.1th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its top-tier wage growth and strong building permits expansion. The city's economic character is most defined by its 7.37% year-over-year wage growth and a 66.30% increase in building permits, signaling a thriving economy with expanding housing supply. These metrics suggest a city with strong labor market dynamics and a growing professional workforce.

Labor Demand

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue area has an employment growth rate of 0.08% and weekly hours 0.670% above its own trend, indicating genuine demand expansion as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. This combination signals that the labor market is experiencing an increase in jobs and working hours, suggesting a healthy and growing economy. The labor demand composite score of 5.08 further supports this, ranking in the 60th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue is 5.10%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 2nd percentile, which means the market is extremely tight with very low unemployment. This tight labor market implies that businesses may face challenges in hiring, with more wage pressure as workers have greater bargaining power. The low unemployment rate suggests that the city's labor market has little slack, making it harder for companies to find and recruit talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue is 7.37%, ranking in the top tier at the 96th percentile, indicating fast and rising labor costs for employers. This strong wage growth is good for worker purchasing power, as employees see significant increases in their earnings. However, for employers, this means a rapidly changing cost environment that may impact profitability and competitiveness.

Cost of Living

Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue has a cost of living score with a PSF of $452/sqft, which has decreased by 3.6% year-over-year, compared to average hourly earnings of $47.57/hr, resulting in a ratio of 9.50, ranking in the 41st percentile. This suggests that the city is near the median in terms of affordability, neither extremely expensive nor highly affordable compared to its peers. The decreasing PSF relative to wages is a positive sign for talent attraction, as it may not require significant wage premiums to compensate for the cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue has contracted by 1.17% year-over-year, ranking in the 30th percentile, indicating a shrinking labor pool. This contraction implies a structural headwind for hiring, as the supply of potential workers is decreasing, which could exacerbate the challenges of finding talent in an already tight labor market.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue has increased by 66.30% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 90th percentile, signaling a significant expansion in housing supply. This rapid growth in permits suggests that developer confidence is high, and future housing supply is expected to increase, which could improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue is 36 days, with a year-over-year increase of 20.0%, ranking in the top tier at the 98th percentile. This indicates a slower market where homes are sitting longer before being sold, which could be beneficial for relocating workers as it provides a more accessible and less competitive housing market.

Office Economy

Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue has a deep professional talent pool, with an office/professional worker share composite score ranking in the top tier at the 84th percentile. This suggests that the city is well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors that require a knowledgeable and specialized workforce. However, it may be less suited for industries that are more industrial or logistics-dominant.

The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, expanding housing supply, and a deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for companies in the knowledge economy. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the extremely tight labor market, which may pose significant challenges in hiring and retaining talent, necessitating strategic planning and potentially higher wage offerings to compete in this market.
