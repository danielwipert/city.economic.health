Economic Analysis

The Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 34.4th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a labor demand composite score of 4.16. This city's economic character is most defined by its low unemployment rate of 3.60% and its struggling labor force growth, which has contracted by -6.97% year-over-year. The combination of these metrics signals a challenging environment for businesses looking to hire and expand.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is -0.10% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -0.605%. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. The negative employment growth rate and below-trend hours suggest that businesses may not be adding new jobs at a significant pace.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is 3.60%, which is relatively low and indicates a tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring new employees, as the pool of available workers is limited. As a result, companies may need to offer competitive wages to attract top talent in this market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is +2.97%, which is below average. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not at an alarming pace. However, workers in this city may not have significant bargaining power, as wage growth is not exceptionally strong.

Cost of Living

Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood has a cost of living percentile rank of 4th, indicating that it is relatively expensive compared to its peers. The city's PSF is $224/sqft, with an 8.7% year-over-year increase, and the ratio of PSF to hourly earnings is 6.88. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums, as the high cost of living may be a deterrent for some workers.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood has contracted by -6.97% year-over-year, indicating that the workforce supply is shrinking. This contraction suggests that businesses may face significant challenges in hiring and expanding their operations in the city, as the available labor pool is decreasing.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is -30.86%, indicating that housing supply is tightening. This decline in building permits signals that future affordability and workforce accommodation may be at risk, as the city's housing stock is not expanding to meet demand.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood is 30 days, with a year-over-year decrease of -3.2%. This relatively fast pace of home sales suggests that the city's housing market is competitive, and relocating workers may face challenges in finding available homes.

Office Economy

Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood has an office economy percentile rank of 10th, indicating that the professional talent pool is relatively shallow. This city is best suited for businesses that do not require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as industrial or logistics companies, and may not be ideal for tech, finance, or consulting firms that rely on specialized office workers.

The Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood metro area offers businesses a relatively low unemployment rate and a moderate wage growth environment, but it is constrained by a shrinking labor force and a tightening housing market. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the city's limited labor pool and high cost of living, which may make it challenging to attract and retain top talent without offering significant wage premiums.
