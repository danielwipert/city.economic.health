Economic Analysis

Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood earns a B- grade, with a below-average economic performance marked by a 4.00% unemployment rate, indicating labor market slack that may weaken local consumer demand. However, the +0.51% nonfarm employment growth YoY suggests a healthy expansion, creating jobs. This tension between labor market slack and job growth defines the city's economic character, making it a complex market for businesses to navigate.

The high cost of living in Grand Rapids, with a composite score of 5.56, is a significant concern, constraining talent acquisition and real consumer spending power. This slowing market could be a stabilizing force, giving buyers more negotiating room and easing affordability pressure.

Grand Rapids offers businesses a unique combination of labor market slack and healthy job growth, making it an attractive location for companies looking to hire. However, the high cost of living and slowing housing market pose significant risks, potentially constraining talent acquisition and consumer spending power.
