Economic Analysis

The San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking 43.7th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score of 3.79 ranking in the 29th percentile, and its high cost of living affordability, with a ratio of 1.09 ranking in the 91st percentile. The labor demand metrics, including employment growth and weekly hours, signal a weak job market.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate is +0.43% year-over-year, and weekly hours are -1.116% below the city's own trend, indicating a labor demand contraction. This combination signals a lack of genuine demand expansion, as hours are below trend despite modest job growth. The labor demand composite score of 3.79 ranks in the 29th percentile, confirming a below-average labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate is 3.70%, ranking in the 49th percentile, indicating a near-median labor market with some slack. This means that businesses may face moderate hiring challenges, but wage pressure is not extreme. The relatively low unemployment rate suggests that the market is not overly loose, but it is not tight enough to drive significant wage inflation.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate is +3.43%, ranking in the 36th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This rate suggests that employer labor costs are rising, but not rapidly, and worker purchasing power is increasing, but not strongly. The moderate wage growth rate may attract businesses seeking to balance labor costs and worker compensation.

Cost of Living

The cost of living ratio of 1.09 ranks in the 91st percentile, indicating that San Antonio-New Braunfels is one of the most affordable cities relative to peers. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums, as the cost of living is relatively low. The high affordability ranking is a significant advantage for talent attraction and retention.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +1.47% year-over-year, ranking in the 79th percentile, indicating an expanding labor force supply. This growth suggests that the hiring capacity is increasing, and businesses may find it easier to attract workers. The positive labor force growth rate is a positive sign for businesses looking to expand their workforce.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is -37.55%, ranking in the 4th percentile, indicating a sharp decline in housing supply expansion. This decline signals a potential future affordability and workforce accommodation challenge, as the housing supply is not keeping pace with demand. The low ranking suggests that businesses may face challenges in attracting workers due to limited housing options.

Days on Market

The median days on market is 61 days, with a year-over-year increase of +1.7%, ranking in the 24th percentile. This indicates a relatively slow market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, the rising days on market may also signal a normalization of the market, rather than a demand erosion.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers is 1.63, ranking in the 28th percentile, indicating a relatively shallow talent pool. This suggests that San Antonio-New Braunfels is not well-suited for businesses requiring a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech or finance companies. However, the city may be more suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The San Antonio-New Braunfels metro area offers businesses a unique combination of affordability and labor force growth, making it an attractive location for companies seeking to balance labor costs and talent attraction. However, the single biggest risk or constraint is the low labor demand and declining housing supply, which may limit the city's ability to accommodate rapid business expansion and attract high-skilled workers.
