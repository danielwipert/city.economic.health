Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic indicators for Virginia Beach suggest a mixed picture, with both positive and negative trends emerging. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, which, although relatively low, has increased by 0.8 percentage points year-over-year. This uptick is accompanied by a decline in the civilian labor force growth rate, with a -2.41% year-over-year change, indicating a potential slowdown in workforce supply growth. However, average hourly earnings have risen by 11.8% year-over-year, suggesting that workers in the city are experiencing significant wage growth.

In terms of business activity, the office worker ratio composite score is 2.4, indicating a moderate level of office worker presence in the city. The labor demand composite score is 1.72, which, although not exceptionally high, suggests some level of employment and weekly hours growth. However, nonfarm payroll employment has declined by -1.34% year-over-year, which may indicate some stress in the labor market. The weekly hours deviation from the city's own 12-month baseline is 0.705%, which is a relatively small deviation, suggesting some stability in working hours. The cost of living composite score is 1.44, which is relatively high, indicating that the city may be experiencing some affordability issues.

The housing market in Virginia Beach is also experiencing some challenges, with residential building permits declining by -46.79% year-over-year. The median days on market has decreased by -5.26% year-over-year, standing at 36 days, which may indicate some level of demand for housing in the city. However, the days on market composite score is 6.47, which suggests that the housing market may be experiencing some stress. Overall, while Virginia Beach has some strengths, such as rising earnings, it also faces challenges in the labor market and housing sector, which may impact its economic growth prospects.

Closing outlook: The economic outlook for Virginia Beach is cautious, with a mix of positive and negative trends. While rising earnings and a low unemployment rate are positives, the decline in workforce supply growth, nonfarm payroll employment, and residential building permits are concerns. The city's high cost of living and stressed housing market also pose challenges. As such, policymakers and businesses in Virginia Beach should focus on addressing these stress points to ensure sustainable economic growth and development.
