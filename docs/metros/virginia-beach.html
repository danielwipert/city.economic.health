Economic Analysis

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking 43.7th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong wage growth, with an 8.49% year-over-year increase in hourly earnings, and its struggling labor demand, with a -0.69% employment growth rate and a labor demand composite score of 2.98, ranking in the 22nd percentile. These metrics signal a complex economic environment with both positive and negative trends.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is -0.69% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating 0.351% above the city's own trend. This combination signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The labor demand composite score of 2.98, ranking in the 22nd percentile, further supports this interpretation.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 4.00%, ranking in the 54th percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This suggests that the market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire, but not as easy as in cities with higher unemployment rates. However, the relatively low unemployment rate still implies some wage pressure.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 8.49%, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating very fast wage growth. This implies rising labor costs for employers, but also strong worker purchasing power. Businesses may need to factor in higher labor costs when considering this location.

Cost of Living

Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk has a cost of living score ranking in the 67th percentile, with a PSF of $221/sqft and average hourly earnings of $35.86, resulting in a ratio of 6.16. This suggests that the city is slightly above average in terms of affordability, but not extremely affordable. The 1.8% year-over-year increase in PSF relative to wages may erode this affordability advantage over time.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is growing at a rate of -0.50% year-over-year, indicating a contracting labor pool. This signals a structural headwind for hiring, as the supply of workers is shrinking. Businesses may face challenges in finding and retaining talent in this environment.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk has decreased by 46.79% year-over-year, ranking in the 0th percentile. This sharp decline signals a tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 36 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 5.3%. This suggests a relatively fast-paced market, which may be challenging for relocating workers to find housing. The low percentile rank of 15th indicates a competitive market.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is relatively low, ranking in the 28th percentile. This suggests that the city may not be the best fit for businesses requiring a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech or finance companies. However, it may be more suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth and a relatively affordable cost of living, but also poses significant challenges, including a contracting labor pool and tightening housing supply. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for decreased affordability and increased competition for housing, which may erode the city's attractiveness to talent and businesses alike.
