Economic Analysis

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 37.7th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong wage growth and relatively affordable cost of living, scoring 98th and 72nd percentiles respectively. The city's economic character is defined by these two metrics, with wage growth at 10.08% year-over-year and a cost of living ratio of 1.59. This combination suggests a city with a high degree of worker purchasing power, but also potential labor cost pressures for employers.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is -0.41% year-over-year, while weekly hours are running 0.302% above trend, resulting in a labor demand composite score of 3.42, which ranks in the 20th percentile. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, as the city is experiencing job losses despite hours being above trend, indicating a survivor squeeze where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles. This scenario suggests that businesses may face challenges in finding talent to fill new positions.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 3.60%, ranking in the 36th percentile, indicating a moderate level of slack in the labor market. This rate suggests that the market is not overly tight, making it relatively easier for businesses to hire, but also implying weaker local consumer demand. As a result, businesses may not face significant wage pressure, but may still need to offer competitive salaries to attract top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 10.08%, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating very strong wage growth. This rapid wage growth implies rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing worker purchasing power, which can drive local economic activity. As a result, businesses may need to budget for higher labor costs, but can also benefit from a more affluent local consumer base.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 1.59, ranking in the 72nd percentile, Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is relatively affordable compared to its peers. This affordability advantage means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums, making it an attractive location for companies looking to relocate or expand. The city's relatively low cost of living can be a major draw for workers and businesses alike.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is contracting at a rate of -2.41% year-over-year, ranking in the 4th percentile, indicating a significant decline in the available workforce. This contraction implies that the labor pool is shrinking, creating a structural headwind for hiring and business expansion. As a result, businesses may face challenges in finding qualified workers to fill open positions.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk has decreased by 46.79% year-over-year, ranking in the 0th percentile, indicating a severe tightening of housing supply. This sharp decline in building permits signals a potential supply squeeze, which can lead to decreased affordability and reduced workforce accommodation in the future. As a result, businesses may need to consider the potential impact of a tightening housing market on their ability to attract and retain talent.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 36 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 5.3%, ranking in the 14th percentile. This relatively fast pace of home sales suggests a competitive market, which can make it challenging for relocating workers to find affordable housing. As a result, businesses may need to consider offering relocation assistance or other incentives to help attract talent to the area.

Office Economy

With an office/professional worker share of 1.69, ranking in the 30th percentile, Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk has a relatively shallow professional talent pool. This suggests that the city is better suited for businesses that do not require a deep pool of specialized office workers, such as industrial or logistics companies. However, businesses in the tech, finance, or consulting sectors may find it more challenging to attract the talent they need.

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth and relatively affordable cost of living, making it an attractive location for companies looking to relocate or expand. However, the city's contracting labor force and tightening housing market pose significant risks to businesses, particularly those that require a deep pool of specialized talent or rely on a steady supply of affordable housing for their workers. As a result, decision-makers should carefully consider these constraints when evaluating the city as a potential business location.
