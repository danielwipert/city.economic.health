Economic Analysis

Virginia Beach's labour market is sending mixed signals, with robust wage growth accompanied by weakening employment and a shrinking workforce. Average hourly earnings have risen 11.8% year-on-year, one of the fastest rates in its peer group, suggesting sector-specific tightness or a repricing of labour following past imbalances. However, the unemployment rate has increased to 3.6%, up 0.8 percentage points from the previous year, while nonfarm payroll employment has declined 1.34% over the same period, indicating moderating labour demand. The civilian labour force has contracted 2.4% annually, one of the weakest national performances, pointing to potential long-term demographic or structural outflows that could constrain growth. Although weekly hours worked remain 0.7% above the 12-month baseline, the labour demand composite is at its lowest percentile, reflecting broad-based softness in hiring momentum.

The housing market has slowed significantly, with residential building permits plummeting 46.8% year-on-year, the weakest rate on record, indicating a substantial near-term contraction in construction. While homes are selling relatively quickly, with a median of 36 days on market, down 5.3% from the previous year, this is largely due to subdued inventory rather than strong demand. The cost of living, measured by a composite of housing and earnings, is moderately high, with a score of 1.44, exerting affordability pressure on middle-income households despite robust nominal wage gains. Office demand, as measured by the office worker ratio, is average, with a score of 2.4, and shows no clear trend towards expansion or contraction.

Looking ahead, near-term headwinds are expected to dominate, particularly in labour supply and construction. Although elevated wage growth may support consumer spending, the structural decline in the workforce and underbuilding in the housing market pose risks to the city's long-term resilience. Without offsetting inflows or policy adjustments, economic momentum is likely to remain subdued.
