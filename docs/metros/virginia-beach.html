Economic Analysis

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 42.9th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong wage growth, with an 8.49% year-over-year increase in hourly earnings, and its weak labor demand, with a -0.69% employment growth rate and a labor demand composite score of 2.98, ranking it in the 20th percentile. These metrics suggest a complex economic environment with both positive and negative trends.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is -0.69% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating 0.351% above the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The labor demand composite score of 2.98, ranking in the 20th percentile, further supports this interpretation.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 4.00%, ranking in the 57th percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This suggests that the market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire, but not as easy as in cities with higher unemployment rates. However, the relatively low unemployment rate may still lead to some wage pressure.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 8.49%, ranking in the 98th percentile, indicating very strong wage growth. This suggests that labor costs for employers are rising rapidly, while worker purchasing power is increasing. Businesses may need to factor in higher labor costs when considering this location.

Cost of Living

Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk has a cost of living score ranking it in the 68th percentile, with a PSF of $223/sqft and an average hourly earnings of $35.86/hr, resulting in a ratio of 6.22. This indicates that the city is slightly above average in terms of affordability, but not extremely affordable. The PSF is increasing by 2.3% year-over-year, which may impact future affordability. This relatively high cost of living may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract talent.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is contracting at a rate of -1.18% year-over-year, ranking in the 16th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is shrinking, which may create structural headwinds for hiring and business expansion. Businesses may need to consider this constraint when evaluating this location.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is decreasing by 46.79% year-over-year, ranking in the 0th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability issues and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. This trend may impact the city's ability to attract and retain talent.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is 35 days, with a year-over-year change of 0.0%. This indicates a relatively slow market, ranking in the 24th percentile. For workers relocating to this city, the housing market may be slightly more accessible, but not extremely competitive.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk is relatively low, ranking in the 26th percentile. This suggests that the city's talent pool is not as deep in these sectors, making it less suited for businesses that require a high concentration of tech, finance, or consulting professionals. However, the city may be more suitable for businesses with industrial or logistics-dominated economies.

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth and a relatively slow housing market, but it also poses significant challenges, including a shrinking labor force and tightening housing supply. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses considering this location is the potential for future affordability issues and workforce accommodation challenges, which may impact their ability to attract and retain talent.
