Economic Analysis

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 45.9th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score in the bottom tier, and its high wage growth, with a year-over-year increase of 7.51%. The labor demand and wage growth metrics are the most critical in understanding this city's current economic situation, with the labor demand score of 1.88 indicating a struggling job market.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in this city is -0.66% year-over-year, and weekly hours are 1.552% above the 12-month trend. This combination signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The low labor demand composite score of 1.88, ranking in the bottom tier, further supports this conclusion.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in this city is 4.00%, ranking near the median at the 54th percentile. This indicates a relatively balanced market, neither extremely tight nor slack. For a business trying to hire in this city, the moderate unemployment rate means that hiring may not be overly challenging, but it also may not be extremely easy, with some wage pressure possible.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in this city is 7.51%, ranking in the top tier at the 88th percentile. This is a fast wage growth rate, indicating rising labor costs for employers and strong purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may face increasing costs, but workers will have more disposable income to spend in the local economy.

Cost of Living

This city has a cost of living score ranking it 67th percentile, indicating that it is slightly above average in terms of affordability. With a PSF of $223/sqft and average hourly earnings of $35.86/hr, the ratio is 6.22, which is above average. However, the PSF is increasing by 2.3% year-over-year, which may impact affordability in the future. The relatively high cost of living percentile means that the city is somewhat affordable, which can be a talent attraction advantage without requiring significant wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in this city is shrinking at a rate of -1.18% year-over-year, ranking in the bottom tier at the 14th percentile. This indicates a contracting labor pool, which can create a structural headwind for hiring and may limit the city's ability to support business growth.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in this city is increasing by 38.19% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 86th percentile. This signals that housing supply is expanding, which can improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce in the future.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 35 days, with a year-over-year change of 0.0%. This indicates a relatively slow market, ranking below average at the 26th percentile. For a worker relocating to this city, the market may be somewhat accessible, but the slow pace of sales may also indicate a lack of demand.

Office Economy

The share of jobs in professional and office sectors in this city is near the median, ranking 50th percentile. This indicates a moderate depth of professional talent pool, making the city suited for a variety of businesses, but not necessarily those requiring a highly specialized or deep knowledge-economy talent pool.

The Virginia Beach-Chesapeake-Norfolk metro area offers a unique combination of high wage growth and expanding housing supply, which can support business growth and attract talent. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker is the low labor demand and shrinking labor force, which can limit the city's ability to support long-term business expansion and create challenges for hiring and workforce development.
