Economic Analysis

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking at the 49.0th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its above-average labor demand, with a composite score at the 70th percentile, and its high cost of living, ranking at the 12th percentile. The labor demand and cost of living metrics are crucial in understanding the city's economic landscape, with employment growth at 0.51% and weekly hours deviation at -0.278%.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 0.51%, and the weekly hours deviation is -0.278%, indicating a genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs, but the hours worked are slightly below trend, suggesting that the growth is not solely driven by increased workloads. The labor demand composite score of 4.74 further supports this notion, ranking at the 70th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 4.70%, ranking at the 31st percentile, indicating a market with some slack. This means that businesses may find it relatively easier to hire workers, but the local consumer demand may be weaker due to the higher unemployment rate. The practical implication for a business trying to hire in this city is that they may have a larger pool of potential candidates to choose from.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 4.61%, ranking at the 62nd percentile, indicating moderate to fast wage growth. This implies that employer labor costs are rising, and worker purchasing power is increasing. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to remain competitive in the market.

Cost of Living

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a cost of living ranking at the 12th percentile, with a PSF of $605/sqft and an earnings ratio of 14.16, making it an expensive city relative to peers. The PSF has decreased by 3.7% year-over-year, which is a positive trend. However, the city's high cost of living may deter talent attraction, as businesses may need to offer wage premiums to compensate for the high cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a year-over-year growth rate of -0.11%, ranking at the 40th percentile, indicating a slightly contracting labor force supply. This implies that the hiring capacity may be limited, and businesses may face challenges in finding skilled workers.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is -0.37%, ranking at the 60th percentile, indicating a slight tightening of housing supply. This signals that future affordability and workforce accommodation may be at risk, as the demand for housing may outstrip the available supply.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 38 days, with a year-over-year increase of 5.6%. This indicates a relatively competitive market, but the rising DOM suggests a healthy normalization. For a worker relocating to this city, the market may be becoming more accessible, but it is still relatively fast-paced.

Office Economy

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a deep professional talent pool, ranking at the 70th percentile, making it well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. However, it may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area offers businesses a unique combination of above-average labor demand and a deep professional talent pool, but the high cost of living and slightly contracting labor force supply pose significant challenges. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker to factor in is the city's expensive cost of living, which may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract and retain talent.
