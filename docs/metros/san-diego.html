Economic Analysis

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area has earned an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking it at the 57.1th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong wage growth, with a year-over-year increase of 6.85%, and its above-average labor demand, driven by employment growth of 0.64% and weekly hours 0.303% above trend. These metrics signal a city with a robust and expanding economy.

Labor Demand

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area has seen employment growth of 0.64% and weekly hours 0.303% above its own 12-month trend, resulting in a labor demand composite score of 5.50, which ranks at the 70th percentile. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as both jobs and hours worked are increasing. This is a positive indicator for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad stands at 4.30%, ranking at the 50th percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This suggests that the labor market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire compared to cities with tighter labor markets. However, this also means that local consumer demand may not be as strong as in areas with lower unemployment rates.

Wage Growth

With a year-over-year wage growth rate of 6.85%, San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad ranks at the 90th percentile, indicating fast and strong wage growth. This implies that labor costs for employers are rising, but it also means that workers have increasing purchasing power, which can benefit local businesses. This strong wage growth is a double-edged sword, offering benefits for workers but potential challenges for employers looking to manage labor costs.

Cost of Living

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a cost of living score that ranks at the 16th percentile, with a price-to-salary ratio of $605/sqft to $43.04/hr, or 14.06. This indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers, as a lower percentile rank suggests higher costs. The fact that PSF is falling by 4.1% year-over-year slightly mitigates this, but overall, the high cost of living may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract talent, potentially offsetting some of the benefits of the area's strong economy.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has contracted by 0.65% year-over-year, ranking at the 28th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is shrinking, which could pose a structural headwind for hiring and business expansion in the area. A contracting labor force can limit the pool of potential employees, making it more challenging for businesses to find the talent they need.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has increased by 12.45% year-over-year, ranking at the 66th percentile. This rise in building permits suggests that housing supply is expanding, which could improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. An increase in housing supply can help mitigate some of the pressures from a high cost of living, making the area more attractive for workers and businesses alike.

Days on Market

Homes in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad currently sit on the market for a median of 38 days, with a year-over-year increase of 2.7%. This ranks at the 50th percentile, indicating a near-median pace of home sales. For workers relocating to this city, the market is neither extremely competitive nor overly accessible, offering a relatively balanced environment for homebuyers.

Office Economy

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has an office economy score that ranks at the 74th percentile, with a share of professional and office workers indicating a deep talent pool. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors that rely on specialized knowledge workers. Conversely, it may be less ideal for industries dominated by industrial or logistics roles.

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area offers businesses a strong economy with expanding labor demand and high wage growth, making it an attractive location for companies looking to tap into a skilled and growing workforce. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the relatively high cost of living, which may necessitate wage premiums to attract and retain talent, potentially offsetting some of the area's economic advantages.
