Economic Analysis

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 51.7th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its above-average labor demand and office economy. The city's labor demand composite score of 4.74, combining a +0.51% employment growth rate and a -0.278% weekly hours deviation, signals genuine demand expansion. The unemployment rate and wage growth also play significant roles in defining the city's economic character, with an unemployment rate of 4.50% and a wage growth rate of +4.61%.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of +0.51% and weekly hours deviation of -0.278% indicate that the city is experiencing genuine demand expansion, with jobs being added and hours running slightly below trend. This combination suggests that the labor market is growing, but not at an unsustainable pace. The labor demand composite score of 4.74, ranking in the 65th percentile, further supports this assessment.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate of 4.50% ranks in the 48th percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This suggests that the labor market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire. However, the relatively low unemployment rate also implies that wage pressure may be moderate, as workers have some bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of +4.61% is above average, ranking in the 61st percentile. This indicates that labor costs for employers are rising, but worker purchasing power is also increasing. The relatively fast wage growth suggests that businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to attract and retain talent.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is relatively high, with a PSF of $605/sqft and an earnings ratio of 14.16, ranking in the 11th percentile. However, the PSF is falling by -4.1% year-over-year, which may help improve affordability. The low affordability score suggests that businesses may need to offer wage premiums to attract talent, as the cost of living is relatively high compared to peers.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +0.35% year-over-year, ranking in the 50th percentile. This indicates that the workforce supply is expanding, but at a moderate pace. The growing labor force suggests that businesses may have access to a slightly increasing pool of potential employees.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is -0.37%, ranking in the 57th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is not expanding rapidly, which may lead to future affordability concerns. The relatively slow growth in building permits may signal that the city's housing market is tightening, making it more challenging for workers to find affordable housing.

Days on Market

The median days on market is 38 days, with a year-over-year increase of +2.7%. This suggests that the housing market is slowing down slightly, making it more accessible for relocating workers. The relatively fast days on market percentile rank of 49th percentile indicates that the market is still competitive, but not extremely so.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers is 3.15, ranking in the 72nd percentile. This indicates that the city has a deep talent pool in professional and office sectors, making it well-suited for tech, finance, and consulting businesses. The city's strong office economy suggests that businesses in these sectors may find it an attractive location.

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area offers businesses a relatively strong labor market and a deep talent pool in professional and office sectors. However, the city's high cost of living and relatively slow growth in building permits may pose challenges for businesses looking to attract and retain talent, making it essential for decision-makers to factor in these constraints when considering the city as a potential location.
