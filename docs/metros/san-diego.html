Economic Analysis

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it at the 49.1th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its above-average labor demand, with a composite score at the 68th percentile, and its high cost of living, ranking at the 11th percentile. The labor demand and cost of living metrics are crucial in understanding the city's economic landscape, with employment growth at 0.51% and weekly hours deviation at -0.278%.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 0.51%, and the weekly hours deviation is -0.278%, indicating a genuine demand expansion. Although the hours deviation is slightly below trend, the positive employment growth rate suggests that the labor market is still experiencing an increase in jobs. This combination signals a moderate labor demand expansion.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 4.70%, ranking at the 35th percentile, indicating a relatively loose labor market. This means that there is some slack in the market, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire. However, the unemployment rate is not low enough to indicate a highly competitive market for workers.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 4.61%, ranking at the 62nd percentile, indicating moderate to fast wage growth. This implies that labor costs for employers are rising, and worker purchasing power is increasing. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to attract and retain talent.

Cost of Living

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a cost of living percentile rank of 11, indicating that it is an expensive city relative to its peers. The PSF to earnings ratio is $605/sqft vs $42.72/hr, resulting in a ratio of 14.16. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums, as the high cost of living may offset the benefits of working in the area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is growing at a rate of -0.11% year-over-year, ranking at the 44th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is contracting, which may pose a structural headwind for hiring in the future. Businesses may need to consider strategies to attract workers from other areas or invest in workforce development programs.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in residential building permits in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is -0.37%, ranking at the 56th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is not expanding rapidly, which may lead to future affordability and workforce accommodation challenges. As a result, businesses may need to consider the potential impact of a tight housing market on their ability to attract and retain workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market in San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad is 38 days, with a year-over-year increase of 5.6%. This indicates a relatively slow market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, the rising days on market may also signal a normalization of the housing market, which could have implications for future affordability.

Office Economy

San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad has a deep professional talent pool, ranking at the 69th percentile. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. However, the city may be less suitable for industries that require a large workforce in industrial or logistics roles.

The San Diego-Chula Vista-Carlsbad metro area offers businesses a unique combination of above-average labor demand and a deep professional talent pool. However, the city's high cost of living and contracting labor force supply pose significant risks and constraints for businesses considering relocation or expansion. Decision-makers should carefully weigh these factors when evaluating the city as a potential location for their business.
