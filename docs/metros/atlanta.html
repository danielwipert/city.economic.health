Economic Analysis

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 73.5th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is largely defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score of 5.82, and its low unemployment rate of 2.80%, which signals a tight labor market. These metrics, combined with a labor force growth rate of 0.86%, indicate a city with a robust and expanding workforce.

Labor Demand

The city's employment growth rate of 0.21% and weekly hours deviation of +1.222% from its own trend suggest genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. This combination signals that the labor market is experiencing organic growth, driven by increasing demand for workers. The labor demand composite score of 5.82, ranking in the 72nd percentile, further supports this assessment.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate of 2.80% indicates a very tight labor market, ranking in the 88th percentile. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is limited, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The tight labor market also implies upward pressure on wages, as workers have more bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of 5.22% is above average, ranking in the 66th percentile. This signals that labor costs for employers are rising, but workers are also experiencing increased purchasing power. The moderate to fast wage growth rate suggests that businesses should factor in rising labor costs when considering relocation or expansion in this city.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 5.36, based on $199/sqft and $37.13/hr, and a percentile rank of 67th, the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. The fact that the PSF is rising by 0.5% YoY, albeit slowly, means that the city's affordability is not rapidly deteriorating. This affordability advantage can help attract talent without requiring significant wage premiums, making it an attractive location for businesses.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of 0.86% year-over-year, ranking in the 80th percentile. This indicates that the workforce supply is expanding, providing businesses with a growing pool of potential employees. The positive labor force growth rate suggests that hiring capacity is increasing, making it easier for businesses to find the talent they need.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in residential building permits is +10.47%, ranking in the 64th percentile. This signals that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. The increase in building permits suggests that the city is investing in its future housing needs, reducing the risk of a supply squeeze and making it more attractive to relocating workers.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 50 days, with a year-over-year increase of 6.4%. This suggests that the housing market is normalizing, with homes taking slightly longer to sell. However, with a percentile rank of 64th, the market is still relatively competitive, and relocating workers may face challenges in finding available housing.

Office Economy

The city's professional and office worker share is 3.36, ranking in the 78th percentile. This indicates a deep talent pool, making it an attractive location for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. The city's strong office economy suggests that it is well-suited for knowledge-intensive industries, but may be less suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a strong labor market, low unemployment, and a growing workforce, making it an attractive location for relocation or expansion. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for upward pressure on wages due to the tight labor market, which may increase labor costs for employers.
