Economic Analysis

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 73.6th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is largely defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score of 78th percentile, and its highly affordable cost of living, ranked at the 88th percentile. These metrics signal a city with a thriving job market and attractive living conditions, with a cost of living ratio of $198/sqft to $37.62/hr, or 5.26.

Labor Demand

The city's employment growth rate of +0.36% YoY and weekly hours deviation of +0.816% from its own trend indicate genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. This combination signals a strong and growing job market, with employers adding jobs and workers putting in more hours. The labor demand composite score of 78th percentile reinforces this assessment, pointing to a city with a robust and expanding workforce.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell is 3.60%, ranking it at the 82nd percentile, which indicates a tight labor market with limited slack. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is relatively small, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The tight market also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power, which could drive up labor costs.

Wage Growth

The city's year-over-year wage growth rate of +5.18% is above average, ranking it at the 66th percentile. This rate of wage growth signals moderate to fast labor cost increases for employers, which could be a challenge for businesses with tight margins. However, it also means that workers have increasing purchasing power, which can benefit local businesses and contribute to a thriving economy.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living percentile rank of 88th, Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell is one of the more affordable cities in the US, relative to its peers. The city's PSF to earnings ratio of $198/sqft to $37.62/hr, or 5.26, indicates that housing costs are relatively low compared to wages. This makes the city an attractive location for businesses looking to recruit talent without having to offer significant wage premiums to offset high living costs.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell is growing at a rate of +1.26% YoY, which indicates that the workforce supply is expanding. This growth in the labor force suggests that businesses will have access to a increasing pool of potential workers, making it easier to hire and staff operations. The growing labor force also points to a city with a strong economy and attractive job market.

Building Permits

The city's residential building permits have decreased by -7.48% YoY, which signals a tightening of the housing supply. This decline in permits may indicate that the city's housing market is becoming less affordable, which could make it more challenging for workers to relocate to the area. The decrease in permits also suggests that developer confidence may be waning, which could have implications for future workforce accommodation and affordability.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell is currently 49 days, with a year-over-year increase of +6.5%. This indicates a relatively slow market, with homes taking longer to sell. For workers relocating to the city, this means that they may have more time to find a home and negotiate a purchase, making the market more accessible.

Office Economy

With an office economy percentile rank of 82nd, Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell has a deep and talented pool of professional workers, making it an attractive location for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors. The city's strong office economy suggests that it is well-suited for businesses that require specialized knowledge workers, but may be less suitable for industries that rely on industrial or logistics workers.

The Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Roswell metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a strong labor market, affordable cost of living, and deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for a wide range of industries. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this city is the potential for a tightening housing market, driven by a decline in residential building permits, which could make it more challenging to attract and retain workers.
