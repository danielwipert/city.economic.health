Economic Analysis

Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin has an overall grade of A- with a composite score ranking at the 59.7th percentile among 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its low unemployment rate of 3.10% and its strong labor force growth of +1.87% YoY. These metrics signal a tight labor market with expanding workforce supply.

Labor Demand

The city's employment growth rate is +0.05% YoY, and weekly hours are deviating +0.024% from its own trend, indicating genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, suggesting a strong labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.39 ranks at the 64th percentile, further supporting this assessment.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate is 3.10%, ranking at the 92nd percentile, indicating a very tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring due to the limited availability of workers, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The tight market implies upward pressure on wages.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate is +1.57%, ranking at the 19th percentile, indicating stagnant wage growth. This slow wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly, but it also implies that workers may have limited bargaining power. The slow wage growth may be a concern for workers' purchasing power.

Cost of Living

The city's cost of living, with a PSF of $260/sqft and average hourly earnings of $35.63/hr, results in a ratio of 7.30, ranking at the 33rd percentile. This indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers. The -1.1% YoY change in PSF suggests some improvement in affordability, but the city still requires wage premiums to attract talent.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +1.87% YoY, ranking at the 90th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth suggests that the city has a strong hiring capacity, with a increasing number of workers available to fill job openings. The expanding labor force is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is -8.94%, ranking near the median at the 46th percentile, indicating a tightening housing supply. This decline in permits suggests that the city's housing supply may not be expanding quickly enough to accommodate growing demand, potentially leading to future affordability concerns and challenges in attracting workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market is 53 days, with a +12.8% YoY increase, ranking at the 50th percentile. This suggests a relatively balanced market, neither extremely competitive nor extremely accessible, for workers relocating to the city. The rising days on market may indicate a healthy normalization of the housing market.

Office Economy

The city's professional and office worker share is 3.60, ranking at the 88th percentile, indicating a deep talent pool. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, but less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

In conclusion, Nashville-Davidson--Murfreesboro--Franklin offers a business a strong labor market with expanding workforce supply, but also presents challenges due to its tight labor market and relatively expensive cost of living. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker is the potential for future affordability concerns and housing supply shortages, which could impact the city's ability to attract and retain workers.
