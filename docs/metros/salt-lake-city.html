Economic Analysis

The Salt Lake City-Murray metro area has an overall grade of A- with a composite score ranking at the 62.7th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with an 84th percentile ranking, and its deep professional talent pool, ranking at the 94th percentile. The labor demand composite score of 6.12, driven by a +1.33% employment growth rate and -0.047% weekly hours deviation, signals genuine demand expansion.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of +1.33% and weekly hours deviation of -0.047% indicate that the city is experiencing genuine demand expansion, with more jobs being added and hours running slightly below trend. This combination suggests that the labor market is robust, with employers hiring and workers having more opportunities. The top-tier ranking of 84th percentile for labor demand composite score reinforces this assessment.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate of 3.60% ranks at the 58th percentile, indicating a near-median level of slack in the labor market. This rate suggests that while the market is not extremely tight, it is still relatively competitive, and businesses may face some challenges in hiring. However, the moderate unemployment rate also implies that there is a pool of available workers, which can be beneficial for companies looking to staff up.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of +4.31% ranks at the 58th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not extremely rapidly, and worker purchasing power is increasing. The moderate wage growth rate may attract businesses that want to balance labor costs with worker satisfaction.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 6.80, which is below average, and a percentile rank of 39th, Salt Lake City-Murray is relatively expensive compared to its peers. The PSF of $266/sqft, which has increased by 0.4% year-over-year, relative to average hourly earnings of $39.12, contributes to this assessment. This means that businesses may need to offer wage premiums to attract talent, as the city's affordability is not a major advantage.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force has contracted by -1.05% year-over-year, ranking at the 36th percentile. This decline in labor force supply may pose a structural headwind for hiring, as the pool of available workers is shrinking. Businesses may need to adapt their recruitment strategies to attract workers from other areas or invest in training and development programs.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in residential building permits is +23.54%, ranking at the 78th percentile. This increase in permits suggests that housing supply is expanding, which may improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. The rising permits also signal developer confidence in the market, which can be beneficial for businesses looking to establish or expand operations.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 45 days, with a year-over-year increase of +2.3%. This relatively moderate days on market, ranking at the 50th percentile, suggests that the housing market is neither extremely competitive nor extremely slow. For workers relocating to this city, the market is relatively accessible, with homes selling at a moderate pace.

Office Economy

With an office/professional worker share ranking at the 94th percentile, Salt Lake City-Murray has a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. The city's strong professional sector makes it an attractive location for businesses in these industries, but it may be less suitable for companies with primarily industrial or logistics-based operations.

The Salt Lake City-Murray metro area offers businesses a strong labor demand, a deep professional talent pool, and an expanding housing supply. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the relatively expensive cost of living, which may require wage premiums to attract talent. Overall, this city is well-suited for businesses that value a robust labor market and a highly skilled workforce, but may need to carefully consider compensation packages to remain competitive.
