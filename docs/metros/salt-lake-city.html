Economic Analysis

The Salt Lake City-Murray metro area has earned an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking it at the 57.1th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a top-tier composite score of 84th percentile, and its deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 94th percentile for office and professional worker share. The labor demand is driven by a 1.33% year-over-year employment growth rate and a -0.047% deviation in weekly hours from its own trend.

Labor Demand

The Salt Lake City-Murray metro area has a labor demand composite score of 6.12, ranking in the top tier at the 84th percentile, driven by a 1.33% year-over-year employment growth rate and a -0.047% deviation in weekly hours from its own trend. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as hours are only slightly below trend during a period of job growth. The strong labor demand indicates a competitive job market with many opportunities for workers.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Salt Lake City-Murray is 3.90%, ranking near the median at the 54th percentile. This rate indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. As a result, businesses may face some wage pressure when trying to attract and retain top talent in this market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Salt Lake City-Murray is 4.31%, ranking near the median at the 58th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively so. At the same time, workers are experiencing an increase in purchasing power, which can contribute to a stronger local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Salt Lake City-Murray has a cost of living ratio of $265/sqft to $39.12/hr, ranking near the median at the 41st percentile. This means that the city is neither extremely affordable nor extremely expensive relative to its peers. However, the fact that the PSF is not falling YoY means that affordability is not improving. As a result, businesses may need to consider wage premiums to attract talent to this city without a significant cost-of-living advantage.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Salt Lake City-Murray is shrinking at a rate of -0.68% year-over-year, ranking below average at the 26th percentile. This contraction in labor force supply poses a structural headwind for hiring, as businesses may face challenges in finding qualified workers to fill open positions.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Salt Lake City-Murray has decreased by 3.28% year-over-year, ranking near the median at the 50th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that housing supply is not expanding rapidly, which may lead to future affordability concerns and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Salt Lake City-Murray is 44 days, with no year-over-year change. This relatively fast pace of home sales indicates a competitive market, which may make it challenging for relocating workers to find and secure housing. However, the stable days on market suggests that the market is not becoming more competitive over time.

Office Economy

Salt Lake City-Murray has a deep professional talent pool, with an office and professional worker share ranking in the top tier at the 94th percentile. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors that require specialized knowledge-economy talent. In contrast, the city may be less suitable for businesses with primarily industrial or logistics-oriented workforces.

The Salt Lake City-Murray metro area offers businesses a strong labor demand and a deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy industries. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the shrinking labor force supply, which poses a structural challenge for hiring and may require businesses to adapt their recruitment strategies to attract and retain top talent in this competitive market.
