Economic Analysis

Salt Lake City earns an A+ grade, with a single most important takeaway being its exceptional economic character, reflected in a low unemployment rate of 3.40% and a labor force participation rate of 72.77%. The city's labor market is both healthy and deep, with nonfarm employment growth at 1.83% year-over-year, signaling rapid job creation and an economically expanding market. This momentum is underscored by a top-tier office and professional worker share, ranking in the 98th percentile, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses and headquarters.

The city's housing market, however, presents a challenge. With days on market at 49 days and decreasing by 15.5% year-over-year, Salt Lake City is among the fastest-moving markets, driven by above-average employment growth. This fast-paced market, coupled with a cost of living that is below average, may pose a relocation challenge for new hires competing for limited housing stock. Above-average building permit growth offers some relief, but does not entirely mitigate the housing competition.

In summary, Salt Lake City offers businesses a thriving economy with a deep labor pool and rapid job creation, making it an excellent location for growth-oriented companies. However, the primary risk lies in the highly competitive housing market, which may hinder talent attraction and retention due to the challenges new hires face in securing affordable housing in a fast-moving market.
