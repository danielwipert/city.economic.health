Economic Analysis

The Providence-Warwick metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 43.6th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low unemployment rate of 4.30% and its high cost of living, with a PSF to wages ratio of 9.95. The labor demand composite score of 4.64, near the median, also plays a significant role in shaping the city's economic landscape.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Providence-Warwick is +0.60% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -0.502% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a moderate labor demand expansion, as the job growth is positive but hours are slightly below trend, indicating some slack in the labor market. This suggests that while there is some genuine demand for labor, it is not overly strong.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Providence-Warwick is 4.30%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 16th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. The practical implication for a business trying to hire in this city is that it may face some competition for talent, but not to the extent that it would in a market with even lower unemployment.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Providence-Warwick is +3.51%, ranking near the median at the 46th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively so. At the same time, worker purchasing power is increasing, which can have a positive impact on local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Providence-Warwick has a cost of living percentile rank of 0, indicating that it is one of the most expensive cities relative to peers, with a PSF to wages ratio of 9.95 and a PSF increase of +9.0% year-over-year. This means that the city is at a significant disadvantage when it comes to talent attraction, as businesses may need to offer wage premiums to compensate for the high cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Providence-Warwick is growing at a rate of +1.69% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 92nd percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire in the city. The implication is that the city has a growing pool of potential workers, making it easier for businesses to find the talent they need.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Providence-Warwick has increased by +137.37% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 98th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is expanding rapidly, which is a positive sign for future affordability and workforce accommodation. As the housing supply increases, it is likely to put downward pressure on housing costs, making the city more attractive to workers and businesses.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Providence-Warwick is 30 days, with a year-over-year change of +0.0%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 10th percentile. This indicates a very competitive market, where homes are selling quickly. For a worker relocating to this city, this means that the housing market is challenging, and they may need to act quickly to secure a home.

Office Economy

Providence-Warwick has an office economy percentile rank of 68, indicating a deeper professional talent pool than average. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, but less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses. The city's professional workforce share of 3.14 suggests a strong foundation for knowledge-economy businesses.

The Providence-Warwick metro area offers businesses a growing labor force and expanding housing supply, but its high cost of living and competitive housing market are significant drawbacks. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker to factor in is the need to offer wage premiums to attract and retain talent in this expensive city, which could increase labor costs and impact profitability.
