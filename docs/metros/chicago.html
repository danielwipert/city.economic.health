Economic Analysis

The Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 39th out of 50 US metros. Specifically, the -0.15% employment growth rate and the lack of data on weekly hours deviation signal a need for closer examination of labor market dynamics.

The employment growth rate in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is -0.15% year-over-year, indicating a slight contraction in job numbers. Without data on weekly hours deviation, it's challenging to determine if this contraction is due to genuine demand issues or a survivor squeeze where remaining workers absorb the load of eliminated roles. This combination suggests caution in interpreting labor demand, as it may not be as strong as other metros.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin stands at 5.30%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 15th percentile. This indicates a labor market with some slack, making it relatively easier for businesses to hire compared to tighter markets. However, this also suggests weaker local consumer demand, which could impact businesses reliant on local spending.

Wage Growth

Wage growth in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is at +3.55% year-over-year, ranking near the median at the 50th percentile. This moderate wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers are increasing at a steady pace, while workers see a modest improvement in their purchasing power. This balance could support a stable business environment.

Cost of Living

Although specific cost of living metrics are not provided, the city's near-median ranking suggests it is neither exceptionally affordable nor expensive compared to its peers. Data on the year-over-year change in residential building permits is not available, making it difficult to assess whether housing supply is expanding or tightening.
