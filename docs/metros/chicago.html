Economic Analysis

The Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking at the 45.0th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its labor demand, which is near median, and its unemployment rate, which is at 5.10% and ranks in the bottom tier at the 16th percentile. The combination of these metrics suggests a mixed economic environment.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is +0.08% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating +0.475% from the city's own 12-month baseline, resulting in a labor demand composite score of 4.89, which is near median at the 58th percentile. This combination signals a genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. However, the growth rate is relatively slow, indicating a moderate pace of labor market expansion.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is 5.10%, which ranks in the bottom tier at the 16th percentile, indicating a relatively high level of unemployment compared to other metros. This suggests that the labor market has some slack, making it easier for businesses to hire workers. However, the practical implication is that local consumer demand may be weaker due to the higher unemployment rate.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is +4.27%, which is near median at the 56th percentile. This rate indicates moderate wage growth, which is likely to result in rising labor costs for employers. At the same time, workers will benefit from increased purchasing power, which can have a positive impact on local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Chicago-Naperville-Elgin has a cost of living score that ranks near median at the 53rd percentile, with a price-to-salary ratio of $217/sqft to $37.11/hr, resulting in a ratio of 5.85. This suggests that the city is neither extremely affordable nor extremely expensive compared to its peers. The relatively moderate cost of living means that businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to attract talent, but it also does not provide a strong competitive advantage in terms of affordability.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is contracting at a rate of -0.92% year-over-year, which ranks below average at the 20th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is shrinking, which can create a structural headwind for hiring and may limit the city's ability to support rapid business growth.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin has increased by +29.19% year-over-year, which ranks above average at the 78th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is expanding, which can improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. This trend is likely to have a positive impact on the city's ability to attract and retain workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Chicago-Naperville-Elgin is 34 days, with a year-over-year increase of +3.0%. This ranks below average at the 34th percentile, indicating a relatively fast-paced market. For workers relocating to this city, the housing market may be competitive, making it challenging to find and secure a home quickly.

Office Economy

Chicago-Naperville-Elgin has a professional and office worker share that ranks below average at the 34th percentile, with a composite score of 1.98 out of 5. This suggests that the city has a relatively shallow talent pool in professional and office sectors, making it less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting firms.

The Chicago-Naperville-Elgin metro area offers businesses a mixed economic environment with moderate labor demand and wage growth, but also a shrinking labor force and a relatively high unemployment rate. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses considering this location is the limited labor force growth, which can create challenges for hiring and talent acquisition.
