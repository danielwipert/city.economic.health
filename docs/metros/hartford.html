Economic Analysis

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area has earned an overall grade of A, ranking in the 65.2th percentile among 50 US metros, with a strong labor demand and low unemployment rate defining its current economic character, at 92nd and 98th percentiles, respectively. The labor demand composite score of 6.41 and unemployment rate of 2.70% signal a thriving job market. These metrics suggest a city with genuine demand expansion and a tight labor market.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of +1.88% and weekly hours deviation of -0.519% indicate a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion, as hours are slightly below trend during a period of job growth. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs at a rapid pace, with a slight adjustment in hours worked. The labor demand composite score of 6.41 reinforces this assessment, placing the city in the top tier.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate of 2.70% is extremely low, indicating a very tight labor market with little slack. This means that businesses trying to hire in this city will face significant competition for talent, potentially driving up wages. With an unemployment rate percentile rank of 98th, the market is highly competitive.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of +0.60% is relatively stagnant, suggesting that labor costs for employers are not rising rapidly. However, this also means that worker purchasing power is not increasing significantly, which could impact local consumer demand. The 8th percentile rank for wage growth indicates that the city's wage growth is slower than most peers.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living percentile rank of 39th, the city is moderately affordable, as evidenced by the PSF of $254/sqft, which is decreasing at a rate of -1.6% YoY, relative to average hourly earnings of $39.66/hr. This affordability can be a talent attraction advantage, as workers may not require significant wage premiums to maintain their standard of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +2.77% year-over-year, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth suggests that the city has a strong hiring capacity, with a increasing pool of potential employees. The 94th percentile rank for labor force growth reinforces this assessment.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is +6.72%, indicating an expansion of housing supply. This growth suggests that the city is likely to see improving affordability and a more accommodating environment for relocating workers. The 60th percentile rank for building permits indicates a moderate level of developer confidence.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 25 days, with a year-over-year decrease of -16.7%. This rapid sales pace indicates a highly competitive market, which may make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing. The 0th percentile rank for days on market suggests an extremely fast-paced market.

Office Economy

With an office economy percentile rank of 46th, the city has a moderately deep professional talent pool, suited for businesses that require a mix of office and non-office skills. The city is likely well-suited for businesses that require a balance of professional and non-professional roles, but may not be the best fit for businesses that require a highly specialized office workforce.

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with low unemployment and expanding workforce supply, making it an attractive location for companies looking to hire and grow. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the highly competitive labor market, which may drive up wages and make it challenging to attract and retain talent.
