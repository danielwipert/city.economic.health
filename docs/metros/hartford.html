Economic Analysis

The Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 68.6th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its exceptional labor demand, with a composite score in the 98th percentile, and its low unemployment rate of 2.70%, which places it in the 94th percentile. The combination of these two metrics suggests a highly competitive job market with strong demand for labor.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford is 1.88% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by +0.495%. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as both jobs and hours worked are increasing, indicating a strong and growing labor market. This is a positive sign for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford is 2.70%, which is in the 94th percentile, indicating a very tight labor market. This means that there is little slack in the market, making it challenging for businesses to hire new employees. As a result, businesses may face upward pressure on wages to attract and retain top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford is +0.13%, which is in the 6th percentile, indicating stagnant wage growth. This slow wage growth means that labor costs for employers are not increasing rapidly, but it also implies that workers may have limited bargaining power and purchasing power. This could have implications for businesses relying on consumer spending.

Cost of Living

Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford has a cost of living ratio of 2.70, which places it in the 48th percentile, indicating a near-median affordability level. This means that the city is neither extremely affordable nor extremely expensive compared to its peers. As a result, businesses may not need to offer significant wage premiums to attract talent, but they also may not have a significant advantage in terms of cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford is growing at a rate of 2.77% year-over-year, which is in the 94th percentile, indicating a rapidly expanding labor force. This means that the supply of workers is increasing, which could ease hiring challenges for businesses in the area. This is a positive sign for businesses looking to expand their operations.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford has decreased by 12.70% year-over-year, which is in the 32nd percentile, indicating a tightening housing supply. This decrease in building permits suggests that the housing supply may not be expanding rapidly enough to accommodate a growing workforce, which could lead to affordability challenges and constraints on workforce growth.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford is 39 days, with a year-over-year increase of 30.0%, which is in the 98th percentile, indicating a slower market. This means that homes are taking longer to sell, which could make it easier for relocating workers to find housing. However, this could also be a sign of a normalization in the market after a period of rapid growth.

Office Economy

Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford has an office economy percentile rank of 43rd, indicating a moderate depth of professional talent. This city is well-suited for businesses that require a mix of office and non-office workers, but may not be the best fit for businesses that require a highly specialized or deep talent pool, such as tech or finance companies.

In conclusion, Hartford-West Hartford-East Hartford offers businesses a highly competitive labor market with strong demand for labor, a growing labor force, and a moderate cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this area is the tightening housing supply, which could lead to affordability challenges and constraints on workforce growth, making it essential for decision-makers to factor this into their location decisions.
