Economic Analysis

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 50.8th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong labor demand and labor force growth, which are at 92nd and 88th percentiles, respectively. The city's economic character is defined by its top-tier employment growth rate of 1.08% and weekly hours deviation of +1.057%, indicating genuine demand expansion. With a labor demand composite score of 6.87, the city is experiencing a significant increase in job creation.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of 1.08% and weekly hours deviation of +1.057% signal genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. This combination indicates that the city is experiencing a strong increase in labor demand, with businesses hiring more workers and existing employees working more hours. The labor demand composite score of 6.87 is in the top tier, at the 92nd percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate of 4.50% is relatively high, ranking at the 8th percentile, indicating a market with some slack. This means that businesses may find it easier to hire workers, as there is a larger pool of available labor. However, the higher unemployment rate also suggests weaker local consumer demand, which could impact businesses that rely on local spending.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of 2.81% is below average, ranking at the 36th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This means that employer labor costs are rising, but at a slower pace, and worker purchasing power is increasing, but not rapidly. The moderate wage growth suggests a relatively stable labor cost environment.

Cost of Living

The city has a cost of living percentile rank of 74th, with a PSF of $224/sqft and average hourly earnings of $33.47/hr, resulting in a ratio of 6.69. This indicates that the city is more affordable than many of its peers, with a lower cost of living. The fact that PSF is falling by 3.5% YoY relative to wages suggests that the city is becoming even more affordable, which is a talent attraction advantage.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of 1.07% year-over-year, ranking at the 88th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This means that businesses have a growing pool of potential workers to hire from, making it easier to find talent. The positive labor force growth rate is a significant advantage for businesses looking to expand their operations.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is -40.79%, ranking at the 2nd percentile, indicating a sharp decline in housing supply. This suggests that the city may face a supply squeeze in the future, which could impact affordability and workforce accommodation. The decline in building permits is a concern for businesses that rely on a steady supply of housing for their workers.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 70 days, with a year-over-year increase of 7.7%, ranking at the 22nd percentile. This indicates a slower market, where homes are taking longer to sell. For workers relocating to the city, this means a more accessible and less competitive housing market, making it easier to find a home.

Office Economy

The city has an office economy percentile rank of 64th, with a share of professional and office workers of 2.98. This indicates a deep talent pool in professional and office sectors, making the city well-suited for tech, finance, and consulting businesses. However, the city may be less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion and a growing workforce supply. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the sharp decline in building permits, which could lead to a supply squeeze and impact affordability and workforce accommodation in the future.
