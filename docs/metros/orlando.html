Economic Analysis

Orlando's economy earns a C- grade, with a labor market exhibiting notable slack and a housing sector under pressure, characterized by a 4.40% unemployment rate and stagnant wage growth of +0.37% YoY, ranking in the bottom tier.

The housing market is experiencing a slowdown, with building permits plummeting by -35.64% YoY, and days on market increasing by +11.7% YoY, with homes taking an average of 67 days to sell, placing Orlando in the bottom tier for housing market speed. This scenario suggests demand erosion rather than a healthy normalization, given the lackluster employment growth of +0.24% YoY.

Orlando's elevated unemployment rate and stagnant wage growth pose significant challenges for businesses, while the slowing housing market and demand erosion could dampen consumer spending and overall economic vitality. The above-average share of office and professional workers, ranking in the 67th percentile, offers a mild positive for office-dependent businesses, but this is overshadowed by the broader economic and housing market concerns.
