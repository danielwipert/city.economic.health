Economic Analysis

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking at the 49.2th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its high wage growth rate of 6.90% year-over-year and its low unemployment rate of 4.50%, which signals a tight labor market. The combination of these two metrics suggests that the city is experiencing strong labor demand, but also faces challenges in terms of hiring and wage pressure.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 0.12% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by 0.409%. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as hours are running above trend during a period of job growth. However, the labor demand composite score is near median at 56th percentile, indicating that the city's labor market is not exceptionally strong.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 4.50%, which is relatively low and indicates a tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is small, and may need to offer higher wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests that there may be upward pressure on wages.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 6.90%, which is relatively high and indicates strong labor cost growth for employers. This also means that workers in the city have good purchasing power, which can drive local consumer demand. However, the high wage growth rate may also lead to increased labor costs for businesses.

Cost of Living

Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater has a cost of living score that ranks at the 82nd percentile, indicating that it is relatively affordable compared to other cities. The city's PSF is $242/sqft, which is decreasing by 2.8% year-over-year, and the average hourly earnings are $37.36/hr, resulting in a ratio of 6.48. This affordability advantage can help attract talent without requiring wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is growing at a rate of 0.14% year-over-year, which is near median and indicates that the workforce supply is slowly expanding. This moderate growth rate suggests that hiring capacity is not severely constrained, but may not be exceptionally strong either.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater has decreased by 12.90% year-over-year, which signals that housing supply is tightening. This decrease in building permits may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation, as the supply of new housing units is not keeping pace with demand.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is currently 67 days, and has increased by 15.5% year-over-year. This indicates a slower market, which can make it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, the relatively low percentile rank of 12th suggests that the market is still relatively competitive.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is near median, ranking at the 52nd percentile. This suggests that the city has a moderate-sized talent pool suited for tech, finance, and consulting businesses, but may not be as deep as in other cities. The city is best suited for businesses that require a general professional workforce, but may not be ideal for those requiring highly specialized talent.

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, low unemployment, and relatively affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the tightening housing supply, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation.
