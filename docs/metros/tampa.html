Economic Analysis

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it at the 44.1th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its high wage growth rate of 8.26% year-over-year and its low unemployment rate of 5.10%, which ranks in the bottom tier at the 6th percentile. These metrics suggest a competitive labor market with rising labor costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is -0.28% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating 0.192% above the city's own trend. This combination signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The labor demand composite score of 3.72 ranks near the median at the 40th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 5.10%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 6th percentile. This indicates a tight labor market with little slack, making it harder for businesses to hire and potentially leading to upward wage pressure. Companies looking to staff up in this city may face challenges in finding available talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 8.26%, ranking in the top tier at the 96th percentile. This fast wage growth rate implies rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing purchasing power for workers. Businesses operating in this city should be prepared for a higher cost environment.

Cost of Living

Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater has a cost of living score ranking it in the top tier at the 83rd percentile, with a price-to-salary ratio of $243/sqft to $38.09/hr, or 6.38. The city's PSF is falling by 1.6% year-over-year, making it more affordable relative to peers. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset the cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is contracting at a rate of -0.72% year-over-year, ranking below average at the 25th percentile. This implies a shrinking labor pool, which may pose a structural headwind for hiring and business expansion in the city.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is falling by 8.56% year-over-year, ranking near the median at the 48th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 66 days, with a year-over-year increase of 15.8%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 9th percentile. This indicates a slower market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city, but potentially less competitive for sellers.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater ranks below average at the 27th percentile, with a composite score of 1.92. This suggests a relatively shallow talent pool for businesses requiring specialized knowledge-economy workers, making the city less suited for tech, finance, or consulting headquarters decisions.

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area offers businesses a unique combination of high wage growth and a relatively affordable cost of living, but the single biggest risk or constraint is the tight labor market and shrinking labor force, which may limit hiring capacity and drive up labor costs. Decision-makers should carefully weigh these factors when considering a location decision in this city. Overall, the city's competitive labor market and rising labor costs may pose challenges for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.
