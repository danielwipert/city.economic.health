Economic Analysis

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 53.6th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong wage growth and affordable cost of living. The city's economic character is defined by a wage growth rate of +6.90% YoY and a cost of living ratio of $241/sqft to $37.36/hr, resulting in a top-tier affordability score. These metrics suggest a city with a competitive labor market and attractive living costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate is +0.12% YoY, combined with a +0.409% deviation in weekly hours from the city's own trend, signaling a genuine demand expansion. This combination indicates that the city is experiencing modest job growth, with hours worked above the trend, suggesting that businesses are increasing their workforce and workload. The labor demand composite score of 4.88 places the city near the median, indicating a stable but not exceptionally strong labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate is 4.70%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 6th percentile, indicating a tight labor market with limited slack. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is relatively small, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate is +6.90%, placing the city in the top tier at the 92nd percentile, indicating fast and rising labor costs for employers. This strong wage growth is good for worker purchasing power, as employees can afford more goods and services, but it may pose challenges for businesses trying to control labor costs. The rapid wage growth suggests a competitive labor market where businesses must offer attractive compensation packages to retain and attract talent.

Cost of Living

The city has a cost of living ratio of $241/sqft to $37.36/hr, with a top-tier affordability score of 86th percentile, indicating that it is more affordable relative to peers. The fact that PSF is falling by -3.2% YoY suggests that housing costs are becoming more manageable, making the city an attractive location for talent without requiring significant wage premiums. This affordability advantage can be a major draw for businesses looking to relocate or expand.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +0.02% YoY, placing the city above average at the 64th percentile, indicating a slowly expanding workforce supply. This modest growth suggests that the city's labor pool is increasing, but at a slow pace, which may not keep up with demand from growing businesses. The implication is that hiring capacity may be limited, and businesses may need to invest in training and development to attract and retain talent.

Building Permits

The city is experiencing a +1.87% YoY increase in residential building permits, placing it near the median at the 54th percentile, indicating a modest expansion of housing supply. This growth suggests that developer confidence is present, and future housing supply is likely to increase, which can improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. However, the moderate pace of growth may not be enough to meet demand, potentially leading to future affordability challenges.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 67 days, with a +11.7% YoY increase, placing the city below average at the 20th percentile, indicating a relatively fast-paced market. This means that homes are selling quickly, making it challenging for relocating workers to find housing, and potentially driving up prices. The rising DOM suggests a competitive market, but the relatively low absolute level of days on market indicates that the city's housing market is still relatively accessible.

Office Economy

The city has an office economy percentile rank of 52nd, indicating a near-median depth of professional talent pool, suited for a mix of industries, but not exceptionally strong in tech, finance, or consulting. The city's economy is likely to be more diverse, with a mix of industrial, logistics, and office sectors, making it a good fit for businesses that require a broad range of skills and talents.

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area offers businesses a competitive labor market, attractive living costs, and a growing workforce, making it an appealing location for relocation or expansion. However, the single biggest risk or constraint is the tight labor market, which may pose challenges for hiring and retaining talent, and the moderate pace of housing supply growth, which may lead to future affordability challenges.
