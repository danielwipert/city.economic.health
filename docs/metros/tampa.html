Economic Analysis

Tampa's economic character is defined by its severe labor market weaknesses, with an unemployment rate of 4.60% and a critically low labor force participation rate of 59.95%, making it a challenging location for businesses and individuals. However, the city's cost of living composite score of 1.83 indicates a below-average cost of living, which could be an attractive feature.

Despite these challenges, Tampa's economy is not without its strengths. The city's nonfarm employment growth of +0.92% YoY and wage growth of +4.86% YoY suggest a healthy expansion, with solid job growth and increasing wages. However, the housing market is slowing, with days on market increasing by 15.8% YoY to an average of 66 days.

Tampa offers businesses an affordable cost of living and a growing economy with solid job growth, but the city's labor market weaknesses and slowing housing market pose significant risks. The primary caveat for businesses considering relocation to Tampa is the potential for labor shortages and housing market instability, which could undermine the city's economic advantages.
