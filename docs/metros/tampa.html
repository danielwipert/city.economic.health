Economic Analysis

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 53.6th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong wage growth and affordable cost of living, scoring 92nd and 86th percentiles respectively. The city's economic character is defined by these two metrics, with a wage growth rate of 6.90% and a cost of living ratio of $241/sqft to $37.36/hr, indicating a relatively affordable environment. This combination suggests a city with a strong labor market and attractive conditions for workers.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 0.12% year-over-year, combined with a weekly hours deviation of 0.409% above its own 12-month baseline, signaling a genuine demand expansion. This indicates that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, suggesting a healthy and growing labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.88 places the city near the median, 56th percentile, indicating a stable but not exceptionally strong labor demand.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 4.70%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 6th percentile, indicating a tight labor market with limited slack. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is relatively small, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests that workers have more bargaining power, which could drive up labor costs.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 6.90%, placing it in the top tier at the 92nd percentile, indicating fast and rising labor costs for employers. This strong wage growth is good for worker purchasing power, as employees see their earnings increase, but it may pose a challenge for businesses looking to keep labor costs under control. The high wage growth rate suggests that the city is an attractive location for workers, but may require businesses to adjust their compensation packages accordingly.

Cost of Living

Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater has a cost of living ratio of $241/sqft to $37.36/hr, with the price per square foot falling 3.2% year-over-year, making it an affordable city, ranking in the top tier at the 86th percentile. This means that the city has a talent attraction advantage, as workers can afford to live and work in the area without requiring significant wage premiums. The falling price per square foot relative to wages is a key driver of the city's high affordability score.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is growing at a rate of 0.02% year-over-year, placing it above average at the 63rd percentile, indicating a slowly expanding workforce supply. This means that the city has a growing pool of potential workers, which can help support business growth and hiring needs. However, the slow growth rate may not be enough to keep up with demand, potentially leading to labor shortages in certain industries.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is increasing by 1.87% year-over-year, ranking near the median at the 54th percentile, indicating a moderate expansion of housing supply. This suggests that the city is experiencing a gradual increase in housing development, which can help improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. However, the moderate growth rate may not be enough to keep up with demand, potentially leading to future affordability challenges.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater is 67 days, with a year-over-year increase of 11.7%, ranking below average at the 20th percentile, indicating a relatively fast-paced market. This means that homes are selling quickly, making it a competitive market for workers relocating to the city, who may face challenges in finding affordable housing. The fast-paced market may require workers to act quickly to secure housing, potentially driving up prices.

Office Economy

Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater has a professional and office worker share of 2.66, ranking near the median at the 52nd percentile, indicating a moderately deep talent pool. This suggests that the city is suited for businesses that require a mix of professional and office workers, but may not be the best fit for companies that require a highly specialized or technical workforce. The city's office economy is relatively balanced, making it a viable location for a range of businesses.

The Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with attractive conditions for workers, driven by its high wage growth and affordable cost of living. However, the city's tight labor market and low unemployment rate may pose challenges for businesses looking to hire, making it essential to offer competitive wages and benefits to attract talent. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this city is the potential for labor shortages and rising labor costs, which could impact their ability to grow and operate effectively.
