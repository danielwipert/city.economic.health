Economic Analysis

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 68.6th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. This city's economic character is largely defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score of 6.66, and its high share of office and professional workers, ranking in the 82nd percentile. The labor demand is driven by a combination of employment growth and hours worked above trend, signaling genuine demand expansion.

Labor Demand

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area has seen employment growth of 1.08% year-over-year, combined with a 0.846% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend, resulting in a top-tier labor demand composite score of 88th percentile. This combination signals a genuine demand expansion, with more jobs being added and hours worked above trend. This is a positive indicator for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is 3.50%, ranking in the 71st percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract top talent. However, this also suggests a strong local economy with low unemployment.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is 2.06%, ranking in the 28th percentile, indicating relatively slow wage growth. This means that employer labor costs are not rising rapidly, but worker purchasing power is also not increasing significantly. As a result, businesses may not face significant pressure to increase wages, but may also not see a significant boost in consumer demand.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is relatively affordable, with a percentile rank of 76th, and a price-to-salary ratio of $221/sqft to $37.49/hr, or 5.89. The fact that the PSF is falling by 1.8% year-over-year relative to wages is a key driver of this affordability. This makes the city an attractive location for businesses looking to recruit talent without having to offer significant wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia has grown by 0.17% year-over-year, ranking in the 69th percentile, indicating a slowly expanding workforce supply. This means that businesses may have a slightly easier time finding workers, but the growth rate is not rapid enough to significantly alleviate hiring challenges.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia has increased by 9.08% year-over-year, ranking in the 60th percentile, indicating an expanding housing supply. This suggests that the city is likely to see improved affordability and a more accommodating environment for relocating workers in the future.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is currently 47 days, with a year-over-year increase of 6.8%, ranking in the 74th percentile. This indicates a relatively slow market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, the rising days on market may also signal a normalization of the market after a period of rapid growth.

Office Economy

The share of office and professional workers in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is high, ranking in the 82nd percentile, indicating a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. This makes the city an attractive location for businesses in these sectors, but may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented workforces.

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area offers businesses a strong labor market, affordable cost of living, and a deep talent pool, making it an attractive location for a variety of industries. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the relatively tight labor market, which may require businesses to offer competitive wages to attract top talent.
