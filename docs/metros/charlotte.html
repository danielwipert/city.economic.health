Economic Analysis

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score ranking in the 68.8th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score in the top tier at the 88th percentile, driven by a 1.08% year-over-year employment growth rate and a 0.846% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend. The combination of these metrics signals genuine demand expansion, indicating a healthy and growing job market.

Labor Demand

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area has seen a 1.08% year-over-year employment growth rate, paired with a 0.846% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend, resulting in a top-tier labor demand composite score. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, indicating that the city is experiencing a period of job growth with increasing hours worked. This is a positive sign for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia stands at 3.50%, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. With an unemployment rate below the national average, businesses may face challenges in finding available talent, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract top candidates. This tight market also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power, potentially driving up labor costs.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is 2.06%, ranking in the 28th percentile, indicating relatively slow wage growth. This moderate pace of wage growth may help keep labor costs in check for employers, but it also means that workers may not see significant increases in their purchasing power. As a result, businesses may need to consider other benefits or incentives to attract and retain top talent.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of $221/sqft to $37.49/hr, and a year-over-year decrease of 1.8% in PSF, Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia ranks in the 76th percentile for affordability. This means that the city is relatively more affordable compared to its peers, making it an attractive location for businesses looking to relocate or expand without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset high living costs. The decreasing PSF relative to wages is a key driver of this affordability.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia has grown by 0.17% year-over-year, ranking in the 70th percentile, indicating a slowly expanding workforce supply. While this growth is modest, it suggests that the city's labor pool is not contracting, providing a stable foundation for businesses looking to hire and expand their operations. However, the slow pace of growth may limit the availability of new talent in the market.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia has increased by 9.08% year-over-year, ranking in the 60th percentile, indicating an expansion of housing supply. This growth in building permits suggests that the city's housing market is likely to become more affordable in the future, making it easier for workers to relocate and for businesses to attract talent. The increasing supply of housing also reduces the risk of a housing shortage constraining workforce growth.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia is 47 days, with a year-over-year increase of 6.8%, ranking in the 74th percentile. This indicates a relatively slow and accessible market for workers relocating to the city, giving them more time to find suitable housing. The rising days on market also suggests a healthy normalization of the housing market, rather than a demand-driven slowdown.

Office Economy

With an office and professional worker share ranking in the 82nd percentile, Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia has a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. The city's strong office economy makes it an attractive location for businesses in these sectors, but it may be less suited for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented workforces. The high percentile rank indicates a significant competitive advantage in attracting and retaining knowledge-economy talent.

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong labor demand, a relatively affordable cost of living, and a deep talent pool in the office and professional sectors. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers to factor in is the relatively tight labor market, which may drive up labor costs and require competitive wages to attract top talent.
