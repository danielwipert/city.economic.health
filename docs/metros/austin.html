Economic Analysis

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 70.5th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its top-tier wage growth and cost of living, with a wage growth rate of 6.47% and a cost of living ratio of 6.19, indicating a highly affordable city. These metrics suggest a city with strong labor market dynamics and attractive living conditions.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 0.98% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -1.488% from the city's own trend. This combination signals a moderate labor demand expansion, with some genuine demand for labor but also a slight decrease in hours worked, indicating a relatively balanced labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.18 is near the median, suggesting a stable labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 3.70%, which corresponds to an above-average percentile rank of 77th. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire new employees. However, the unemployment rate is still low enough to suggest some wage pressure and competition for talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 6.47%, which is in the top tier of US metros. This fast wage growth rate implies rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to remain competitive in this market.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 6.19, which is in the top tier of US metros at the 96th percentile, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is a highly affordable city. The PSF of $241/sqft is decreasing by 7.0% year-over-year, making the city even more attractive for talent without requiring wage premiums. This affordability advantage can be a significant draw for businesses looking to relocate or expand.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is growing at a rate of 2.39% year-over-year, which is in the top tier of US metros. This expanding labor force supply suggests a positive hiring capacity, with a growing pool of potential employees available to businesses. As a result, companies can expect to find the talent they need to support their growth.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is -29.44%, which is in the bottom tier of US metros. This sharp decline in permits suggests a tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future. This could pose a risk to workforce accommodation and talent attraction.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 53 days, with a year-over-year increase of 20.4%. This rising days on market indicates a slowing market, making it more accessible for relocating workers to find housing. However, the market is still relatively competitive, and businesses may need to provide relocation assistance to support their employees.

Office Economy

With an office/professional worker share of 4.50, which is in the top tier of US metros at the 96th percentile, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has a deep and specialized talent pool. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, but less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong labor market dynamics, attractive living conditions, and a deep talent pool. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future, posing a risk to workforce accommodation and talent attraction.
