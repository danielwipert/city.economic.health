Economic Analysis

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 68.5th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its top-tier wage growth of 6.75% year-over-year and its highly affordable cost of living, with a PSF to wages ratio of 6.41, ranking it in the 92nd percentile. These metrics signal a strong and attractive labor market.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 0.63% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -0.498% from the city's own trend. This combination signals a near median labor demand, indicating neither strong expansion nor contraction. The labor demand composite score of 4.68 further supports this assessment, suggesting a stable but not exceptionally strong job market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 3.40%, ranking it in the 78th percentile, indicating a tight labor market with relatively low unemployment. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the market has less slack, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. The low unemployment rate also suggests that workers have more bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 6.75%, ranking it in the 86th percentile, indicating fast and strong wage growth. This implies that employer labor costs are rising, but workers also have increasing purchasing power. The rapid wage growth may attract high-skilled workers but also increases the cost burden for businesses.

Cost of Living

Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has a cost of living percentile rank of 92, indicating that it is highly affordable relative to its peers. The PSF to wages ratio of 6.41, with PSF decreasing by 7.7% year-over-year, suggests that the city offers a competitive advantage in talent attraction without requiring significant wage premiums. This affordability is a major draw for businesses and workers alike.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is growing at a rate of 0.81% year-over-year, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This growth suggests that the city has a increasing pool of potential workers, making it easier for businesses to hire and expand their operations.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is -19.65%, indicating a tightening housing supply. This decline in permits suggests that the city may face future affordability challenges and workforce accommodation constraints, potentially impacting its attractiveness to businesses and workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of 15.9%, ranking it in the 86th percentile. This indicates a slower market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. The rising days on market suggests a healthy normalization of the housing market, rather than a demand erosion.

Office Economy

Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has a deep professional talent pool, ranking in the 92nd percentile, making it well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. The city's strong office economy is a major advantage for businesses requiring specialized knowledge workers, but it may be less suitable for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented workforces.

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, affordable cost of living, and a deep professional talent pool, making it an attractive location for businesses in the knowledge economy. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the tightening housing supply, which may impact future affordability and workforce accommodation, potentially eroding the city's competitive advantage.
