Economic Analysis

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A, ranking in the 65.4th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its top-tier cost of living and strong office economy. The city's economic character is defined by its high cost of living percentile rank of 94th and its office/professional worker share ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a deep talent pool. With a wage growth rate of 5.84% year-over-year, this city is poised for continued economic expansion.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 1.36% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -1.509% from the city's own 12-month baseline, resulting in a labor demand composite score of 4.70, which ranks in the 57th percentile. This combination signals a moderate expansion of genuine demand, as the city is adding jobs, albeit with hours slightly below trend. This suggests a relatively stable labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 3.20%, ranking in the 77th percentile, indicating a tight labor market with relatively low unemployment. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer competitive wages to attract talent. As a result, wage pressure is likely to be higher in this market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 5.84%, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicating relatively fast wage growth. This implies that employer labor costs are rising, but worker purchasing power is also increasing, which can have positive effects on local consumer demand. As a result, businesses may need to factor in higher labor costs when operating in this city.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living percentile rank of 94th, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is one of the more affordable cities relative to its peers, with a price per square foot to hourly earnings ratio of 1.02. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset high housing costs, providing a talent attraction advantage. As a result, this city is an attractive location for businesses looking to hire and retain workers.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is growing at a rate of 1.01% year-over-year, ranking in the 62nd percentile, indicating a moderate expansion of the workforce supply. This suggests that the city has a relatively stable hiring environment, with a growing pool of available workers. As a result, businesses are likely to have a relatively easy time finding and hiring talent.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in residential building permits in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is -29.44%, ranking in the 11th percentile, indicating a significant tightening of the housing supply. This signals that future affordability and workforce accommodation may be at risk, as the city's housing stock is not expanding to meet demand. As a result, businesses may need to factor in potential housing cost increases when considering this location.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 53 days, with a year-over-year increase of 20.4%, ranking in the 71st percentile. This suggests that the housing market is normalizing, with homes taking slightly longer to sell, making it relatively more accessible for workers relocating to the city. As a result, businesses may find it easier to attract and retain workers who can find and purchase homes in a relatively stable market.

Office Economy

With an office/professional worker share ranking in the 96th percentile, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has a deep and talented pool of professional workers, making it an ideal location for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors. This city is well-suited for businesses that require specialized knowledge-economy talent, but may be less suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a talented workforce, affordable cost of living, and strong office economy, making it an attractive location for businesses looking to expand or relocate. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers to factor in is the tightening housing supply, which may lead to increased housing costs and decreased affordability, potentially affecting the city's attractiveness to workers and businesses alike.
