Economic Analysis

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 75.8th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is largely defined by its exceptional labor force growth, with a year-over-year increase of 3.00%, and its strong wage growth, with a 7.75% year-over-year change in average hourly earnings. These metrics signal a thriving economy with a growing and well-compensated workforce.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 1.13% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -1.208%. This combination signals a genuine demand expansion, as the city is adding jobs and hours are running above trend, indicating a strong labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.67 further supports this assessment, ranking the city in the 62nd percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 3.70%, ranking the city in the 80th percentile. This indicates a tight labor market with limited slack, making it challenging for businesses to hire new employees. As a result, companies may face upward pressure on wages to attract and retain top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 7.75%, placing the city in the 92nd percentile. This rapid wage growth signals a strong labor market with rising labor costs for employers. However, it also translates to increased purchasing power for workers, making the city an attractive location for businesses that value a skilled and motivated workforce.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of $241/sqft to $38.61/hr, resulting in a ratio of 6.24, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos ranks in the 96th percentile for affordability. This means that the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers, making it an attractive location for talent without requiring significant wage premiums. The 7.7% year-over-year decrease in PSF further enhances the city's affordability.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is growing at a rate of 3.00% year-over-year, ranking the city in the 98th percentile. This expansion in labor force supply indicates a growing pool of potential employees, making it easier for businesses to hire and expand their operations.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is -19.65%, ranking the city in the 22nd percentile. This decline in permits signals a tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for workforce accommodation in the future.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of 15.9%. This ranks the city in the 86th percentile, indicating a slower market. For workers relocating to the city, this means a more accessible and less competitive housing market.

Office Economy

With an office and professional worker share of 4.34, Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos ranks in the 92nd percentile, indicating a deep and skilled talent pool. This makes the city an ideal location for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but less suited for industries with limited office-based roles.

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a growing and skilled workforce, rapid wage growth, and relatively low cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the declining housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for workforce accommodation in the future.
