Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Austin's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.2%, with a modest year-over-year increase of 0.1 percentage points. While this suggests a relatively stable jobs market, the civilian labour force growth rate of 1.01% is somewhat subdued, indicating a potential slowdown in workforce supply expansion. Average hourly earnings, however, have risen by 4.49% year-over-year, outpacing many other cities and suggesting a strong demand for labour.

In terms of business activity, Austin's labour demand composite score of 4.4 and nonfarm payroll employment growth rate of 0.73% year-over-year indicate a moderate expansion. However, the weekly hours deviation from the city's 12-month baseline is -0.918%, suggesting a slight decline in working hours. The office worker ratio composite score of 2.48 is also relatively low, which may indicate some challenges in attracting and retaining office-based businesses. On the housing front, residential building permits have declined by 29.44% year-over-year, while the median days on market have increased by 20.45% to 53 days, indicating a potential slowdown in the housing market.

Despite these mixed signals, Austin's overall economic grade is a B+, indicating an above-average performance. The city's cost of living composite score of 1.35 and relatively high percentile score of 79.2 suggest that it remains an attractive location for businesses and individuals. However, the decline in residential building permits and increase in days on market may be stress points to watch in the coming months. Closing outlook: Austin's economy is likely to continue growing, albeit at a moderate pace, driven by its strong labour market and attractive business environment. However, policymakers and businesses should monitor the housing market and labour force growth to ensure that the city remains competitive and sustainable in the long term.
