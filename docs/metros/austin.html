Economic Analysis

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area has earned an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 71.8th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is largely defined by its strong wage growth, with a year-over-year increase of 6.47%, and its highly affordable cost of living, with a PSF to earnings ratio of 6.19, ranking it in the 95th percentile. These metrics signal a city with a thriving economy and attractive living conditions.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 0.98% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -1.488%. This combination signals a genuine demand expansion, as the city is adding jobs and hours are running above trend, indicating a strong labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.18 further supports this assessment, ranking the city in the 62nd percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 3.70%, ranking it in the 75th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market, with some slack but generally favorable conditions for employers. For a business trying to hire in this city, the tight market may lead to slightly higher wage pressure and increased competition for top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 6.47%, ranking it in the 87th percentile. This rapid wage growth signals a strong labor market with rising labor costs for employers, but also increasing worker purchasing power. As a result, businesses may face higher labor costs, but workers will have more disposable income to spend locally.

Cost of Living

Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos has a cost of living percentile rank of 95, indicating that it is highly affordable relative to its peers. The PSF to earnings ratio of 6.19, with a PSF of $241/sqft and earnings of $38.91/hr, further supports this assessment. This affordability advantage will be a significant talent attraction factor, as workers can maintain a high standard of living without requiring excessive wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is growing at a rate of 2.39% year-over-year, ranking it in the 92nd percentile. This expanding labor force supply will provide businesses with a growing pool of potential employees, making it easier to hire and staff operations.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is -29.44%, ranking it in the 12th percentile. This decline in permits signals a tightening housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future. This could pose a risk to workforce accommodation and attraction.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is 53 days, with a year-over-year increase of 20.4%. This indicates a relatively accessible market for workers relocating to the city, as homes are not selling extremely quickly. However, the rising days on market may signal a normalization of the market, rather than a demand erosion.

Office Economy

The office economy in Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos is highly developed, with a professional and office worker share ranking in the 96th percentile. This deep talent pool makes the city an ideal location for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but may be less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The Austin-Round Rock-San Marcos metro area offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, affordable living conditions, and a highly developed office economy. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the declining housing supply, as signaled by the sharp drop in building permits, which may lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for housing in the future.
