Economic Analysis

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 41.8th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its near median labor demand composite score of 3.98 and its bottom tier labor force growth rate of -1.44% YoY. These metrics signal a challenging environment for businesses looking to expand or relocate.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is -0.23% YoY, and weekly hours are deviating -0.241% from the city's own 12-month baseline, resulting in a near median labor demand composite score. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. The negative employment growth rate and hours deviation suggest a slowdown in the labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 4.20%, ranking it near median at the 47th percentile. This rate indicates a relatively balanced market, neither extremely tight nor slack. For a business trying to hire in this city, the moderate unemployment rate means that hiring may not be overly competitive, but it also may not be extremely easy to find qualified candidates.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is +3.68%, ranking it near median at the 48th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not extremely rapidly. As a result, worker purchasing power is increasing, but not at an exceptional rate.

Cost of Living

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler has a cost of living score ranking it near median at the 47th percentile, with a PSF of $272/sqft and average hourly earnings of $37.45/hr, resulting in a ratio of 7.26. The city's PSF is falling -1.8% YoY, making it relatively more affordable. This means that the city has a talent attraction advantage, as workers can afford to live there without requiring significant wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is contracting at a rate of -1.44% YoY, ranking it in the bottom tier at the 12th percentile. This decline in labor force supply signals a structural headwind for hiring, making it more challenging for businesses to find qualified candidates.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is falling -9.77% YoY, ranking it below average at the 38th percentile. This decline in building permits signals a tightening of the housing supply, which may lead to decreased affordability and make it more difficult for workers to relocate to the city.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 57 days, with a YoY increase of +9.6%, ranking it below average at the 38th percentile. This means that homes are sitting on the market for a relatively longer period, making it a more accessible market for workers relocating to the city.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 2.64, ranking it near median at the 56th percentile. This indicates a moderately deep talent pool, making the city suitable for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but less ideal for those in industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area offers a relatively balanced labor market and a moderate cost of living, making it an attractive location for businesses that can navigate its challenges. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the city's contracting labor force supply, which may limit hiring capacity and require businesses to adapt their recruitment strategies to succeed in this market.
