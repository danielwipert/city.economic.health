Economic Analysis

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it at the 47.5th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its below-average labor demand, with a composite score of 3.46, and its near-median wage growth of 4.00% year-over-year. The labor demand and unemployment metrics, with a rate of 4.40%, are key indicators of the city's current economic state.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 0.22% year-over-year, while weekly hours are deviating -1.153% from the city's own trend. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. The negative hours deviation suggests that the labor market is not as strong as the modest employment growth might suggest.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 4.40%, which is below average, ranking at the 29th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire in this city. As a result, businesses may face some wage pressure when trying to attract and retain talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 4.00%, which is near the median, ranking at the 58th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively, while worker purchasing power is increasing at a steady pace. This balance is likely to support a stable business environment.

Cost of Living

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler has a cost of living score ranking at the 52nd percentile, with a price-to-salary ratio of $272/sqft to $37.34/hr, or 7.28. This near-median ranking indicates that the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. The fact that PSF is falling -1.1% year-over-year relative to wages suggests that the city is becoming more attractive for talent without requiring significant wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is growing at a rate of 1.27% year-over-year, which is above average, ranking at the 77th percentile. This expanding labor force supply is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire, as it indicates a growing pool of potential workers. This growth is likely to support the city's economic development and provide a competitive advantage for businesses.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is increasing by 16.86% year-over-year, which is above average, ranking at the 74th percentile. This expansion in housing supply is a positive sign for future affordability and workforce accommodation, suggesting that the city is likely to remain attractive to workers and businesses alike.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 54 days, with a year-over-year increase of 5.9%. This below-average ranking, at the 28th percentile, indicates a relatively fast-paced market, which may make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing. However, the rising days on market could also signal a healthy normalization of the housing market.

Office Economy

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler has an office economy ranking at the 54th percentile, with a share of professional and office workers indicating a moderately deep talent pool. This city is best suited for businesses that can leverage a generalist workforce, but may not be ideal for those requiring highly specialized or niche talent, such as tech or finance companies.

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area offers businesses a relatively affordable cost of living and a growing labor force, but the below-average labor demand and tight labor market may pose challenges for hiring and talent retention. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the potential for wage pressure and competition for talent in a market with moderate unemployment and rising labor costs.
