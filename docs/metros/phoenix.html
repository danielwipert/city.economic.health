Economic Analysis

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area earns an A- grade, driven by a low 3.50% unemployment rate, 66.34% labor force participation rate, and 4.52% year-over-year wage growth. This strong labor market, with a top-tier ranking in office and professional worker share at the 88th percentile, provides a deep professional talent pool.

However, beneath this surface, nuances and tensions emerge. Nonfarm employment growth lags at +0.87% year-over-year, a risk signal worth watching. The days on market for homes are increasing by +5.9% year-over-year, indicating a slowing housing market. This trend, combined with below-average employment growth, suggests demand erosion rather than a healthy normalization.

Phoenix offers businesses a talented workforce, a relatively affordable cost of living, and a growing housing supply. However, the primary risk is demand erosion, driven by below-average employment growth, which could impact the city's long-term economic momentum.
