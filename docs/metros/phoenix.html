Economic Analysis

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area earns a B+ grade, ranking 58.8th among 50 US metros, driven by a healthy labor market with a 3.50% unemployment rate and 66.34% labor force participation. This indicates a constructive and engaged workforce, making it an attractive location for knowledge-economy businesses. The area also boasts a deep professional talent pool, ranking 88th percentile for office and professional worker share.

However, beneath this positive surface, there are nuances and tensions worth exploring. The nonfarm employment growth rate of +0.87% YoY is below average, ranking 38th percentile, suggesting economic deceleration and a risk signal worth watching. The days on market are increasing by 5.9% YoY, indicating a slowing market, which may be a sign of demand erosion, particularly given the weak employment growth.

Phoenix offers a business a talented and engaged workforce, a competitive housing market, and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the primary risk is the potential for economic deceleration, driven by below-average employment growth, which may impact the demand for housing and ultimately, the overall attractiveness of the location for businesses.
