Economic Analysis

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 45.3th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its above-average labor demand, with a composite score of 5.36, and its below-average labor force growth, which is contracting at a rate of -2.53% year-over-year. The labor demand is driven by employment growth of 0.62% and weekly hours 0.193% above trend.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of 0.62% and weekly hours deviation of 0.193% above trend signal genuine demand expansion in the Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area. This combination indicates that the city is experiencing an increase in jobs and hours worked, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to locate here. The labor demand composite score of 5.36 ranks in the 68th percentile, further supporting the notion of a strong labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 3.80%, which ranks in the 43rd percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This rate suggests that the market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire workers. However, the relatively low unemployment rate also means that businesses may face some competition for top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 3.26%, which ranks in the 42nd percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively so. At the same time, workers are experiencing some increase in purchasing power, which can be beneficial for local businesses.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler, with a PSF of $271/sqft and average hourly earnings of $37.62, results in a ratio of 7.20, ranking in the 49th percentile. This indicates that the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. The fact that PSF is falling by 1.8% year-over-year further supports the city's affordability. This can be a talent attraction advantage for businesses, as workers may not require wage premiums to afford the cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is contracting at a rate of -2.53% year-over-year, which ranks in the 6th percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is shrinking, which can be a structural headwind for hiring. Businesses may face challenges in finding qualified workers, particularly in industries with high labor demands.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is -16.51%, which ranks in the 30th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is tightening, which can lead to future affordability issues and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. This trend may negatively impact the city's ability to attract and retain workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 60 days, with a year-over-year increase of 3.5%. This ranks in the 38th percentile, indicating a relatively slow market. For workers relocating to this city, the slower market may make it easier to find a home, but it also suggests that the housing market is not as competitive as in other cities.

Office Economy

The share of professional and office workers in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 3.14, ranking in the 68th percentile. This indicates a deep talent pool in the professional and office sectors, making the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting industries. However, the city may be less suitable for industries with high logistics or industrial demands.

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area offers businesses a relatively affordable cost of living and a deep talent pool in the professional and office sectors. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses locating in this city is the shrinking labor force supply, which can make it challenging to find qualified workers and may limit the city's ability to accommodate a growing workforce.
