Economic Analysis

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 41st out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its near-median labor demand and labor force growth, with a labor demand composite score of 3.98 and a labor force growth rate of -1.44% YoY. The combination of these metrics, along with a wage growth rate of +3.68% YoY, suggests a mixed economic environment.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is -0.23% YoY, and weekly hours are deviating -0.241% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. The negative employment growth rate and hours deviation suggest that the labor market is experiencing a slowdown.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 4.20%, which is near the median. This rate suggests that the labor market has some slack, making it slightly easier for businesses to hire. However, the relatively low unemployment rate also implies that there may be some upward pressure on wages, as workers have more bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is +3.68%, which is near the median. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively. At the same time, workers are experiencing an increase in purchasing power, which can have a positive impact on local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler has a cost of living score with a PSF of $272/sqft, which is decreasing by -1.8% YoY, and an average hourly earnings of $37.45/hr, resulting in a ratio of 7.26. With a percentile rank of 49th, the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. This affordability can be a talent attraction advantage, as workers may not require wage premiums to maintain their standard of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is contracting at a rate of -1.44% YoY. This negative growth rate indicates that the labor force supply is shrinking, which can create a structural headwind for hiring. Businesses may face challenges in finding qualified workers, as the pool of available labor is decreasing.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is decreasing by -9.77% YoY. This decline suggests that housing supply is tightening, which can lead to decreased affordability and increased competition for workforce accommodation. The reduction in building permits may signal a future supply squeeze, making it more challenging for workers to find affordable housing.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is 57 days, with a YoY increase of +9.6%. This increase in days on market suggests that the housing market is slowing down, making it more accessible for relocating workers to find homes. However, the relatively low days on market still indicates a competitive market, and workers may face challenges in finding suitable housing.

Office Economy

The office economy in Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler has a professional worker share of 2.63, ranking 56th percentile. This suggests that the city has a relatively deep talent pool in professional and office sectors. Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler is well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting industries, but may not be as attractive for industrial or logistics-dominant companies.

The Phoenix-Mesa-Chandler metro area offers a mixed economic environment, with a relatively affordable cost of living and moderate wage growth. However, the city's contracting labor force and tightening housing supply pose significant risks for businesses, particularly those relying on a large and growing workforce. The single biggest constraint for decision-makers is the shrinking labor pool, which can create challenges in finding and retaining qualified workers.
