Economic Analysis

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking at the 58.4th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with a composite score at the 78th percentile, and its high wage growth rate of 5.42% year-over-year. The combination of these metrics suggests a city with a thriving job market and rising labor costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is 0.46% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by -0.050%. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as the city is adding jobs and hours are running slightly below trend, indicating a healthy and sustainable labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.89 further supports this assessment, ranking at the 78th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is 4.40%, ranking at the 60th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. As a result, businesses may face some wage pressure when trying to attract and retain talent in this market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is 5.42%, ranking at the 70th percentile. This represents fast wage growth, which implies rising labor costs for employers and strong purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may need to budget for higher labor costs, while workers will have increased disposable income to spend in the local economy.

Cost of Living

San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont has a cost of living percentile rank of 23, indicating that it is an expensive city relative to its peers. The PSF to earnings ratio is $654/sqft vs $50.48/hr, resulting in a ratio of 12.96. This suggests that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums, as the high cost of living may offset the benefits of working in the area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is contracting at a rate of -0.98% year-over-year, ranking at the 19th percentile. This indicates a shrinking labor pool, which may pose a structural headwind for hiring and business expansion in the area. As a result, businesses may need to consider alternative locations or strategies to attract talent.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is 86.01%, ranking at the 96th percentile. This represents a significant expansion in housing supply, which may improve affordability and accommodation for the workforce in the future. As a result, the city may become more attractive to businesses and workers alike, as the housing market becomes more accessible.

Days on Market

The median days on market in San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont is 29 days, with a year-over-year decrease of -6.5%. This indicates a highly competitive market, where homes are selling quickly. As a result, workers relocating to the city may face challenges in finding and securing housing, which could impact their decision to move to the area.

Office Economy

San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont has a deep professional talent pool, with an office economy percentile rank of 67. This suggests that the city is well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require specialized knowledge-economy talent. However, the city may be less suitable for businesses in industrial or logistics-dominant sectors, which require different types of skills and expertise.

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area offers businesses a thriving job market with strong labor demand and high wage growth, making it an attractive location for companies in the tech and finance sectors. However, the city's high cost of living and shrinking labor pool pose significant risks and constraints, which decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating this location for business expansion or relocation. Ultimately, businesses will need to weigh the benefits of accessing a deep talent pool against the challenges of attracting and retaining workers in a competitive and expensive market.
