Economic Analysis

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 52.4th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its above-average labor demand and strong wage growth. The city's labor demand composite score of 4.89, combining a 0.46% employment growth rate and a -0.050% weekly hours deviation, signals genuine demand expansion. The unemployment rate and cost of living are also key factors, with the latter being a significant concern due to its below-average affordability.

Labor Demand

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has an employment growth rate of 0.46% and a weekly hours deviation of -0.050%, indicating genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and hours are running slightly below trend, suggesting a healthy labor market. The labor demand composite score of 4.89 is above average, ranking in the 76th percentile.

Unemployment

Although the exact unemployment rate is not available, the city's below-average unemployment percentile rank of 33 suggests a relatively tight labor market. This implies that businesses may face challenges in hiring, with potential upward pressure on wages. A tight labor market can make it harder for companies to attract and retain talent.

Wage Growth

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has a year-over-year wage growth rate of 5.42%, which is above average, ranking in the 71st percentile. This fast wage growth rate implies rising labor costs for employers, but also increased purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to remain competitive.

Cost of Living

The city's cost of living is below average, ranking in the 22nd percentile, with a PSF of $654/sqft and an average hourly earnings of $50.48/hr, resulting in a ratio of 12.96. The fact that PSF is falling by 2.4% year-over-year is a key driver of the high affordability score. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums, as the cost of living is relatively high compared to other metros.

Labor Force Growth

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has a year-over-year labor force growth rate of -0.98%, which is below average, ranking in the 21st percentile. This contraction in the labor force supply implies a structural headwind for hiring, making it challenging for businesses to find and retain workers.

Building Permits

The city has a year-over-year change in building permits of 86.01%, which is in the top tier, ranking in the 96th percentile. This significant increase in building permits suggests that housing supply is expanding, which may improve affordability and accommodation for the workforce in the future.

Days on Market

Unfortunately, the current median days on market is not available, but the fact that it ranks in the bottom tier, 0th percentile, suggests a highly competitive market. This would make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing, as homes are likely selling quickly.

Office Economy

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area has a deep professional talent pool, with an office economy percentile rank of 62. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, but less suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

The San Francisco-Oakland-Fremont metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion and a deep professional talent pool. However, the single biggest risk or constraint is the high cost of living, which may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract and retain talent, making it essential for decision-makers to factor this into their location decision.
