Economic Analysis

Kansas City earns an overall grade of A+ with a composite score ranking at the 71.1th percentile among 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its top-tier wage growth of 6.99% year-over-year and its highly affordable cost of living, with a PSF to earnings ratio of $199/sqft to $35.79/hr, ranking in the 90th percentile. These metrics suggest a city with strong labor market dynamics and attractive living costs.

Labor Demand

Kansas City's labor demand composite score is 4.58, ranking near the median at the 58th percentile, driven by employment growth of 0.04% year-over-year and weekly hours 0.223% above trend. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, albeit at a moderate pace. The city's labor market is adding jobs, and hours are running above trend, indicating a healthy demand for labor.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Kansas City is 4.20%, ranking above average at the 71st percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market with some slack, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. However, the unemployment rate is not excessively low, suggesting that wage pressure is manageable.

Wage Growth

Wage growth in Kansas City is 6.99% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 88th percentile. This rapid wage growth implies rising labor costs for employers, but it also means strong purchasing power for workers. Businesses should factor in higher labor costs, but the upside is a talented and productive workforce.

Cost of Living

Kansas City's cost of living is highly affordable, with a PSF to earnings ratio of $199/sqft to $35.79/hr, ranking in the 90th percentile. This means that the city offers a significant talent attraction advantage without requiring wage premiums. The stable YoY change in PSF of 0.0% suggests that affordability is likely to persist.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Kansas City is growing at a rate of 1.87% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 90th percentile. This expanding labor force supply implies that businesses have a growing pool of potential hires, making it easier to find talent.

Building Permits

Building permits in Kansas City are increasing by 34.49% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 82nd percentile. This surge in permits signals that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Kansas City is 41 days, with a YoY decrease of 12.8%, ranking in the bottom tier at the 8th percentile. This fast-paced market means that relocating workers may face competition for housing, making it challenging to find a home quickly.

Office Economy

Kansas City's professional and office worker share is 1.84, ranking below average at the 26th percentile. This suggests that the city's talent pool is not as deep in professional services, making it less suited for tech, finance, or consulting businesses. However, it may be more attractive for industrial or logistics-dominant economies.

In conclusion, Kansas City offers businesses a unique combination of strong wage growth, affordable living costs, and expanding labor force supply. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the highly competitive housing market, which may pose challenges for relocating workers and require creative solutions to attract and retain talent.
