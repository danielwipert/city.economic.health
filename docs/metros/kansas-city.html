Economic Analysis

Kansas City earns an overall grade of A+ with a composite score of 74.3th percentile, ranking it among the top US metros. The city's economic character is defined by its exceptional labor demand, with a composite score in the 96th percentile, driven by a 1.39% employment growth rate and a 1.070% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend. Additionally, its strong labor force growth rate of 2.10% YoY further solidifies its position as a thriving economic hub.

Labor Demand

Kansas City's labor demand is characterized by a 1.39% employment growth rate and a 1.070% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend, signaling genuine demand expansion. This combination indicates that the city is experiencing a surge in job creation, with hours worked above the trend, suggesting a healthy and growing economy. The labor demand composite score of 7.32 places it in the top tier, at the 96th percentile.

Unemployment

The city's unemployment rate stands at 3.50%, ranking it at the 60th percentile, indicating a near-median level of unemployment. This suggests that the market has some slack, making it relatively easier for businesses to hire compared to cities with tighter labor markets. However, this also implies that local consumer demand may not be as strong as in cities with lower unemployment rates.

Wage Growth

Kansas City's wage growth is robust, with a year-over-year change in average hourly earnings of 6.97%, placing it in the top tier at the 85th percentile. This fast wage growth signals rising labor costs for employers, but it also translates to increased worker purchasing power, benefiting the local economy. As a result, businesses may face higher labor costs, but they can also tap into a workforce with growing disposable income.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 1.44, Kansas City ranks at the 76th percentile, indicating that it is more affordable than many of its peer cities. This affordability advantage means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums to offset high living costs. As a result, Kansas City has a talent attraction advantage, making it an attractive location for companies looking to expand or relocate.

Labor Force Growth

The city's labor force is growing at a rate of 2.10% YoY, placing it in the top tier at the 89th percentile. This expansion in the labor force supply suggests that businesses will have access to a growing pool of potential employees, making it easier to hire and staff operations. The increasing labor force also signals a structurally sound economy with a strong foundation for future growth.

Building Permits

Kansas City has seen a 5.41% year-over-year increase in residential building permits, ranking it at the 62nd percentile. This rise in permits indicates that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. As a result, the city is likely to maintain its attractiveness to relocating workers and businesses, as housing availability and affordability are critical factors in location decisions.

Days on Market

The current median days on market stands at 45 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 11.8%, placing it in the bottom tier at the 4th percentile. This fast-paced market means that homes are selling quickly, which can make it challenging for relocating workers to find and secure housing. However, this also signals a strong and competitive local real estate market, which can be beneficial for businesses looking to attract and retain top talent.

Office Economy

Kansas City's professional and office worker share is below average, ranking at the 38th percentile. This suggests that the city's economy is not as deeply rooted in professional services as some of its peers, making it less suited for businesses that require a large pool of specialized office talent, such as tech, finance, or consulting firms. However, the city may be more attractive to industries with different talent requirements, such as logistics or manufacturing.

In conclusion, Kansas City offers businesses a unique combination of strong labor demand, affordable cost of living, and a growing labor force, making it an attractive location for expansion or relocation. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers to consider is the fast-paced and competitive housing market, which may pose challenges for relocating workers and require businesses to offer competitive relocation packages or housing assistance to attract and retain top talent.
