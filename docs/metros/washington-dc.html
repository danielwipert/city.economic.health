Economic Analysis

The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 35.9th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score largely defined by its weak labor demand and slow labor force growth. The labor demand composite score of 1.50, combining a -2.86% employment growth rate and a -0.505% weekly hours deviation from trend, signals a contraction in job opportunities. This, coupled with a labor force growth rate of -2.52%, paints a challenging picture for businesses looking to expand or relocate in this area.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Washington-Arlington-Alexandria is -2.86% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.505% from the city's own 12-month baseline, indicating a clear contraction in labor demand. This combination signals a lack of genuine demand expansion, suggesting that the area is not adding jobs at a pace that would support business growth. The negative hours deviation alongside negative employment growth points to a straightforward contraction rather than a survivor squeeze.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Washington-Arlington-Alexandria stands at 3.90%, placing it in the 34th percentile, which is below average. This rate indicates a relatively tight labor market, suggesting that there is less slack and potentially more competition for hiring. For a business trying to hire in this area, the implication is that it may face challenges in finding suitable candidates, potentially leading to higher wage offers to attract talent.

Wage Growth

Wage growth in the area is +4.85% year-over-year, ranking in the 62nd percentile, which is above average. This level of wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, which can be beneficial for worker purchasing power but may pose a challenge for businesses looking to keep costs under control. The relatively fast wage growth indicates a competitive labor market where workers have stronger bargaining power.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of $310/sqft to $44.71/hr, resulting in a ratio of 6.93, and considering the PSF is falling -3.1% year-over-year, Washington-Arlington-Alexandria ranks in the 69th percentile for affordability. This high percentile rank means the city is more affordable relative to its peers, which can be a significant advantage for talent attraction without needing to offer wage premiums. The falling PSF relative to wages is a key driver of this affordability.

Labor Force Growth

The labor force in Washington-Arlington-Alexandria is growing at a rate of -2.52% year-over-year, indicating a contraction in the supply of potential workers. This negative growth rate suggests that the labor pool is shrinking, posing a structural headwind for hiring and business expansion. Businesses may find it challenging to find new talent as the labor force contracts.

Building Permits

The area has seen a +58.73% year-over-year increase in residential building permits, placing it in the 88th percentile. This significant rise in permits signals that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodate workforce growth in the future. The increase in building permits is a positive indicator for future workforce attraction and retention.

Days on Market

Homes in Washington-Arlington-Alexandria are currently sitting on the market for 30 days, with a year-over-year increase of +3.5%. This increase in days on market, although still relatively fast at 30 days, suggests a slight normalization in the housing market. For a worker relocating to this city, the market remains competitive but is becoming slightly more accessible.

Office Economy

The share of jobs in professional and office sectors in Washington-Arlington-Alexandria places it in the 34th percentile, indicating a below-average depth of professional talent pool. This city is less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, consulting, or HQ decisions, but may be more appropriate for industries with different workforce needs.

The Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metro area offers a mixed bag for businesses, with advantages in affordability and wage growth but significant challenges in labor demand and labor force growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker considering this location is the weak labor market conditions, which could hinder hiring and business expansion plans. Despite some positive indicators like increasing building permits, the overall economic character suggests caution and careful consideration of these factors before making a location decision.
