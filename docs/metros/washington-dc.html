Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Washington DC's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both short-term and longer-term trends warranting attention. The labour market, a key driver of economic activity, shows a relatively low unemployment rate of 3.8%, although this has increased by 1.1 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labour force, however, has contracted by 1.82% over the same period, suggesting a decline in workforce supply growth. Average hourly earnings have grown by 2.39% year-over-year, a modest increase that may not be sufficient to keep pace with the city's high cost of living, as reflected in a cost of living composite score of 1.97.

The city's business activity and housing markets also exhibit contrasting trends. Nonfarm payroll employment has declined by 1.63% year-over-year, while the labour demand composite score stands at 2.02, indicating a relatively weak employment market. The office worker ratio composite score of 2.22 suggests a moderate level of office worker activity. In the housing market, residential building permits have decreased by 3.97% year-over-year, and the median days on market have increased by 25% over the same period, reaching a level of 30 days. The days on market composite score of 8.5 and the high percentile score of 86.0 for this metric indicate a relatively slow housing market.

The city's economic stress points are evident in its declining labour force and employment growth, as well as its high cost of living and slow housing market. However, the relatively low unemployment rate and modest earnings growth are strengths that could support economic stability. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline, at -0.215%, suggests a slight decline in labour market activity. Overall, Washington DC's economic outlook is cautious, with a need to address the declining labour force and slow employment growth to sustain economic momentum. Closing outlook: the city's economy is likely to face challenges in the short term, but its strong fundamentals and modest earnings growth could support a recovery in the longer term.
