Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Los Angeles economy is exhibiting a mix of positive and cautionary signals. On the labour market front, the unemployment rate has declined by 0.4 percentage points year-over-year, indicating a moderate improvement in job market conditions. However, the civilian labor force growth rate of 0.69% year-over-year suggests a relatively slow expansion of the workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have risen by 4.84% year-over-year, outpacing the national average and suggesting a degree of wage pressure.

In terms of business activity, the labor demand composite score of 6.59 and nonfarm payroll employment growth of 1.83% year-over-year point to a robust job market. The office worker ratio composite score of 1.48 is relatively low, indicating a potential mismatch between job creation and office space demand. On the housing front, residential building permits have surged by 20.24% year-over-year, while the median days on market have increased by 7.14% year-over-year to 45 days, suggesting a potential slowdown in the housing market. The cost of living composite score of 5.59 is relatively high, indicating that Los Angeles remains an expensive city to live in.

The overall economic outlook for Los Angeles is one of cautious optimism. While the labour market and business activity indicators are generally positive, the housing market and cost of living metrics suggest potential stress points. The decline in weekly hours worked, as indicated by a -0.274% deviation from the 12-month baseline, may also be a sign of slowing economic momentum. As such, policymakers and businesses should continue to monitor these indicators closely to ensure that the city's economic growth remains sustainable and inclusive.

Closing outlook: Los Angeles' economic growth is expected to continue, driven by a strong labour market and business activity. However, policymakers should be mindful of potential stress points in the housing market and cost of living, and take steps to address these issues to ensure that the city's economic growth remains balanced and sustainable.
