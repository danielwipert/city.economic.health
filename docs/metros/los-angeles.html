Economic Analysis

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 51.4th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its strong labor demand and building permits growth. The city's economic character is defined by its top-tier labor demand composite score of 6.14, which combines a +1.83% employment growth rate and a -0.727% weekly hours deviation from its own trend. This signals a genuine demand expansion, with jobs being added and hours running above trend.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of +1.83% and weekly hours deviation of -0.727% combine to signal a genuine demand expansion, indicating that the city is experiencing an increase in jobs and hours worked. This suggests that businesses are hiring and workers are putting in more hours, which is a positive sign for the local economy. The labor demand composite score of 6.14 is in the top tier, ranking 86th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate is not available, but the bottom tier percentile rank of 18th suggests that the market may have some slack. This could make it easier for businesses to hire workers, as there may be a larger pool of available labor. However, the practical implication for a business trying to hire here is that it may not face as much competition for talent as it would in a tighter market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of +4.84% is above average, ranking 60th percentile. This indicates that wages are rising at a moderate pace, which could lead to increased labor costs for employers. However, it also means that workers have more purchasing power, which could be beneficial for local businesses that rely on consumer spending.

Cost of Living

The city has a cost of living percentile rank of 6th, with a PSF of $667/sqft and an earnings ratio of $41.74/hr, resulting in a ratio of 15.98. This suggests that the city is relatively expensive, which could make it difficult to attract talent without offering wage premiums. The fact that PSF is falling by -3.0% YoY is a positive sign, but the overall affordability score is still low.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is contracting at a rate of -1.55% year-over-year, which is below average, ranking 20th percentile. This suggests that the supply of workers is shrinking, which could make it more difficult for businesses to hire and expand their operations. The implication for hiring capacity is that businesses may need to offer more competitive wages and benefits to attract and retain talent.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is +79.98%, which is in the top tier, ranking 96th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is expanding rapidly, which could lead to improved affordability and a more attractive environment for workers. The signal for future affordability and workforce accommodation is positive, as the increased supply of housing could help to reduce costs and make the city more competitive.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 47 days, with a year-over-year increase of +4.4%. This suggests that the housing market is slowing down slightly, which could make it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. The above-average percentile rank of 62nd suggests that the market is still relatively competitive, but the rising DOM could indicate a healthy normalization.

Office Economy

The city has an office economy percentile rank of 60th, with a share of professional and office workers of 2.96. This suggests that the city has a deep talent pool in these sectors, making it well-suited for businesses in tech, finance, consulting, and HQ operations. However, it may be less suited for businesses in industrial or logistics-dominant sectors.

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area offers businesses a strong labor demand and expanding housing supply, which could make it an attractive location for companies looking to hire and grow. However, the single biggest risk or constraint is the relatively high cost of living, which could make it difficult to attract talent without offering wage premiums. Overall, businesses should carefully consider these factors when making a location decision.
