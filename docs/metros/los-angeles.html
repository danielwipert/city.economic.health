Economic Analysis

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has an overall grade of A- with a composite score ranking at the 60.1th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with an employment growth rate of +1.83% and a labor demand composite score in the top tier at the 84th percentile, as well as its high wage growth rate of +5.68%. The combination of these metrics signals a city with genuine demand expansion and rising labor costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is +1.83%, and weekly hours are -2.224% below the city's own trend, indicating a slight deviation from the norm but still within a context of job growth. This combination signals genuine demand expansion, as hours are not excessively above trend during job growth, suggesting that the labor market is absorbing new jobs without overburdening existing workers. The labor demand composite score of 5.36 further supports this interpretation, ranking in the top tier at the 84th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is 5.10%, ranking near the median at the 48th percentile. This rate indicates a labor market with some slack, rather than being extremely tight, which means businesses may find it somewhat easier to hire here compared to cities with lower unemployment rates. However, the practical implication for a business trying to hire in this city is that while it may not face extreme competition for talent, it still needs to offer competitive wages and benefits to attract the best candidates.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is +5.68%, ranking above average at the 75th percentile. This rate indicates fast wage growth, which implies rising labor costs for employers but also good worker purchasing power. As a result, businesses operating in this city should anticipate higher labor costs, but they can also expect their employees to have increased disposable income, potentially benefiting local consumer demand.

Cost of Living

Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim has a cost of living ratio of $666/sqft (with PSF falling -3.3% YoY) to $41.96/hr, resulting in a ratio of 15.87, which ranks in the bottom tier at the 4th percentile. This low percentile rank indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers, making it challenging to attract talent without offering wage premiums. The falling PSF YoY is a positive sign, but the overall affordability score suggests that businesses may need to compensate employees for the high cost of living in this area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is growing at a rate of +0.08% year-over-year, ranking near the median at the 46th percentile. This slow growth rate indicates that the labor force supply is barely expanding, which may pose a structural headwind for hiring in the long term. Businesses should be aware that the labor pool is not rapidly increasing, which could affect their ability to find new talent.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim has increased by +20.24% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 80th percentile. This significant increase signals that housing supply is expanding, which should improve affordability and accommodate workforce growth in the future. As a result, businesses can expect a more favorable environment for relocating workers, with potentially more housing options becoming available.

Days on Market

The current median days on market for homes in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is 45 days, with a year-over-year increase of +7.1%. This moderate days on market figure, ranking near the median at the 54th percentile, suggests a relatively balanced market that is neither extremely competitive nor very slow. For a worker relocating to this city, the housing market is accessible but not overly competitive, making it easier to find and purchase a home.

Office Economy

The share of jobs in professional and office sectors in Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim is significant, with a composite score ranking above average at the 79th percentile. This indicates a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions, making the city an attractive location for businesses in these sectors. However, it may be less suited for industries that are more industrial or logistics-dominant.

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion and a deep professional talent pool, but it also comes with the challenge of a relatively high cost of living and potentially rising labor costs due to fast wage growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for a decision-maker to factor in is the need to offer competitive wages and benefits to attract and retain talent in this expensive city, which could impact operational costs and profitability.
