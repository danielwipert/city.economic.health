Economic Analysis

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has earned an overall grade of B+ with a composite score ranking at the 55.5th percentile among 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its strong labor demand, with an 80th percentile ranking, and its high cost of living, which ranks at a low 4th percentile. The labor demand composite score of 5.36, driven by 1.83% employment growth and -1.772% weekly hours deviation, signals a genuine demand expansion.

Labor Demand

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has seen employment growth of 1.83% year-over-year, combined with a -1.772% deviation in weekly hours from its own trend, indicating a genuine demand expansion. This combination suggests that the city is experiencing an increase in job creation, with hours worked also above trend, signaling a strong labor market. The labor demand composite score of 5.36 further reinforces this notion, ranking at the 80th percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area stands at 4.80%, ranking at the 36th percentile, indicating a moderate level of slack in the labor market. This rate suggests that while the market is not extremely tight, it is still relatively competitive, making it somewhat challenging for businesses to hire. As a result, businesses may face moderate wage pressure when trying to attract talent.

Wage Growth

The city has experienced a year-over-year wage growth rate of 5.44%, ranking at the 66th percentile, indicating a moderate to fast pace of wage increases. This rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, while worker purchasing power is also increasing. As a result, businesses may need to factor in higher labor costs, but can also expect a more affluent local consumer base.

Cost of Living

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has a cost of living ratio of 5.63, ranking at a low 4th percentile, indicating that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers. This low ranking means that the city is less affordable, requiring businesses to offer wage premiums to attract talent. As a result, businesses may face higher labor costs to compensate for the high cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has grown by 0.69% year-over-year, ranking at the 47th percentile, indicating a moderate expansion of the workforce supply. This growth rate suggests that the labor pool is slowly expanding, providing a moderate level of hiring capacity for businesses. As a result, businesses may face some challenges in finding talent, but the growing labor force can help mitigate these issues.

Building Permits

The city has seen a 20.24% year-over-year increase in residential building permits, ranking at the 81st percentile, indicating a strong expansion of housing supply. This increase suggests that developer confidence is high, and future housing supply is expected to improve, leading to better affordability and workforce accommodation. As a result, businesses can expect a more stable and affordable housing market for their employees.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in the Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area stands at 45 days, with a 7.1% year-over-year increase, ranking at the 57th percentile. This increase suggests that the housing market is normalizing, becoming more accessible for relocating workers. As a result, businesses can expect a more competitive but still relatively accessible housing market for their employees.

Office Economy

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area has a professional and office worker share of 3.40, ranking at the 79th percentile, indicating a deep talent pool suited for tech, finance, consulting, and HQ decisions. This high ranking suggests that the city is well-suited for businesses requiring specialized knowledge-economy talent, but may be less suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion, moderate wage growth, and a deep professional talent pool. However, the city's high cost of living and relatively low affordability may pose a significant risk or constraint for businesses, requiring them to offer wage premiums to attract and retain talent.
