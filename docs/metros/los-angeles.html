Economic Analysis

Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim earns a below-average overall grade, with a 4.80% unemployment rate, a significant concern indicating severe labor market weakness and flagging consumer spending risk. The city's strong nonfarm employment growth of +1.83% year-over-year and solid wage growth of +5.33% year-over-year suggest an economically expanding market with rapid job creation and positive consumer spending.

However, the city's cost of living composite score of 5.59 is a major constraint, ranking it among the most expensive metros. This high cost of living, combined with a low office and professional worker share, ranking in the 8th percentile, poses significant challenges for talent acquisition and real consumer spending power. The tension between strong wage growth and high cost of living is particularly noteworthy, as workers may be gaining in nominal terms but seeing limited real purchasing power gains.

The city's housing market is showing signs of stabilization, with building permits growing at +20.24% year-over-year, which could help alleviate some of the affordability pressures. Overall, Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim offers a rapidly expanding job market, but its highly expensive cost of living and shallow labor pool pose significant challenges for businesses, including constrained consumer spending power and talent acquisition challenges.
