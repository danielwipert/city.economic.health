Economic Analysis

Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario metro area earns a C grade, ranking 35th among 50 US metros, driven by severe labor market weakness, with an unemployment rate of 5.10% and a low labor force participation rate of 60.37%. The area's nonfarm employment growth is stagnant, at +0.18% year-over-year, indicating a contraction story that dominates the city's economic narrative.

Wage growth in the area is a notable bright spot, with hourly earnings rising by +7.54% year-over-year, placing it in the top tier. However, this strong wage growth is at odds with the stagnant employment picture, suggesting that wages may be rising due to a mix shift toward higher-paying jobs rather than broad labor market health. The area's low concentration of office and professional workers, ranking 16th percentile, poses a significant constraint for knowledge-economy businesses.

The housing market also tells a nuanced story, with building permits growing at an above-average rate of +18.04% year-over-year, but days on market increasing by +5.9% year-over-year, indicating a slowing market. Overall, Riverside-San Bernardino-Ontario offers a complex and challenging economic environment, with strong wage growth but significant labor market weaknesses, posing a risk for businesses considering relocation to this area.
