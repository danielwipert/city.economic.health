Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Oklahoma City's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.6%, which, although relatively low, has increased by 0.8 percentage points year-over-year. This uptick is somewhat mitigated by a modest growth in the civilian labour force, with a 0.23% year-over-year change. Average hourly earnings, however, have seen a more substantial increase, rising 4.75% year-over-year, which could help support consumer spending and overall economic activity.

In terms of business activity, Oklahoma City is experiencing a notable expansion in residential construction, with a 20.14% year-over-year increase in building permits. This surge in construction activity could be driven by the city's relatively low cost of living, as reflected in its cost of living composite score of 1.17. The office worker ratio composite score of 2.64 also suggests a relatively strong demand for office space. However, the labour demand composite score of 6.11, combined with a modest 0.52% year-over-year growth in nonfarm payroll employment, indicates that the city's job market may not be as robust as other metropolitan areas.

The housing market in Oklahoma City appears to be experiencing some stress, with median days on market increasing by 17.02% year-over-year to 55 days. This could be a sign of slowing demand or an oversupply of housing stock. Nevertheless, the city's overall economic performance, as reflected in its weighted percentile score of 61.1, suggests that Oklahoma City remains a relatively strong performer. With its low cost of living and growing earnings, the city may continue to attract businesses and talent, supporting its long-term economic prospects.

Closing outlook: Oklahoma City's economy is expected to continue growing, albeit at a moderate pace, driven by its low cost of living, expanding residential construction, and rising earnings. However, the city's labour market and housing market will require close monitoring, as they may be subject to fluctuations in response to broader economic trends.
