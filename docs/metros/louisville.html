Economic Analysis

The Louisville-Jefferson County metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 39.7th percentile out of 50 US metros. The city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score in the bottom tier at 14th percentile, and its tight unemployment market, with a rate of 4.20% ranking it in the top tier at 83rd percentile. These metrics signal a challenging environment for businesses looking to hire and expand.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is -1.10% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.245% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is experiencing a decline in job creation and hours worked. This suggests that businesses may face a challenging environment for expansion.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is 4.20%, which ranks in the top tier at 83rd percentile. This indicates a tight labor market with limited slack, making it harder for businesses to hire new employees. As a result, businesses may face upward pressure on wages to attract and retain talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is +2.35%, which ranks below average at 30th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but at a slower pace than in many other cities. This may limit worker purchasing power and make it more challenging for businesses to attract top talent.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in Louisville-Jefferson County is below average, with a percentile rank of 33rd. The city's PSF is $180/sqft, and the ratio of PSF to hourly earnings is 5.71, with PSF rising 1.7% year-over-year. This suggests that the city is relatively expensive, which may make it more challenging to attract talent without offering wage premiums.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Louisville-Jefferson County is contracting at a rate of -1.35% year-over-year, ranking it in the bottom tier at 6th percentile. This decline in labor force supply signals a structural headwind for hiring, making it more challenging for businesses to find and recruit new employees.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Louisville-Jefferson County is increasing at a rate of +2.98% year-over-year, ranking it above average at 62nd percentile. This expansion in housing supply suggests that the city is experiencing developer confidence and a growing inventory of homes, which may improve affordability and workforce accommodation in the future.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Louisville-Jefferson County is 45 days, with a year-over-year increase of +9.8%, ranking it above average at 77th percentile. This suggests a slower and more accessible market for workers relocating to the city, giving them more time to find and purchase a home.

Office Economy

The professional talent pool in Louisville-Jefferson County is relatively shallow, with a percentile rank of 15th. This suggests that the city is less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting firms, and more suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Louisville-Jefferson County metro area offers businesses a relatively tight labor market with moderate wage growth, but also presents challenges in terms of labor demand and labor force growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the city's low labor demand and declining labor force supply, which may limit the ability of businesses to hire and expand in the area.
