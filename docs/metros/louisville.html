Economic Analysis

The Louisville-Jefferson County metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 47.4th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low unemployment rate of 3.10% and its below-average labor demand composite score of 3.96, which ranks in the 35th percentile. The combination of these metrics suggests a tight labor market with limited job growth.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is -0.24% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.221% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not experiencing genuine demand expansion. The low labor demand composite score of 3.96, ranking in the 35th percentile, further supports this conclusion.

Unemployment

The actual unemployment rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is 3.10%, which is a top-tier ranking of 92nd percentile. This tight labor market means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as there is limited slack in the workforce. As a result, companies may need to offer competitive wages to attract and retain talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Louisville-Jefferson County is +2.36%, which is below average, ranking in the 23rd percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but at a slower pace than in other cities. While this may be beneficial for worker purchasing power, it also means that employers may need to budget for increasing labor costs.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 3.37, ranking in the 30th percentile, Louisville-Jefferson County is considered an expensive city relative to its peers. This means that businesses may need to offer wage premiums to attract talent, as the high cost of living may deter workers from relocating to the area without sufficient compensation.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Louisville-Jefferson County is growing at a rate of +0.38% year-over-year, which is below average, ranking in the 30th percentile. This slow growth rate indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, but at a limited pace. As a result, businesses may face challenges in finding qualified workers, particularly in industries with high labor demand.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Louisville-Jefferson County is increasing by +2.98% year-over-year, ranking near the median at 57th percentile. This expansion in housing supply suggests that the city is experiencing developer confidence and a growing availability of housing options. However, the relatively slow pace of growth may not be sufficient to meet future demand, potentially leading to affordability concerns.

Days on Market

The current median days on market for homes in Louisville-Jefferson County is 45 days, with a year-over-year increase of +9.8%, ranking in the 78th percentile. This slower market means that workers relocating to the city may have a more accessible and less competitive housing market, with more time to find and purchase a home.

Office Economy

With an office/professional worker share composite score of 1.35, ranking in the 21st percentile, Louisville-Jefferson County has a relatively shallow professional talent pool. This city is best suited for businesses that do not require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as industrial or logistics-dominant companies. However, it may not be the ideal location for tech, finance, or consulting firms that rely on specialized office workers.

The Louisville-Jefferson County metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a tight labor market and moderate wage growth, but its slow labor force growth and high cost of living may pose challenges for talent attraction and retention. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the limited labor demand and slow labor force growth, which may hinder a company's ability to expand and find qualified workers in the area.
