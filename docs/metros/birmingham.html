Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Birmingham's economic indicators suggest a city with a strong labour market, as evidenced by a low unemployment rate of 2.2% and a notable decline of 0.8 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labour force has grown at a rate of 1.06% over the past year, indicating a steady supply of workforce. Average hourly earnings have increased by 1.16% year-over-year, although this growth is relatively modest. The labour demand composite score of 6.65, combined with employment growth of 0.61% year-over-year, points to a stable and slightly expanding job market.

The city's housing market is characterized by a significant increase in residential building permits, with a year-over-year growth rate of 55.25%. This surge in construction activity may help alleviate some of the pressure on the housing market, as the median days on market has increased by 3.7% year-over-year, reaching a level of 56 days. The cost of living composite score of 3.61 suggests that Birmingham remains relatively affordable, which could be an attractive feature for businesses and individuals considering relocation. The office worker ratio composite score of 1.4 is somewhat lower than might be expected, potentially indicating opportunities for growth in this sector.

Despite these positive trends, there are some areas of concern. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is 1.729%, which may indicate some volatility in the labour market. Additionally, the earnings growth rate is relatively low, which could impact the overall purchasing power of residents. Nevertheless, Birmingham's economic fundamentals appear sound, with a strong labour market and growing business activity. The city's affordability and relatively low cost of living are likely to remain key strengths in attracting businesses and talent.

Closing outlook: Birmingham's economy is expected to continue on a stable growth trajectory, driven by its strong labour market and increasing business activity. While there are some areas of concern, such as modest earnings growth and potential labour market volatility, the city's overall economic fundamentals remain robust. As the housing market continues to evolve, it will be important to monitor the impact of increased construction activity on affordability and demand.
