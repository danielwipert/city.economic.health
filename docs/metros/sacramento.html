Economic Analysis

Sacramento-Roseville-Folsom metro area earns a D grade, with a struggling economy driven by a severely weak labor market. The unemployment rate is 4.80%, and the labor force participation rate is a critically low 60.12%. This is compounded by below-average nonfarm employment growth of -0.27% year-over-year, indicating economic deceleration.

The data reveals tensions in the housing market, with a cost of living composite score of 4.08, suggesting a relatively affordable environment. However, the decline in building permits by -15.51% year-over-year raises concerns about future affordability. The increase in days on market by 8.3% year-over-year, resulting in an average of 39 days, may indicate softening demand rather than a healthy normalization of the market.

The Sacramento market offers a relatively affordable cost of living, but its severely weak labor market and declining economic growth pose significant risks for businesses. The primary caveat for businesses considering relocation or expansion in this market is the structural constraint of a critically low labor force participation rate, which may limit workforce availability and hinder growth prospects.
