Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The Sacramento economy presents a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals emerging from its key indicators. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 4.8%, with a year-over-year change of 0.3 percentage points, indicating a relatively stable jobs market. However, the civilian labour force growth rate of 1.2% year-over-year suggests a modest expansion in the workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have grown by 3.56% year-over-year, a respectable increase that should support consumer spending.

In terms of business activity, the labour demand composite score of 3.72 suggests a sluggish employment market, which is reinforced by the -0.27% year-over-year change in nonfarm payroll employment. The weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is a positive 0.203%, indicating some upside in labour demand. The office worker ratio composite score of 0.08 is a concern, as it suggests a relatively low proportion of office workers in the city. On the housing front, residential building permits have declined by 15.51% year-over-year, while the median days on market have increased by 8.33% year-over-year to 39 days, indicating a softening housing market.

The cost of living composite score of 4.02 is a notable stress point, as it suggests that Sacramento's cost of living is relatively high compared to other cities. This could potentially erode the purchasing power of residents and make the city less attractive to businesses and talent. Despite these challenges, the city's earnings growth and relatively stable labour market suggest some underlying strengths. Overall, Sacramento's economy appears to be navigating a period of slow growth, with some signs of stress in the housing market and labour demand.

Closing outlook: Sacramento's economic prospects are likely to remain subdued in the near term, driven by sluggish labour demand and a softening housing market. However, the city's stable labour market and respectable earnings growth provide a foundation for potential recovery and growth in the longer term. Policymakers and businesses will need to address the city's cost of living challenges and invest in initiatives that support labour demand and housing affordability to unlock Sacramento's full economic potential.
