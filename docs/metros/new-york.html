Economic Analysis

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 53.7th percentile among 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its above-average labor demand and strong wage growth, at 66th and 72nd percentiles, respectively. The city's labor market is characterized by a 0.39% year-over-year employment growth rate and a 0.456% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend. These metrics signal a genuine demand expansion in the city's labor market.

Labor Demand

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an employment growth rate of 0.39% year-over-year and a weekly hours deviation of 0.456% above its own trend, indicating a genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, suggesting a strong labor market. The labor demand composite score of 5.30 places the city at the 66th percentile, further reinforcing the notion of a thriving labor market.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 4.50%, which is below average, ranking at the 36th percentile. This indicates a relatively tight labor market, making it harder for businesses to hire and potentially leading to upward pressure on wages. As a result, businesses may need to offer competitive salaries to attract top talent in this market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 5.68%, which is above average, ranking at the 72nd percentile. This rapid wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers may be rising, but it also indicates strong purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may need to factor in higher labor costs, but they can also expect a skilled and affluent workforce.

Cost of Living

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has a cost of living score that places it at the 10th percentile, with a price-to-salary ratio of $540/sqft to $41.11/hr, or 13.14. Although the PSF is decreasing by 1.3% year-over-year, the city remains relatively expensive. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums to offset the high cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is growing at a rate of 0.73% year-over-year, which is above average, ranking at the 70th percentile. This expansion in labor force supply suggests that the city has a growing pool of potential workers, making it easier for businesses to hire and expand their operations.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is increasing by 64.22% year-over-year, which is a top-tier growth rate, ranking at the 92nd percentile. This surge in building permits suggests that the city's housing supply is expanding, which may lead to improved affordability and a more attractive environment for workers to relocate.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 41 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 8.9%. This relatively fast pace of home sales, ranking at the 10th percentile, may make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing, potentially affecting their decision to move to the city.

Office Economy

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an office economy score that places it at the 44th percentile, with a share of professional and office workers at 2.40 out of 5. This suggests that the city has a moderately deep talent pool, making it suitable for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors, but potentially less attractive for industrial or logistics-dominant companies.

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area offers businesses a strong labor market with genuine demand expansion and rapid wage growth, but it also presents challenges such as a tight labor market and high cost of living. The single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the city's expensive housing market, which may require businesses to offer wage premiums to attract and retain top talent.
