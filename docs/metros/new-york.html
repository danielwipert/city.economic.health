Economic Analysis

New York's economy earns a below-average overall grade of 48.2nd percentile, driven by a mix of strengths and weaknesses. The city's nonfarm employment growth of +0.48% YoY and wage growth of +4.84% YoY are notable bright spots, indicating a healthy expansion and solid consumer spending power.

The tension between New York's strong job market and its high cost of living is a key consideration. The city's above-average office and professional worker share is a mild positive for office-dependent businesses, but the elevated cost of living poses a significant constraint on talent acquisition and real consumer spending power. The housing market, with building permits growing at +14.97% YoY, is keeping pace with demand, but the days on market are increasing by +6.2% YoY, indicating a stabilizing force.

Ultimately, New York offers businesses a strong job market with solid wage growth, but at a high cost. The primary risk is the city's extremely high cost of living, which may limit the appeal to businesses and consumers alike, and could potentially weaken local consumer demand due to the labor market slack signaled by the elevated unemployment rate of 4.50%.
