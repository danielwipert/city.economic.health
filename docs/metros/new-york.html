Economic Analysis

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 52.4th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its above-average labor demand and strong wage growth, at 66th and 72nd percentiles, respectively. The city's labor market is characterized by a 0.39% year-over-year employment growth rate and a 0.456% deviation in weekly hours above its own trend. These metrics define the city's current economic character, signaling a moderate expansion in job opportunities.

Labor Demand

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an employment growth rate of 0.39% and weekly hours 0.456% above its own trend, indicating genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is experiencing an increase in job opportunities, with hours worked above the trend, suggesting that businesses are hiring and workers are absorbing the additional workload. This is a positive indicator for businesses looking to expand or establish operations in the area.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 4.20%, ranking near the median at the 41st percentile. This rate indicates that the labor market has some slack, making it relatively easier for businesses to hire workers compared to cities with tighter labor markets. However, this also means that local consumer demand may be weaker due to the relatively higher unemployment rate.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 5.68%, ranking above average at the 72nd percentile. This fast wage growth rate implies rising labor costs for employers, but it also means that workers have increased purchasing power, which can benefit local businesses. This is a significant factor for businesses to consider when evaluating labor costs and potential returns on investment.

Cost of Living

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has a cost of living score that ranks at the 10th percentile, with a PSF of $532/sqft and average hourly earnings of $41.11/hr, resulting in a ratio of 12.94. This low percentile rank indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers, making it challenging to attract talent without offering wage premiums. The slight decrease in PSF of 0.2% year-over-year is a positive sign, but the overall cost of living remains a significant constraint.

Labor Force Growth

The labor force growth rate in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is not available, but it is ranked near the median at the 50th percentile. This suggests that the workforce supply is neither expanding nor contracting significantly, which may imply a stable but not particularly dynamic labor market. This stability can be beneficial for businesses looking for a predictable workforce, but it may also limit the potential for rapid growth.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 45.32%, ranking at the 86th percentile. This significant increase in permits suggests that housing supply is expanding, which can improve affordability and accommodate a growing workforce. This is a positive sign for businesses concerned about the ability of their employees to find affordable housing.

Days on Market

The current median days on market in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 42 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 6.7%. This relatively fast market, ranked at the 16th percentile, indicates that homes are selling quickly, which can make it challenging for relocating workers to find housing. This fast pace may require businesses to provide relocation assistance or adjust their hiring timelines accordingly.

Office Economy

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an office economy score that ranks at the 44th percentile, with a share of professional and office workers at 2.40 out of 5. This near-median rank suggests that the city has a moderately deep talent pool in professional and office sectors, making it suitable for businesses in these industries, such as tech, finance, or consulting. However, it may not be as competitive for businesses in industrial or logistics sectors.

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area offers businesses a unique combination of moderate job growth, strong wage growth, and an expanding housing supply, making it an attractive location for companies in professional and office sectors. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for decision-makers is the relatively high cost of living, which may require wage premiums to attract and retain talent, potentially impacting business profitability and competitiveness.
