Economic Analysis

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it 40.4th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low unemployment rate of 5.30% and its above-average wage growth of 5.48% year-over-year. The combination of these metrics suggests a tight labor market with rising labor costs.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in this city is -0.47% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating 0.178% above the city's own 12-month trend. This combination signals a survivor squeeze, where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles, rather than genuine demand expansion. The labor demand composite score of 3.46 ranks below average at the 33rd percentile.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in this city is 5.30%, ranking it in the bottom tier at the 4th percentile. This indicates a very tight labor market with little slack, making it challenging for businesses to hire new workers. The practical implication is that businesses may face significant wage pressure and competition for talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in this city is 5.48%, ranking it above average at the 74th percentile. This signals fast wage growth, which implies rising labor costs for employers and increased purchasing power for workers. As a result, businesses may need to adjust their compensation packages to remain competitive.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in this city is relatively expensive, with a percentile rank of 16th, indicating that it is less affordable compared to its peers. The PSF to earnings ratio is $540/sqft vs $41.77/hr, resulting in a ratio of 12.93. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums to offset the high cost of living.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in this city is growing at a rate of 2.43% year-over-year, ranking it in the top tier at the 91st percentile. This indicates that the workforce supply is expanding, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire. The growing labor force provides a larger pool of potential workers, making it easier for companies to find the talent they need.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in this city has increased by 14.97% year-over-year, ranking it above average at the 70th percentile. This signals that housing supply is expanding, which may lead to improved affordability and a more attractive environment for workers to relocate. As a result, businesses may find it easier to attract and retain talent in the future.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 41 days, with a year-over-year decrease of 8.9%. This indicates a competitive market where homes are selling relatively quickly. For workers relocating to this city, the fast-paced market may make it challenging to find a home, potentially affecting their decision to move.

Office Economy

The professional talent pool in this city is near median, ranking it at the 56th percentile. This suggests that the city has a moderate depth of knowledge-economy talent, making it suitable for businesses in the tech, finance, and consulting sectors. However, it may not be the best fit for companies requiring a highly specialized or abundant office workforce.
