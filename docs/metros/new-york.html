Economic Analysis

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it at the 48.0th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its below-average labor demand, with a composite score of 3.46 ranking it at the 36th percentile, and its above-average wage growth of 5.48% year-over-year. The combination of these metrics suggests a complex economic environment with both opportunities and challenges for businesses.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in this city is -0.47% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating from the trend by +0.178%. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, with hours above trend during job losses indicating a survivor squeeze where remaining workers are absorbing the load of eliminated roles. This suggests that businesses may face challenges in finding the right talent, but may also have opportunities to attract workers from other industries.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in this city is 4.80%, ranking it at the 33rd percentile, indicating a relatively loose labor market. This means that businesses may have an easier time hiring workers, but may also face weaker local consumer demand. With a relatively high unemployment rate, businesses may have more negotiating power when it comes to wages and benefits.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in this city is 5.48%, ranking it at the 74th percentile, indicating fast wage growth. This means that employer labor costs are rising, but worker purchasing power is also increasing, which can be beneficial for businesses that rely on local consumer demand. However, fast wage growth can also put pressure on businesses to increase prices or reduce profit margins.

Cost of Living

The cost of living in this city is relatively expensive, with a PSF of $542/sqft and an earnings ratio of 12.98, ranking it at the 19th percentile. This means that the city is less affordable than many of its peers, which can make it harder to attract talent without offering wage premiums. The fact that PSF is rising by 0.4% year-over-year further exacerbates the affordability issue.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in this city is growing at a rate of 1.68% year-over-year, ranking it at the 79th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This means that businesses have a growing pool of potential workers to hire from, which can make it easier to find the right talent. However, this growth also puts pressure on the housing market and infrastructure to accommodate the increasing workforce.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in this city is increasing by 14.97% year-over-year, ranking it at the 75th percentile, indicating an expansion of housing supply. This suggests that the city is investing in its infrastructure and housing stock, which can improve future affordability and workforce accommodation. However, the current cost of living and housing prices may still be a challenge for relocating workers.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in this city is 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of 6.2%, ranking it at the 40th percentile. This means that the housing market is relatively slow, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, this can also indicate a weakening demand for housing, which can have implications for the local economy.

Office Economy

The professional and office worker share in this city is 2.70, ranking it at the 52nd percentile, indicating a near-median depth of talent pool. This means that the city is relatively well-suited for businesses that require a mix of professional and office workers, but may not be the best fit for businesses that require highly specialized or technical talent.

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area offers businesses a unique combination of fast wage growth and expanding workforce supply, but also poses challenges such as a relatively expensive cost of living and a slow housing market. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this city is the high cost of living, which can make it harder to attract and retain talent without offering wage premiums, and may require businesses to adapt their compensation and benefits strategies to remain competitive.
