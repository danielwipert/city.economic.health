Economic Analysis

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an overall grade of B, ranking 50.7th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score driven largely by its above-average labor demand and strong office economy. The city's labor demand composite score of 4.91, which combines a +0.13% employment growth rate and a +0.432% weekly hours deviation from trend, signals genuine demand expansion. Additionally, the city's office/professional worker share of 3.20, ranking in the 72nd percentile, highlights its deep talent pool.

Labor Demand

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area has an employment growth rate of +0.13% and a weekly hours deviation of +0.432% from its own trend, indicating genuine demand expansion. This combination signals that the city is adding jobs and workers are putting in more hours, suggesting a strong and growing economy. The labor demand composite score of 4.91, ranking in the 63rd percentile, further supports this assessment.

Unemployment

The city's unemployment rate is 4.50%, ranking in the 26th percentile, indicating a relatively tight labor market. This means that businesses may face challenges in hiring, as the pool of available workers is smaller, and may need to offer higher wages to attract talent. The tight market also suggests that workers may have more bargaining power.

Wage Growth

The city's year-over-year wage growth rate is +5.47%, ranking in the 68th percentile, indicating fast wage growth. This suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but workers are also experiencing increased purchasing power. The strong wage growth may attract high-skilled workers to the area.

Cost of Living

The city's cost of living, with a price per square foot to hourly earnings ratio of 4.93, ranks in the 11th percentile, indicating that it is relatively expensive. This means that the city may struggle to attract talent without offering wage premiums, as the high cost of living may offset the benefits of working in the area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is growing at a rate of +1.20% year-over-year, ranking in the 70th percentile, indicating an expanding workforce supply. This suggests that businesses will have a larger pool of potential workers to hire from, making it easier to find talent.

Building Permits

The city's residential building permits are increasing at a rate of +14.97% year-over-year, ranking in the 70th percentile, indicating an expansion of housing supply. This suggests that the city is likely to experience improved affordability and a more accommodating environment for workers in the future.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in the New York Newark-Jersey City metro area is 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of +6.2%, ranking in the 41st percentile. This suggests that the housing market is relatively balanced, neither extremely competitive nor extremely accessible, for workers relocating to the area.

Office Economy

The city's office economy, with a professional/office worker share of 3.20, ranking in the 72nd percentile, indicates a deep talent pool. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, but less suited for industries with more industrial or logistics-oriented workforces.

The New York Newark-Jersey City metro area offers businesses a strong and growing economy, with a deep talent pool and expanding workforce supply. However, the city's high cost of living and relatively tight labor market may pose challenges for businesses looking to attract and retain talent, making it essential for decision-makers to factor in the potential need for wage premiums to remain competitive.
