Economic Analysis

Pittsburgh's labour market is showing resilience in the face of modest cyclical headwinds, with an unemployment rate of 3.6%, up 0.3 percentage points year-on-year. Although this represents a slight softening, the rate remains below the national average. Labour demand is robust, as evidenced by a strong labour demand composite score of 6.96 and nonfarm employment growth of 1.07% year-on-year, outpacing the median US metro performance. Average hourly earnings are rising at an annual rate of 8.01%, placing Pittsburgh in the 92nd percentile nationally, driven by tightness in certain skill segments and sustained wage pressure. The civilian labour force has expanded 0.73% over the past year, indicating modest workforce supply growth, albeit at a pace insufficient to offset broader demographic constraints.

In contrast, the city's housing and commercial real estate markets are exhibiting divergent trends. Residential building permits have declined 10.55% year-on-year, suggesting waning builder confidence, likely due to higher financing costs and limited developable land. The median home is taking 65 days to sell, up 4.84% from a year ago, with a composite days-on-market score in the 20th percentile, indicating a significant slowdown in turnover. However, Pittsburgh's cost of living remains a relative strength, with a composite score of 1.0, among the lowest in peer metros, making it an attractive destination for in-migration. On the other hand, office demand is robust, with an office worker ratio composite score of 3.46, in the 82nd percentile, reflecting above-average utilisation, likely driven by the city's growing tech and healthcare sectors.

Looking ahead, Pittsburgh is navigating a transition from post-industrial renewal to a knowledge-driven economy, leveraging its structural advantages in affordability and talent retention. While near-term housing supply constraints and modest labour force growth pose limitations, strong wage gains and resilient employment demand suggest underlying economic momentum. Barring external shocks, the city is well-positioned for steady, moderate expansion.
