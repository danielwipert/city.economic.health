Economic Analysis

The Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking it 44.9th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its low labor demand, with a composite score in the 20th percentile, and its tight unemployment market, with a rate of 2.90% ranking in the 96th percentile. The combination of these two metrics suggests a challenging environment for businesses looking to hire and expand.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is -0.95% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.372% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is experiencing a decline in job growth and hours worked. This suggests that businesses may face a more competitive environment for talent, but also that there may be fewer opportunities for expansion.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is 2.90%, ranking in the 96th percentile, indicating a very tight labor market. This means that there is little slack in the market, making it challenging for businesses to hire new employees. As a result, businesses may face upward pressure on wages and benefits in order to attract and retain top talent.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is +2.01%, ranking in the 26th percentile, indicating moderate wage growth. This suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but at a slower pace than in many other cities. While this may be beneficial for workers' purchasing power, it also means that businesses may need to balance wage increases with other costs in order to remain competitive.

Cost of Living

Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood has a cost of living score ranking it in the 7th percentile, with a PSF of $168/sqft and average hourly earnings of $33.41/hr, resulting in a ratio of 5.03. This indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers, making it less attractive for talent without wage premiums. As a result, businesses may need to offer higher salaries or other incentives to attract top talent to the area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is growing at a rate of +0.96% year-over-year, ranking in the 72nd percentile. This indicates that the labor force supply is expanding, providing a positive environment for businesses looking to hire and grow. However, the tight unemployment market may still pose challenges for finding available talent.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is declining at a rate of -10.26% year-over-year, ranking near the median. This suggests that the housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability concerns and challenges for workforce accommodation. As a result, businesses may need to consider the potential impact of housing costs on their employees' quality of life and ability to attract talent.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood is currently 51 days, with a year-over-year increase of +15.9%, ranking in the 73rd percentile. This indicates a slower market, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, this may also signal a normalization of the market after a period of rapid growth, rather than a decline in demand.

Office Economy

Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood has a professional and office worker share ranking it in the 22nd percentile, indicating a relatively shallow talent pool in these sectors. This suggests that the city may be less suited for businesses in the tech, finance, or consulting industries, but may be more attractive for industries with fewer specialized roles, such as logistics or manufacturing.

The Indianapolis-Carmel-Greenwood metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a tight labor market and moderate wage growth, but also poses challenges in terms of labor demand and cost of living. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses considering this location is the potential difficulty in attracting and retaining top talent due to the competitive labor market and relatively high cost of living.
