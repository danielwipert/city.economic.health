Economic Analysis

St. Louis earns a B- grade, ranking 47th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a low unemployment rate of 3.50% and above-average labor force participation of 65.27%, indicating a healthy and constructive labor market. However, stagnant wage growth of +0.66% YoY raises concerns about consumer spending power and the overall vitality of the local economy.

The city's affordable cost of living, with a composite score of 3.50, contrasts with the slowing housing market, where homes are taking 17.9% more days to sell than last year, with an average of 46 days on market. This slowdown may indicate a normalization of the market, providing buyers with more negotiating room, or it could signal demand erosion due to economic softness. The decline in building permits by 6.99% YoY suggests the latter scenario is more likely, as it indicates a lack of supply response to meet demand.

St. Louis offers businesses a relatively affordable cost of living and a stable labor market, but the primary risk lies in the city's sluggish wage growth and potentially weakening housing market. As a business location, St. Louis presents a mixed opportunity, where companies must balance the benefits of a low-cost environment with the potential risks of a softening local economy.
