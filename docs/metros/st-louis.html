Economic Analysis

St. Louis presents a mixed economic picture, characterised by a stable labour market and sluggish employment trends. The city's unemployment rate of 3.5% is slightly below the national average, having changed little over the past year, with a modest year-on-year increase of 0.2 percentage points. The civilian labour force has grown 1.01% over the same period. However, labour demand remains subdued, with nonfarm employment growth flat at -0.01% year-on-year and a composite labour demand score of 4.29, indicating below-average performance. Notably, average hourly earnings growth is weak at 0.71%, ranking St. Louis in the 8th percentile nationally and suggesting limited wage pressure and potentially subdued consumer income growth.

The housing market is also showing signs of deceleration. Residential building permits have fallen 7.0% year-on-year, while homes are taking longer to sell, with median days on market rising 17.95% to 46 days. This trend ranks among the slowest in the peer group. Although the cost of living remains moderate relative to incomes, this advantage is offset by limited office occupancy momentum and compressed working hours, with weekly hours 1.8% below their 12-month trend. These indicators collectively point to constrained business activity and housing turnover.

Looking ahead, St. Louis is likely to experience continued modest labour force expansion, but faces challenges from stagnant employment growth, weak earnings momentum, and a cooling housing market. Without a pickup in private sector job creation or wage growth, the city's economic trajectory is likely to remain below potential.
