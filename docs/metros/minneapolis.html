Economic Analysis

Minneapolis's labour market is exhibiting signs of moderation, following a period of recent strength. The unemployment rate has risen to 4.0%, a 1.6 percentage point increase from a year ago, indicating a loosening of the market. Although workforce supply growth remains above average, it has slowed to 0.64% year-on-year, while employment growth is modest at 0.55%. Labour demand, as measured by the composite index, is near neutral at 4.8, with weekly hours 0.27% below the 12-month trend. Wage growth, at 1.77%, remains subdued, lagging national trends and limiting real income gains.

The housing market is also showing signs of softening demand. Residential building permits have declined sharply, down 12.25% year-on-year, one of the weakest performances nationally. The median days on market has risen to 36 days, a 9.1% increase from last year, consistent with higher financing costs and reduced buyer affordability. The cost of living, at 2.74, is slightly elevated relative to incomes, and office occupancy, at 2.12, remains below par, reflecting the persistence of hybrid work patterns and commercial real estate challenges.

Looking ahead, Minneapolis faces near-term headwinds in housing and labour demand, with limited wage growth and construction activity constraining momentum. While the economy is not in contraction, the pace of expansion has cooled. A return to stronger growth will depend on renewed business investment and stabilization in the office sector.
