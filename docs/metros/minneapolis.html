Economic Analysis

The Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 34.6th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a composite score that reflects a mix of mediocre labor demand and high cost pressures. The city's economic character is most defined by its near-median labor demand composite score of 4.15 and its bottom-tier unemployment rate, which signals a challenging hiring environment. Specifically, the labor demand is characterized by a -0.11% employment growth rate and a -1.135% weekly hours deviation from trend.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate of -0.11% and weekly hours deviation of -1.135% from trend suggest a labor market that is not expanding, with hours worked also below the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, rather than genuine demand expansion or survivor squeeze. The labor demand composite score of 4.15, ranking near the median at 41st percentile, further reinforces this assessment.

Unemployment

Although the exact unemployment rate is not available, the city's bottom-tier ranking at 19th percentile implies a relatively high unemployment rate. This suggests a labor market with some slack, making it easier for businesses to hire, but also potentially weakening local consumer demand. As a result, businesses may face less wage pressure, but also a less robust local economy.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate of +1.47% is relatively slow, ranking in the bottom tier at 15th percentile. This stagnant wage growth implies a flat cost environment for employers, but also weak bargaining power for workers. As a result, businesses may enjoy lower labor costs, but workers may have limited purchasing power.

Cost of Living

With a cost of living ratio of 2.00 and a percentile rank of 59th, the city is relatively affordable compared to its peers. This means that businesses can attract talent without needing to offer significant wage premiums, providing a talent attraction advantage. The city's affordability is a notable strength in its economic profile.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force is growing at a rate of +0.64% year-over-year, ranking near the median at 43rd percentile. This expanding labor force supply suggests that the city has a growing pool of potential workers, making it easier for businesses to hire and expand. However, the moderate growth rate may not be sufficient to meet the needs of rapidly growing businesses.

Building Permits

The year-over-year change in building permits is -12.25%, ranking below average at 34th percentile. This decline in permits suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation. As a result, businesses may face increasing competition for talent and rising labor costs.

Days on Market

The current median days on market is 36 days, with a year-over-year increase of +9.1%, ranking above average at 69th percentile. This slower market means that homes are sitting longer before being sold, making it more accessible for workers to relocate to the city. However, this trend may also signal a weakening housing market.

Office Economy

The city's professional and office worker share is 0.60, ranking in the bottom tier at 6th percentile. This suggests a relatively shallow talent pool in professional and office sectors, making the city less suited for businesses that require a deep knowledge-economy talent pool, such as tech, finance, or consulting. Instead, the city may be more suitable for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses.

The Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington metro area offers businesses a relatively affordable cost of living and a growing labor force supply, but also poses significant challenges, including a tight labor market and stagnant wage growth. The single biggest risk or constraint for businesses is the potential for future affordability challenges and workforce accommodation constraints due to the decline in building permits, which may limit the city's ability to attract and retain talent.
