## Original analysis draft (for reference)

Minneapolis’s labour market shows signs of moderation after recent strength. The city’s unemployment rate stands at 4.0% (101A_unemployment), up 1.6 percentage points from a year ago (101A_unemp_yoy_pp), indicating a loosening market. While still above average, workforce supply growth has slowed to 0.64% year-on-year (102A_clf_yoy), and employment growth is modest at 0.55% (107E_employment_growth_yoy). Labour demand, as reflected in the composite index (107E_ldc_composite: 4.8), is near neutral, with weekly hours slightly below the 12-month trend (-0.271%). Wage growth remains subdued at 1.77% (103B_earnings_yoy), lagging behind national trends and limiting real income gains.

Housing market dynamics suggest softening demand. Residential building permits have declined sharply, falling 12.25% year-on-year (200B_permits_yoy), among the weakest performances nationally. Homes are taking longer to sell, with median days on market rising to 36 days (204A_dom_level_days), up 9.1% from last year (204A_dom_yoy_pct), a trend consistent with higher financing costs and reduced buyer affordability. The cost of living, while not extreme, is slightly elevated relative to incomes (104C_col: 2.74), and office occupancy remains below par (105C_owr: 2.12), reflecting persistent hybrid work patterns and commercial real estate challenges.

**Outlook:** Minneapolis faces near-term headwinds in housing and labour demand, with limited wage growth and construction activity constraining momentum. While the economy is not in contraction, the pace of expansion has cooled, and a return to stronger growth will depend on renewed business investment and stabilization in the office sector.
