Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Minneapolis's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. The labour market shows a moderate unemployment rate of 4.0%, although this has increased by 1.6 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labor force has grown at a modest pace of 0.64% year-over-year, suggesting a relatively stable workforce supply. Average hourly earnings have risen by 1.77% year-over-year, indicating some upward pressure on wages. However, the cost of living composite score of 2.74 suggests that the city's affordability is a concern, which may impact labour market dynamics.

Business activity in Minneapolis appears to be experiencing some stress, with nonfarm payroll employment growth slowing to 0.55% year-over-year. The labor demand composite score of 4.8 indicates a relatively weak employment signal, while the weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline is -0.271%, suggesting a slight decline in working hours. The office worker ratio composite score of 2.12 is also relatively low, which may indicate some challenges in the city's office market. On the other hand, the residential housing market shows some signs of strength, with a median days on market of 36 days, although this has increased by 9.09% year-over-year. The decline in residential building permits by 12.25% year-over-year may indicate some caution in the housing market.

The housing market's performance is a notable aspect of Minneapolis's economy, with the days on market composite score ranking relatively high at 68. However, the decline in residential building permits and the increase in median days on market year-over-year suggest some potential stress points. Overall, Minneapolis's economic indicators suggest a city that is experiencing some challenges, particularly in the labour market and business activity. While there are some positive signals, such as rising average hourly earnings, the city's affordability concerns and slowing employment growth are notable weaknesses.

Closing outlook: Minneapolis's economy is likely to face some headwinds in the short term, driven by slowing employment growth and affordability concerns. However, the city's relatively stable workforce supply and rising wages may provide some support for the labour market. Policymakers and businesses will need to monitor the city's economic indicators closely to address the challenges and capitalize on the strengths, particularly in the housing market.
