Economic Analysis

The Boston-Cambridge-Newton metro area has an overall grade of C+ with a composite score ranking it at the 40.0th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its weak labor demand, with a labor demand composite score ranking in the bottom tier at the 18th percentile, and its strong labor force growth, with a year-over-year growth rate of 2.87%. The combination of these metrics suggests a complex economic environment with both challenges and opportunities.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is -0.96% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.622% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is experiencing a decline in job growth and hours worked. This suggests that businesses may face less competition for workers, but also may indicate a weaker local economy.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is 3.90%, ranking near the median at the 44th percentile. This suggests that the labor market has some slack, but is not extremely loose. For a business trying to hire in this city, this means that there may be a sufficient pool of available workers, but wages may not be under significant upward pressure.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth rate in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is +2.98%, ranking below average at the 40th percentile. This moderate wage growth rate suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not extremely quickly. This also means that workers in the city have some, but not exceptional, bargaining power and purchasing power.

Cost of Living

Boston-Cambridge-Newton has a cost of living score ranking in the 24th percentile, with a PSF of $500/sqft and average hourly earnings of $46.57/hr, resulting in a ratio of 10.74. This indicates that the city is relatively expensive compared to its peers. The fact that the PSF is not falling year-over-year suggests that affordability is not improving. This means that businesses may need to offer wage premiums to attract talent to the area.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is growing at a rate of 2.87% year-over-year, ranking in the top tier at the 96th percentile. This suggests that the workforce supply is expanding rapidly, which is a positive sign for businesses looking to hire in the area. This growth in labor force supply can help support business expansion and reduce the risk of labor shortages.

Building Permits

The number of residential building permits in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is declining at a rate of -14.97% year-over-year, ranking below average at the 24th percentile. This suggests that housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability challenges and constraints on workforce accommodation. This could be a concern for businesses that rely on a mobile workforce or need to attract talent from other areas.

Days on Market

The median days on market for homes in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is currently 30 days, with a year-over-year increase of 20.0%, ranking above average at the 76th percentile. This suggests that the housing market is slowing down, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. However, this slowdown may also indicate a weakening of demand in the local economy.

Office Economy

Boston-Cambridge-Newton has a deep professional talent pool, with an office economy score ranking above average at the 71st percentile. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors that require a high concentration of specialized knowledge workers. However, the city may be less suitable for businesses in industrial or logistics-dominant sectors that require a different type of workforce.

The Boston-Cambridge-Newton metro area offers businesses a unique combination of a strong and growing labor force, a deep professional talent pool, and a slowing housing market. However, the city's weak labor demand, high cost of living, and tightening housing supply are significant risks and constraints that decision-makers should carefully consider when evaluating this location for their business.
