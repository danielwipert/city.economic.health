Economic Analysis

The Boston-Cambridge-Newton metro area has an overall grade of C, ranking 36.7th percentile out of 50 US metros, with a labor demand composite score of 3.73. This city's current economic character is most defined by its below-average labor demand and extremely low days on market, with homes selling very quickly at a median of 25 days, down 3.9% year-over-year. The combination of -0.41% employment growth and -0.399% weekly hours deviation signals a contraction in labor demand.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is -0.41% year-over-year, and weekly hours are deviating -0.399% from the city's own 12-month baseline. This combination signals a contraction in labor demand, indicating that the city is not adding jobs at a pace that would suggest genuine demand expansion. Instead, it may be experiencing a period of payroll contraction.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is 3.90%, which is below average, ranking in the 20th percentile. This suggests that the labor market has some slack, but not excessively so, making it moderately challenging for businesses to hire. However, the relatively low unemployment rate also implies that there may be some wage pressure, as workers have more bargaining power in a tighter labor market.

Wage Growth

The year-over-year wage growth in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is +3.66%, which is near the median, ranking in the 48th percentile. This moderate wage growth suggests that labor costs for employers are rising, but not excessively so, and worker purchasing power is increasing. However, the wage growth is not strong enough to suggest a highly competitive labor market.

Cost of Living

Boston-Cambridge-Newton has a cost of living score that ranks in the 24th percentile, with a PSF of $497/sqft, which is decreasing by 1.6% year-over-year, and an average hourly earnings of $46.24/hr, resulting in a ratio of 10.75. This suggests that the city is relatively expensive, making it challenging to attract talent without offering wage premiums. The decreasing PSF is a positive sign, but the overall cost of living remains a concern.

Labor Force Growth

The civilian labor force in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is growing at a rate of +2.87% year-over-year, which is in the top tier, ranking in the 98th percentile. This suggests that the workforce supply is expanding rapidly, making it easier for businesses to hire and find talent. The strong labor force growth is a significant advantage for companies looking to locate in the area.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is decreasing by -20.56% year-over-year, which is in the bottom tier, ranking in the 18th percentile. This suggests that the housing supply is tightening, which may lead to future affordability concerns and challenges in accommodating a growing workforce. The decline in building permits is a significant risk factor for businesses considering location in the area.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Boston-Cambridge-Newton is 25 days, which is decreasing by 3.9% year-over-year. This suggests that the housing market is highly competitive, making it challenging for relocating workers to find homes. The fast-paced market may require businesses to offer relocation assistance or other incentives to attract talent.

Office Economy

Boston-Cambridge-Newton has a deep professional talent pool, with an office economy score ranking in the 60th percentile. This suggests that the city is well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require a high concentration of specialized knowledge workers. However, the city may be less suitable for industries that require a large workforce in industrial or logistics roles.

The Boston-Cambridge-Newton metro area offers businesses a highly educated and growing workforce, with a strong labor force growth rate and a deep professional talent pool. However, the city's extremely low days on market and tightening housing supply pose significant risks to affordability and workforce accommodation, making it essential for decision-makers to factor in these constraints when considering location in the area.
