## Original analysis draft (for reference)

San Jose’s economy shows a mixed but increasingly strained profile, with labor market stability masking underlying weaknesses in employment growth and wage momentum. The unemployment rate holds steady at 4.0% (101A_unemployment), unchanged from a year ago (101A_unemp_yoy_pp = 0.0), while the labor force has expanded modestly by 0.78% year-on-year (102A_clf_yoy). However, nonfarm employment has contracted slightly, with a -0.16% year-on-year decline (107E_employment_growth_yoy), and the labor demand composite score (107E_ldc_composite = 4.08) ranks in the 32nd percentile, indicating tepid job market expansion. Wage growth is particularly weak, with average hourly earnings rising just 0.56% over the past year (103B_earnings_yoy), placing it in the 6th percentile nationally—suggesting limited real income gains amid persistent cost pressures.

Housing market indicators reveal both supply improvements and entrenched affordability challenges. Residential building permits have surged 195% year-on-year (200B_permits_yoy), a sharp short-term rebound that signals responsive supply-side activity and ranks in the 98th percentile. Yet, despite this construction boom, the cost of living remains extremely high, with a composite score of 6.02 (104C_col, 2nd percentile), reflecting deep structural imbalances between housing costs and earnings. Homes are selling quickly—just 24 days on market (204A_dom_level_days), though that represents a 9.1% increase from last year (204A_dom_yoy_pct)—indicating demand remains robust even as affordability constrains transactions. Office utilization, measured by the office worker ratio (105C_owr = 2.02), remains subdued (26th percentile), consistent with long-term hybrid work trends weighing on downtown economic vitality.

**Outlook:** San Jose faces persistent affordability and wage stagnation headwinds, despite recent housing supply gains and labor market resilience. Without stronger employment and income growth, economic momentum will remain constrained.
