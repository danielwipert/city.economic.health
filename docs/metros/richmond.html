Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

Richmond's economic indicators present a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 3.3%, with a year-over-year increase of 0.7 percentage points. However, average hourly earnings have risen by 6.81% over the same period, suggesting that workers are experiencing wage growth. The civilian labor force has contracted by 1.22% year-over-year, which may indicate a tightening labour market or a decline in workforce supply.

In terms of business activity, Richmond's nonfarm payroll employment has grown by 0.29% year-over-year, a modest expansion. The labor demand composite score is 5.99, indicating a relatively strong demand for labor. The office worker ratio composite score is 2.98, suggesting a moderate level of office-based employment. However, the residential building permits have declined by 20.27% year-over-year, which may indicate a slowdown in the housing market. The median days on market have decreased by 11.36% year-over-year, currently standing at 39 days, suggesting a relatively fast-paced housing market.

The cost of living composite score is 2.28, indicating a moderate cost of living in Richmond. The city's overall economic grade is a "B", with a weighted percentile score of 53.0, suggesting an average performance. While there are some stress points, such as the decline in residential building permits and the increase in unemployment, the city's labour market and business activity indicators suggest a relatively stable economy. The key strength of Richmond's economy is its wage growth, which has outpaced many other cities. Overall, Richmond's economy appears to be navigating a period of moderate growth, with some areas of concern that warrant close monitoring.

Closing outlook: Richmond's economy is expected to continue on a path of moderate growth, driven by its relatively strong labour market and business activity. However, the decline in residential building permits and the increase in unemployment rate warrant attention from policymakers and businesses. With its average economic grade and moderate cost of living, Richmond remains an attractive location for businesses and workers, but it will be important to monitor the city's economic indicators closely to ensure that it can address its challenges and continue to grow.
