Economic Analysis

Richmond's labour market presents a mixed picture, with underlying strengths offset by emerging challenges. The unemployment rate of 3.3% is within a healthy range, although it has risen by 0.7 percentage points year-on-year, indicating some softening. A 1.22% decline in the civilian labour force over the past year may be attributed to demographic trends or decreased worker attraction, rather than immediate job losses. Nevertheless, labour demand remains relatively firm, as evidenced by a strong labour demand composite score of 5.99 and a 0.29% year-on-year increase in nonfarm employment. Average hourly earnings are growing at a robust 6.81% rate, outpacing inflation and supporting household resilience.

In contrast, the housing and business sectors are showing signs of cooling momentum. Residential construction has slowed significantly, with building permits down 20.27% year-on-year, one of the weakest national performances. The housing market is characterised by tight supply, with homes selling quickly - a median of just 39 days on market, representing an 11.36% decrease from the previous year. The cost of living is moderate, at 2.28, while office activity is slightly above average, at 2.98, although this does not indicate strong commercial revitalisation.

Looking ahead, Richmond's economy is navigating a transition, underpinned by wage growth and steady employment, but constrained by a declining construction sector and stagnant labour supply. Without an influx of workers or renewed business investment, growth is likely to remain subdued in the near term, with housing affordability pressures persisting despite broader economic stability.
