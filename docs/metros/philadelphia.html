Economic Analysis

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metro area has an overall grade of B- with a composite score ranking at the 48.3th percentile out of 50 US metros. This city's economic character is most defined by its near-median labor demand composite score of 4.68 and its relatively slow labor force growth rate of +0.93% YoY. The labor demand and labor force growth metrics are crucial in understanding the city's current economic landscape, with the labor demand score indicating a moderate level of job growth and the labor force growth rate suggesting a slowly expanding workforce.

Labor Demand

The employment growth rate in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is +0.27% YoY, and while the weekly hours deviation from trend is not available, the labor demand composite score of 4.68 suggests a near-median level of job growth. This combination signals a moderate expansion of genuine demand, rather than a strong contraction or survivor squeeze. The labor market is growing, but not at a rapid pace.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is not available, but its percentile rank of 19th suggests a relatively high unemployment rate compared to other metros. This implies a labor market with some slack, making it easier for businesses to hire workers without facing intense competition. However, this also means that local consumer demand may be weaker due to the higher unemployment rate.

Wage Growth

The wage growth rate in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is not available, but its percentile rank of 50th suggests a moderate level of wage growth. This implies that labor costs for employers are rising at a moderate pace, and worker purchasing power is increasing, but not at a rapid rate. The moderate wage growth rate may help attract and retain workers, but it also means that businesses may face rising labor costs.

Cost of Living

Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington has a cost of living percentile rank of 50th, indicating a near-median level of affordability relative to peers. This means that the city is neither particularly affordable nor expensive, and businesses may need to offer moderate wage premiums to attract talent. The city's affordability is average, making it neither a major advantage nor a disadvantage for talent attraction.

Labor Force Growth

The labor force in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is growing at a rate of +0.93% YoY, indicating a slowly expanding workforce. This suggests that the supply of workers is increasing, but at a moderate pace, which may help businesses meet their hiring needs. However, the slow growth rate may also limit the city's ability to support rapid business expansion.

Building Permits

The number of building permits in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is increasing at a rate of +4.14% YoY, indicating an expansion of housing supply. This suggests that developer confidence is rising, and future housing supply is likely to increase, which may improve affordability and support workforce growth. The growing housing supply may help accommodate a growing workforce and make the city more attractive to businesses.

Days on Market

The median days on market in Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington is 43 days, with a YoY increase of +10.3%, ranking at the 84th percentile. This suggests a slower market, where homes are taking longer to sell, making it more accessible for workers relocating to the city. The slower market may give relocating workers more time to find a home, reducing the stress and competition associated with rapid sales.

Office Economy

Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington has an office economy percentile rank of 60th, indicating a deep professional talent pool. This makes the city well-suited for businesses in the tech, finance, consulting, and HQ sectors, which require specialized knowledge-economy workers. However, the city may be less suited for industrial or logistics-dominant businesses, which require a different type of workforce.

The Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington metro area offers businesses a moderate level of labor demand, a slowly expanding workforce, and a relatively affordable cost of living. However, the single biggest risk or constraint for businesses in this city is the relatively high unemployment rate, which may limit local consumer demand and create challenges for businesses that rely on a strong local market.
