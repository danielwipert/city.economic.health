Economic Analysis

Philadelphia's economy earns a grade of B, or 54.3rd percentile out of 50 US metros, with a top-tier nonfarm employment growth of +1.16% YoY, signaling an economically expanding market. However, this growth is juxtaposed with an elevated unemployment rate of 4.00%, indicating labor market slack and potentially weakened local consumer demand. Sluggish wage growth of +2.21% YoY implies a lower cost environment for employers, but may also signal weak worker bargaining power or softening demand.

The housing market shows a nuanced story, with days on market increasing by 10.3% YoY to an average of 43 days, indicating a moderate buyer advantage. This slowing market, combined with strong employment growth, suggests a healthy normalization, where buyers gain negotiating room and affordability pressure moderates. However, the city's below-average wage growth and elevated unemployment rate raise questions about the sustainability of this trend.

Philadelphia offers a dynamic and expanding economy, with rapid job creation and a relatively low-cost environment. However, labor market slack and sluggish wage growth pose a primary risk, potentially impacting the long-term sustainability of the city's economic momentum.
