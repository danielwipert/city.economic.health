Economic Analysis

## Original Qwen draft (for reference)

The economic indicators for Fresno suggest a mixed picture, with both positive and negative signals. On the labour market front, the city's unemployment rate stands at 8.2%, which, although high, has seen a modest decline of 0.3 percentage points year-over-year. The civilian labour force has grown at a rate of 1.87% year-over-year, indicating a moderate expansion of the workforce supply. Furthermore, average hourly earnings have risen by 7.01% year-over-year, a notable increase that could support consumer spending and overall economic activity.

However, other indicators point to stress points in the economy. The residential building permits have declined by 22.67% year-over-year, suggesting a slowdown in the housing market. Additionally, the median days on market for homes has increased by 10.87% year-over-year, reaching a level of 51 days, which could indicate a softening of demand. The labour demand composite score is also relatively low, and the weekly hours deviation from the 12-month baseline has fallen by 1.104%, indicating a potential weakening of labour market conditions. The cost of living composite score is moderate, at 2.04, but the office worker ratio composite score is relatively low, at 0.78, which may limit the city's attractiveness to businesses and talent.

In terms of business activity, the nonfarm payroll employment growth has been sluggish, at 0.22% year-over-year, which may be a concern for the city's economic expansion. Despite these challenges, Fresno's earnings growth and moderate workforce expansion are positives that could support the local economy in the longer term. However, the city's economic outlook is likely to be influenced by its ability to address the stress points in the housing market and labour demand.

Closing outlook: Fresno's economy faces challenges, particularly in the housing market and labour demand, but its earnings growth and workforce expansion are positives. To achieve more robust growth, the city will need to address these stress points and attract more businesses and investment.
